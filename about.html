About

What this is.

The Quarterly Ledger is an independent reference work for public securities that fall outside the attention of Wall Street. It is published four times a year, free, without registration.

The premise

There are roughly ten thousand companies filing with the SEC. Fewer than a thousand receive meaningful sell-side coverage. The remainder — small-capital issuers, insider-controlled floats, orphaned spin-offs, quiet compounders in unsexy industries — are priced by whoever happens to be at the screen that day. That is where this publication looks.

Each quarter, we pull the full EDGAR XBRL universe, run it through a Munger-framework screen, and publish tear sheets on every name that passes. Names are sorted into four tiers and an extended register. Between issues we flag promotions, demotions, and special-situation clocks.

The format

One page per security. Ten-year financial history, fundamental metrics, a tier classification with a numerical score, an analyst summary written from the filings, a catalyst/risk ledger, and a section called What a screen can't see — the filing-level detail that doesn't surface in any screener.

The editorial stance

This is not a newsletter of hot picks. It is a reference. Tear sheets are meant to be revisited — the next issue will re-rank the same universe, and most names will still be there. A stock that is cheap in April is often still cheap in July; conviction comes from watching a thesis survive four quarters of fresh filings, not from the first call.

We do not sell trading signals. We do not predict quarterly earnings. We do not publish price targets. What we publish is a standing register — the kind of reference Value Line produced for generations, updated for the small-capital universe the majors no longer cover.

Who reads this

Long-duration individual investors, small partnerships, family offices, and professionals in adjacent seats who want a standing reference without a Bloomberg subscription. If you measure holding periods in years and read 10-Ks on weekends, this is for you.

Who writes this

The editorial team is small and anonymous by design. No positions are disclosed on individual names. The disciplines applied — the screen, the scoring framework, the filing-level verification — are the product; the byline is not.
