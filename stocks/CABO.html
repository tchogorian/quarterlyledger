Analyst Summary

Cable One is a rural-focused U.S. cable broadband operator (~999K data subs, 88K video, 94K voice as of 12/31/2025). 2025 revenue $1.50B; Adjusted EBITDA $801.7M (53.4% margin); capex $285.3M; total debt ~$3.2B. Residential data 60.1% of revenue, business data 15.3%, residential video 12.5%. The stock at $107 implies ~$600M equity cap and ~5x EV/EBITDA on a high-margin infrastructure business — historically cable has traded 8-10x. The compression is real: rural fiber overbuilders (Brightspeed, Conexon, Tilson, T-Mobile FWA, Starlink) are encroaching on Cable One's footprint, and the company has been losing data subs and pricing power.

The bear case is structural: Cable One bought into a thesis (rural broadband monopolies) that subsidies (BEAD) and capital availability have invalidated. Moody's December 2025 SGL-2 downgrade and dividend cut signal cash strain. Drawing $575M on a $1.25B revolver to fund convert maturity leaves $675M unfunded capacity — workable but tight, especially with the Mega Broadband Investments (MBI) put-call dynamics still unresolved. Permanent refi via high-yield will price at much wider spreads than the 0% converts; interest expense will jump materially. Subscriber base shrinking + EBITDA compressing + capex needed to defend = the textbook "melting ice cube" risk.

The bull case is cyclical-trough math: at 5x EBITDA the market is pricing structural decline. If subscribers stabilize (FWA peaks, fiber buildout slows under cost pressure, competitor capex retreats in 2026-27), Cable One's free cash flow generation is meaningful — $801M EBITDA less ~$285M capex less ~$200M+ interest = ~$300M FCF. Debt paydown of $403M in 2025 demonstrates the model still throws cash. A take-private bid (Eagle Communications, Searchlight, GTCR have all been rumored historical names in cable) at $200-250 would more than double the equity.

What a Screen Can't See

The 10-K disclosure on overlapping fiber overbuild percentage (the % of homes-passed where a fiber competitor exists) is the single most important number. Management has guided that competitive overbuild is now meaningfully above 30% of footprint. The MBI minority stake structure embeds put rights that could force Cable One to buy back the remaining MBI equity for cash — a hidden ~$500M+ liability that pressures the balance sheet alongside the now-drawn revolver. Also note the Q4 2025 EPS comps: Adjusted EBITDA margin holding above 53% on declining revenue indicates aggressive opex management — a sign the team is pulling cost levers, but those levers don't repeat indefinitely.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months) - **May 2026:** Q1 2026 earnings — first read on subscriber trends post-convert refi. - **Mid-2026:** Permanent high-yield bond issuance to term out revolver draw. - **H2 2026:** MBI put/call window resolution; potential strategic alternatives review.
