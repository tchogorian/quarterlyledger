Analyst Summary

Brighthouse is one of the largest U.S. annuity and life insurance providers — over 2.0 million policies in force, ~$206B AUM at year-end 2025, spun out of MetLife in 2017. Per the 10-K (Feb 2026): combined RBC ratio 456%, total adjusted statutory capital $5.4B, both above the 400-450% target range. Aquarian Capital (a private insurance-focused holding/PE platform) agreed Nov 6, 2025 to acquire BHF for $70/share = $4.1B equity value, all cash. Aquarian stated the deal is fully committed financing — not subject to a financing condition — and there will be no incremental debt at Brighthouse or Aquarian's existing insurance businesses. BHF special meeting Feb 12, 2026 approved the merger.

Bear case: U.S. life insurance deals require approval from every state where the insurer is domiciled or licensed. New York DFS in particular has historically imposed structural conditions (capital maintenance agreements, dividend restrictions, additional surplus contributions) that have killed or delayed deals — most notably the failed Apollo/Athene-PHL Variable arbitrage and prior PE-into-insurance battles. Aquarian is a relatively new sponsor with limited public regulatory track record. State commissioners may demand a "Form A" Holding Company Act filing review of 6-12 months minimum. Variable annuity book sensitivity to equity markets means a 2026 selloff could prompt regulators to demand additional capital — possibly forcing Aquarian to renegotiate.

Bull case: shareholder vote already passed (Feb 12, 2026) — that risk is gone. RBC at 456% gives Aquarian a massive capital cushion to absorb regulatory demands without renegotiating price. The deal is committed-financed with no MAC tied to capital markets. Brighthouse is not a stranger to insurance regulators — it has been a standalone public insurer since 2017 with established Form A precedents in its key states. Spread of 12.5% gross / 22.7% annualized is generous relative to the residual risk. Cushion: at $62.22, downside on deal break is probably $50-55 (pre-deal trading range), so risk/reward is roughly $7.78 up vs. $7-12 down — slightly skewed favorably.

What a Screen Can't See

The 10-K (per Stocktitan summary) explicitly addresses how the merger limits the company's flexibility: covenants restrict capital actions, M&A, and material commitments while the deal pends. More importantly, BHF's variable annuity hedging program (Shield Annuities, the largest registered index-linked annuity book in the U.S.) has historically caused statutory capital volatility — Aquarian and the NY DFS will both want to confirm hedging assumptions hold up under the Form A review's stress tests. The 456% RBC is well above any reasonable regulatory ask, but a sharp Q2/Q3 2026 equity drawdown could compress the cushion fast and slow the timeline.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months) - Form A regulatory filings (NY DFS, Delaware, Massachusetts) decisions Q2-Q3 2026 - Possible Q4 2026 closing payment of $70 cash - Q1 2026 earnings (any RBC weakness flagged by regulators)

Principal Risks

State insurance commissioners (esp NY) could impose conditions; Aquarian financing/credit; long regulatory timeline.
