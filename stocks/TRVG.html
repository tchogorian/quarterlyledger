Analyst Summary

trivago operates a hotel meta-search platform that aggregates rates from online travel agencies (OTAs) and direct hotel sites, monetizing on a cost-per-click (CPC) and cost-per-acquisition (CPA) basis. The company is incorporated in the Netherlands, has its operating center in Düsseldorf, and trades in New York via American Depositary Shares (each ADS represents five Class A shares). Expedia Group has been the controlling majority shareholder since 2013 through Class B super-voting stock. The screen flagged TRVG at P/B 0.79, P/E 15.4 on a $196M market cap. The 2025 20-F shows a real operational rebound: revenue €548.9M (vs. €460.8M in 2024, +19%), net income €11.2M (vs. a €23.7M loss prior year), Adjusted EBITDA €15.8M, and cash & equivalents of €130.9M plus €11.9M short-term investments — roughly €143M in net cash with no funded debt of consequence.

The bear case is structural revenue concentration. Per the 20-F, "we continue to generate the great majority of our revenue from our largest OTA advertisers, including brands affiliated with Booking Holdings… and Expedia Group." Two parents (one of them the controlling shareholder) determine the bid environment, and either can throttle CPC budgets at any moment. trivago has now alternated between profit and loss for several years, with 2024's net loss of €23.7M proving this is not a steady-state earner. Expedia's dual-class control means minority ADS holders cannot vote out management, force a sale, change the dividend policy, or even compel buybacks. Television-led marketing means costs are committed in advance; if advertiser CPCs drop mid-year, operating leverage works in reverse. Google's hotel module continues to siphon top-of-funnel intent.

The bull case is that the rebound is real: 19% revenue growth, swing to GAAP profit, €143M of net cash on a €196M market cap implies the operating business trades for ~$50M, or roughly 3-4x trailing operating cash. Management has been repurchasing shares opportunistically. Catalysts: continued Booking/Expedia ad-spend recovery as OTA share wars intensify, possible take-private bid from Expedia at a modest premium (Expedia would consolidate fully and remove a redundant public listing), and AI-search integrations that could re-rate meta-search assets. Capital allocation has been disciplined — no major acquisitions, light cost base, balance sheet preserved. With 353M shares outstanding (115.6M Class A + 237.5M Class B), the float is small post-Expedia.

What a Screen Can't See

The screen sees P/B 0.79 and P/E 15.4 but misses three filing-level realities. First, the P/E is on a year that swung from loss to profit; on the trailing two-year average, EPS is roughly break-even — multiple is meaningless. Second, the dual-class structure: Class B shares carry super-voting rights and are held almost entirely by Expedia, so corporate-action optionality belongs to one shareholder. Reserved-matter list in the 20-F includes share repurchases — trivago cannot buy back stock without Expedia's blessing. Third, the related-party accounts receivable line of €21.8M is from Expedia entities, meaning a meaningful share of working capital is collected from the controlling parent. If Expedia ever shifts its bidding logic, trivago feels it in cash before P&L. The "majority shareholder is also a top customer" pattern is a governance red flag deserving a discount, not a premium.
