Analyst Summary

Chemed operates two segments: VITAS Hospice (~70% of revenue, growing 10-11% in 2025 ex-Medicare cap with 18-19% EBITDA margins) and Roto-Rooter (~30%, 2.4-3.0% growth in 2025 with 25-26% EBITDA margins). 2025 service revenues $2.53B (+4.1% YoY); adjusted diluted EPS $21.55 (-6.8% as Roto-Rooter softened). Q4 2025 buyback: 400K shares for $174.6M at $436.39 avg. Remaining authorization at year-end ~$127.3M plus a fresh $300M added. Dividend $0.60 quarterly (+20% increase). 2026 guidance: adjusted EPS $23.25-$24.25, implying recovery and the algorithm restarting.

The bear case is real near-term softness: Roto-Rooter's residential demand has been weak through 2025, lead generation declined as Google/Yelp ad costs rose and DIY behavior post-COVID stuck. The Q2 2025 executive management departure announcement injected uncertainty. VITAS faces ongoing Medicare hospice cap exposure (limits Medicare reimbursement at the program level) and DOJ scrutiny historically — VITAS settled a False Claims Act case in 2017. Hospice utilization growth post-COVID has decelerated. CHE multiple at ~18x EPS and ~13x EV/EBITDA isn't cheap on a single-year basis. Insider ownership is low (<2%), so this is family-disciplined-by-history, not family-controlled in equity terms.

The bull case is the model: capital-light services, two non-cyclical end markets (death and clogged drains), pricing power, dual-segment diversification, and management that has demonstrated 50+ years of disciplined capital return. The 2025 EPS dip is largely Roto-Rooter cyclical/lead-gen — fixable. Hospice (VITAS) growth at +10% with population aging and Medicare Advantage hospice carve-in pilots expanding addressable market is structural. The $300M buyback at $400-500/share (vs. recent $700+ peak) is opportunistic; coupled with the dividend hike it signals board confidence. 2026 EPS guide of $23.25-$24.25 implies +10-13% growth, putting forward P/E at ~17-18x for a defensive compounder.

What a Screen Can't See

The 10-K segment footnotes break out VITAS revenue between "routine home care" (commodity hospice) and "general inpatient" / "continuous care" (high-acuity, much higher reimbursement). The mix-shift toward higher-acuity patients drives blended revenue per patient day and is a hidden margin lever. VITAS's Medicare cap calculation is patient-cohort-based (not aggregate), so cap impact is geographically lumpy and disclosed by program. Roto-Rooter's franchise vs. company-owned mix matters for incremental margins on demand recovery — company-owned operations carry higher fixed costs. The company's net cash position and absence of meaningful debt give the buyback authorization real teeth — at ~$436/share execution, $300M = ~4-5% of shares retired.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months) - **April 2026:** Q1 2026 earnings — first read on Roto-Rooter recovery and 2026 guide. - **2026:** $300M+ buyback execution into any further weakness. - **2026:** New CEO/Roto-Rooter leadership impact post mid-2025 management departure.
