Analyst Summary

Motorcar Parts of America is a remanufacturer and supplier of aftermarket automotive hard parts — alternators, starters, brake calipers, brake master cylinders, brake boosters, turbochargers, wheel hubs, and rotating-electrical components — sold primarily into the U.S. retail aftermarket through the largest national chains. The fiscal-2025 (year ended March 31, 2025) 10-K confirms the customer roster: Advance Auto Parts, AutoZone, Genuine Parts (NAPA), and O'Reilly Auto Parts, distributing through approximately 26,000 retail outlets. The screen flagged MPAA at P/B 0.83, P/E 124.8 on a $220M market cap (the elevated P/E reflects depressed trailing earnings, not premium quality). Fiscal 2025 highlights from the 10-K: net sales increased 5.5% to a record $757.4M, gross profit increased 16.1% to $153.8M, and gross margin expanded 1.8 percentage points to 20.3%. Share count is 19,435,706 as of June 2, 2025. The company operates in the U.S., Mexico (~5,000 employees, primarily production), Canada, Malaysia, and China, with ~5,300 production employees in total and ~4,900 Mexican production workers covered by a local union.

The bear case is severe customer concentration combined with persistent margin pressure. The 10-K states: "Sales to our three largest customers in the aggregate represented 86%, and sales to our largest customer represented 39% of our net sales during fiscal 2025." That is monopsony-grade exposure. The same disclosure flags ongoing pressure from major customers to "offer lower prices," extend payment terms, and accept core-return adjustments — structural margin headwinds that recur every renewal. MPAA's business model requires carrying massive inventories of "Used Cores" plus remanufactured stock, making working capital chronically heavy and inventory-days extended. Recent fiscal years saw GAAP net losses with diluted EPS reported at -$0.99, -$2.51, and -$0.22 across fiscal 2025/2024/2023 (potential common shares anti-dilutive). Tariff exposure on Chinese components and Mexican production is real and rising under shifting trade policy. Foreign currency exposure (peso, ringgit, RMB) adds non-operating volatility.

The bull case is operational inflection: revenue at a record $757M, gross margin recovery to 20.3% (up 1.8pp), and gross profit up 16% — all evidence the working-capital reset and price actions are flowing through. The aftermarket parts category benefits from rising vehicle age, deferred new-car purchases, and post-warranty repair demand — all secular tailwinds independent of new-vehicle SAAR. Catalysts include continued margin restoration (a return to mid-teens operating margin would translate to $50-70M operating income on $760M sales), normalization of inventory financing costs as rates recede, and potential strategic interest from larger industrial roll-ups. Capital allocation: the 10-K discloses a modest share-repurchase program with remaining authorization at year-end, plus debt paydown as cash generation improves. With only 8 holders of record listed (likely most shares in street name), institutional concentration is meaningful.

What a Screen Can't See

Several filing-level points reshape the thesis. First, the trailing P/E is misleading because GAAP losses include large non-cash items (FX revaluation, core-accounting adjustments, mark-to-market on supply-chain financing). EBITDA and operating cash flow have been positive while GAAP net income was negative — the working-capital cycle drives the disconnect. Second, MPAA uses extensive non-recourse accounts-receivable factoring with its three large customers; this is a hidden financing line that is not "debt" on a screen but functions like one and re-prices when SOFR moves. Third, the 39%-of-revenue largest customer is not named in the public filing, but trade press strongly suggests AutoZone — losing or even renegotiating that single program would be company-defining. Fourth, the company invested in diagnostic and EV-component capabilities (e.g., test equipment for hybrid/EV motors and inverters) that gives it a foothold as drivetrain technology shifts; this optionality is invisible on a P/B screen.
