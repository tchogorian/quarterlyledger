Analyst Summary

Arcellx (ACLX) is a clinical/early-commercial cell-therapy company being acquired by Gilead Sciences via Ravens Sub, Inc. (a Delaware merger sub) for $115.00 cash per share PLUS one CVR worth $5.00 contingent on cumulative worldwide sales of anitocabtagene autoleucel (anito-cel) exceeding $6.0 billion on or before December 31, 2029, payable March 31, 2030. Total enterprise value approximately $4.5B. The SC 14D9/A filed April 17, 2026 confirms the offer was extended to April 27, 2026 (from April 24) "to allow additional time for the satisfaction of the remaining condition" — essentially the ACCC 14-day waiting period that runs to 10:00 a.m. ET April 27. As of April 16, 2026, 10,271,823 shares had been tendered (17.5% of outstanding). The Minimum Tender Condition is a majority of outstanding shares; given the deal premium and clean approvals, the back-end DGCL §251(h) short-form merger should follow promptly after the offer closes.

The bear case is narrow but real. CVR realization probability: anito-cel's $6B cumulative-sales hurdle by end-2029 requires the product to ramp from current pre-/early-launch revenue to a multi-billion-dollar annual run rate within ~3 years. CAR-T BCMA competition (Bristol-Myers Squibb's Abecma, J&J/Legend's Carvykti) is fierce; Carvykti alone did >$1B in 2024. Arcellx/Gilead's anito-cel must capture meaningful share in relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma to clear $6B cumulative through 2029 — possible but not free money. CVR holders also bear the risk that Gilead chooses commercial strategies (pricing, geographic launches) that maximize long-term NPV but suppress near-term sales below the threshold. Tender-offer mechanics: even with all approvals, a small chance exists that minimum tender condition isn't met, requiring extensions.

The bull case is the dominant case here: at $115.05 vs $115.00 cash floor, you're paying a $0.05 premium for a CVR that an internal probability-weighted expected value of $0.50–$2.00 per share (10–40% probability of hitting $6B sales hurdle). Annualized return on the cash leg is essentially negative carry (you're paying through par by $0.05 for ~10 days = ~ -1.5% annualized), but the CVR optionality more than compensates. All regulatory hurdles cleared (HSR cleared earlier; FCA Austria cleared April 14, 2026; ACCC published positive determination April 13, 2026, with 14-day waiting period expiring at 10:00 a.m. ET April 27, 2026 — the EXACT timing aligns with the tender expiration). Squeeze-out merger at $115 cash for non-tendering holders follows under DGCL §251(h).

What a Screen Can't See

The CVR is structured with a single bullet payment date (March 31, 2030) tied to a cumulative-sales (not annual-sales) threshold. This means EVERY dollar of anito-cel revenue from launch through Dec 31, 2029 counts — including any 2026/2027 launch revenue, prior-period US sales, and ex-US launches Gilead pursues. The "subject to cumulative worldwide Sales (as defined in the CVR Agreement) of the Company's anitocabtagene autoleucel (anito-cel) product exceeding $6.0 billion" wording is critical — "Sales" definitions in CVR agreements often net out rebates/chargebacks/returns, so the gross revenue threshold is effectively higher than $6B. The CVR Agreement is custodied by Computershare. Gilead has motivation to drive sales (it's their product post-close) but no obligation to maximize the CVR — there's no "commercially reasonable efforts" enforcement teeth visible in the SC 14D9 disclosures.
