Analyst Summary

Exor is the holding vehicle of the Agnelli/Elkann family, controlled via Giovanni Agnelli BV (~53% of votes). Its dominant single asset is Ferrari (RACE) — even after a EUR 3B placement in H1 2025, Exor still holds ~20% economic / ~30% voting. Stellantis (STLA), CNH, and the now-being-sold Iveco stake (Tata acquired Exor's 27.1% for EUR 3.8B in 2025) anchor the auto/industrial sleeve. Healthcare is the growing pillar — Philips at 18.4%, plus bioMérieux. Lingotto, the family's internal asset manager, holds ~EUR 4.2B in funds and direct stakes. NAV is reported semi-annually with mark-to-market for listed and book/fund values for unlisted.

The bear case is structural: 53% family voting control kills any takeover premium; the holdco discount has averaged ~35% over a decade and has widened to ~45-50% as auto cyclicality bites. Ferrari fell 22% in 2025 and Stellantis fell 19%, dragging NAV per share down 8% to EUR 164.4. Italian/Dutch listing complexity, EUR currency exposure for USD investors, and the OTC ADR (EXOSF) being a stale-quoted ghost line all add friction. Lingotto carries fund-level fees, and central holdco overhead (~EUR 70m/yr) is a permanent NAV drag. There is no catalyst calendar — buybacks are episodic.

The bull case is brutal arithmetic. Ferrari alone, at Exor's ~20% stake and Ferrari's $66B market cap, is worth ~EUR 12-13B vs Exor's entire EUR 16-17B market cap. Everything else — Philips, Stellantis, CNH, Lingotto, Iveco cash — is approximately a free option. The Iveco sale closing brings ~EUR 3.8B of recyclable cash. Each EUR 1 of NAV growth at Ferrari translates to roughly EUR 0.50 of EXO price; if the discount even normalizes to its 10-year average of ~35%, EXO re-rates ~25% before any NAV growth. Buybacks at half NAV are ~2x accretive per share retired.

What a Screen Can't See

The Iveco-to-Tata transaction (announced July 30, 2025) hands Exor ~EUR 3.8B of cash mid-2026 — recycled into either a new EUR-billion buyback or healthcare deployment. John Elkann's letters increasingly emphasize Lingotto, which now owns stakes in Christian Louboutin, Shang Xia, Welltec, and a venture book — a future Lingotto IPO would crystallize ~EUR 4B of currently-marked-at-fund-NAV value. The Stellantis CEO transition (Antonio Filosa took over in mid-2025 after Carlos Tavares departed) creates execution risk but also turnaround optionality. Note also: Exor took a stake in Royal Philips at distressed levels in 2022-23-25 and is now the largest shareholder ahead of any margin recovery. The OTC EXOSF symbol is stale — execute via EXXRF (more liquid OTC ADR) or the Amsterdam EXO listing for any size.

Catalysts
