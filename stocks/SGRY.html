Analyst Summary

The activist case rests on four pillars. First, persistent underperformance versus same-industry peers despite better secular tailwinds — SGRY operates exactly the businesses (ASCs) the market loves elsewhere, suggesting the gap is operational and capital-structure related, not end-market related. Second, the balance sheet: leverage has been the key drag, and an asset-sale program targeting underperforming centers could de-lever materially while simultaneously improving consolidated margins by cutting the long tail of low-yield facilities. Third, the prior Bain Capital offer at $25.75 establishes a real-world buyer-side anchor — that price was set with full data-room access and represents a sophisticated PE shop's view of intrinsic value. The fact that the bid was withdrawn rather than topped suggests it may not have been bid up adequately, and the door for a return engagement (or another PE bidder) is open. Fourth, board-level changes are already happening — Lloyd Dean (former CEO of CommonSpirit Health) was appointed to the board, lending operating credibility to any strategic review. The asymmetry is meaningful: at $14, downside on continued operating drift is real but limited by the underlying ASC asset value; upside on either a renewed Bain bid, a different PE/strategic transaction, or a successful operational turnaround targets the prior $25.75 offer or higher.

What a Screen Can't See

Three things. First, leverage screens make SGRY look statistically expensive — high net debt / EBITDA ratios show up as a red flag — but the asset-sale plan is specifically designed to take down debt with proceeds from selling underperforming centers, which would simultaneously improve debt ratios and consolidated margins (a "double benefit" that DCFs miss). Second, the ASC industry has consolidation tailwinds beyond just procedure migration — physician partners aging out, hospitals divesting outpatient assets, and PE buyers competing for scaled platforms. SGRY is one of the few publicly-listed scaled operators, which makes it strategically scarce. Third, the prior Bain bid establishes that informed money saw real value here recently — that information doesn't show up on any quant screen but materially raises the floor on the activist outcome.

Catalysts

• 2026 annual meeting • Possible re-emergence of Bain or other PE bid • Asset sale announcements • Q1/Q2 2026 earnings ACTIVIST DEMANDS Asset sales (sell underperforming centers); debt reduction; board and management changes; strategic review; refocus on highest-margin facilities.
