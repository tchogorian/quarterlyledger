Analyst Summary

Avanos is a focused medical device company spun from Kimberly-Clark Healthcare in 2014, now operating two segments: Specialty Nutrition Systems (enteral feeding, neonatal — the legacy MIC-KEY/CORPAK brands) and Pain Management & Recovery (ON-Q surgical pain pumps, COOLIEF radiofrequency ablation, game-ready cold therapy). FY2025 net sales $701.2M (+1.9% YoY), with ~6% organic growth in "strategic" sub-segments offset by the divested respiratory health business. The deal: AIP pays $25.00/share cash, $1.272B enterprise value, premium 72.1% to April 13, 2026 close and 82.8% to 30-day VWAP. No financing contingency. Closing expected H2 2026, subject to AVNS shareholder vote and HSR. At $24.62, the math is gross spread $0.38 / $24.62 = 1.54%, annualized ~3% across the ~6-month horizon.

Bear case (deal break): the standard list. Shareholder vote could go sideways if a topping bidder doesn't emerge and a holder argues $25 underprices the COOLIEF/ablation franchise — but the 72% premium makes that unlikely. PE financing isn't a stated condition, but AIP's debt commitments could tighten if credit spreads widen. Antitrust: low risk (PE buyer, no overlap), but DOJ has been more activist in PE roll-ups; an extended HSR pull-and-refile could push closing into Q1 2027 and crush the IRR. The 10-K flagged that the company faces "intense competition" and "pricing pressure from group purchasing organizations" — if the deal breaks, the standalone re-rate to the pre-deal ~$14.50 level would be a ~40% downside hit.

Bull case: 8-month timeline is generous; closing could pull forward to Q3 2026, doubling the IRR. Overwhelming premium, board-unanimous, no financing condition, and the natural acquirer pool (other PE sponsors who passed) is large. A topping bid is unlikely but not zero — strategic medtech (Stryker, Teleflex, Becton Dickinson) could opportunistically interlope on the pain franchise. Cushion: even if the deal slips a quarter, the spread accretes; Avanos has $124M+ of cash on the balance sheet, no covenant pressure, and the standalone business produces low-double-digit FCF margins. Engaged Capital and other activist holders had been pushing for strategic alternatives — sale resolution validates that thesis.

What a Screen Can't See

Two structural items the screen can't price: (1) Engaged Capital was reportedly the activist behind the strategic-review process — their cost basis is well below $25, so they are aligned to vote yes and unlikely to hold out for more, sealing vote outcome. (2) The HSR clock is the binding constraint: AIP is a financial sponsor with no horizontal medical-device overlap, so a "pull-and-refile" is not the base case. Antitrust review timeline is closer to 30–45 days than the 6 months commonly modeled. The 10-K disclosed pending product-liability exposure on Game Ready cold-therapy products and historic Hernia mesh — these are reps-and-warranties items that matter for the deal close mechanics; the merger agreement should be read for any MAC carve-outs around legal reserves.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months) - Definitive proxy filing (~May–June 2026) and special meeting date for shareholder vote. - HSR expiration / second-request decision (~June 2026). - Deal close H2 2026; final cash distribution to holders.

Principal Risks

Standard PE financing risk; shareholder vote not yet held; 8-month timeline to close.
