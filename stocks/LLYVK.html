Analyst Summary

LLYVK is the non-voting Series C of Liberty Live Holdings, the post-split-off entity holding 29.8% of Live Nation Entertainment, 100% of Quint hospitality, and a Meyer Shank Racing minority. At ~$91-96 with ~$5.8-8.8B market cap (depending on snapshot), LLYVK trades at a modest spread below LLYVA. Look-through math: 69.6M LYV shares at recent prices imply ~$10.8B of equity, less ~$1.3B attributed holdco debt = ~$9.5B equity stub on a combined A+K market cap of ~$14B — a discount typical of newly-spun Liberty entities and historically one that compresses.

The bear case is identical to LLYVA plus a small structural overhang: non-voting shares trade at persistent discounts in dual-class structures, and the post-split-off forced selling window from Liberty Media holders, ETFs that couldn't hold the new ticker, and tax-loss sellers will linger through Q2 2026. Net debt at the holdco level (margin loan, exchangeables) makes equity sensitivity to LYV ~1.5x. DOJ Ticketmaster overhang is real. And the dual-class A/K structure means LLYVK holders cannot vote on any future buy-out, special distribution, or roll-up — they take what LLYVA voters approve.

The bull case is the same Malone playbook plus liquidity advantage: LLYVK has the larger float between the two classes, making it the institutional-sized entry point. Pair-trade opportunities exist between LLYVA and LLYVK as the spread oscillates. Most importantly, Liberty Media's history shows that dual-class structures eventually collapse into single-class (FWONA/FWONK collapse precedent) — when that happens, LLYVK typically trades up to the LLYVA price. Buybacks at material NAV discount, possible eventual roll-up into LYV directly, and the seasoning of forced selling all argue for a 12-18 month re-rating.

What a Screen Can't See

Screens treat LLYVA and LLYVK as separate tickers with separate prices and miss the pair-trade structure entirely. The economic interest is identical; the only differences are voting rights and float. Liberty has historically collapsed similar dual-class pairs (FWON, BATR) into single classes — when that happens, the discount class snaps to parity. Additionally, the December 2025 424B3 prospectus discloses that the C shares received no special distribution rights or preference — meaning future buybacks can target whichever class is cheaper, structurally favoring LLYVK on a per-share accretion basis. Most quant screens will not pick up this asymmetry until Q2 2026 disclosures show actual repurchase mix.
