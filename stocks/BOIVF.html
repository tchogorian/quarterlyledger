Analyst Summary

Bolloré SE (BOIVF on US OTC, BOL on Euronext Paris) is the Bolloré family's flagship holdco. Mcap ~$14.8B at $5.50/share, indicative NAV ~$9.20/share = ~40% discount. Holdings: 29.3% controlling stake in Vivendi SE (Vivendi itself trades at ~50% discount to its own UMG/portfolio NAV — discount-on-discount); 18% direct + ~10% indirect (via Vivendi) total ~28% in Universal Music Group; substantial residual cash from the 2022 Bolloré Logistics divestment to CMA CGM (€4.65B); media stakes in Canal+, Havas, and indirect Lagardère/Editis interests via the Vivendi spin restructuring. Plus a meaningful net cash and equivalents position on balance sheet. Family control via Compagnie de l'Odet (>70% of Bolloré SE).

Bear case: structural French holdco discount has persisted for decades and there's no obvious reason it must close. Triple-decker pyramid (Odet → Bolloré → Vivendi → UMG) means each layer takes a discount, and Vincent Bolloré has shown willingness to use the structure for family-friendly transactions that minorities perceive as unfair (the 2024 Vivendi spin restructure being Exhibit A — and the subject of pending litigation). Court of Cassation could rule against the minority shareholders in May 2026, removing the mandatory tender catalyst entirely. Vincent Bolloré is 73; succession to his son Yannick is in motion but not seamless. UMG itself faces streaming-economics revaluation risk if Spotify/Apple Music renegotiate terms. Pershing's $60B UMG bid is one investor's proposal — not a deal; could fizzle. French withholding tax on dividends is a frictional drag for US holders.

Bull case: this is the cheapest blue-chip holdco in Europe per AGT (Asset Value Investors) and other dedicated holdco specialists. NAV discount at ~40% vs. 26% historical average implies 22% mean-reversion alone before any catalyst. Pershing Square's April 2026 $60B UMG offer (and proposed NYSE re-listing) marks UMG well above current trading levels — Bolloré's effective 28% UMG ownership crystallizes at premium if the deal advances. Court of Cassation hearing May 22, 2026: if Bolloré loses, mandatory tender at fair value re-rates the Vivendi stake (could be 30-50% above current Vivendi price) — pure free option. Bolloré is aggressively buying back shares ("cannibalising structure" in AGT's words) — every buyback collapses the discount and accretes NAV/share faster.

What a Screen Can't See

The Compagnie de l'Odet pyramid is a tax-optimized vehicle that allows Bolloré family to control >70% of voting rights with <50% economic interest. Ironically, Vincent Bolloré has been collapsing the pyramid via cross-buybacks that concentrate his ownership and reduce the layers — bullish for minorities at every level if they can get out at NAV. The 2022 Bolloré Logistics sale to CMA CGM brought €4.65B in cash that has not yet been fully redeployed; per AGT and similar specialist pieces, that cash is sitting on the balance sheet earning 3-4% short-rate yield while waiting for a Vincent-style asset deal. Vivendi spin litigation: in 2024 Vivendi spun Canal+, Havas and Louis Hachette into separate vehicles via a controversial scheme of arrangement; minority shareholders sued, and the appeal reaches the Court of Cassation May 22, 2026. A loss for Bolloré triggers a mandatory tender for Vivendi at independently-determined fair value — potentially €15-18 vs. current ~€10. Buyback firepower: Bolloré has consistently used 5-10% of its mcap annually on buybacks at deep NAV discounts.

Catalysts

• Court of Cassation ruling May 2026 • Pershing Square / UMG transaction • UMG NYSE re-listing • Continued Bolloré buybacks (cannibalising structure) • Possible simplification of Bolloré/Odet pyramid
