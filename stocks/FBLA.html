Analyst Summary

FB Bancorp is the New Orleans-headquartered holding company for Fidelity Bank, a 1908-vintage Louisiana community bank with 18 full-service branches across central and southern Louisiana, including its main office, two drive-up branches and 14 stand-alone ATMs. The bank converted from mutual-to-stock form on October 22, 2024, completing its IPO on the NASDAQ at $10/share and raising $198.4 million from the sale of nearly 19.9 million shares. Stated strategy at IPO was to diversify the loan book, expand activity in Baton Rouge and Lafayette, and pursue branch/bank M&A. Screen prints P/B 0.74, P/E 63.5, MCap $192M — a textbook freshly-converted thrift profile: equity inflated by IPO proceeds, ROE temporarily near-zero while management deploys the cash. The 10-K describes the lending mix as commercial real estate, residential, and consumer, with all the standard CECL allowance for credit losses methodology.

The bear case starts with the optically punitive 63.5x trailing P/E — that figure reflects an over-capitalized post-IPO balance sheet earning low single-digit ROE while sitting on excess proceeds and amortizing IPO/ESOP/foundation costs. Louisiana has structurally weaker demographics than higher-growth Sun Belt markets; insurance market dislocation post-Ida and rising property reinsurance costs pressure CRE collateral values. Management's own forward-looking statements highlight risks from "fluctuations in real estate values," demand for loans in the local market, and ability to access cost-effective funding. Hurricane and flood concentration in Louisiana creates a tail risk that does not exist for, say, a Midwest community bank. There is no Sun Belt growth tailwind to mask sub-scale operating leverage.

The bull case is the second-step conversion playbook with an above-average jurisdiction premium. On November 13, 2025 FB Bancorp announced a stock buyback authorizing repurchase of up to 1,983,750 shares (~10% of outstanding), echoing the SRBK template — buy back stock at 0.74x book, return excess capital, then sell to a regional acquirer in 2-4 years. The book value per share floor (~$13.50 implied at P/B 0.74 on a $10/share IPO) is meaningful, the loan book is plain-vanilla community lending, and Fidelity Bank's $1.1B in assets and $769M in deposits is the right size to be either the consolidator or the consolidated party in the gradually-shrinking Gulf community bank ecosystem. Capital ratios should be very high; tangible book per share should grow as buybacks shrink the denominator.

What a Screen Can't See

The 10-K notes that book value of land, buildings, leasehold improvements, furniture, fixtures and equipment was $57 million — non-trivial real estate exposure for a $192M-market-cap holding company headquartered in downtown New Orleans (Louisiana 70130 per the cover page). Several Fidelity branches are owned rather than leased; in a community-bank acquisition, owned premises are typically liquidated post-deal, providing additional asset-realization optionality not visible in tangible book. Equally important: the IPO's ~19.9M share issuance at $10 means the entire current public float was created in October 2024 — selling pressure from IPO-allocated thrift depositor accounts and arbitrage funds is still washing through, which partially explains the persistent below-book trade. As the lockup-style technical pressure decays, the discount should naturally compress.
