Analyst Summary

Cal-Maine Foods is the largest US shell-egg producer, controlling roughly the largest share of branded and private-label table eggs through 50 processing/packaging plants and an estimated ~285 million-bird US table-egg flock backdrop. Fiscal 2025 (ended May 31, 2025) was a banner year: total net sales of $4.3 billion versus $2.3 billion in fiscal 2024, with the net average selling price per dozen at $3.134 (vs $1.932 prior year), a direct beneficiary of HPAI-driven supply destruction. Management closed the Echo Lake Foods acquisition June 2, 2025, expanding into prepared/value-added foods. The screen flags net-cash, sub-3x EV/EBITDA, and a P/E around 5x, but those numbers reflect peak-cycle profitability — every long-term investor in CALM has to underwrite the next downcycle.

The bear case is straightforward: this is a commodity producer trading on cyclical-peak earnings. Conventional egg prices per dozen of $3.49 in FY25 (vs $1.73 in FY24) won't persist once the depopulated layer flock recovers. Customer concentration is extreme — Walmart/Sam's alone is 33.6% of net sales, top three customers 49.2% — and these grocers are already pushing back on pricing. The variable dividend policy (one-third of net income) means payouts will collapse alongside earnings. Management acquired Echo Lake at peak-cycle cash flow, and as of April 14, 2025 the founder Adams family ceded "controlled company" status, removing a long-standing alignment anchor and opening the door to less-disciplined capital allocation.

The bull case rests on structural egg-supply tightness. HPAI-driven culls (over 39 million additional layer hens depopulated through May 2025) have made supply restoration a multi-year process. Cage-free mandates in CA, MA, CO, WA and other states force capex that smaller competitors can't absorb, consolidating share toward CALM. The $500 million share repurchase authorization (Feb 2025) plus 32% net-cash gives optionality to buy back stock through any trough. Specialty/branded eggs (Egg-Land's Best, Land O' Lakes, 4-Grain) carry better margins and customer stickiness than commodity. Echo Lake adds a non-shell prepared-foods leg. If you assume a normalized through-cycle EPS of $4–6 and a 10–12x multiple, fair value lands materially above today's screen-trough framing.

What a Screen Can't See

The 36% operating margin in FY25 is a once-in-a-decade outlier driven by HPAI-induced shortage; FY24 operating margin was 13.4% and the prior cyclical trough was negative. The $46.8 million of goodwill is small (1.5% of total assets) — pre-Echo Lake. Family control just ended April 14, 2025, which is governance-positive on paper but removes the founder-family discipline that historically constrained M&A. The Echo Lake purchase will introduce branded-foods integration risk and cage-free capex acceleration. Cage-free now represents a growing share of "specialty" volume; specialty pricing of $2.519/dozen actually underperformed conventional in FY25 because long-term cage-free contracts didn't fully reset. Watch the company's first downcycle response: will it draw down the new $500M repo authorization aggressively into the trough?
