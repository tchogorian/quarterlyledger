Analyst Summary

Groupe Bruxelles Lambert (Euronext Brussels: GBLB; OTC ADR: GBLBF) is the Belgian listed vehicle of the Frère family (consolidated with the Desmarais Power Corp side via the legacy Pargesa structure). It owns a portfolio of European blue-chip listed minorities — Pernod Ricard, Adidas, Imerys, SGS, Holcim — and a growing private/alternative bucket including Affidea (private healthcare), Canyon Bicycles, Sanoptis (dental), and the Sienna Investment Managers alt-asset platform. NAV per share was reported at roughly EUR 113-121 against a current share price near EUR 80 (USD 84 ADR), implying a ~31% discount that is wide even by holdco standards.

The bear case is gravitational: family-controlled European holdcos almost never close their NAV discount, and several of GBL's largest listed positions are themselves under pressure. Adidas has been volatile post-Yeezy. Pernod Ricard is in a multi-year premium-spirits down-cycle as Chinese demand weakens. Holcim is cyclical. Imerys is structurally challenged in industrial minerals. The private bucket is opaque and arguably marked optimistically. And the Sienna alt-platform is a multi-year build that consumes capital before producing it. The discount has been wide for two decades; expecting it to close is the kind of mean-reversion bet that has cost value investors a lot of money in European holdcos (c.f. Pargesa, Investor AB, Industrivärden).

The bull case is that this iteration of GBL is acting differently. CEO Sophie Lambin has accelerated divestments — the Carlyle AlpInvest secondary in late 2025/early 2026 sold roughly EUR 1.7B of NAV at only a ~9% discount, dramatically inside GBL's own 31% trading discount, and recycled proceeds into share buybacks. Buying back your own stock at 0.69x NAV with cash raised at 0.91x NAV is one of the cleanest accretion trades in capital allocation. Continued private monetization plus Sienna IPO/sale optionality plus an Adidas exit on any rally would compound NAV per share even if the discount never narrows.

What a Screen Can't See

GBL's accelerated treasury share program is structurally accretive but doesn't show up in a P/E screen because the holdco doesn't earn an operating P/E. The Carlyle AlpInvest secondary deserves attention from the November 2025 Capital Markets presentation — selling EUR 1.7B at a 9% discount and using proceeds to buy back stock at a 31% discount is per-share NAV-accretive on the order of 3-4% in a single transaction. Also note that the family holding (via Power Corp / Desmarais and Frère Bourgeois) is over 50% — there is no takeover scenario, only self-driven discount narrowing.

Catalysts

• Continued private portfolio monetization • Buyback acceleration • Possible Adidas exit at premium • Sienna alt platform IPO/sale
