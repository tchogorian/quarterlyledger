Analyst Summary

Corebridge Financial is the former AIG Life & Retirement business, IPO'd by AIG in September 2022 and progressively distributed to AIG shareholders and to anchor investor Blackstone (which also took a stake in the asset management arm and provided private credit origination). The business spans individual retirement (annuities, mutual funds), group retirement (recordkeeping for K-12, higher ed, healthcare), life insurance, and institutional markets. Equitable Holdings is similarly composed: variable annuities, group retirement (former AXA Equitable), and a 65% stake in AllianceBernstein. The combined entity would be one of the largest US life and retirement platforms with substantial scale in annuity manufacturing and distribution.

The bear case starts with the deal itself. All-stock merger of equals deals historically underperform, integration of two life insurance balance sheets is genuinely complex (capital regimes, hedge programs, asset/liability matching across two general accounts), and there is meaningful regulatory risk in life insurance (state insurance commissioners across multiple states must approve). Combined entity will have significant variable annuity exposure — a product line with hedging and equity market sensitivity. Synergy estimates from these deals are typically optimistic. With essentially zero arb spread, holders take all the deal break risk for very little reward. Blackstone's role as anchor investor and asset manager creates governance complexity.

The bull case requires looking past the deal arithmetic to the pro forma franchise. The combined entity would have differentiated scale in retirement, particularly in tax-exempt markets (K-12, healthcare). Cost synergies from duplicative corporate functions are real, and revenue synergies from cross-selling Corebridge's individual annuities into Equitable's group retirement footprint could be material. The Blackstone relationship continues to provide private credit yield enhancement on the general account — a genuine structural advantage versus mutual life insurers. If the deal closes and synergies materialize, the combined entity should command a higher multiple than either standalone.

What a Screen Can't See

The Corebridge 10-K's general account asset disclosures detail the Blackstone-originated private credit allocation, which is rapidly growing as a percentage of investment portfolio and earning meaningfully higher yields than legacy public corporate bonds — providing a structural NIM advantage that takes time to compound. Also: the proxy filings for the merger will detail break fees, "go-shop" provisions (or absence thereof), and which board seats Blackstone retains in the combined entity — these governance details often determine whether minority holders get fair treatment in subsequent transactions.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months) - Definitive merger proxy filing (likely Q2 2026); shareholder vote (Q3 2026) - State insurance regulatory approvals across multiple jurisdictions (rolling through Q3-Q4 2026) - Expected close Q4 2026; first combined-entity guidance issued
