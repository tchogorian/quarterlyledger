Analyst Summary

From the actual 10-K (fiscal year ended February 1, 2025): Build-A-Bear generated $496.4M in revenue with $51.8M in net income, 55.0% retail gross margin (expanding for three consecutive years), and absolutely zero long-term debt. The company retired 6.5% of its shares outstanding in a single year through aggressive buybacks, with an additional $85M remaining on a $100M authorization.

The hidden story is the Commercial segment. Revenue grew from $18.5M to $25.4M to $31.4M over three years — a 70% cumulative increase — at a 57.2% gross margin that is HIGHER than the retail segment. This is pure brand monetization: licensing the Build-A-Bear brand to third-party retail partners (Carnival Cruise Line, Great Wolf Lodge, Girl Scouts), wholesale accounts, and entertainment ventures. The contribution margin is $16.8M on $31.4M revenue — essentially free money flowing from the brand's recognition.

The stock dropped 49% from its September 2025 high of ~$75 to ~$38. At 8.8x trailing P/E with zero debt and a growing licensing business, the market is pricing this as a dying mall retailer. But Build-A-Bear has no real competitor in experiential children's retail. The brand creates emotional attachment — children literally build their own stuffed animals. The third-party retail locations grew from 70 to 92 to 138 in three years, demonstrating that partners are paying to associate their venues with the Build-A-Bear experience.

### WHAT THE 10-K REVEALS 1. **Store productivity**: Net retail sales per square foot $492 (FY2024) — strong for specialty retail. 2. **Third-party retail**: 138 partner locations (up from 70 two years ago) including Carnival Cruise, Great Wolf Lodge, Landry's. 3. **International franchise**: 83 locations across South Africa (22), Australia (21), Gulf States (20), Chile (12), China (8). 4. **Risk Factor #3**: Dependence on licensors — specifically names Disney, Pokemon, NBCUniversal, Nintendo, Sanrio. If any major IP partner pulls licensing, traffic would drop. 5. **Share count**: 14,172,362 → 13,257,131 in one year. Net reduction of 915,231 shares = 6.5%.
