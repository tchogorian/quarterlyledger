Analyst Summary

MainStreet Bancshares is the Fairfax, Virginia-headquartered holding company for MainStreet Bank, operating seven branches in the affluent Northern Virginia / Washington D.C. metro (Herndon, Fairfax, McLean, Clarendon, Leesburg, Middleburg, plus a leased D.C. branch). At year-end 2025 the company had $2.21 billion in total assets, $1.8 billion in net loans, $1.9 billion in deposits, and stockholders' equity of $218.6 million. Tangible book value per common share rose to $25.52 (from $23.77) against a market price of $20.36 — a 20% discount to tangible book. EPS rebounded sharply to $1.76 from a $(1.60) loss in 2024, as NIM expanded from 3.13% to 3.46% and the company began winding down its money-losing Avenu Banking-as-a-Service platform.

The bear case is that Avenu's pivot is incomplete and expensive. The 2025 financial technology segment expenses remain elevated due to "shutting down the segment, such as canceling contracts." Non-performing loans of $31.5 million plus $1.7 million OREO equate to 1.50% NPAs/total assets — meaningfully above peer averages (LCNB and ENBP run near 0.1-0.2%). The efficiency ratio of 74.15%, while improved from 110.85% in 2024, remains well above the ~55-60% community-bank benchmark. The bank operates only 6 offices with 171 FTE — a small operating base for a $2.2 billion balance sheet, suggesting the Avenu wind-down hasn't yet produced peer-level cost efficiency. Northern Virginia commercial real estate — particularly office near D.C. — faces secular headwinds from federal-workforce reductions and remote work.

The bull case is a clean cyclical-trough setup. Bank capital ratios are robust: Tier 1 leverage 13.28%, CET1 15.05%, total risk-based 16.08%. With Avenu shut down, 2026 reported earnings should reflect the underlying community bank only — a much cleaner story trading at 0.80x tangible book. NIM expanded 33 basis points in 2025 with further runway as the wholesale deposit mix rolls. Net charge-offs were 0.00% in 2025 versus 0.25% in 2024 — credit is healing. The $0.40 annualized dividend has been maintained through the loss year. Northern Virginia remains one of the wealthiest U.S. metros, and MainStreet's commercial banking franchise is genuinely differentiated. If NPAs work out and efficiency drops below 65%, 2026-2027 EPS could reach $2.50-3.00, supporting a re-rate well above tangible book.

What a Screen Can't See

The single most important narrative in MNSB's 10-K is the explicit decision to "pivot away from operating certain BaaS services." Avenu was MainStreet's strategic bet on Banking-as-a-Service — the segment burned cash, drove the 2024 loss, and ballooned the efficiency ratio. The 10-K confirms management is incurring one-time costs to cancel contracts and accelerate exit, meaning 2025 reported earnings still understate the run-rate of the core community bank. The company's geographic concentration in McLean/Fairfax/Leesburg places it adjacent to wealthy federal-contractor clients and Loudoun County data-center growth — both meaningful tailwinds offsetting D.C. office weakness. With FTEs already cut from 204 to 171, operating leverage on revenue growth into 2026 should be substantial.
