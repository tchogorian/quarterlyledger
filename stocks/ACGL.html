Analyst Summary

From the actual 10-K (FY2025): Arch Capital produced record net income of $4.40 billion on $17.07 billion in net premiums earned, with a consolidated combined ratio of 82.8% — meaning the company earns nearly 17 cents of underwriting profit on every dollar of premium before investment income. Return on equity was 20.1%. The company repurchased $1.89 billion in stock (6% of shares) at an average price of $89.26, and has bought back 455 million shares for $7.8 billion since 2007.

The investment portfolio is $46.5 billion with an average credit quality of AA-/Aa3 and duration of 3.34 years — conservative and high-quality. Prior year reserve releases of $600M ($507M in 2024, $538M in 2023) demonstrate consistent reserving conservatism — reserves keep proving to be more than adequate. The reserve simulation shows an 18.4% cushion between recorded reserves and the 90th percentile adverse scenario.

At 8.3x trailing earnings for a 20% ROE specialty insurer buying back 6% of its shares annually, the market is applying the permanent "insurance discount" to a business that compounds intrinsic value faster than most tech companies. Buffett built Berkshire Hathaway on exactly this model — using insurance float to invest while underwriting generates its own profits. Arch is executing the same playbook.

### WHAT THE 10-K REVEALS 1. **Reinsurance segment** (47.6% of premiums): 80.8% combined ratio — printing money. 2. **Mortgage segment**: 14.6% combined ratio. Essentially a license to collect premiums. 3. **Long-tail adverse development**: $82M in 2025 from casualty (2021-2024 accident years) — a yellow flag worth monitoring but small relative to $24.5B total reserves. 4. **$702M remaining buyback authorization** as of Feb 24, 2026. Post year-end: 4.3M additional shares bought for $405M.
