Analyst Summary

Elliott's track record on consumer staples activism is mixed but trending positive. Their successful campaigns at Suncor and Salesforce show patience and operational depth; their less successful ones (AT&T, Twitter pre-Musk) show that even Elliott can struggle with truly entrenched cultures. PepsiCo CEO Ramon Laguarta is in a vulnerable position — under his tenure North American beverage volumes have lagged, snack pricing has run too hot, and the FritoLay momentum that defined PEP's premium-to-KO multiple in 2018-22 has faded.

The December 2025 SKU rationalization agreement is a real win for Elliott — 20% of products is a serious cut that touches both Frito and PBNA portfolios, eliminating low-velocity items and freeing shelf space for higher-margin brands. The harder question is refranchising. Refranchising bottlers takes 3-5 years to fully execute and creates near-term EPS headwinds before the multiple re-rate plays out. Coca-Cola's experience is the playbook: KO went from 60% bottler-owned to <5% over four years, with EPS roughly flat during transition but multiple expanding from 18x to 25x.

The thesis monetizes through three legs: (1) operational margin lift from SKU rationalization, ~$1B annualized run-rate by end of 2026, (2) potential refranchising announcement at 2026 investor day generating multiple expansion toward 19-20x, and (3) governance refresh that disciplines capital allocation. Stock has not fully re-rated to KO multiple — there's still 15-20% of compression to close, plus underlying earnings growth.

What a Screen Can't See

The Frito-Lay business is the crown jewel that screens treat as a single line. Frito accounts for ~55% of PEP operating profit at >25% margins. The beverage drag is masking how strong the snacks franchise actually is. If Elliott successfully forces PBNA refranchising, the residual PEP becomes essentially a global snacks pure-play with KO's Beverage capital model — a structurally higher-multiple business than the current consolidated entity.

The other invisible dynamic: Elliott is methodical and persistent. They rarely accept partial wins as "done." Expect continued board pressure through 2026 investor day and beyond, even after the SKU agreement.
