Analyst Summary

Inter Parfums licenses fragrance rights from luxury and lifestyle brands (Jimmy Choo, Coach, Montblanc, Lacoste, Karl Lagerfeld, GUESS, Donna Karan, Oscar de la Renta, Roberto Cavalli, Salvatore Ferragamo and others) and runs the entire value chain — formulation, manufacturing, packaging, distribution. 2025 was a record year: sales of $1,490M with Q4 at $386M (best-ever Q4). Management 2026 guidance is approximately $1.48B in sales and $4.85 diluted EPS. In January, the company announced exclusive long-term worldwide fragrance licenses with David Beckham and Nautica plus a 15-year GUESS extension through 2048 — three contractual annuities added in a single month.

The bear case: 2026 guide of $1.48B implies flat-to-slightly-down sales versus 2025, suggesting the post-COVID fragrance boom has peaked. Inter Parfums is at the mercy of license counterparties — Burberry's 2012 in-housing remains the cautionary tale. SG&A is climbing as the company invests in proprietary brand Solferino (40 doors at end-2025, +50 in H1 2026) and Lacoste's 2024 license addition. Founder-controlled governance limits activist optionality. At a ~25x P/E on $4.85 EPS, the stock is not statistically cheap.

The bull thesis is a multi-decade compounder hiding in plain sight: founder-CEO Jean Madar bought $25k of stock recently (small but meaningful given his existing stake), the 15-year GUESS renewal extends a critical license to 2048, Beckham and Nautica add fresh growth platforms, and proprietary brand Solferino opens an ultra-luxury channel where Inter Parfums captures retail-style margins instead of license royalties. Recession-resistant category, asset-light model, family-aligned capital allocation, and steady FCF that funds dividends and small buybacks. Compounders rarely look "cheap" — they look durable.

What a Screen Can't See

The January 2026 announcement bundling David Beckham, Nautica, and the 15-year GUESS extension to 2048 is a contractual annuity stack that cannot be picked up by quantitative screens — it shows up only in management commentary and the eventual 10-K license schedule. The CEO's open-market purchase, while small in absolute dollars, is meaningful insider-alignment signal in a name where founders Madar and Benacin already control voting power. Solferino — the company's first proprietary ultra-luxury brand — is the optionality nobody is paying for: at 40 doors today and adding 50 more in H1 2026, even a modest hit-rate adds margin layers far above license-royalty economics.
