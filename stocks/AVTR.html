Analyst Summary

Avantor is the #2 global distributor of lab supplies, chemicals, and instruments to pharma/biotech, healthcare, and education customers — competing principally with Thermo Fisher's Fisher Scientific channel. The business was rolled up by New Mountain Capital and IPO'd in 2019; today does ~$6.7B revenue with mid-teens EBITDA margins. Engine Capital targeted AVTR because: (1) margins are 400-500bps below Thermo's distribution segment despite similar product mix, (2) the post-COVID destocking cycle in biopharma was particularly punishing for Avantor's mid-stream position, (3) ~$5.5B of debt creates equity convexity if EBITDA inflects, and (4) the Education and Services categories are obvious divestiture candidates worth several billion to focused buyers (private label, education-only distributors).

Bear case: Lab supply distribution is a mature, low-growth, capital-intensive business with structural pricing pressure from large pharma customers consolidating spend. Thermo's scale advantage compounds — their integrated proprietary product portfolio (Gibco, Invitrogen, Applied Biosystems) gives them margin leverage Avantor cannot match. The $5.5B debt load means a small EBITDA miss translates to large equity drawdown — leverage cuts both ways. Cost programs are typically half as effective as announced. PE buyers (Carlyle, KKR, Bain) all looked at AVTR previously and passed. New CEO is a strong operator but inherits a structurally challenged asset.

Bull case: Biopharma capex cycle is bottoming — biotech funding has stabilized, large pharma is restarting R&D spend, and inventory destocking is largely complete. Operating leverage is enormous — every 100bps of margin expansion = ~$300M of EBITDA = $2-3B of equity value at current multiples. Engine Capital's intrinsic value bridge ($17-19 sale, $22-26 standalone) is anchored to peer multiples (Thermo distribution at 13-14x EBITDA vs AVTR at 8-9x). Ligner's Cytiva pedigree is exactly right for the role — he ran a similarly structured business. PE sponsors like Carlyle/KKR have done multiple lab supply deals (Catalent, IQVIA) and at $8 the equity check is small enough to be digestible. Re-rate to $13-15 on operational milestones; $17-22 on sale outcome.

What a Screen Can't See

The Engine Capital letter (August 2025) explicitly named the Education segment and the Pharma Services category as divestiture candidates with named potential buyers — a tell that Engine has done the SOTP work and identified actual willing acquirers, not just hypothetical. New CEO Ligner came directly from Cytiva, which Danaher itself extracted enormous margin from (~30%+ EBITDA margins on a similar mid-stream lab business) — the playbook is well-established. Insider buying by directors in late 2025 / early 2026 was modest but notable in an industry where insider buying is rare. The capital structure has multiple bond maturities in 2027-2028 that act as forcing function — refinancing risk pressures the board to monetize. New Mountain Capital still owns a meaningful stake from the IPO and is a natural ally for a sale process.
