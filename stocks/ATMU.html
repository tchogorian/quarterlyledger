Analyst Summary

Atmus is the Fleetguard filtration business that Cummins housed for decades — fuel, lube, air, hydraulic and crankcase filtration consumed by heavy-duty diesel engines (on-highway trucks, off-highway equipment, marine, power gen). FY2025 net sales $1,764M, GAAP net income $207M (~11.8% margin), adjusted EBITDA $354M (20.0% margin), adjusted FCF $158M. Balance sheet shows $236M cash vs ~$540M long-term debt at year-end, with the Koch Filter close in January 2026 adding ~$450M of incremental debt against ~$156M of acquired revenue (13.9x EBITDA paid). New segment reporting in 2026: Power Solutions (legacy diesel) + Industrial Solutions (Koch + air filtration). The screen catches a low-teens EV/EBITDA cyclical industrial; the reality is an aftermarket-led specialty filtration franchise with consolidation runway.

Bear case: heavy-duty truck cycle is mature; EV and hydrogen powertrains structurally erode the 20-year tail of diesel filtration consumables. Customer concentration is severe — Cummins remains a top-3 customer under a long-term supply agreement that anyone can poke holes in. Koch Filter integration risk is real (paid 13.9x trailing EBITDA for a private company), and post-deal leverage moves toward 2.5x+ before synergies. Tariffs on imported filter media and Chinese components squeeze gross margin. The 10-K flags risk that "we may not realize the anticipated benefits" of acquisitions, and that "demand for diesel-powered" equipment could decline. A cyclical industrial trading at 16x earnings is not screaming cheap on multiples.

Bull case: aftermarket ~80% of segment revenue — recurring, pricing-led, and tied to installed base, not new-build cycle. Industrial air filtration is structurally faster-growing (data center buildout, IRA-funded U.S. manufacturing, indoor air quality post-COVID) at 5–7% organic vs 2–3% for diesel. Management has run capital allocation cleanly post-spin: paid down separation debt, initiated dividend, executed buyback, and now bolt-on M&A at reasonable multiples. Cushion: $160M+ FCF with no near-term maturities, ~$5B mcap = 3.1% FCF yield pre-Koch, mid-single-digit accretion in 2026. Even modeling diesel decline, industrial mix shift drives margin into the 22%+ range over 3–5 years. Optionality on full strategic acquisition by larger industrial (Donaldson, Parker, Cummins re-acquisition not impossible).

What a Screen Can't See

The Koch Filter deal converts the segment narrative entirely — pre-deal screens classify ATMU under "Construction & Farm Machinery" (Cummins comp), pricing it as a derivative diesel cyclical. Post-deal, ~10% of revenue and a faster-growing slice belongs to industrial HVAC/data-center filtration with mid-single-digit organic growth and 20%+ EBITDA margins. The 10-K discloses Cummins concentration via a long-term supply agreement that auto-extends — a hidden moat: ATMU is the single-source filter spec on Cummins-platformed engines and that spec sticks for the engine's 15–20 year service life regardless of who owns the truck. The exchange-offer mechanic in March 2024 left CMI index funds force-selling ATMU into a thin float; that overhang is largely cleared but explains why the stock still trades below pure-play filtration peers (Donaldson at ~16–18x EBITDA).

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months) - Q1 2026 earnings (May 2026): first quarter with Koch Filter consolidated; segment disclosure for new Industrial Solutions segment. - Synergy/integration update mid-year: management guided to high-single-digit ROIC by 2028; any signs of revenue cross-sell into data center customers re-rates the multiple. - Buyback expansion / second bolt-on M&A announcement; Investor Day H2 2026 plausible.
