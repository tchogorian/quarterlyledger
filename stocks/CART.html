Analyst Summary

Maplebear (Instacart) operates the largest U.S. online grocery marketplace (~75% category share by transactions). 2025 GTV $37.2B (+11% YoY); revenue $3.74B (+11%); advertising & other $1.07B (+11%); operating cash flow $971M; share buybacks $1.4B in 2025. Q4 2025 GTV grew 14% — strongest in three years. Stock at $45 = ~$12B market cap on a business generating ~$1B+ FCF and holding net cash. The Sequoia/D1/Tiger sponsor base has been steadily distributing/selling, but the equity has absorbed it cleanly because management has been buying back roughly at the pace of secondary supply.

The bear case: Instacart's GTV growth depends on partnerships (Costco, Sam's, Aldi, Kroger, etc.) that are increasingly building their own first-party delivery (Walmart Spark, Kroger Boost, Amazon Fresh). DoorDash entered grocery aggressively in 2024-25 with its own ad-tech stack. Consumer spend on third-party grocery delivery is a luxury and recession-sensitive. The advertising business — while high-margin — is concentrated among CPG brands whose ad spend is being squeezed by retail-media competition (Walmart Connect, Amazon, Kroger Precision Marketing). Mix-shift toward in-store pickup (lower take-rate) compresses revenue/GTV ratio. And a $12B cap on $3.7B revenue is no longer cheap.

The bull case is operating leverage on a maturing platform. The ad business at $1.07B is roughly 28% of revenue but contributes meaningfully more than 28% of contribution profit — likely 50%+ — and compounds at 11%+ as more brands enter the platform. Q4 GTV reacceleration to +14% suggests the platform's value prop (selection + Caper Carts + Connected Stores enterprise SaaS) is compounding network effects. Management buying back $1.4B/yr against a $12B cap is an 11%+ shareholder-yield rate while the business grows double-digit. Sponsor exhaustion + buyback floor = classic post-IPO setup that typically rerates 12-24 months after the last sponsor exit.

What a Screen Can't See

The 10-K segment disclosures show advertising-and-other revenue carries far higher contribution margin than transaction revenue — and the company's enterprise platform (Caper smart carts, Connected Stores, Storefront Pro) generates recurring SaaS-like revenue from retailers themselves, not just consumers. This high-margin enterprise revenue is buried in the "advertising and other" line. Sponsor 13F/13D filings reveal Sequoia, D1 Capital, and Tiger Global stake levels — incremental quarterly disposals are now small relative to buyback authorization, meaning supply/demand is flipping in favor of holders. Watch for any "release from registration" filings indicating final sponsor exit.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months) - **May 2026:** Q1 2026 earnings — read on advertising growth and GTV. - **2026:** Sponsor secondary completion (Sequoia, D1) clears overhang. - **2026:** Continued ~$300M+ quarterly buyback execution at sub-$50.
