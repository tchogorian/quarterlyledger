Analyst Summary

LCNB Corp. is the holding company for LCNB National Bank, a Lebanon, Ohio-headquartered community bank with roughly $2.27 billion in average assets, $1.84 billion in deposits, and a wealth management division providing trust, fiduciary, and investment management services. Net interest income for 2025 was $70.4 million, producing a tax-equivalent NIM of 3.50% — a meaningful expansion from 2.91% in 2024 as fixed-rate assets repriced and deposit costs stabilized. EPS rebounded to $1.63 diluted on 14.09 million weighted average shares, against book value supporting a P/B near 0.86. Capital is healthy, with the company falling under the Basel III framework as a covered bank holding company. Net charge-offs were just $273 thousand in 2025 — extraordinarily clean credit.

The bear case starts with deposit composition: non-interest-bearing demand deposits averaged $468 million versus $1,374 million interest-bearing — only ~25% NIB, well below sticky-deposit franchises like Glacier or Hingham. Long-term debt of $104.4 million (down from $155 million) still represents real wholesale funding dependence. The 2024 acquisition of Eagle Financial Bancorp added complexity and goodwill that pressures tangible book. NIM expansion is rate-cycle dependent — if the Fed cuts further and asset yields lag, the 3.50% margin compresses. Ohio community banking is competitive (Park National, Civista, First Financial Bancorp), and LCNB has no obvious moat in deposit gathering. The wealth management line is meaningful but not disclosed at AUM-scale specificity that would let an analyst value it as a separate asset.

The bull case is the cleanest credit in the batch combined with a real franchise. NCOs of $273K on a ~$1.6 billion loan book is a 0.02% loss rate. ACL/loans rose to 0.80% from 0.70%, building cushion. Capital ratios remain well-capitalized with CET1 well above regulatory floors. The dividend has been maintained, the buyback program is in place, and the wealth management division provides fee income that smooths cyclicality. At 0.86x book and 10.2x earnings — with NIM still expanding off a low base and credit pristine — this is the kind of "boring quality" community bank that re-rates to 1.0–1.1x book in any normalization of regional bank sentiment. Eagle Financial integration should produce cost synergies in 2026.

What a Screen Can't See

LCNB's 10-K reports the "ratio of allowance for credit losses to total non-accrual loans" at 763.88% — meaning non-accruals are essentially de minimis relative to the reserve. That's a tell that the credit book is exceptionally clean and management is reserving conservatively. The Wealth Management Division offers full trust administration and IRA management through LCNB National Bank, providing a fee-income stream rare among $2 billion community banks. Related-party loans to directors and officers are disclosed as ordinary-course with normal collectability — not the red-flag pattern of self-dealing seen at some smaller controlled banks. The Federal Reserve has authority to block dividends if capital is impaired; LCNB management explicitly references maintaining a target Tier 1 ratio before paying dividends.
