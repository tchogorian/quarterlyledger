Analyst Summary

Paycom is a vertically-integrated cloud HCM (human capital management) platform serving small and mid-sized US employers. Founded by CEO Chad Richison in 1998, the company built its reputation on a single-database architecture that allows employees to self-serve payroll, benefits, and time/attendance — culminating in the BETI ("Better Employee Transaction Interface") product that lets employees run their own payroll. The 2023 BETI rollout cannibalized service revenue and rattled investors, the stock collapsed from $400+ to under $150, and the S&P 500 committee removed it on March 23, 2026 as market cap fell below the inclusion threshold.

The bear case is genuine. SMB HR SaaS is a competitive market with Paylocity, ADP Run, Gusto, and Rippling all attacking from different angles. BETI's self-service value proposition is compelling but creates pricing pressure if competitors copy it. Founder/CEO Richison has had governance flare-ups (compensation controversy) and the dual-class-ish power dynamics frustrate institutional investors. Index removal forces selling from S&P 500 ETFs, which can produce a quarter or two of overhang and depress the price even after the rebalance date as portfolio managers lazily liquidate.

The bull case is post-overhang. Index removals historically mark technical bottoms — Paycom is being sold not because of fundamentals but because of mechanical fund flows. The company still generates healthy FCF margins (rule-of-40 on lower growth), the founder owns 12%+ and is aligned, and capital return (buybacks + dividend) accelerates after the index forced-selling clears. If 2026 BETI engagement metrics show re-acceleration, the stock can rerate toward $250+. Q1 2026 earnings in May 2026 are the first post-removal print and the key tell on whether SMB hiring trends and BETI traction support a multi-year compounding thesis.

What a Screen Can't See

A screen sees declining growth and elevated EV/EBITDA — it misses the index-removal forced-selling dynamic and the FCF inflection. Paycom's true normalized FCF margin (after stripping out one-time BETI cannibalization) is likely 25%+, which combined with a still-growing top line produces a Free Cash Flow Yield well above the headline EPS multiple. Also invisible: the BETI engagement disclosures buried in 10-K MD&A — specifically the percent of clients using BETI and the per-employee transaction count, both of which leading-indicate retention and pricing power. Founder Richison's recent share repurchases (if any in the 2026 proxy) would be the cleanest insider signal.
