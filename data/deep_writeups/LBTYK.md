# {NUMBER}. LBTYK — Liberty Global Ltd. (Class C)
## HOLDCO — NAV DISCOUNT | ~53% discount to NAV | Score: 76/100

### THE SETUP
Liberty Global trades at a market cap of ~$8.3B against an estimated underlying NAV of $20-25B+ — a ~50% discount that has persisted despite a ~50% share count reduction over five years and the November 2024 Sunrise (Switzerland) spin-off. Mcap of ~$8B versus 50% stakes in Virgin Media O2 (UK) + VodafoneZiggo (Netherlands) + 100% of Telenet + ~$2-3B in Liberty Ventures + cash — the math has been screaming for years and Malone keeps grinding the float.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Liberty Global is John Malone's European cable holdco. After a decade of restructuring, the asset stack today consists of: 50% of Virgin Media O2 (UK joint venture with Telefonica — fixed/mobile combination, BT competitor, mid-fiber buildout); 50% of VodafoneZiggo (Netherlands JV with Vodafone); 100% of Telenet (Belgium, taken private in 2023); a Liberty Ventures portfolio (LatAm telecom stakes, Atlas Edge, Plume, ITV stake, public/private positions); and several billion in cash and credits. Sunrise (Switzerland) was distributed to shareholders in November 2024. The dual-share structure (LBTYA/LBTYK/LBTYB) and JV-heavy asset mix makes it ungainly to model — exactly the kind of complexity Malone uses to mint long-term shareholder returns through buybacks at deep discounts.

The bear case is the persistence of the discount. JVs are notoriously hard to unlock — partners (Telefonica, Vodafone) hold ROFRs and historically use them to block crystallization events. European telecom is a brutal industry with regulatory pressure on roaming, wholesale access, and fiber. FX (USD reporting on EUR/GBP assets) creates noise. Malone is in his 80s; succession planning at the holdco level is opaque. The "discount narrows at the IPO" thesis has been pitched for half a decade with no IPO. Even successful asset sales tend to fund more buybacks rather than special distributions, which the market has stopped rewarding.

The bull case is the reflexivity of buybacks at a 50% discount. Every dollar of buyback creates ~$2 of NAV per remaining share. Liberty has shrunk shares outstanding from ~720M in 2018 to ~350M today. With Telenet now wholly-owned and clean, a re-listing is straightforward; Virgin Media O2 has been preparing IPO machinery for two years; VodafoneZiggo's recap distributions return cash to LG. Sunrise was the proof point — distribution to holders crystallized real value. One JV monetization at fair value would erase the discount overnight.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K/20-F discloses Virgin Media O2's net leverage covenants and dividend recap mechanism — VMO2 has been distributing meaningful cash to its parents (LG and Telefonica) via leveraged recaps roughly every 18 months, which is the actual operating cash return mechanism. The Liberty Ventures portfolio is marked at cost in many cases (private positions like Plume, Atlas Edge); fair value would add several dollars per LBTYK share. Class C shares (LBTYK) are non-voting and trade at a structural ~3-5% discount to Class A (LBTYA), but they're the share Liberty repurchases against — meaning all buyback accretion accrues disproportionately to LBTYK holders.

### CATALYSTS
- Virgin Media O2 IPO or partial monetization — telegraphed for 2026
- Continued aggressive buybacks (next 10-15% of float guided)
- Telenet re-listing or secondary monetization
