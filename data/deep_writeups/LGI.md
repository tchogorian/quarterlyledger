Discounts in the 10-15% range have been a multi-year fixture, and Bulldog's amended 13D filing flags renewed activist intent. The bear case is that Bulldog's campaigns at Lazard funds have historically yielded modest results. Lazard's CEF board includes long-tenured incumbents with strong sponsor ties; voter turnout typically favors management. The fund's currency overlay strategy is opaque to retail investors and has produced uneven results across cycles, making the underlying NAV itself a moving target. Global equity CEFs face structural headwinds from ETF competition — even if LGI tenders 15-20% at 98% NAV, the residual fund continues to discount. The 11% discount has persisted through multiple activist cycles; mean reversion to discount close requires a structural change (open-ending, liquidation) that Bulldog rarely fully achieves.

The bull case is that Bulldog is patient and persistent. The all-in NAV-arbitrage return over a 12-18 month holding period plausibly hits mid-teens annualized. Downside is narrow — investors clip a ~10% distribution while waiting, and the residual exposure to a global equity portfolio at an 11% discount is benign in most market regimes.
