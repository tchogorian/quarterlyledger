# {NUMBER}. FWONK — Liberty Media Corp - Formula One Group (Series C)
## HOLDCO — NAV DISCOUNT | F1 + 86% MotoGP tracker | Score: 78/100

### THE SETUP
Tracker stock holding 100% of Formula One plus 86% of MotoGP (Dorna closed Nov 2024) trading at ~24% discount to indicative NAV of $115. Liberty Media has a multi-decade track record of collapsing tracker stocks into operating companies (SiriusXM, Live Nation, Atlanta Braves) — collapsing the FWONK tracker is the multi-year catalyst.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
FWONK tracks Liberty's premium global motorsports portfolio. Formula One generates ~$4.5B revenue (2025, +23% YoY) from race-promotion fees, broadcasting rights (Sky, ESPN, F1+ streaming), sponsorships and Paddock Club hospitality. The Concorde Agreement with the 10 F1 teams runs through 2025 and is being renegotiated. MotoGP — acquired November 2024 from Bridgepoint/CPP for EUR 4.2B — adds the world's premier motorcycle racing series with ~$500M revenue and similar broadcast-rights flywheel. Together: pure-play global motorsports rights at $87.86 versus $115 sum-of-parts.

Bear case: Tracker stocks structurally trade at discounts because the holders own no claim on F1's actual cash — the parent (Liberty Media) controls capital allocation. Wells Fargo cut PT to $89 in April 2026 citing race-calendar saturation (24 races) and Las Vegas GP attendance softness. The "Drive to Survive" Netflix-driven US fan growth has plateaued. Andretti/Cadillac entry as 11th team dilutes prize-money pool. MotoGP integration is unproven and was acquired at a full price (~17x EBITDA). Concorde Agreement renewal could shift profit share toward teams, compressing F1 EBITDA margin from ~35% toward 28-30%.

Bull/catalyst: Sell-side consensus values F1 alone at $20-22B (vs Liberty's 2017 acquisition price of $4.4B) — this is real compounding. Liberty's stated intent to eventually convert tracker into operating-company shares is well-telegraphed; the SiriusXM precedent (collapsed in 2024) closed a similar 15-20% discount. Goldman maintains Buy. F1+ direct-to-consumer streaming launches monetize the long-tail US/India/Brazil markets. With LLYV split-off complete and SIRI simplified, F1 group is the next obvious Liberty cleanup. A tracker-collapse announcement is the catalyst that closes 15-20% discount in days.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Liberty's 10-K segment disclosures show F1's "Other F1 revenue" line (Paddock Club, sponsorship outside the title sponsor pool) growing 30%+ — this is the highest-margin revenue and rarely modeled by sell-side. The Concorde Agreement clause on prize-money pool (currently 50% of EBITDA goes to teams) is up for renegotiation; Liberty's leverage is the F1+ streaming product, which it can use to argue for a higher revenue base before splitting. Quint hospitality (acquired 2022) cross-sells into MotoGP and PGA events — a hidden vertical-integration play not in any DCF. Lastly, 86% of MotoGP means Liberty has a path to 100% via Dorna minority buyouts at conservative multiples, further consolidating IP rights.

### CATALYSTS
- 2026 Concorde Agreement renewal (terms with teams)
- F1+ streaming ARPU and subscriber disclosure (H2 2026)
- Possible tracker-collapse announcement (any time; SiriusXM precedent)
