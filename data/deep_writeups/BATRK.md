### THE SETUP
Series C non-voting class of Atlanta Braves Holdings — identical economic interest to BATRA (voting Series A) but trades at a small persistent discount and offers materially better float for institutional sizing. Same underlying SOTP: 100% of the Atlanta Braves MLB franchise + 100% of The Battery Atlanta mixed-use development. In any take-private or strategic sale, BATRK and BATRA receive the same per-share consideration — so the discount collapses on event. Better entry vehicle than BATRA for value-conscious holders that don't need the vote.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Atlanta Braves Holdings owns 100% of the Atlanta Braves MLB club and 100% of The Battery Atlanta. FY2025 total revenue $732M (+11%): Baseball $635M (+7%, broadcast/streaming additions and contractual rate increases) and Mixed-Use Development $97M (+45%). Adjusted OIBDA $107.8M (+172%). Three share classes: BATRA (Series A voting, Nasdaq), BATRB (Series B super-voting, OTC, Liberty/Malone-controlled), BATRK (Series C non-voting, Nasdaq, largest float). BATRK at ~$51.50 = $3.03B mcap, indicative SOTP NAV $3.5–4.5B per management & comp framework = ~25% discount. Pair-trade vs BATRA: small spread (BATRK trades $1–3 cheaper) reflects the non-voting structure but is mostly noise — same economic claim, larger liquidity, and identical merger consideration in a sale.

Bear case: MLB CBA expires December 2026 — labor stoppage would hit 2027 baseball revenue. Regional sports network model post-Diamond Sports bankruptcy is structurally weaker; local broadcast renewals look more like floors than ceilings. Player payroll inflation outpaces revenue, compressing baseball OIBDA. The Battery Atlanta is a single-market (metro Atlanta) commercial real estate concentration with rate-sensitive tenants. Sports franchises trade infrequently in public markets, so SOTP "discount" is partially a structural discount that may never close absent a sale. BATRK has no voting rights at all, so on any contested sale or governance event a holder is purely a free-rider on BATRA/BATRB voters.

Bull case: comparable franchise prints validate the SOTP — Orioles $1.7B (2024, smaller market), Twins discussions ~$1.5B+, Marlins/Angels rumored. Forbes 2025 valuation of the Braves: $2.95B; the Battery worth $1–1.5B given 50%+ OIBDA growth. NAV $3.5–4.5B vs $3.0B mcap = 17–33% discount. Liberty/Malone historically monetizes holdings — Live Nation, Sirius, Formula One, Charter, Discovery all proved patient capital cashes in. BATRK is the better arb vehicle: in a take-private, both classes paid the same; the BATRA premium converges to zero. Larger BATRK float supports institutional sizing; pair-trade (long BATRK / short BATRA) captures the voting-spread compression at low carry cost.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K shows ~$725M of TeamCo-level debt that is non-recourse to ABH equity — a structural feature that materially de-risks holdco-level NAV calculation but is invisible to the typical leverage screen. The Diamond Sports bankruptcy and the Q4 2025 contract-asset impairment ($30M+) related to the prior local broadcasting agreement is largely backward-looking; the new streaming/RSN structure is contributing positively to 2025 baseball revenue. Voting-rights discount in non-voting Liberty trackers historically compresses when sale or strategic event approaches (Liberty Capital, Sirius XM precedents). BATRK's higher float ($300M+ daily turnover regions vs BATRA's thinner trading) makes it the institutional-friendly leg for sizing — many activist and event-driven funds use BATRK rather than BATRA precisely because BATRA's lower float makes building a position price-impactful.
