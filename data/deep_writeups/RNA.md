# {NUMBER}. RNA — Atrium Therapeutics, Inc.
## SPINOFF | ~$270M cash vs ~$220M mkt cap | Score: 64/100
### THE SETUP
Atrium Therapeutics launched February 27, 2026 as a publicly traded spin-off created in connection with Novartis AG's $12B acquisition of Avidity Biosciences. Avidity holders received Atrium shares as a non-cash sweetener alongside the Novartis cash consideration; many sold immediately to crystallize the deal value. Atrium inherited Avidity's old "RNA" ticker, a circumstance that has confused traders who associated the symbol with Avidity's siRNA-conjugate platform. The new entity launched with approximately $270M in cash and cash equivalents against a market cap that has hovered near $220M — investors are buying the platform at a discount to net cash. Pipeline is two preclinical candidates: ATR-1086 for PLN (phospholamban) cardiomyopathy and ATR-1072 for PRKAG2 syndrome, plus two undisclosed research targets. The platform is built on the AOC RNA-targeted delivery technology developed at Avidity, and Atrium has a licensing arrangement with Novartis for the conjugate chemistry. Leadership: Kathleen Gallagher (former Avidity CPO) as CEO, Sarah Boyce (former Avidity CEO) as Chair.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Atrium is the cleanest version of a "negative enterprise value biotech" trade — a preclinical platform company trading below its cash balance because the holders who received the shares did not want them. The setup pays you to own the science. The science itself is non-trivial: PLN cardiomyopathy affects an estimated 2,000-4,000 patients in the U.S. with pathogenic variants and has no disease-modifying therapy; PRKAG2 syndrome is similarly an unmet, monogenic, orphan-eligible indication. Cardiac-targeted RNA conjugates have been the holy grail of the AOC modality, and Avidity's neuromuscular data established that the broader chemistry can hit non-hepatic tissue.

The risks are pure preclinical biotech. Both lead candidates remain pre-IND; the company will likely file INDs in 2026-2027 with first-in-human dosing thereafter. Cash runway at current burn appears multi-year, but every read-out cycle is years away and dilution risk emerges well before commercial visibility. Cardiac delivery has historically been harder than skeletal muscle, and translating mouse efficacy to human cardiomyocytes is non-trivial. Importantly, the Novartis license terms and economics on the platform are critical and not fully transparent — investors should treat the license as both a validation and a constraint on independent value capture.

The asymmetry is favorable but not unique. With cash above market cap, downside in a wind-down or quiet-quit scenario approximates the cash-per-share floor minus an executive-compensation drag. Upside on a positive Phase 1 readout — particularly with biomarker movement on PLN expression — could be 5-10x as the Street re-prices the platform; a Novartis re-acquisition is the obvious exit pathway. The trade is best sized as a basket position alongside other "trading near cash" preclinical biotechs rather than as a high-conviction single name. Wait for IND filing or first dosing to true up the burn assumptions.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A screen will show no revenue, GAAP losses, and a "biotech with $270M cash" descriptor that triggers its own auto-skip from many quant universes. What it cannot show: (1) the technical credibility of the team — Avidity's neuromuscular Phase 1/2 data is the relevant precedent for whether AOC can hit non-hepatic tissue at all; (2) the specific terms of the Novartis license, which determine whether Atrium captures most or only a slice of the platform's value; (3) the orphan-disease economics of PLN and PRKAG2, which are narrow but have ultra-orphan pricing precedent; (4) the practical reality that the seller flow from former Avidity holders may take quarters to clear; (5) the cash-floor downside, which is real but evaporates with each quarter of opex burn.

### CATALYSTS
- **2026-2027 IND filings** for ATR-1086 (PLN) and ATR-1072 (PRKAG2).
- **First Phase 1 dosing milestones** (likely 2027) — first human pharmacokinetic and safety reads on cardiac-delivered AOC.
- **Cash-burn discipline updates each quarter** — each print recalibrates how long the cash floor protects the option.
