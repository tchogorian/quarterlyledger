# {NUMBER}. CPKRF — Canada Packers Inc.
## SPINOFF | Maple Leaf Foods pork spin (Oct 1, 2025) | Score: 68/100

### THE SETUP
Maple Leaf Foods spun out its pork operations as Canada Packers on October 1, 2025. The new entity is North America's largest "raised without antibiotics" (RWA) pork producer, but trades under an OTC pink sheet ticker (CPKRF) that is invisible to most US screens — the textbook orphan-spin setup.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Canada Packers operates a vertically integrated pork supply chain in Canada: company-owned and contract-grown hogs, two large processing plants, and an evergreen supply agreement with parent Maple Leaf Foods for downstream branded products. The differentiating positioning is RWA — animals raised without antibiotics — a category that commands a meaningful retail and foodservice price premium versus commodity pork. The spin completes Maple Leaf's strategic pivot to a pure-play branded-protein business (poultry, plant-based, and prepared meats) while letting Canada Packers stand alone with its own capital structure and capital allocation flexibility. Maple Leaf retained 16% of the float, modest but enough to align interests during the transition.

The bear case is that this is a commodity pork business with cosmetic differentiation. Pork-cycle economics are notoriously volatile — Tyson, Smithfield, and JBS have all experienced multi-quarter losses in their pork segments through 2024-2025 as Chinese demand softened, US herd expansion outpaced demand, and grain costs whipsawed. RWA premiums can collapse if oversupply from competing certified producers materializes. The OTC pink listing means no S&P/Russell index inclusion catalyst, no analyst coverage, and limited US institutional buyers. Maple Leaf's 16% retained stake is also a future overhang. The Canadian small-cap ecosystem is thin and illiquid.

The bull case is the classic spin-off setup: an orphaned, under-followed asset with a structural advantage (RWA), a captive offtake agreement (Maple Leaf), and clean balance sheet at separation. Trades at a material discount to US-listed peer Smithfield (SFD) on EBITDA. The catalysts are sequential: first standalone print, potential US dual listing on NYSE/Nasdaq (which would unlock institutional buyers), pork-cycle recovery, and potential strategic interest from larger pork players (JBS, WH Group/Smithfield, Seaboard) for whom RWA capability is a missing capability. At ~C$12.50 / US$0.60B market cap, Canada Packers is roughly the right size for a strategic acquisition without anti-trust complications.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Maple Leaf supply agreement is structurally different from typical spin commercial agreements — it is "evergreen" (auto-renewing) with priced-formula pork supply to Maple Leaf's branded prepared-meats business. This stabilizes Canada Packers' base load and reduces revenue concentration risk vs. selling pure commodity pork into the open market. Filings around the spin (Form F-1/40-F equivalent on SEDAR) detail the RWA-capable hog barn footprint, which is non-trivial capex to replicate — competitor RWA scale-up is constrained by both biology (mortality) and infrastructure. Working capital release post-separation is also a likely tailwind not in consensus.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- First standalone quarterly print (likely Q4 2025 or Q1 2026 results, expected early-to-mid 2026)
- US dual-listing announcement on NYSE or Nasdaq
- Margin commentary on RWA pricing premium scaling; potential strategic transaction with global pork player
