### THE SETUP
Ionis pre-funded its 2026 0% convertible wall in November 2025 with $700M of new 2030 converts and started repurchasing the old notes — removing the most cited overhang on the equity. Days later (March 30, 2026), the FDA approved a high-dose Spinraza regimen, extending the royalty stream Ionis collects from Biogen on a franchise the market had written off as biosimilar/Evrysdi roadkill.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Ionis Pharmaceuticals is the antisense-oligonucleotide pioneer behind Spinraza (nusinersen, partnered with Biogen for SMA), Wainua (eplontersen, partnered with AstraZeneca for ATTR), Tryngolza (olezarsen, wholly-owned for FCS), Donidalorsen (HAE) and a deep RNA-targeted pipeline. 2025 revenue hit $944M, ahead of guidance, and Tryngolza generated $108M in its first year on the market. As of December 31, 2025, Ionis held $2.7B in cash, cash equivalents and short-term investments — bolstered by the convertible refinancing — and has been paying down 2026 notes via repurchases. The company is in a transition from royalty-collector to commercial biotech.

The bear case is straightforward: Ionis still loses money on a GAAP basis, three concurrent product launches (Tryngolza, Donidalorsen, Wainua) require heavy SG&A and field-force build-out, and the operating expense step-up is real. Spinraza royalties have been declining for years as Evrysdi (oral) and Zolgensma (gene therapy) eat market share — high-dose nusinersen helps, but does not reverse the long-arc franchise erosion. The pipeline is broad but binary: any pivotal-trial miss in cardiovascular or neuro programs would re-rate the stock down. The market-cap of roughly $6.8B is not "cheap" until launches prove out at scale.

The bull thesis is a 2026–2027 inflection: 2026 converts now de-risked; high-dose Spinraza approval (March 30, 2026) extends the royalty annuity; Tryngolza, Donidalorsen and Wainua all ramping into a multi-product commercial company; cash runway into 2028+ with $2.7B liquidity. Catalysts cluster around Q1 2026 earnings (May), launch updates, and pivotal readouts later in the year. The asymmetry is that the equity has historically traded on convertible-overhang fear; with the wall pushed to 2030, attention can return to the pipeline.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The November 2025 convertible refinancing is structurally important — Ionis priced $700M of 0.00% Convertible Senior Notes due 2030 and concurrently repurchased $200M of the 0% 2026 notes, with explicit plans to repay any remaining 2026 notes at maturity from proceeds. That single transaction removes the most-cited bear point on the equity and converts a "going-concern-ish" narrative into a "cash-rich biotech with pipeline optionality" narrative. The Biogen-partnered high-dose Spinraza approval is not in any screen as a near-term royalty event — Ionis collects mid-teens royalties without bearing commercial cost, so it is high-incremental-margin revenue.
