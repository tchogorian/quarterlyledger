# {NUMBER}. WED.TO — Westaim Corporation
## HOLDCO (NAV DISCOUNT) | Mkt Cap ~C$530M / ~30% NAV discount | Score: 70/100

### THE SETUP
Westaim is a TSX Venture-listed Canadian financial-services holding company with two principal assets and one notable shareholder. The principal assets are (1) a residual stake in Skyward Specialty Insurance Group (NASDAQ: SKWD), a US specialty P&C insurer that IPO'd in January 2023 and has compounded ~70%+ since, and (2) a 51% interest in Arena FINCO, a private-credit investment platform managed in partnership with Arena Investors LP, which has scaled to roughly $3B+ AUM. The notable shareholder is HG Vora Capital (Parag Vora), a respected event-driven hedge fund that has been involved in the name for years. At C$4.40 per share against an indicative NAV of approximately C$6.30, the stock trades at a ~30% discount to its sum-of-the-parts.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The Westaim setup is one of the cleanest holdco-discount stories on either Canadian exchange right now, and the discount mechanics are doing the work for shareholders even if nothing dramatic happens. Skyward Specialty has been a genuine compounder since IPO, and Westaim has methodically monetized its position through secondary offerings — most notably the May 2024 trim of 4.4 million shares at $36.50 — and recycled proceeds into share buybacks at a steep discount to NAV. That dynamic is mathematically accretive: every dollar of Skyward stock sold near intrinsic value and used to repurchase Westaim stock at 70 cents on the dollar of NAV creates roughly 30 cents of incremental per-share value. Management has executed this playbook consistently, and there is still a multi-million share Skyward position remaining. The Arena FINCO stake is harder to value — private-credit valuations are opaque and the platform's underlying loan book is not transparent to outside investors — but Arena has scaled AUM steadily and the management-fee economics alone are meaningful.

The discount persists because Westaim is structurally hard to own: TSX Venture listing limits institutional ownership; Canadian holdco discounts are sticky; the float is small; the Skyward overhang of "more secondary sales coming" weighs on the perceived NAV; and the Arena valuation is genuinely uncertain. None of these reasons are fundamental — they are technical and disclosure issues that can resolve over time. The asymmetric outcome here is a substantial issuer bid (SIB) that would buy back a meaningful chunk of the float at a premium-to-market but discount-to-NAV, or a full take-private by HG Vora. Either action collapses the discount in a single event.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Most quant screens cannot value a Skyward residual stake (which is publicly listed but inside a holdco structure), let alone a private-credit JV stake. The mechanical NAV-discount narrowing from each Skyward sale + Westaim buyback is invisible to factor models that look only at trailing earnings or book value. HG Vora's involvement signals an aligned, sophisticated owner who is patient but transactional — exactly the type of shareholder that engineers structural value events. The TSX Venture listing also depresses passive demand to nearly zero, which inflates the discount.

### CATALYSTS
- Continued Skyward Specialty share sales at higher prices, recycled into buybacks
- Arena FINCO valuation step-ups as AUM grows
- Substantial Issuer Bid (SIB) announcement
- Possible take-private led by HG Vora
- Reorganization separating Arena into its own listed vehicle
- Migration to TSX main board (would unlock institutional demand)
