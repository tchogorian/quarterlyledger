# {NUMBER}. SEM — Select Medical Holdings Corporation
## TAKE-PRIVATE PENDING | $16.50 MBO with WCAS, expected mid-2026 | Score: 68/100

### THE SETUP
On March 2, 2026, Select Medical announced a definitive agreement to be acquired by a consortium led by Executive Chairman Robert A. Ortenzio, former CFO Martin F. Jackson, and private equity firm Welsh, Carson, Anderson & Stowe (WCAS) at $16.50 per share in cash, an 18% premium to the prior unaffected close. Total transaction value is approximately $3.9B equity / $8B+ enterprise value including debt assumption. The Schedule 13E-3 was filed April 15, 2026 (three days ago), and a special meeting is expected in Q2 or Q3 2026 with closing targeted mid-2026. The current quote of $16.39 leaves a 0.7% gross spread, ~2.7% annualized — a tight, "expected to clear" arbitrage with conflicted-MBO litigation as the principal residual risk.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Select Medical operates four segments: critical illness recovery hospitals (long-term acute care, the largest LTACH operator in the US), inpatient rehabilitation hospitals (joint ventures with major health systems including Cleveland Clinic, Banner, Baylor Scott & White), outpatient rehabilitation (~1,900+ Concentra and Select clinics), and Concentra (occupational health, recently spun out via partial IPO in 2024 with SEM retaining a controlling stake). The take-private follows several years of underperformance versus the rehab-focused peer set (Encompass Health) and a Concentra IPO that did not meaningfully unlock multiple expansion. Founder Robert Ortenzio (son of co-founder Rocco Ortenzio) and former CFO Jackson chose to take the residual asset private with WCAS rather than wait for a public re-rate.

The merger-arb mechanics are clean but the deal type carries elevated legal risk. Conflicted MBOs — where management is rolling equity rather than cashing out — face heightened Delaware fiduciary scrutiny under MFW (Kahn v. M&F Worldwide). The transaction is structured with a special committee of independent directors, an independent financial advisor fairness opinion, and a majority-of-the-minority vote condition — the standard MFW dual protection that should secure the business-judgment rule rather than entire-fairness review. Plaintiffs' firms have already begun the standard "investigation" press releases, but these have not produced a material legal threat in similar recent MBO precedents (e.g., Endeavor Group, ZipRecruiter chatter).

The honest risks: (1) majority-of-minority vote — Concentra public shareholders vote separately on the SEM controlling-stake transaction implications, and minority opposition could materialize; (2) financing — WCAS sponsor equity plus founder rollover plus secured debt commitments are reportedly in place but not fully syndicated; (3) HSR is not the bottleneck (no horizontal overlap), but state hospital licensure transfers across SEM's footprint take 4-6 months minimum; (4) regulatory headline risk on LTACH reimbursement (CMS site-neutral payment expansions) could theoretically create a MAC argument for the buyer, though prior post-acute MBOs have not been blown up on this basis.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A spread screen ranks this deal mid-pack at 2.7% annualized — competitive with treasuries plus 50-100bps. It misses two things. First, the structural cleanness — WCAS is a healthcare-services specialist (long history with US Oncology, Concentra itself originally, etc.) and is the rare PE firm where deal certainty is genuinely high. Second, the fact that Ortenzio is rolling rather than cashing reduces the equity-financing risk substantially — sponsors do not back out of deals where management is putting in real money alongside.

### CATALYSTS
- **Definitive proxy statement (mailed shortly after 13E-3 effective):** sets the special meeting date and provides the fairness opinion banker book details.
- **Special meeting and shareholder vote (Q2 / Q3 2026):** the binary event for arb-spread closure.
- **Expected close mid-2026:** state hospital licensure transfers are the gating long-tail item.
