# {NUMBER}. PEN — Penumbra, Inc.
## M&A ARBITRAGE | 12.6% Gross Spread / 22.9% Annualized | Score: 62/100

### THE SETUP

On January 15, 2026, Boston Scientific (BSX) announced an agreement to acquire Penumbra for $374 per share in a mixed cash-and-stock deal valued at approximately $14.5 billion. Holders may elect either $374 cash or 3.8721 BSX shares per Penumbra share, subject to a 73% cash / 27% stock proration. The current market price of $332.13 implies a 12.61% gross spread and roughly 22.88% annualized assuming a Q4 2026 close.

This is a strategic re-entry for Boston Scientific into neurovascular — a market BSX exited in 2017 when it sold its neurovascular franchise to Stryker. Penumbra brings the dominant U.S. mechanical thrombectomy platform (the JET 7, Indigo, Lightning) plus a peripheral embolization business that fits BSX's interventional cardiology and peripheral playbook. There is essentially no overlap in current product portfolios, which is why FTC review is expected to be straightforward — though the deal has not yet cleared HSR.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

The 12.6% spread is meaningfully wider than typical strategic medtech deals, which usually trade at 3-6% gross. The widening reflects three things: (1) the stock-portion exposure to BSX share price drift between signing and close, (2) HSR/FTC timing uncertainty given the political environment around healthcare M&A in 2026, and (3) the proration mechanic — anyone electing all-cash gets prorated down, so the practical realized value depends on election dynamics across the holder base.

Penumbra's standalone value is also a real consideration. The company put up strong thrombectomy growth in 2025 and has a pipeline of vacuum-aspiration thrombectomy expansions that could justify a higher standalone multiple if the deal breaks. That said, Penumbra had its own execution missteps in prior years (the JET 7 Reperfusion Catheter recall in 2020-21) and the strategic logic for Boston Scientific is real — neurovascular margins are 75%+ gross and the call-point overlaps with BSX's existing neuromodulation rep base.

The deal economics are clean enough that this is a "rate of return" arb: the question is not whether the deal closes, but when. A Q3 2026 close would push annualized returns above 30%; a slip to Q1 2027 brings it to roughly 16%. Risk-adjusted, this looks like a high-teens IRR opportunity in a deal with very high probability of close.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

Stock-deal arb spreads embed a hedging cost that pure ratio screens miss: the 27% stock portion needs to be hedged by shorting 3.8721 × 0.27 = 1.045 BSX shares per PEN share. Anyone running this without the BSX hedge is taking a directional bet on Boston Scientific. The proration also means the realized cash/stock mix depends on what other holders elect — index funds and event-driven funds tend to elect different ways.

There's also a quiet "second leg" — Penumbra's immersive healthcare (REAL System VR rehabilitation) business is being divested as part of the deal terms, so any post-close stub remainder for Penumbra holders is essentially zero.

### CATALYSTS

- HSR clearance (expected Q2-Q3 2026)
- Penumbra shareholder vote (pending)
- BSX share price stability through close
- Election deadline announcements

### MUNGER CHECKLIST

- Simple business: Yes — strategic medtech roll-up

- Margin of safety: 12.6% gross / 22.9% annualized provides cushion for 6-month delay
- Reversion risk: If deal breaks, PEN likely trades to $250-280 (pre-deal levels were $260s) — call it 20% downside
- Skip if: You can't hedge the BSX leg, or you need certainty of timing
