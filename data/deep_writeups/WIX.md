# {NUMBER}. WIX — Wix.com Ltd.
## SELF-TENDER (RECENTLY COMPLETED) | $92 tender / Mkt Cap ~$3.7B | Score: 66/100

### THE SETUP
On April 1, 2026, Wix.com completed a modified Dutch-auction self-tender offer that repurchased approximately 17.6–18.7 million shares at $92.00 per share for total consideration of roughly $1.62–1.72 billion. The transaction represented a retirement of roughly 29-30% of the pre-tender share count — an unusually aggressive capital-return action, far larger than what most SaaS peers have been willing to do. Shares jumped 9.9% on announcement. Post-tender, the stock has re-rated lower, trading around $68-70 versus the $92 tender price, implying roughly 30% upside back to the level at which the company itself was willing to retire stock.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Self-tenders at a premium to the market are among the most reliable positive signals in event-driven investing because they combine three things: (1) explicit management conviction on intrinsic value, (2) a structural reduction in share count that mechanically raises per-share metrics, and (3) a temporary overhang from arbitrageurs and short-term holders who tender and exit. The post-tender drift lower is the textbook outcome of this third dynamic — the holders who were in the stock specifically to tender are out, and the stock now has to find new marginal buyers at a lower base rate of demand. This creates a window.

The more interesting question is whether $92 actually reflects intrinsic value or whether it was simply an opportunistic use of Wix's balance sheet at a moment when management had conviction but the broader SaaS tape was weak. The company's 2025 results have been mixed: ARR growth has moderated into the mid-teens, the AI agent / "Wixel" monetization story is still early, and competitive pressure from Shopify, Squarespace and Canva remains intense. However, Wix's free-cash-flow generation has improved materially, the subscription base is sticky, and the ~30% float reduction is immediately accretive to per-share FCF. At the current ~$70 level against a management-blessed $92 reference point, there is an implicit put underwritten by the fact that the company has both the cash and the willingness to buy back stock. If Q1 2026 earnings (May 2026) confirm that FCF trajectory is intact, the stock should mechanically re-rate toward the tender level. If growth decelerates further, the $92 signal becomes less authoritative and the thesis weakens.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Screens see the share-count reduction but not the post-tender arb-exit drift that creates the entry window. Screens also don't distinguish between a self-tender at market (defensive) and a self-tender at a premium (offensive conviction signal) — Wix's was explicitly the latter. The AI agent monetization layer is not yet in consensus numbers but is reflected in management's willingness to lever the balance sheet for buyback. Israel-domicile geopolitical discount has also weighed on the multiple versus US-domiciled SaaS peers at similar growth/FCF profiles.

### CATALYSTS

- Additional capital-return authorization (ordinary-course buyback)
- ARR reacceleration / AI agent monetization disclosure
- Geopolitical risk-premium compression
