# {NUMBER}. FORA — Forian Inc.
## TAKE-PRIVATE PENDING | $2.17 cash tender | Score: 50/100

### THE SETUP
CEO Max Wygod-led consortium tender offer at $2.17 (22.6% premium); SC TO-T filed April 16, 2026; 5.6% annualized spread reflects financing thinness and minority litigation risk. Classic conflicted micro-cap MBO with skinny equity backing.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Forian is a healthcare data analytics company spun out of Helix Technologies in 2021, providing prescription/claims data and analytics services to pharma and life-sciences customers. The business throws off ~$20M revenue, modestly cash-generative, with a $30M+ cash balance against a $68M deal value. CEO Max Wygod (son of co-founder Marty Wygod, the WebMD/Medco veteran) leads a consortium tender offer at $2.17/share announced 4/2/2026 with the formal SC TO-T filed 4/16/2026. Tender expiration TBD; close projected Q2 2026.

Bear case: At $5.5M Wygod family-trust equity commitment against a $68M deal, financing is dangerously thin — the deal relies almost entirely on rollover stakes from existing insiders. If a rollover holder backs out, the deal cannot close. The premium of 22.6% is below typical MBO premiums of 30-40%, and the tender mechanism (versus a long-form merger) bypasses a full shareholder vote, intensifying minority pushback. Litigation seeking higher consideration has already been threatened. A break would send FORA toward $1.65-1.80 (-15-20%).

Bull/catalyst: Wygod controls a meaningful slug of FORA stock personally, and rollover commitments from related-party trusts likely exceed the 50% minimum-tender condition before the offer even opens. The short-form merger backstop after >50% tender means the deal can clear without a full vote. The 5.6% annualized return on a 60-90 day work-out compensates better than the FFIC/FOLD spreads despite the smaller deal size and worse optics.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Schedule TO-T discloses that the Wygod family and certain insiders own approximately 35-40% of Forian on a fully-diluted basis and have committed to tender — meaning the offer effectively needs only ~10-15% additional acceptance to clear the minimum-tender condition. This makes the deal mechanically much more likely than the financing optics suggest. The proxy/13E-3 also reveals a Special Committee fairness opinion at $1.95-$2.30 — placing $2.17 mid-range but giving litigation plaintiffs a credible "could have gotten more" argument. Forian's data assets (prescription transaction data) carry strategic value to pharma data buyers (IQVIA, Veeva, Komodo) but no competing bid emerged in the go-shop period, weakening minority-shareholder leverage.

### CATALYSTS
- Tender offer expiration (typical 20-business-day window from April 16 = ~May 14, 2026)
- Short-form merger if >50% tendered
- Resolution of minority-shareholder litigation
