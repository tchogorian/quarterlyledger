### THE SETUP
Nonprofit/social-good SaaS leader with Clearlake Capital sitting on a 20.45% stake (entered 2022), now post-EVERFI divestiture and showing organic recurring revenue +5.8% with 35.9% adjusted EBITDA margins in FY25. The company has crossed Rule of 40 and is guiding 2026 revenue $1.17-1.18B with EBITDA $430-438M and FCF $280-290M. Clearlake is the largest holder; a sale or take-private is a non-trivial outcome given the financials and PE-friendly recurring revenue profile.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Blackbaud is the dominant SaaS provider to nonprofits, foundations, K-12 private schools, faith communities, and corporate social responsibility programs (Raiser's Edge, Financial Edge, Blackbaud CRM, Blackbaud Tuition Management). Per the Feb 18, 2026 10-K and Q4 2025 release: FY25 GAAP revenue ~$1.1B (down YoY due to EVERFI divestiture), non-GAAP organic revenue +5.5%, organic recurring revenue +5.8%, non-GAAP adjusted EBITDA $405.3M at 35.9% margin, Rule of 40 = 41.4, returning to GAAP profitability. 2026 guide: revenue $1.173-1.179B, adjusted EBITDA $430-438M, non-GAAP EPS $5.15-5.25, FCF $280-290M. Clearlake Capital owns ~20.45% — entered with a rejected $71/share take-private bid in 2022; never sold down.

Bear case: Blackbaud's organic growth at mid-single-digits trails best-in-class vertical SaaS peers — nonprofit software has TAM constraints, customer budgets are donation-dependent and rate-sensitive, and competition from generalist CRM/ERP (Salesforce nonprofit cloud, Microsoft Dynamics) is intensifying. The 2020 ransomware breach and resulting class actions still drive litigation accruals years later. EVERFI was sold at a write-down — capital allocation history is mixed. Clearlake has held since 2022 without forcing a deal — that's a 4-year stalemate with no formal proxy process.

Bull case: at $61-65/share area (research note didn't include current price; stock has traded $60-75 range in 2025-2026), the company trades at roughly 14-15x 2026 EBITDA — modest for a 35%+ EBITDA-margin recurring SaaS. PE comps for vertical SaaS (Veeva, Tyler Tech segments) are 18-25x. Clearlake's 20% stake gives it veto power on any deal and standing to push for a sale. Blackbaud's 2026 guide is the cleanest in years post-EVERFI; FCF yield ~7-8% at current cap. Cushion: even without an M&A outcome, $280-290M FCF supports an aggressive buyback that compounds EPS at high single digits.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Clearlake stake plus a refreshed board (post-2022 cooperation agreement added Clearlake-aligned directors) means BLKB is governed like a quasi-private. The 2022 rejected $71 bid is the public floor — any new offer would have to top that, and 2026 EBITDA is materially higher than 2022 baseline. Blackbaud's nonprofit moat is real: switching costs in donor management software are extreme (data migrations, gift records spanning decades, IRS-required donor receipts) — Raiser's Edge has ~80%+ retention in its core customer base. The Rule of 40 = 41.4 inflection is the type of fundamental that PE buyers (Vista, Thoma Bravo) target for take-privates with debt-funded LBOs.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Q1 and Q2 2026 earnings: trajectory toward 2026 guide upper end
- Possible refreshed Clearlake bid or strategic review announcement
- Buyback execution (BLKB has been aggressive on repurchases)
