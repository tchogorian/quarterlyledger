### ANALYST SUMMARY
Day One Biopharmaceuticals is a California-based rare pediatric oncology biotech with its lead drug OJEMDA (tovorafenib) approved for BRAF-altered pediatric low-grade glioma. On March 6, 2026, Servier — a French privately-held specialty pharma with a focused oncology franchise — announced a tender offer for Day One at $21.50 per share, a 68% premium to the undisturbed price. The enterprise value of $2.5B reflects a fit-with-portfolio premium: Servier already markets Voranigo in glioma and adds OJEMDA to build a pediatric CNS-tumor platform. The tender was launched via SC TO-T in late March 2026.

The bear case is almost entirely regulatory. Servier is a French acquirer, so CFIUS review may be triggered despite a minimal national-security profile (oncology, no dual-use tech). HSR antitrust clearance is the second gate; given Servier's limited US oncology presence, a second request is unlikely but not impossible. A small probability exists of a competing bid — but at 68% premium, other strategics are unlikely to top, and no interloper has surfaced. The spread at 0.14% gross implies the market assigns >98% probability of closing on schedule.

The bull case is simply that the deal closes in Q2 2026 on announced terms. At the current price, annualized return to a Q2 close is approximately 0.85% — unattractive as a standalone position versus T-bills, but useful as a cash-equivalent overlay in a merger-arb book. The main "edge" here is sizing: for institutional cash parking at a yield floor, clean strategic deals with low regulatory gates and a de minimis spread are common landing spots. Treat this as a deal-closing event, not an alpha trade.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The SC TO-T schedule and Day One's 14D-9 recommendation statement disclose the standard termination fee, force-the-vote provisions, and a customary go-shop — but the Servier proxy filings are private given Servier is a foundation-owned French entity with no public equity. A screener assumes standard US-US M&A; the hidden detail is that Servier's foundation ownership structure simplifies the buyer-side approvals but complicates CFIUS timing disclosures. Background-of-the-merger section of the 14D-9 will show how competitive the process was.
