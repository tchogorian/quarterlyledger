### THE SETUP
Saba Capital owns 11.88% of a sleepy NJ muni CEF that Nuveen has agreed to merge into Nuveen Municipal High Income Opportunity Fund (NMZ-style vehicle) around February 9, 2026. Trading at $12.42 vs. $13.85 NAV, the merger is the explicit discount-narrowing catalyst — Saba's pressure already won.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
NXJ is a Nuveen single-state (New Jersey) leveraged municipal bond closed-end fund holding investment-grade NJ paper financed with VRDP / preferred leverage. Like virtually every Nuveen state CEF, it has traded at a persistent 8-12% discount to NAV for years thanks to the "CEF tax" — fixed distributions, leverage costs, ETF substitutes, and zero retail bid for state-specific muni complexity. The fund is small (~$300m) and illiquid, which is exactly the profile that lets Saba Capital build double-digit positions cheaply and force board action.

The bear case: even after merger, the combined entity is still a Nuveen muni CEF — and Nuveen muni CEFs trade at discounts. NXJ holders receive newly issued shares of the acquirer (Nuveen Municipal High Income Opportunity Fund) at NAV-to-NAV exchange, which mechanically does not deliver a cash premium. If the acquirer trades at -8%, you've swapped a -10% discount for a -8% discount: a 2-point gain net of transaction costs and Saba's eventual exit pressure. Munis themselves are also rate-sensitive — any back-up in long rates hits NAV directly, which can offset the discount-narrowing arithmetic.

The bull case: discount typically tightens 200-400 bps in the 60 days into a Nuveen merger close as arb traders front-run the NAV-conversion. Saba has run this playbook a dozen times across NUM, NEA, NMZ, etc. — the pattern is real. If Saba elects to redeem or tender into the combined fund post-close, expect another leg of demand for NXJ shares. Catalyst is dated and binary: Feb 9, 2026 close. Tax-equivalent yield on NJ residents is ~6%+ while you wait. Asymmetry is modest but the timeline is short.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The DEF 14A (filed early 2025) details the NAV-for-NAV share exchange mechanics — there is no cash component, so traditional merger-arb spread math does not apply. What screens miss: Saba's 13D/G filings show share count drifting from 4.19M to 5.07M (= 11.88%), meaning Saba was still buying into the merger announcement. That signals high conviction the post-merger combined fund will trade tighter than NXJ standalone — Saba is essentially rolling its position into the bigger acquirer. Also missed: the New Jersey state-tax exemption disappears for non-NJ residents holding the merged fund (which is national muni), which is why some legacy NJ retail holders may sell into the conversion, creating short-term pressure.
