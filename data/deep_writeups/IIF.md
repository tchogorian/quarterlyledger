### THE SETUP
Saba Capital (Boaz Weinstein) holds an 8% stake in IIF, disclosed via 13D/A March 2026, with stated intent to pursue trustee nominations and discount-narrowing actions. Stock trades at $23.01 vs. $25.83 NAV — roughly a 10.9% discount on an India equity portfolio post-2025 underperformance.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
IIF is a Morgan Stanley-managed closed-end fund focused on Indian equities, structured as a non-diversified country fund with no leverage. NAV per share around $25.83 against a market price of $23.01 yields a ~10.9% discount — meaningful for a country fund in a market still considered an emerging-market growth story. India equity benchmarks underperformed in 2025 after several years of outperformance, widening the discount as retail enthusiasm cooled. Fund AUM is modest, fees are relatively high for the strategy, and the fund's portfolio overlaps materially with cheaper India ETF alternatives (INDA, EPI), which is exactly the structural setup activists exploit.

The bear case for the activism path: Morgan Stanley has fought Saba elsewhere (and lost some, won some), and the fund's small size limits the urgency of management's response. Saba trimmed below 10% in January 2026 sales — possibly signaling reduced commitment — though they re-added in February-March. Country-fund liquidations can take 12-24 months from first 13D to actual tender, and during that period the discount can widen as much as it narrows. The underlying India market faces real risks: stretched valuations on small/midcaps, election-cycle policy uncertainty, and a strong dollar pressuring repatriated returns.

The bull case is Saba's track record. Boaz Weinstein has run this CEF activist playbook dozens of times and generally wins something — a tender at 95-99% of NAV, a managed-distribution policy adoption, or board concessions. With the discount at 10.9%, even a modest tender at 98% of NAV would deliver a clean ~8% pickup against a portfolio whose underlying NAV is itself a real-economy India exposure that long-term holders might want anyway. The March 2026 13D/A explicitly preserves trustee nomination rights for the next annual meeting. Catalyst clarity is high; the question is timing and whether Morgan Stanley settles or fights.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 13D/A wording matters: Saba's filings escalate language gradually from "may nominate" to "intends to nominate" — this March 2026 amendment moved closer to the latter. Annual meeting timing and the by-law nomination window are the binding deadlines. IIF's investment management agreement renewal vote is a separate pressure point Saba can use even without a board fight. Watch the fund's monthly portfolio disclosures for any defensive moves (de-risking, cash buildup) that would signal management is preparing for a tender. Also check Form 4 filings — Saba's Feb-Mar 2026 add-backs after the January trim suggest tactical, not strategic, position management.

### CATALYSTS
- Annual meeting trustee nomination deadline (typically 90-120 days before meeting)
- Possible Morgan Stanley settlement (tender or open-end conversion)
- India equity NAV recovery into mid-2026
