# {NUMBER}. YEXT — Yext, Inc.
## SELF-TENDER (COMPLETED) | $140M DUTCH AUCTION @ $5.75-$6.50 | Score: 42/100

### THE SETUP
Yext ran a $140M modified Dutch auction self-tender that expired March 18, 2026 in a $5.75-$6.50 range. The tender was originally announced at $180M but was reduced and extended (amendment filed February 23, 2026) — a subtle tell that the prior pricing band didn't fully clear. Post-tender, the stock has cratered to $3.71, roughly 36% below the tender floor price and 43% below the ceiling. That gap between tender price and current market is the single most important fact in this writeup.

Yext is a digital presence / knowledge graph SaaS company — the tech that makes sure your business hours and address show up correctly across Google, Apple Maps, Yelp, Alexa, and hundreds of other endpoints. It acquired Hearsay Systems in 2024 to expand into regulated-industry social selling. Revenue sits in the ~$400-420M range with mid-single-digit growth at best and frequent quarters of decline. The AI-search-eating-SEO narrative has become both its opportunity and its existential threat.

### ANALYST SUMMARY (3p)
**(1) The tender was a signal, not a bottom.** Boards run self-tenders when they believe the stock is undervalued and they have excess cash. Yext's board paid up to $6.50 with the full knowledge of what Q4 FY26 looked like internally. Six weeks later the stock is at $3.71. That is a brutal signal: either (a) the post-tender quarter came in materially worse than management telegraphed, (b) the float reduction exposed thin buyer base and the stock fell through its natural clearing level, or (c) both. All three interpretations are bearish.

**(2) The "AI search will kill SEO, ergo Yext wins as the structured-data provider" thesis is not obviously working.** The bull case for Yext has always been that as LLMs and AI search agents replace the ten blue links, those agents need authoritative structured data about businesses — and Yext is the plumbing. That's logically coherent. The problem is Google, Apple, and OpenAI have every incentive to scrape or compel the data directly rather than pay Yext a subscription. Four years into the AI search era, Yext is not growing meaningfully. The thesis is getting harder to defend with each quarter of flat results.

**(3) The valuation math depends heavily on FCF, not revenue.** At $3.71 and a post-tender share count meaningfully reduced (roughly 22-25M shares retired at $6.125 midpoint = ~$140M/avg price), market cap is in the $400-450M range. Yext generates $50-70M of FCF in a decent year. That's 7-9x FCF — cheap on paper. The catch: FCF quality is poor (heavy stock-based comp, long-dated deferred revenue cushion), and a decelerating SaaS business re-rates to lower multiples, not higher ones. Private equity could theoretically take this out at $5-6 given the FCF profile, but no such buyer has materialized despite years of depressed pricing.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Screens see "recent self-tender closed at $6.50" and assume that's a floor. It's not. A tender sets a reservation price for *shareholders who tendered*. Everyone who held through it is marked to the new market, which is $3.71. The screen also can't see the float dynamics: post-tender liquidity is thinner, so selling pressure from a disappointed holder disproportionately moves the stock.

The other invisible layer: Hearsay acquisition integration. The financial services vertical is the part of Yext that could plausibly get sold to a strategic (Salesforce, HubSpot, or a compliance-focused private equity rollup). If management splits the company — sell Hearsay, keep the core listings business — there may be a sum-of-parts case at $5-6. No public indication of that path today.

### CATALYSTS
- **Q1 FY27 earnings (May 2026):** First post-tender print; confirms or denies the "tender masked weak quarter" thesis
- **AI search partnership / licensing deal:** Would validate the structural bull case; absence continues to erode it
- **Activist emergence:** At $400M market cap with depressed multiple and completed tender, a 13D filer demanding sale or split is plausible
- **Strategic review announcement:** Low probability near-term but rising if the stock stays below $4

### MUNGER CHECKLIST
- **Circle of competence:** B2B SaaS with AI disruption overlay — not simple. The "is AI search a tailwind or existential threat" question is genuinely hard to answer confidently.
- **Moat:** Modest and eroding. Network of listing endpoints has some stickiness; switching costs are real but not enormous. Hearsay has a compliance moat in regulated verticals.
- **Management:** Michael Walrath (CEO) is a competent operator with public-markets pedigree. The decision to run a tender at $6.50 with the stock now at $3.71 is either unlucky timing or capital-allocation miss.
- **Margin of safety:** $3.71 price with ~$50M+ FCF and net cash gives a decent floor, but melting-ice-cube risk is real. PARTIAL.
- **Price:** Optically cheap, fundamentally uncertain. The tender-gap is a red flag, not a green one.

**Bottom line:** The tender is done and was not a bottom. This is now a fundamental call on whether Yext can grow again in the AI era — and the evidence does not yet support that. Pass unless Q1 FY27 print surprises meaningfully to the upside or an activist/strategic catalyst emerges.
