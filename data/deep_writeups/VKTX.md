# {NUMBER}. VKTX — Viking Therapeutics, Inc.
## FDA BINARY | $28.00 / ~$3.1B mkt cap | Score: 52/100

### THE SETUP
Viking Therapeutics is a clinical-stage biotech with one asset that matters: VK2735, a dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist for obesity, in both injectable (subQ) and oral formulations. The company is a pure-play obesity biotech competing in a category whose two incumbents (Eli Lilly's tirzepatide / Mounjaro / Zepbound and Novo Nordisk's semaglutide / Ozempic / Wegovy) have minted hundreds of billions in market cap. Viking's market cap of $3.1B is a rounding error against that TAM, which is exactly why the stock has acted like a perpetual M&A candidate.

The next event that matters is the Phase 1 maintenance dosing study readout, expected mid-2026, with Phase 2 oral data flowing in H2 2026. Each readout is a binary: clean efficacy / tolerability data could re-rate the stock 30-50% in a day; muddy data or a tox surprise could halve it. That is what "FDA Binary" means as a tier label.

### ANALYST SUMMARY (3p)

**Paragraph 1 — The asset.** VK2735 has shown competitive weight-loss efficacy in early-stage data, with Phase 2 subQ results suggesting weight loss in the same general league as the GLP-1 incumbents on a relative basis. The oral program is the higher-leverage shot — if Viking can demonstrate competitive oral efficacy with an acceptable tolerability profile, it leapfrogs into the same conversation as Lilly's orforglipron and Novo's oral semaglutide. The maintenance dosing study is important because real-world obesity therapy is chronic; demonstrating that patients can hold weight loss with a less-frequent or lower-dose maintenance regimen is what payers and prescribers need to see for sustainable franchise economics.

**Paragraph 2 — Why it's binary.** Viking has no approved products, no revenue of consequence, and burns cash funding its trials. The entire equity value is a discounted call option on (a) clinical success, (b) commercial reach via partnership or strategic sale, and (c) competitive positioning against LLY/NVO and the next wave of asset-class entrants (Roche's CT-388, AMGN's MariTide, Structure Therapeutics, Altimmune, etc.). Any one of these factors moving against Viking compresses the option value sharply. The flip side: a single positive readout in a hot category, with an acquirer hungry to backfill its obesity pipeline, can multiply the stock.

**Paragraph 3 — The M&A overlay.** Big Pharma is openly chasing obesity TAM, and Viking has been on every "potential takeout" list for two-plus years. The strategic logic for Pfizer (failed danuglipron), Merck, AstraZeneca, or even Roche to acquire Viking is straightforward: you buy a Phase 2/3 asset with proof-of-concept data and a real shot at a top-3 oral position in the largest pharma category in history. The reason it hasn't happened yet is price discipline — acquirers want to wait for derisking events; Viking's board wants to wait for upside catalysts. The mid-2026 readouts could be the alignment trigger.
