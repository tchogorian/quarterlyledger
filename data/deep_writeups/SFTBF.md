# {NUMBER}. SFTBF — Sofina S.A.
## HOLDCO — NAV DISCOUNT | ~25% discount to indicative NAV | Score: 74/100

### THE SETUP
Sofina is a 130+ year-old Belgian listed holding company controlled by the Boël family — one of the older industrial dynasties in Belgium, with a track record of compounding NAV at roughly 13% annually over three decades. Unlike a traditional European holdco that owns one or two industrial flagships, Sofina runs a diversified late-stage venture and growth-equity book organized in three pillars: Long-term Minority Investments (~50% of NAV — direct minority stakes in private and listed growth companies), Sofina Growth (~28% — late-stage tech), and Sofina Private Funds Investment (~22% — LP positions in top-decile VC/PE managers including Sequoia, Insight Partners, and a roster of Asian growth funds). Indicative NAV per share at H2 2025 sat around EUR 297–298. Today's price (~EUR 240 / ~$240 USD on the SFTBF pink sheet) implies roughly a 25% discount to NAV — versus only 6% in mid-2025 and 17% at year-end 2025. This is one of the wider NAV discounts Sofina has traded at in the last decade and reflects a confluence of overhangs rather than any change in management quality.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
What you're buying at today's discount is a high-quality, relationship-driven late-stage venture book at roughly 75 cents on the euro, with a Belgian family operator that has decades of disciplined compounding behind it. The portfolio includes residual exposure to Byju's (already heavily written down), Stripe (multi-year IPO candidate), ByteDance (held indirectly via several top-tier VC funds), Coursera, Mango, Aleph Farms, plus a diversified LP book in Sequoia, Insight, and other premier managers. The widening of the discount from 6% to 25% over nine months is not driven by NAV deterioration — H2 2025 NAV actually rose ~1% — but by sentiment: skepticism on private-company marks generally, China-internet exposure (ByteDance), Byju's writedown headlines, Belgian small-cap illiquidity, and family-control discount. None of those reasons is permanent. Sofina is actively repurchasing shares below NAV (a direct per-share NAV accretion), and even modestly conservative private marks would still leave the implied discount at >20%. The asymmetric bet is straightforward: at 13% historical NAV CAGR plus a 25% discount that could partially close on any single liquidity event (Stripe IPO, ByteDance secondary), total returns of 20%+ annualized are mathematically plausible without heroic assumptions.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Three things US screens miss entirely. First, SFTBF on US pink sheets is a thin echo of the much more liquid Brussels SOF.BR listing — you're buying the same security but US screens may show stale quotes and zero institutional ownership, falsely flagging it as "uninvestable." Second, the NAV calculation is published semi-annually with a lag, so the headline "discount" you see is calculated against a stale NAV; in periods when private valuations have likely moved (Stripe last secondary at $91B+, Insight Partners' marks tightening), the look-through discount is even wider than the headline. Third, Sofina runs a permanent-capital model — no LP redemption pressure means they can be patient through full venture cycles, the way Berkshire is patient through equity cycles. Most peer European holdcos (Investor AB, Wendel) own listed industrial blue chips; Sofina's edge is its access to top-decile private growth managers.

### CATALYSTS
- Stripe IPO (long expected; would mark up Sofina's indirect stake materially)
- ByteDance partial monetization, secondary, or eventual IPO
- Continued at-discount buybacks (every one is per-share NAV-accretive)
- H1 2026 NAV update with stable or rising private marks
- NAV-discount mean reversion toward the historic 10–15% range
- Possible upgrade to MSCI Belgium or other index inclusion as float dynamics normalize

- Durable moat: Yes — multi-decade relationships with top VC/PE managers and direct deal access are not easily replicated
- Honest, capable management: Yes — Boël family has compounded NAV at ~13% over 30+ years; disciplined buyback below NAV
- Bought at fair price: Yes — ~75 cents on the euro for a quality late-stage portfolio is one of the wider discounts in a decade
- Margin of safety: Strong — discount provides cushion; even modest private-mark write-downs leave look-through value above current price
