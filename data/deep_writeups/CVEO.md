### THE SETUP
Civeo trades at $30.18 against Engine Capital's $47 standalone fair value, with two activist-nominated directors (Jeffrey Scofield and Daniel Silvers) seated on the board as of November 25, 2025 to enforce capital-return discipline and operational fix at this $330M micro-cap workforce-accommodations operator.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Civeo owns and operates remote workforce villages serving the Australian iron-ore and metallurgical-coal corridors and Canadian oil sands — essentially camps with rooms, food service, and logistics for resource workers. Revenue is occupancy-driven and tied to commodity-cycle capex by majors like BHP, Rio Tinto, and the Canadian heavy-oil players. The screen flags it because of Engine Capital's 9.8% 13D filed March 20, 2025, the public letter campaign, and the November 25, 2025 cooperation agreement that placed two activist directors on the board. Stock at $30.18 has rallied from sub-$20 but trails the activist's $47 standalone math (4–5x EBITDA on a normalized base).

Bear case: workforce accommodations is a brutally cyclical, customer-concentrated business with low switching costs once a project ends. A roll-over in iron-ore prices or a delay in major Canadian oil-sands sustaining-capex cycles can crush occupancy and FCF in two quarters. The Australian segment competes with Civeo's own customers' in-house camps and with private competitors. The dividend was eliminated as part of the activist deal, which removes one source of holder demand. Micro-cap liquidity ($330M) means any institutional exit is painful, and the float is tiny.

Bull case: Engine has won board representation and committed CEO to 1.75x leverage, aggressive buybacks, and cost cuts — exactly the playbook that re-rated similar small-cap services names. Australian iron-ore demand from China remains supportive into 2026, and Canadian oil sands operators are stepping up sustaining capex. At ~$30 versus the $47 standalone path, there is roughly 50% upside before considering any sale process. PE or strategic interest is plausible — Civeo is exactly the kind of cash-generative niche asset a sponsor would tuck into a portfolio. Engine has skin in the game with 9.8% economic exposure.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Nov 25, 2025 cooperation agreement is a true behavior contract, not a token concession — the board now has two activist seats out of the slate, and the agreement explicitly references capital allocation, leverage targets, and review of the dividend, all of which were conceded by management. The 10-K segment disclosures show Australian segment EBITDA margins materially above Canadian, but the consolidated multiple values both equally — a sale would let a buyer tax-efficiently separate the two and capture sum-of-parts uplift. Engine's public deck specifically called out a $47 standalone target derived from 4-5x EBITDA on what they view as run-rate FCF, and the activists are now inside the boardroom to hold management accountable to that math.
