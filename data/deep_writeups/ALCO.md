# {NUMBER}. ALCO — Alico, Inc.
## HOLDCO — NAV DISCOUNT | Florida land-bank | Score: 70/100

### THE SETUP
Alico is exiting 100+ years of citrus and pivoting to a pure Florida land-development play: ~53,371 acres owned plus ~48,700 acres of oil/gas/mineral rights, with the Corkscrew Grove Villages entitlement decision pending in 2026. Stock prices the operating losses; the land-bank optionality is the asymmetry.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Alico (NASDAQ: ALCO) announced its strategic transformation in January 2025: wind down Alico Citrus after the FY2025 harvest and reposition as a diversified Florida land company. As of Sep 30, 2025 the company controlled 49,537 acres in Florida (39,297 in citrus groves, 10,240 in land management), with 75% targeted to remain agricultural and 25% identified for commercial/residential development. FY2025 (ended Sep 30, 2025) net loss was $147.3M (driven by impairments and wind-down charges), but adjusted EBITDA was $22.5M (above $20M guidance) and land sales hit $23.8M generating a $20.3M gain. Year-end balance sheet: $38.1M cash, net debt $47.4M. The flagship project — Corkscrew Grove Villages — contemplates two 1,500-acre villages with ~9,000 homes and 560,000 sq ft commercial; final Collier County approval expected 2026.

Bear case: citrus greening disease and hurricane damage drove a 73% decade decline in production, validating the wind-down but also signalling secularly impaired core operations and ongoing decommissioning costs. Florida entitlement risk is real — Collier County approval is not pro forma; environmental, water-management, and growth-cap politics can delay or strip density. Debt-to-assets rose to 0.43 (from 0.23) as land-bank value backs new borrowing rather than recurring cash flow. Until entitlements clear, Alico is an illiquid, lumpy, low-multiple holding-company that consumes cash. Float and trading liquidity are thin; small-cap discount may persist.

Bull case: Florida raw land in a path-of-growth corridor (Collier, Hendry, Glades counties) is structurally scarce; reported land sales already exceed guidance and provide independent third-party value marks (~$8.5K/acre on FY25 sales). With ~50K acres and a fortress entitlement project, NAV materially exceeds the ~$300M-ish equity market value implied. Cash + planned land sales fund operations through FY2027, giving runway to monetize entitlements without distress. Insider/founder family ownership aligns long-term decisions.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 48,700 acres of oil, gas and mineral rights are essentially zero-carried optionality — never picked up by a screener that focuses on operating segments. Beyond Corkscrew, the company has agreed to lease another 5,250 acres to third-party citrus growers, generating cash without owning the operating risk — a hidden landlord business inside what looks like a wind-down. The fact that adjusted EBITDA exceeded guidance during a transformation year suggests management can sequence land sales to defend covenants while unlocking entitlements. Watch the 10-K for any per-acre carrying-value disclosure — book value is depreciated agricultural cost and dramatically understates SW Florida residential land marks.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)

- FY2026 land-sales pace (run-rate trending ~$30-50M annually)
- Updated NAV/value framework at next investor presentation (post wind-down)
