# {NUMBER}. AXSM — Axsome Therapeutics, Inc.
## FDA BINARY | PDUFA Apr 30 2026, AD agitation | Score: 55/100

### THE SETUP
April 30, 2026 PDUFA decision for AXS-05 (Auvelity / dextromethorphan-bupropion) in Alzheimer's disease agitation — would be the first oral therapy approved in a 6M+ patient indication with no current standard of care. Unlike a typical biotech binary, AXSM has a real $638M revenue base from Auvelity in MDD providing a downside floor: even on label rejection, the company has $323M cash and a path to cash-flow positivity. Asymmetric setup with a hard date.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Axsome is a CNS-focused commercial-stage biotech with three approved products: Auvelity (dextromethorphan-bupropion, MDD — $507M FY25 sales, +74%), Sunosi (solriamfetol, narcolepsy/EDS — $125M, acquired from Jazz), and Symbravo (migraine, launched June 2025 — $7M ramping). Total FY25 revenue $638.5M (+66% YoY), Q4 net loss $28.6M, FY net loss $183M (narrowed from $287M in 2024). Cash $322.9M at YE25 — management states sufficient to fund operations into cash flow positivity. The binary event: sNDA for AXS-05 in Alzheimer's disease agitation accepted with Priority Review, PDUFA April 30, 2026; submission supported by four Phase 3 trials and a long-term safety study. Breakthrough designation since 2020. Approval would expand the addressable market materially — up to 76% of AD patients experience agitation, and there is no FDA-approved oral treatment.

Bear case (AdComm/CRL risk): FDA agitation indications have historically been treacherous. Brexpiprazole (Otsuka/Lundbeck) was approved in May 2023 for AD agitation but with a black-box warning and limited uptake. AXS-05's MDD label already includes warnings around bupropion (seizure risk, suicidality) and dextromethorphan abuse potential — reviewers may demand REMS or restrictive labeling that throttles the AD launch. The 10-K acknowledges that "we may not be successful in obtaining or maintaining" regulatory approvals. Sell-side has already de-risked the launch into the price ($9B mcap). On a CRL, the stock could re-rate to the standalone MDD/Sunosi value of ~$5–6B (-30%+). Pipeline depth (AXS-12 narcolepsy, solriamfetol Phase 3s, AXS-17 epilepsy) helps but won't catch a CRL fall.

Bull case: differentiated MoA (NMDA antagonist + sigma-1 agonist) vs Otsuka's atypical antipsychotic, cleaner safety profile in dementia patients, and dosing is oral BID — fits the long-term-care setting where IM antipsychotics struggle. AD agitation TAM = 5–6M U.S. patients, even modest penetration at $7–10K annual net price puts peak sales at $1.5–3B. Auvelity MDD is already a hit (74% growth, 225K Q4 prescriptions), so the commercial infrastructure is in place — incremental sales force expansion is the only meaningful spend. Cushion: $323M cash + $640M revenue base + clear path to profitability means even a CRL-and-resubmit scenario is survivable. Approval-day gap-up could be 30–50%; CRL gap-down 25–35%; expected value asymmetric to the upside given pipeline optionality.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K and prospectus disclose AXS-05 composition-of-matter and method-of-use patents extending into the late 2030s with deuterated-formulation IP layering further protection — not the typical "patent cliff in 5 years" biotech screen flag. The Sunosi acquisition from Jazz (closed 2022) also brought a March 2026 IP win that extended exclusivity, removing the most obvious bear case. Auvelity scripts grew 42% YoY in Q4 2025 — well above sell-side modeling. Importantly, Axsome did NOT take an FDA AdComm meeting on file (so far), which historically signals FDA confidence in the dataset; competitor brexpiprazole's AD-agitation review went to AdComm and still got approved. Cash burn deceleration from -$287M to -$183M in one year is not a screen-visible inflection.
