# {NUMBER}. OGN — Organon & Co.
## TAKE-PRIVATE | Sun Pharma + Grünenthal Bidding War | Score: 72/100

### THE SETUP
Bloomberg reported April 16, 2026 that Sun Pharmaceutical and Grünenthal are submitting binding bids for Organon at a reported ~$12B / ~$45-per-share level — implying a 22.95% gross spread to the current $9.76 price. Board has not yet accepted; this is bid-on-bid speculation, not a signed deal.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Organon is the women's-health and established-brands spinoff Merck completed in June 2021. The portfolio is Nexplanon (long-acting reversible contraceptive — the crown jewel, growing double digits and patent-protected to 2027 with formulation extensions through 2030+), a biosimilars portfolio (Hadlima, Renflexis, Brenzys, Aybintio), and a long tail of established off-patent brands (Singulair, Nasonex, Cozaar, Zetia, Vytorin) that throw off cash but decline 3-7% annually. The company has been a serial value-trap since the spin: leverage was excessive at separation (~4x), the dividend was cut in 2024, and Nexplanon LOE (loss of exclusivity) hangs over the model.

The bear case for the deal: there is no signed merger agreement. "Binding bids" reported in Bloomberg can collapse — boards reject offers, financing falls through, due diligence finds skeletons. Sun Pharma is Indian-domiciled and Grünenthal is German private — both face CFIUS / cross-border review, and women's-health products may attract political sensitivity. The reported $12B price is 8x EBITDA, which is rich for a declining-asset pharma roll-up. If the deal falls apart, OGN reverts to its pre-rumor trading range ($7-9), implying ~20% downside vs. ~36% upside if it closes at $45 — symmetric, not asymmetric. Nexplanon LOE in 2027 is the elephant: any acquirer is buying a wasting asset.

The bull case: bidding wars typically settle higher than initial bids — Sun and Grünenthal both want the women's-health franchise, and a third bidder (rumored: a sovereign wealth-backed consortium, possibly EQT or KKR) could enter. Nexplanon generates $1B+ in revenue at 80%+ gross margin — even with LOE, follow-on next-gen formulations (Nexplanon NXT in late-stage trials) extend the franchise. The biosimilars business is structurally undervalued in the standalone trading multiple. If a deal closes anywhere from $42-50, the IRR over a 9-12 month timeline annualizes to 25-35%.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Organon's 10-K discloses Nexplanon revenue concentration explicitly — it is the single largest product and the asset any acquirer is really buying. Hidden in segment disclosure: the Established Brands portfolio still throws off ~$3B in highly-cash-generative revenue at minimal R&D reinvestment, which is exactly what a PE-style sponsor or generics consolidator wants. Sun Pharma specifically has built its U.S. franchise via established-brands acquisitions (Taro, Ranbaxy assets) and knows how to run wind-down portfolios. The spread to the rumored bid (~23%) is unusually wide for a confirmed-bidders situation — the market is pricing meaningful (~50%) probability of no deal, which screens as "M&A arb" but is really event-probability arbitrage on whether a binding bid converts to a signed agreement.
