### THE SETUP
Black Pearl Equities filed an SC TO-T in March 2026 to take Selectis private at $5.05 per share — a 45% premium to the pre-announcement price — but the board has not endorsed it, the financing comes from a single thinly-known lender, and the 51% minimum-tender condition is the kind of trip-wire that kills micro-cap hostile bids.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Selectis Health (OTCQB: GBCS) operates skilled-nursing and assisted-living facilities across the southeastern U.S. — small fleet, long-lived real estate, tight Medicaid economics. Like many sub-$30M micro-cap nursing operators, it trades by appointment, has minimal float in unaffiliated hands, and almost no sell-side coverage. Black Pearl Equities' all-cash hostile tender at $5.05 totals roughly $15.5M of equity and is being financed via Milrose Capital LLC. The bid expires May 11, 2026 at 5pm New York time. Current price of $4.40 implies a 14.8% gross spread — extraordinarily wide for a tender supposedly closing in three weeks, which tells you the market is pricing meaningful execution risk, not skepticism about Black Pearl's intent.

The bear case dominates the spread. First, the board has not recommended the offer. In a hostile tender for a controlled or insider-heavy micro-cap, board hostility is often dispositive — management can deploy a poison pill, dilutive issuance, or simply refuse to release diligence to push the bid back. Second, the 51% minimum-tender condition is brutal in OTC stocks where shares are scattered across cost-basis-anchored retail holders who don't read tenders. Third, Milrose Capital LLC is not a household name; financing risk on a $15.5M micro-cap deal where the lender controls the closing condition is real. Fourth, even if the tender succeeds in reaching majority, going-private squeeze-out litigation in micro-cap SNF deals is endemic.

The bull case is the asymmetry: $5.05 vs $4.40, with a hard May 11 stop. If the tender fails the stock probably round-trips to the pre-bid level near $3.50, implying ~20% downside vs ~15% upside — but the catalyst window is 3 weeks, so on annualized terms the bet is mathematically attractive for someone who can size it small. A friendly counter-bid or board capitulation at a token bump would close the gap quickly. Real-estate-rich SNF operators have attracted multiple PE bids in the past two years.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K filed April 15, 2026 should be read for two things only: lease structure (master leases or facility-by-facility) and Medicaid receivables aging. Master-lease structures with cross-default provisions can torpedo a take-private if the lessor has change-of-control consents — a frequent deal-breaker in nursing home M&A that is invisible from screens but lives in lease exhibits. Also check for any shareholder agreements or voting trusts; Selectis has a concentrated insider register, and a single non-tendering 10%+ holder can sink the 51% condition single-handedly.

- Schedule 14D-9 board response (10 business days from commencement)
- Any superior-proposal announcement or financing-condition update from Black Pearl
