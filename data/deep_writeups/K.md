# {NUMBER}. K — Kellanova
## M&A ARBITRAGE | Mars cash deal at $83.50; close mid-2026 | Score: 78/100

### THE SETUP
Kellanova trades at $82.00 against a binding Mars cash offer of $83.50, a ~1.8% gross spread on a deal that has cleared most major antitrust reviews and is on track to close mid-2026. The asymmetry is structural: a confectionery + pet giant buying a global savory-snack platform has minimal product overlap, the financing is committed, and Mars is a 100-year-old privately held buyer with no shareholder vote of its own. This is a low-drama spread with optional upside if a competing bidder emerges or the timetable accelerates.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Kellanova is the post-2023-spin global snacking and international cereal business — Pringles, Cheez-It, Pop-Tarts, Rice Krispies Treats, Eggo, plus international cereal and noodles markets where Kellogg has presence (LATAM, AMEA). The August 2024 Mars acquisition agreement at $83.50 cash valued the company at roughly $36B including debt. Mars wants the salty-snack platform to round out its Snickers/M&M's confectionery base and to leverage Pringles' global distribution; the strategic logic is unusually clean. Most US and EU antitrust reviews have advanced, with only narrow remedies expected in markets where snack-bar/cracker overlap exists.

The bear case: deal breaks. Antitrust risk is concentrated in a few SKUs (snack bars, certain crackers) and in jurisdictions like the EU where Mars/Wrigley overlap with select Kellanova products. China's SAMR is a wildcard given current geopolitical tensions and historic delays. If the deal collapses, K's standalone valuation likely resets toward $70 — still a tolerable floor (mid-teens P/E on demonstrated mid-single-digit organic growth) but a ~15% drawdown from the deal price. Some legacy holders forced to sell on receipt (mandates restricting PE/foreign-controlled investments) provide modest sustained selling pressure into close.

The bull case: at $82 with a $83.50 ceiling and an estimated mid-2026 close, the gross spread of ~1.8% annualizes to roughly mid-single-digits depending on close date — attractive in absolute terms in a market starved of deal flow, and the downside is fundamentally protected. Catalysts are crisp: each remaining antitrust clearance compresses spread, and any indication of a competing bidder (PepsiCo briefly explored before Mars locked it up) would punch through the ceiling. Even without a topping bid, the deal arbitrage is one of the cleaner large-cap setups available in 2026.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The August 2024 merger proxy details a $1.25B reverse termination fee payable by Mars if antitrust kills the deal — a hard floor on broken-deal damages. The proxy also confirms Mars committed financing in place from JPMorgan and Citi, eliminating financing risk that has plagued other 2024-2025 deals. Less obvious: Kellanova's standalone 10-K shows international snacking growing high-single-digits in constant currency with margin expansion as the post-spin SAP migration completes — meaning if the deal extends or breaks, the underlying business is in better shape than the headline NA-flat snacks narrative suggests. The MD&A specifically calls out productivity savings programs running ahead of plan.

### CATALYSTS

- Mid-2026 — Deal close and cash payment
- Pre-close — Possible competing bid or revised terms
