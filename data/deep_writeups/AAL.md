### THE SETUP
A speculative spread on the most-blocked merger imaginable: United CEO Scott Kirby pitched a tie-up with American to the Trump White House on Feb 25, 2026. ### ANALYST SUMMARY

American Airlines Group (AAL) is the largest US carrier by passengers, with $54.6B in 2025 revenue, just $111M of net income, $0.17 diluted EPS, and total assets of $61.8B. The 10-K (filed Feb 18, 2026) discloses $9.2B total liquidity ($5.8B unrestricted cash and short-term investments + $3.4B undrawn revolver capacity), and a covenant-mandated minimum of $2.0B unrestricted cash. American carries massive unionized labor (14,970 APA pilots, 26,400 APFA flight attendants, 13,030 customer service, 19,460 fleet service) with most contracts amendable in 2027–2029. The 2025 financial performance was hammered by the January 2025 PSA midair collision near Reagan National (~$200M Q1 revenue impact) and a sharp drop in operating income from $2,614M (2024) to $1,467M (2025) on flat revenue.

The bear case is overwhelming: this is not a deal — it's reported chatter that AAL's own CEO publicly disowned. American on April 17, 2026 stated unequivocally it was "not engaged with or interested in any discussions regarding a merger" with United. Even if a deal were proposed, the combined US-domestic market share of 30–40% would face near-certain DOJ block (top-4 carriers already control ~80%); FAA slot/route reviews; pilot seniority integration would take years. Beyond the rumor, AAL's underlying business has standalone problems: 76 pilot/labor contracts amendable in 2027 = wage-pressure cliff; 6.50% convertible notes already paid; aircraft delivery commitments require ongoing long-term debt issuance; basic-economy fare competition from low-cost carriers compressing yields.

The bull case requires either (a) the rumor having more substance than current public disclosures suggest, or (b) AAL's standalone equity working as a cyclical recovery + deleveraging story. On (a), Trump's DOJ has signaled openness to consolidation broadly, but Transportation Sec. Duffy already indicated "significant divestitures would be required." On (b), at $12.78 with $54.6B revenue and 660M shares, market cap is ~$8.4B against trailing operating income of $1.47B — that's ~6x EV/EBIT before mid-cycle margin recovery. If pilot CBA negotiations stay rational and fuel cooperates, a return to $2.5B+ operating income is plausible by 2027, making this a 5x earnings story without any deal.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K explicitly contemplates merger risk as a one-way negative: "Other mergers and other forms of airline partnerships, including regulatory approvals such as antitrust immunity grants, may take place and may not involve us, or could result in unforeseen impacts on the industry." American's strategic posture is defensive — they are NOT the consolidator. Republic Airways (a regional partner) is referenced in the XBRL tags multiple times as Oct/Nov 2025 transactions, suggesting AAL has been quietly restructuring its regional-feed economics. The pilot union (APA) contract is amendable in 2027 — a high-stakes negotiation with no merger leverage to deflect labor demands. The Reagan National PSA crash also created multi-plaintiff lawsuits against American/PSA (separate from US Government suits), creating tail litigation exposure not yet quantified in financials.
