# {NUMBER}. FOLD — Amicus Therapeutics, Inc.
## M&A ARBITRAGE | $14.50 all-cash | Score: 65/100

### THE SETUP
$4.8B all-cash acquisition by BioMarin at $14.50; HSR cleared early February 2026, shareholders approved March 3, only foreign regulators outstanding. Spread of 28 bps reflects market conviction Q2 2026 close happens — pure arbitrage with low-but-real EU/Japan tail risk.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Amicus is a rare-disease biotech with two commercial assets — Galafold (Fabry disease) generating ~$500M annually with high gross margins, and Pombiliti+Opfolda (Pompe disease, FDA approved Sept 2023) ramping toward $200M+. BioMarin's $14.50/share cash offer (announced 12/19/2025) values Amicus at ~5.5x trailing revenue — a standard rare-disease premium. The strategic logic is clean: BioMarin already owns Vimizim, Naglazyme, Brineura — adding Galafold and Pombiliti consolidates the rare-LSD category and provides commercial-team leverage in Europe.

Bear case: At $14.46 vs $14.50, the spread is statistically zero; capital is dead-weight unless an investor needs cash equivalents with binary tail risk. The remaining EU/Japan regulatory approvals are routine but not guaranteed — EU sometimes demands divestiture remedies on rare-disease consolidations (BioMarin/Amicus overlap on Pompe-adjacent indications). A deal break would crater FOLD by 30-40% to standalone fair value of ~$9-10. Annualized return at sub-2% does not compensate for that 2-3% break probability.

Bull/catalyst: HSR early termination in February signals zero antitrust concern at home; EU Phase II review window typically resolves within 90 days of filing. BioMarin paid 25% premium to the unaffected price, satisfying typical shareholder expectations and reducing appraisal-action risk to nominal levels. With shareholder vote already passed, the execution path is purely procedural. For arb desks willing to lever 5-10x, a 1.66% gross spread becomes meaningful absolute return at near-100% certainty over a 2-3 month window.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The merger proxy reveals Amicus's CEO Bradley Campbell received an enhanced change-of-control package totaling ~$28M, vesting on the standard "single-trigger" basis, removing executive incentive to delay close. More significantly, the merger agreement contains a "Most Favored Nation" reverse-termination fee of $250M payable by BioMarin if it walks for any non-regulatory reason — large enough to deter a renegotiation gambit. The Galafold patent estate runs to 2031 in the US (with possible PED extension) — BioMarin's bid implicitly values this royalty stream at ~$3B alone, leaving Pombiliti as essentially "free" optionality that BioMarin clearly believes will outperform Street.
