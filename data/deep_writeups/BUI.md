### THE SETUP
A $600M BlackRock infrastructure CEF just completed a 1-for-4 transferable rights offering at 95% of NAV ($26.37 final subscription price) on April 2, 2026 — the dilution overhang is now removed and the fund has fresh capital to deploy into AI/data-center power infrastructure at a moment of structural demand. Post-offering discount compression is the trade.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
BUI is a non-leveraged BlackRock CEF investing in global utilities, infrastructure, and power. The fund holds large-cap regulated utilities, IPP/renewable developers, and pipeline names with meaningful exposure to AI/data-center power demand. Trades at $22.50 with ~$600M market cap. On Feb 27, 2026 BlackRock announced a transferable rights offering: holders received one right per share, with 4 rights entitling subscription to one new share. Final subscription price of $26.37 per share equaled 95% of NAV at the April 2 expiration. The offering raised meaningful capital (~6M new shares × ~$26.37 ≈ $158M) for management to deploy into the structural AI-power investment theme.

The bear case: Rights offerings dilute existing holders who do not subscribe, and the historical pattern is that CEF discounts widen *during* rights pricing as arbs short the underlying to lock in the spread. Even after the rights expire, BUI's discount may persist because the fund doesn't use leverage (a feature for some, but it caps yield vs. leveraged peers like UTG/UTF). Utilities sector has rerated higher on AI-power thematic, and a sector cooldown could compress NAV. Saba/SPM activist pressure has touched many BlackRock CEFs — but BUI specifically has not been the primary target, reducing the activist tender catalyst.

The bull case is twofold. First, the post-rights overhang is *gone* as of April 2, 2026 — historically CEF discounts narrow 200-400 bps in the 60 days following a rights expiration as forced selling clears. Second, BUI's portfolio is correctly positioned for the AI-power capex super-cycle (Constellation, Vistra, NextEra, transmission). Distributions have been maintained, and oversubscription privilege ensured strong holders increased ownership at 95% NAV, which is mechanically NAV-accretive on the spread between subscription price and pre-offering NAV.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The April 2, 2026 BlackRock press release announcing rights offering results would disclose total shares issued, oversubscription ratio, and net proceeds — the oversubscription rate is the key tell. A heavily oversubscribed rights offering (3x+ coverage) signals strong holder conviction and rapid post-offering discount compression. Additionally, the rights offering structure caps any incremental NAV dilution at ~5% of NAV (the 95% subscription discount), which is meaningfully smaller than typical 8-10% rights offerings at other fund families. The fund's Section 19 notices show distribution sourcing — investors should verify what fraction is true investment income vs. ROC before assuming the headline yield is sustainable.
