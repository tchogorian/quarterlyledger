### ANALYST SUMMARY
Mitsubishi Corporation is the largest of Japan's five sogo shosha (general trading houses) and Buffett's largest sogo shosha holding inside Berkshire Hathaway. Through ten business groups, MC owns or operates positions across natural gas / LNG, mineral resources (notably copper via the Anglo American JV), industrial materials, automotive (50% of Mitsubishi Motors), food, infrastructure, and consumer/urban development (including 50% of Lawson convenience-store chain alongside KDDI). At ~$35.10 per ADR (8058.T in Tokyo) versus indicative NAV near $47, the discount is roughly 25%, with Berkshire's recent step-up to 10.23% providing institutional validation.

The bear case rests on commodity cyclicality and structural Japan-discount stickiness. MC's resource-heavy mix means earnings re-rate down with copper, met coal, and LNG cycles. Western analysts struggle with the sogo shosha model — equity-method investments dominate the income statement, segment disclosure is dense, and JPY translation drags reported USD figures. Regulatory and political risk in resource-extraction geographies (Chile, Australia, Russia historically) is real. The Japan listing premium may not close as fast as the bull case implies; the TSE "PBR<1" campaign is moral suasion, not a binding requirement.

The bull case is conviction-grade. Berkshire's ongoing accumulation past 10% — and Buffett's repeated public admiration for the sogo shosha model — is the strongest possible third-party validation. The Aethon shale acquisition (~$5.2B, closing 2026) demonstrates the capital-deployment muscle Western generalists discount. Management's MC Shared Value 2024 mid-term plan commits to 9–10% ROE and aggressive buybacks (1T+ JPY year-to-date). Lawson's 2024 take-private with KDDI cleaned up a noisy quasi-listed asset. Catalysts are stacked through 2026.
