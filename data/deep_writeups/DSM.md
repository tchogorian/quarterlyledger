### ANALYST SUMMARY
DSM is a small (~$300M) leveraged municipal bond CEF run by BNY Mellon. Like most muni CEFs in the post-2022 rate regime, persistent NAV-discount erosion has invited Saba Capital, the most successful CEF activist of the past decade. Saba has filed Schedule 13D/A reflecting 5.18M shares (10.31%), nominated Paul Kazarian for the 2026 annual meeting, and BNY's board has filed a preliminary contested proxy (PREC14A) urging a WHITE card. The asymmetry is structural: NAV $6.52 vs. $6.11 price = 6.3% spot discount, with a 3-year average closer to -11.4%.

The bear case is that muni CEF discounts are sticky for a reason — leverage costs are high (SOFR-linked preferreds eat into distributable income), and sponsors have repeatedly out-lasted activists at the ballot box by mobilizing retail. Even if Saba wins, a tender capped at 10–15% of shares outstanding only nibbles at the discount; a full liquidation/open-ending requires a supermajority and a long legal slog. Tax-exempt income preference also means the underlying portfolio is hard to unwind without harming remaining holders. Activism in muni-CEF land has historically yielded 3–6% pops, not 20%.

The bull case is more nuanced: Saba's track record vs. BNY specifically is strong, and BNY has already settled with Saba on prior funds via tender offers. A standstill agreement with a 25–40% NAV-tender at minimum closes the spot 6.3% gap and likely re-rates the long-run discount inward. The Kazarian nomination is procedurally clean (2026 annual meeting), so the contest is on the calendar and ISS will weigh in. With the stock at $6.11 and downside protected by tax-exempt yield (~5%+ tax-equivalent), a 6–8 month holding period to either a settlement or vote outcome offers an attractive risk/reward.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The PREC14A and 13D/A pair is the entire alpha. A standard yield/discount screen flags DSM only by its current 6.3% discount — it misses that Saba's filing day cost basis (~$28.9M at 10.31%) means Boaz needs only ~3% NAV-gap closure to break even on principal alone, and the entire campaign is funded by Saba's normal management fees. The contested-proxy filing also forces BNY to disclose 12-month average discount data and to offer concessions early — virtually every prior Saba/BNY contest settled before the actual vote with a tender or DRIP modification.
