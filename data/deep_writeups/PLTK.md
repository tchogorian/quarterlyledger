# {NUMBER}. PLTK — Playtika Holding Corp.
## TAKE-PRIVATE | M&A SPECULATION | Score: 38/100

### THE SETUP

Playtika is an Israeli mobile gaming company (Slotomania, Bingo Blitz, June's Journey, Solitaire Grand Harvest) that has been in slow decline as legacy social-casino bookings shrink and user acquisition economics deteriorate. The stock trades around $3.62 against a speculative take-private narrative: per InsideArbitrage, a "special committee" was formed on April 7, 2026 to enhance shareholder value at an indicative $5.00/share all-cash valuation (~$1.7B equity check). That implies a 38% gross spread and ~57% annualized return if a deal closes by Q4 2026. There is no definitive agreement, no named acquirer, and no shareholder vote scheduled. This is a hope-trade dressed up as merger arbitrage.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

The defining feature of Playtika's cap table is its controlling shareholder Alpha Frontier, a Chinese consortium that acquired its stake from Giant Network (Yuzhu Shi) in 2019. Alpha Frontier holds majority voting control, which means: (1) any take-private cannot happen without their consent, but also (2) any minority squeeze-out is functionally a negotiation between Alpha Frontier, the special committee, and a hypothetical PE buyer. Minority public holders are price-takers. CFIUS is unlikely to be triggered (Playtika's operations are Israel/EU/US-facing consumer, not defense), but the optics of a Chinese-controlled Israeli mobile-gaming asset make the buyer slate awkward — Saudi PIF (which has bid for similar assets), Apollo, or Embracer-style consolidators are conceivable but each carries structural friction.

Operationally, Playtika has retreated from growth M&A and shifted to capital return (dividend, buybacks) while DAU and bookings have decompressed. EBITDA is still material but the business is structurally challenged: Apple's IDFA deprecation crushed paid UA, Slotomania faces sweepstakes-casino cannibalization, and the core "casual social casino" cohort is aging. A $5 take-private would be a 5–6x EBITDA exit — cheap for a public market but consistent with where private gaming deals have printed in 2024–2025 (e.g., Jagex, Embracer divestitures).

The market's pricing of $3.62 reflects ~30–40% probability of a deal at $5, with a downside case of ~$2.50–$3.00 if the special committee concludes "no deal" and discloses status quo. That's a binary, news-driven bet — not a fundamental thesis.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

A screen sees an undervalued mid-cap with a "strategic review" headline. It does not see that the controlling shareholder owns the deal economics; that no acquirer has been named or even rumored; that mobile gaming PE multiples have compressed; or that "special committees" frequently dissolve with a press release saying "the Board has determined to remain independent." This is a 38% spread because the market is correctly pricing low deal probability — not because there is a market inefficiency.

- Termination of process (downside catalyst)
