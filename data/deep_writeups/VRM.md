# {NUMBER}. VRM — Vroom, Inc.
## POST-BANKRUPTCY | $15.43 / ~$80M mkt cap | Score: 68/100

### THE SETUP
Vroom filed a prepackaged Chapter 11 on November 13, 2024, and emerged on January 14, 2025, after wiping out legacy equity, discharging roughly $290M of unsecured convertible notes, and issuing approximately 5.16M new common shares plus 364K warrants to creditors. Stock relisted on Nasdaq Global Market on February 20, 2025 under the same VRM ticker; warrants trade OTCQX as VRMWW. Approximately $780M of debt remains, almost entirely securitization warehouse debt at the UACC subsidiary — non-recourse to the parent. Fresh-start accounting was adopted at emergence.

What's left of "Vroom" is no longer an online used-car dealer. The ecommerce business was wound down. What you actually own is:
1. **UACC** — United Auto Credit, a subprime auto-loan platform, in run-off mode generating positive cash flow as the existing portfolio amortizes.
2. **CarStory** — an AI/data analytics platform for automotive retailers; small but with potential strategic value to a larger industry buyer.
3. **NOLs** — approximately $1.5B in net operating loss carryforwards, preserved through the Section 382 carve-out for bankruptcy emergences (the L5(h) safe harbor / G(1)(b) treatment), meaning they should be largely usable rather than wiped out by the ownership change.

### ANALYST SUMMARY (3p)

**Paragraph 1 — Why the float is invisible.** Five-million-and-change shares is below the radar of nearly every institutional buyer; index funds can't own it; mutual funds with size mandates can't own it; ETFs ignore it. The shares are concentrated with creditors who took them in the plan — names like Mudrick, Soros Fund Management, and other distressed-debt funds — and who face restricted-stock distribution mechanics until S-1 registration clears. That creates a pure technical overhang: known supply, no natural buyers, no sell-side coverage. Classic post-BK micro-cap setup.

**Paragraph 2 — What you're paying for at $15.43.** At ~$80M market cap, you're paying for: (a) the run-off cash flow of UACC — a subprime portfolio amortizing predictably, throwing off cash that returns to the parent; (b) CarStory — small-but-real AI/data asset that could be sold to AutoNation, Cars.com, CarGurus, or a private buyer; (c) the NOL shield, which has theoretical value of hundreds of millions if the company ever generates taxable income or merges into a profitable acquirer; and (d) optionality on net cash returns to shareholders as UACC winds down. The thesis is not "Vroom revives ecommerce"; it's "creditor-shareholders eventually rotate, and what's left is a cash-generative wind-down with embedded NOL optionality".

**Paragraph 3 — The path to value realization.** Three things need to happen, in any order, to monetize: (1) S-1 registration of creditor shares clears, allowing legitimate distribution and price discovery; (2) UACC continues to throw cash to the parent and management decides to return it — either via tender, special dividend, or buyback at a premium to current price; (3) NOL valuation allowance gets revisited, either through actual taxable-income generation or a strategic transaction that uses the shield. None of these has a fixed date, which is exactly why the stock is cheap. The risk is that the wind-down is messier than expected — UACC subprime credit losses spike, or capital is consumed by securitization make-whole mechanics. Non-recourse debt limits the downside to the parent.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A screen sees: micro-cap, post-BK, no revenue trajectory, "melting ice cube" optics. It misses: (1) the NOL value preserved through bankruptcy under Section 382's safe harbor, (2) the non-recourse nature of the $780M securitization debt — it does NOT threaten the parent equity, (3) the creditor-shareholder distribution mechanics that are creating temporary supply pressure, (4) the strategic optionality of CarStory as an asset sale, and (5) the run-off math of UACC, which is a known-shape cash flow rather than an operating uncertainty.

### CATALYSTS

- S-1 registration of creditor shares clearing technical overhang
- Potential reverse merger or use of NOL shell
