It is the smallest of the "Big Five" Medicaid MCOs (vs. CNC, ELV, UNH, CVS), which is exactly why an index drop hit it first. The S&P 500 removal effective March 23, 2026 forces hundreds of millions in mechanical passive selling regardless of fundamentals. The bear case is straightforward: Medicaid redetermination has stripped an estimated 25 million Americans from rolls since 2023, and the survivors skew sicker, raising the medical loss ratio (MLR). Marketplace risk pool is deteriorating as enhanced subsidies face 2025 cliff risk, and federal Medicaid policy under the new administration is unsettled. Until rate adequacy returns, every quarter is a guidance reset.

The bull case is mean reversion plus forced selling. Medicaid MLR cycles historically last 6–8 quarters; we are now past quarter five for the worst of the cohort. State rate cycles re-price annually, and the 2026 actuarial books are being negotiated assuming the new acuity. Index drops historically produce 5–10% short-term lows that reverse within 60–90 days as quants rebalance. Q1 2026 earnings April 23, 2026 is the next chance for management to mark trough MLR and signal contract-win momentum from new state RFPs.
