# {NUMBER}. DNLI — Denali Therapeutics
## FDA BINARY | Post-Approval Ramp | Score: 68/100

### THE SETUP
Denali's AVLAYAH (tividenofusp alfa-eknm) received FDA accelerated approval on March 25, 2026, for Hunter syndrome (MPS II) — the first BBB-crossing enzyme replacement therapy approved. Stock at $15.50, $2.3B mcap. The binary is past; thesis pivots to commercial launch ramp and platform validation for the Transport Vehicle (TV) technology.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Denali Therapeutics is a South San Francisco neuro-biotech focused on degeneration of the central nervous system. Its proprietary Transport Vehicle (TV) platform engineers protein therapeutics that cross the blood-brain barrier via the transferrin receptor. AVLAYAH is the first commercial validation: an enzyme replacement therapy for Hunter syndrome (MPS II) that, unlike Takeda's Elaprase, penetrates the CNS to address the cognitive decline component of the disease. FDA accelerated approval came March 25, 2026 — 11 days ahead of the April 5 PDUFA. Commercial launch is in process; ultra-orphan economics imply pricing in the $400-700K/patient/year range against a US patient population of ~500-1,000.

The bear case: accelerated approval requires confirmation via the COMPASS Phase 2/3 readout, which is still pending. If COMPASS misses the cognitive endpoint, the FDA can withdraw approval — a meaningful tail risk. Launch ramp is gated by patient identification (Hunter is rare and pediatric, requiring genetic confirmation), payer prior-authorization friction, and switching dynamics from Elaprase. Cash burn remains substantial as the broader pipeline (BIIB080 with Sanofi in Alzheimer's, LRRK2 program with Biogen in Parkinson's) consumes R&D. The $2.3B mcap already prices in significant launch success.

The bull case is platform validation: AVLAYAH's approval is the first regulatory endorsement of the TV platform, which has multiple shots on goal across Alzheimer's (Sanofi BIIB080), Parkinson's (Biogen LRRK2), and additional internal lysosomal storage disease programs. A successful Hunter launch — even capturing only 30-40% of the addressable market — supports $200-400M peak sales. More importantly, every successful TV-platform readout re-rates the rest of the pipeline. Catalysts include 2026 launch metrics quarterly disclosure, COMPASS confirmatory readout, and Sanofi BIIB080 Alzheimer's data.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The collaboration agreements with Sanofi (BIIB080 Alzheimer's) and Biogen (LRRK2 Parkinson's) include milestone schedules and royalty rates disclosed in 10-K Note on Collaboration Arrangements but not in any consensus model. Specifically, the Sanofi agreement carries up to $1B+ in development and commercial milestones plus tiered royalties on net sales. The Biogen LRRK2 deal includes participation rights. A screener sees "biotech, $2.3B mcap, no profits" and assigns a clinical-stage discount; the hidden value is the milestone optionality across three large-pharma partnerships.

### CATALYSTS
- Q2-Q3 2026 AVLAYAH launch metrics on next earnings calls
- COMPASS Phase 2/3 confirmatory readout (timing 2026-2027)
- Sanofi BIIB080 Alzheimer's interim/topline readouts
