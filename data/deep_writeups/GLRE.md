### THE SETUP
Greenlight Re trades at ~$14.95 against fully-diluted year-end 2025 BVPS of $20.43 — a 27% discount to book. Book value per share grew 13.8% in 2025 (8.1% in Q4 alone), well above cost of equity, while the company keeps repurchasing shares meaningfully below book. David Einhorn's investment vehicle plus a turning underwriting cycle is being priced as if neither will compound.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Greenlight Capital Re is a Cayman-domiciled property/casualty reinsurer focused on event-driven and specialty lines. Its differentiated feature is that the investment portfolio is managed by Einhorn's Greenlight Capital — a long/short equity strategy that has delivered strong post-2022 performance after a difficult 2018-2021 stretch. Underwriting has been repositioned away from legacy 2018-2020 capacity into more disciplined specialty programs, and the Innovations Unit holds minority equity stakes in insurance fintechs (Hippo, Cytora, Galileo) that are periodically marked up. Q4 2025 BVPS growth of 8.1% in a single quarter shows both legs working.

The bear case is the persistent "Einhorn discount." Long/short equity volatility is hard to underwrite, and Cayman domicile narrows institutional ownership. Float is small (~$480M market cap), making the stock illiquid for funds. Combined ratio improvement is real but reinsurance pricing — after several years of hardening — is starting to soften; a single catastrophe season could wipe out a year of underwriting gains. Innovations Unit private marks are model-driven and could reverse in a venture downturn. The discount to book has held for years; there is no obvious mechanism to close it absent a take-private.

The bull case is mathematical. BVPS compounding at 14% with shares purchased below book means intrinsic value per share grows faster than reported book. Continued buybacks at $14 against $20.43 book are immediately accretive. A take-private by Einhorn — long-rumored, never executed — at any price between current book and 1.0x book would deliver a 35%+ uplift overnight. Even absent that, Innovations Unit step-ups, a 2026 reinsurance pricing rebound after the recent Atlantic season, or simply a P/B re-rating to 0.85x represents 15%+ upside on top of underlying compounding.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Q4 2025 release confirms BVPS of $20.43 and notes continued buyback execution — but screens use stale annual book and miss the 8.1% single-quarter step-up. The Innovations Unit carries equity stakes that are NOT marked-to-market each quarter; realized gains on a Hippo/Cytora monetization could surprise to the upside. Einhorn's own Greenlight Capital LP holds a meaningful GLRE position, creating a structural floor — any take-private would require him to negotiate against himself, which historically has produced fair-to-favorable terms for minorities (see Loral, Compaq precedents). The Cayman domicile means dividends to U.S. holders avoid foreign withholding.

- Potential take-private announcement by Einhorn — speculative, no timeline
