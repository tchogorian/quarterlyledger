# {NUMBER}. VLTO — Veralto Corporation
## SPINOFF — QUALITY-AT-DISCOUNT | $105.00 / ~$25.8B mkt cap | Score: 84/100

### THE SETUP
On September 30, 2023, Danaher Corporation completed the spin-off of its Environmental & Applied Solutions segment as Veralto Corporation. Two-and-a-half years post-spin, this is no longer a recency-of-spin trade; it's a "quality compounder still trading at a discount to where its DNA argues it should be" trade. Veralto inherited:

1. **Water Quality** — Hach, Trojan Technologies, ChemTreat, and related franchises in water analytics, treatment, and chemistry. Recurring consumables and service revenue. High-margin, sticky, regulatory-driven.
2. **Product Quality & Innovation (PQI)** — Videojet (industrial coding/marking), X-Rite/Pantone (color management), Esko (packaging software), Linx. Boring industrial-tech with monopoly-ish positions in narrow niches.
3. **Danaher Business System** — the operating cadence and M&A playbook that made DHR one of the great compounders of the past 30 years.

### ANALYST SUMMARY (3p)

**Paragraph 1 — Why it spun.** Danaher had completed its evolution from industrial conglomerate to life-sciences/diagnostics pure-play (Cytiva, Beckman, Leica, Pall, Cepheid, Aldevron). E&AS was the last "old industrial" segment that didn't fit the bioprocess/diagnostics narrative. Spinning it freed DHR to trade at a life-science multiple while letting VLTO trade on its own merits. DHR shareholders, weighted toward growth/quality mandates, were indifferent or actively wanted to dump VLTO; many did. That dynamic created the initial gap to peer multiples.

**Paragraph 2 — What you actually own.** Roughly 60% of revenue is Water Quality, which has near-pharmaceutical recurring economics: Hach test kits and reagents are consumed every day at municipal water plants and industrial customers; switching costs are high because plants are validated to specific instruments. Roughly 40% is PQI, where Videojet alone is a near-duopoly in industrial inkjet coding (with Domino) and X-Rite/Pantone owns the color-management value chain. Margins are mid-20s EBIT, FCF conversion is typically >100% of net income, and capital intensity is low. The DBS operating system is real — kaizen events, daily management, value-stream mapping — and it's the same playbook that compounded DHR for decades.

**Paragraph 3 — Why the discount persists.** VLTO trades at a meaningful discount to peers like Roper (ROP), IEX, and Bio-Techne despite a similar margin profile and identical DBS-derived operating model. Part of this is the "show me" demand for a few more years of standalone capital allocation; part is sector mix (PQI is exposed to global industrial/packaging demand, which has been mixed); part is simply that quality-at-a-discount setups take time to close. Even modest multiple convergence to peer levels — without heroic earnings growth — produces meaningful upside from $105. With management starting to flex bolt-on M&A using DBS's diligence-and-integration playbook, the EPS compounding story has years to run.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A screen sees: industrial-classified, mid-20s EBIT margins, mid-single-digit organic growth — perfectly fine but not exciting. It misses: (1) the DBS operating system and the cultural moat it represents, (2) the recurring consumables share of water-quality revenue (much higher than peers), (3) the M&A optionality with a clean balance sheet and a proven playbook, (4) the structural reshoring tailwind for U.S. water infrastructure investment, and (5) the compounder math — small multiple expansion + mid-single-digit organic + bolt-ons + buybacks compounds to a very different IRR than a static screen suggests.

### CATALYSTS
- Quarterly margin and DBS-driven productivity updates
- Bolt-on M&A in water analytics or PQI software
- Buyback authorization expansions as deleveraging completes
- Multi-year reshoring tailwind for U.S. water-treatment capex
- Eventual analyst re-rating to peer multiples (ROP, IEX, BIO-Techne band)

### MUNGER CHECKLIST
- **Circle of competence:** Industrial tech with recurring consumables — knowable. PASS.
- **Moat:** Switching costs in regulated water testing; near-duopoly in industrial coding; brand/standard in color management. STRONG.
- **Management:** Jennifer Honeycutt (CEO), Danaher veteran, deeply steeped in DBS. PROVEN OPERATOR.
- **Margin of safety:** Modest balance-sheet leverage post-spin, FCF conversion strong, recurring revenue base. ADEQUATE.
- **Mr. Market:** Discount to peers persists 2.5 years after spin — patience is the price of admission. FAVORABLE-TO-PATIENT.

**Bottom line:** This is the rare spin where the parent's quality DNA travels with the child. Not a deep-value entry, but compounder-at-discount setups are how you get to 10x without ever needing a "moment". Score reflects high quality with moderate margin of safety.
