### THE SETUP

TG Therapeutics is a multiple sclerosis-focused biotech with a single commercial asset, Briumvi (ublituximab) — a CD20-directed monoclonal antibody approved in late 2022 for relapsing forms of multiple sclerosis. Market cap is ~$5.5B at ~$35/share. After a long history of clinical disappointments and dilutive financings, TGTX has finally delivered the thing equity holders spent a decade waiting for: a profitable, commercially scaling drug. Briumvi quarterly script trends have shown consistent sequential growth as the sales force expands reach into community neurology, and the company has guided to sustained profitability in 2026.

The near-term FDA binary is the **subcutaneous Briumvi sBLA** expected to be filed during 2026 based on completed bioequivalence studies. Subcutaneous administration is a meaningful competitive differentiator: the current IV infusion requires hospital/infusion-center visits, while a subcutaneous formulation can be administered in a neurologist's office or potentially self-administered at home. This directly addresses Briumvi's main competitive disadvantage versus Roche's Ocrevus and (more recently) Ocrevus Zunovo — the subcutaneous Ocrevus formulation launched in late 2024.

### ANALYST SUMMARY (3p)

The investment thesis rests on three legs: (1) profitable commercial MS franchise with script trends continuing to beat Street, (2) subcutaneous Briumvi approval expanding addressable share, and (3) operating leverage on the P&L as a fixed sales force sells more volume.

Briumvi's commercial trajectory has outpaced initial Wall Street expectations. The drug benefits from a shorter infusion time (1 hour vs Ocrevus's 2–3.5 hours) and a different dosing paradigm (every 6 months maintenance, similar to Ocrevus). Community neurologists have adopted it more rapidly than academic centers. Management has executed on commercial milestones — this is not the same TG Therapeutics that disappointed on ublituximab's CLL development a decade ago.

The subcutaneous filing is the key 2026 catalyst. If bioequivalence data reads cleanly (subcutaneous PK matches IV exposure), the sBLA should proceed to PDUFA without drama. A subcutaneous approval would let TGTX capture a materially larger share of CD20 MS scripts, particularly as Ocrevus Zunovo still requires facility administration. A clean sub-Q approval could re-rate the stock toward the higher end of analyst PTs (~$45–55).

Risks are threefold: (a) script growth deceleration as MS market saturates across CD20s, (b) competitive pressure from Ocrevus Zunovo and emerging oral BTK inhibitors in MS (Tolebrutinib, evobrutinib-class molecules), (c) sBLA approval delay or unexpected regulatory questions on sub-Q comparability data.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

A standard quant screen sees a recently-profitable single-product biotech with mediocre growth forecasts and a history of dilution. It misses (a) the script tracker acceleration that's been running ahead of consensus, (b) the subcutaneous formulation's commercial importance as a share-capture mechanism, (c) the operating leverage inflection as fixed commercial costs are already paid, and (d) the management credibility transition from a value-destroying dev-stage company into a functioning commercial organization. Screens also don't distinguish between catalyst timing — 2026 has multiple event-driven data points clustered within a 12-month window.

A clean sub-Q approval with continued script growth supports $45–55; a stumble (script deceleration + sub-Q delay) likely takes the stock back to $25–28.

The setup is a genuine FDA/commercial binary with meaningful asymmetric upside on a clean sub-Q filing and continued script acceleration, balanced against real single-product concentration risk. Score reflects solid fundamentals plus catalyst density, tempered by the inherent risks of single-asset biotechs and the maturing competitive backdrop in MS.
