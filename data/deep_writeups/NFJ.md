### ANALYST SUMMARY
NFJ is the Virtus-managed, NFJ-subadvised closed-end fund formerly branded "AllianzGI Convertible & Income" — historically a covered-call equity strategy with a sleeve of dividend-paying stocks and option-overwrite income to fund a high distribution rate. Like most equity CEFs of its vintage, NFJ has traded at a persistent discount to NAV that widened to double digits during periods of redemption-fund outflows. Saba Capital, the most prolific CEF activist of the past decade, accumulated 19.6% of the fund and pressed for a discount-narrowing action. The April 2026 standstill is the resolution: a 25% self-tender at 99% NAV, settled by October 14, 2026, in exchange for Saba's voting cooperation through the 2028 proxy season.

The bear case for owning NFJ today is mostly mechanical: not all shares tendered are accepted (proration is likely if Saba and other arbitrageurs all submit), so the realized return on the tender component is diluted. The post-tender fund is smaller, which raises the expense ratio on the survivor — a permanent drag. The covered-call strategy structurally caps upside in strong equity rallies, and if the manager underperforms, the discount can re-widen even after the tender. Standstill expiration after the 2028 proxy season also means another two years of "managed" governance during which the manager can reset terms. Finally, total return depends on the underlying NAV — a sharp equity drawdown into the September window would compress the absolute exit price even if the discount narrows.

The bull case is the math. Buying NFJ today at a ~10.5% discount with a contracted 25% tender at 99% of NAV is a gross arbitrage of roughly 9–10% on the tendered portion (timing-adjusted), with the stub continuing to trade at what should be a tighter post-event discount because supply has been retired and Saba's ongoing presence anchors governance. Add the covered-call distribution yield (running roughly 9–10%) collected over the 4–6 month hold, and the all-in return profile is attractive on a risk-adjusted basis. Saba's track record on these standstills is excellent — once the tender language is in writing, execution risk is low.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The April 15, 2026 standstill filing and the subsequent fund 8-K spell out the precise mechanics: 25% of shares outstanding, 99% of NAV pricing, settlement by October 14, 2026, and the standstill expiration date through the 2028 proxy season. The realized return depends on (1) the proration ratio if oversubscribed — which is very likely given Saba alone holds 19.6% and other arb funds will pile in, so model 30–40% acceptance; (2) the NAV path between purchase and tender — a delta-hedge of the underlying equity exposure removes this risk for institutions but is impractical for most retail; and (3) the post-tender discount — Saba-touched CEFs historically trade at 6–8% discounts versus the 10–12% pre-action level. Distributions during the holding period are not adjusted in the tender price and represent additional return.
