# {NUMBER}. ALGN — Align Technology, Inc.
## ACTIVIST — CAPITAL RETURN | $13.6B mkt cap @ $190 | Score: 68/100

### THE SETUP
Invisalign is a category-defining medical device with 22M+ patients treated, but the stock is down ~75% from its 2021 ~$750 peak after multiples compressed to ~12x earnings. Elliott Investment Management disclosed an activist position in September 2025 — pushing for cost discipline, capital return, and possibly a strategic review.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Align designs and manufactures Invisalign clear aligners and the iTero intraoral scanner / exocad CAD/CAM system that anchors the dentist workflow. FY2025 revenue was ~$4.0B (+0.9% YoY), with Q4'25 at $1.05B (+5.3% YoY). Reported FY2025 GAAP operating margin was ~13.6–13.8% (depressed by restructuring); 2026 guidance is ~18% (a ~400 bps recovery). The balance sheet is fortress: ~$1.0B cash, no debt as of Q3'25, with $831M of buyback capacity authorized into 2026 ($225M remaining under prior 2023 authorization). Elliott took a stake disclosed in September 2025; though demands have not been fully publicized, Elliott's playbook is well-established — operational efficiency, board refresh, accelerated buybacks, and option for strategic review. Q4 2025 growth was driven by EMEA, LATAM, and APAC, with North America stable, suggesting the international ramp Elliott would want to accelerate.

Bear case: clear-aligner growth has structurally decelerated as the post-COVID pull-forward exhausted and DSOs resist average selling price increases; competitors (SmileDirectClub bankrupt but Spark/Henry Schein, Dentsply) hold material share at lower price points; teen segment penetration is plateauing in mature markets. Multiple compression from 30x+ earnings to ~12x reflects the market's view that ALGN is now a ~mid-single-digit-grower medical device and should trade closer to a low-teens multiple. Elliott activism is not a guaranteed catalyst — Align's founder-led culture and a strong board could resist aggressive measures, and a strategic review of a ~$14B medical device with no obvious strategic acquirer is far from automatic.

Bull case: the asymmetry sits in the gap between current ~12x earnings and medical-device peer ~25x. Even partial multiple recovery to 18x with 2026 EBIT margin recovery to 18% and modest 4% revenue growth implies a $260–290 fair value (vs $190). Elliott pressure is the unlock — they have a track record of dragging boards into buyback programs, cost cuts, and strategic alternatives. Net cash balance sheet allows for accelerated repurchase or even leveraged recap. Insider buying signals confidence.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The proxy filing (DEF14A) referenced in early-2026 includes board refreshment language consistent with Elliott settlement template; that is Elliott's typical first win. The 22M cumulative-patient data point is undermarketed — it represents an enormous referral and case-history moat that DSO competitors cannot match in 2-3 years. Systems and Services (iTero, exocad) revenue grew faster than Clear Aligner in 2025 — a signal that the chairside workflow is becoming the primary lock-in (rather than aligner pricing). And operating-margin guidance of ~18% in 2026 implies ~$720M EBIT against an EV of ~$12.6B — meaningfully cheaper on a normalized basis than headlines suggest.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- 2026 annual meeting and any Elliott settlement / board refresh
- Q1/Q2 2026 earnings showing margin path to ~18% guidance
- Potential strategic review or large incremental buyback announcement
