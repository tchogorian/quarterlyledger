# {NUMBER}. MATW — Matthews International Corporation
## ACTIVIST | Barington Capital — Sum-of-Parts | Score: 70/100

### THE SETUP
Barington's second proxy fight at Matthews in two years culminated in a January 15, 2026 cooperation agreement — but only after Matthews had already sold SGK Brand Solutions for $350M and Warehouse Automation for $230M. With market cap ~$857M at $27.54, the question is what management does with the proceeds versus the implied value of the remaining memorialization and industrial pieces.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Matthews International is a microcap industrial conglomerate whose crown jewel is Memorialization (caskets, bronze plaques, cremation equipment) — a slow-growing but durable cash generator with high market share and pricing power. Around it sit Industrial Technologies (warehouse automation, energy storage product lines) and the now-divested SGK Brand Solutions packaging business. The $580M in announced divestiture proceeds — large relative to the equity cap — is the entire investment case lever.

The bear case starts with two consecutive proxy fights in two years and a board that defended itself the first time: governance is contested rather than aligned, and the Q4 fiscal results and forward guidance through 2025 included multiple "lumpy" project deferrals in industrial that suggest the standalone Industrial segment is harder to value and harder to sell than bulls assume. Memorialization is also slowly secularly pressured by rising cremation rates, even as Matthews has built the largest cremation equipment business in the country to capture the offset. The cooperation agreement may end the proxy heat without ending the value gap.

The bull case is straightforward arithmetic: pure-play memorialization peers (Service Corp, Carriage) trade at 9–11x EBITDA versus Matthews' implied conglomerate multiple in the mid-single digits. If the company applies the $580M to debt paydown and a sizable buyback at this share price, EPS accretion is meaningful and the remaining business comps to a higher multiple. Sum-of-parts work pencils to north of $40/share before any further divestiture of industrial pieces, which Barington is pushing for and which the cooperation agreement explicitly preserves.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Read the cooperation agreement 8-K closely — the standstill conditions and the strategic-review language define how much further pressure Barington can apply between now and the 2026 annual meeting. Also check the SGK and Warehouse divestiture closing 8-Ks for net cash proceeds after taxes and transaction expenses; gross announced figures overstate deployable capital. Pension obligations within Memorialization are also under-discussed — they materially affect any take-private or sponsor bid economics.
