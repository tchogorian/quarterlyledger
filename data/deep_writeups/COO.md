# {NUMBER}. COO — The Cooper Companies, Inc.
## ACTIVIST — SALE OR BREAKUP | Browning West 4% stake / cooperation agreement | Score: 74/100

### THE SETUP
Browning West, a $500M+ activist holder, secured a board seat (Walter Rosebrough, ex-Steris CEO) on January 3, 2026 in exchange for standing down on a full proxy fight. The agreement explicitly contemplates a strategic review of CooperSurgical, the contact lens conglomerate's underperforming women's health arm.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Cooper Companies is a $13.7B medical device holding company with two distinct businesses: CooperVision (one of the global "big four" contact lens makers competing with J&J Acuvue, Alcon, and Bausch + Lomb) and CooperSurgical (fertility, IVF consumables, OB/GYN devices, paragard IUDs). The two divisions share virtually no commercial logic, customer base, or technology platform — they coexist purely because the same management team built the conglomerate via decades of bolt-on M&A. At $70/share, the stock has materially underperformed contact lens peers, which trade at premium high-teens EBITDA multiples reflecting the recurring-revenue, oligopoly economics of soft contact lenses.

The bear case starts with execution. CooperSurgical has missed numbers repeatedly, integrating Cook Medical's reproductive health assets and Generate Life Sciences proved harder than guided. Contact lens unit growth has decelerated as myopia management (MiSight) revenue normalizes after a torrid Covid-era ramp. A breakup is taxable, complicated by shared corporate overhead, and may not be embraced by long-tenured CEO Al White. If management drags its feet on the strategic review, the stock could languish for another twelve months. Margins remain pressured by FX and a costly Puerto Rico/UK manufacturing footprint. Browning West only secured one board seat — not control.

The bull case is straightforward sum-of-the-parts. CooperVision standalone, generating roughly two-thirds of EBITDA at high-20s margins, would plausibly trade at 18-20x EBITDA as a pure-play oligopoly franchise. CooperSurgical, even at a modest mid-teens multiple, fetches a meaningful price from a strategic (Hologic, Boston Scientific) or PE buyer flush with healthcare-services dry powder. Browning West's intrinsic value estimate of $100+ implies roughly 40% upside. The cooperation agreement keeps activist pressure on — and Walter Rosebrough has actual breakup credentials from his Steris tenure. A CooperSurgical sale could fund a transformative buyback at a depressed multiple.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K segment disclosure understates CooperVision's true unit economics because corporate overhead, IT, and ERP costs are allocated by revenue rather than by complexity — CooperSurgical, with its dozens of acquired ERP systems and post-merger integration spend, is the disproportionate consumer. Standalone, CooperVision's operating margin is likely 200-300bp higher than reported. Also overlooked: the silicone hydrogel patent estate around MyDay and Biofinity, several of which extend into the 2030s, and CooperVision's #1 share position in toric lenses (the highest-margin sub-segment). Cooper is also one of the few contact lens makers with full vertical integration in MiSight myopia management, a rapidly growing pediatric category.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)

- Q2/Q3 fiscal 2026 earnings (June and September 2026) — first full quarters with Rosebrough on board
- Potential CooperSurgical sale announcement or formal spin-off plan; additional board changes if review disappoints
