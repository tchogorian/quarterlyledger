# {NUMBER}. WW — WW International, Inc. (WeightWatchers)
## POST-BANKRUPTCY | $109.8M MARKET CAP, ~10M FLOAT | Score: 58/100

### THE SETUP
WW International filed prepackaged Chapter 11 on May 6, 2025 and emerged just 49 days later on June 24, 2025 — a record-fast restructuring that wiped out $1.15 billion of debt in exchange for converting noteholders into equity owners. Pre-petition common stock was cancelled (now trades as WGHTQ on OTC, holders received warrants only). Reorganized common continues on Nasdaq under the same WW ticker, with roughly 10 million shares outstanding and ~91% owned by former noteholders (Goldman Sachs, Permira, Atlas Merchant, and a syndicate of credit funds).

Post-emergence capital structure: ~$469M total debt and ~$160M cash. Market cap at $10.98 sits at roughly $110M, against a brand still generating $750M+ of revenue and a clinical/GLP-1 telehealth business (the Sequence acquisition) that finally gives the company a structurally relevant product in the Ozempic era.

### ANALYST SUMMARY (3p)
**(1) Fresh start accounting + tiny float = pricing dislocation.** With only 10M shares outstanding and 91% locked up with creditor-owners who want cash exits, true float is somewhere south of 1 million shares. Sell-side coverage is gone. Index funds don't own it. Most fundamental investors won't touch a name fresh out of its second bankruptcy in a decade. This is exactly the structural setup that has historically produced 3-5x returns in post-BK equity (HTZ, Avaya, GTT pattern) — though it has also produced zeros when the operational thesis fails.

**(2) The clinical pivot is the entire thesis.** Legacy WeightWatchers (in-person workshops, the app, the points system) is in secular decline — GLP-1 drugs are eating its lunch. The Sequence telehealth acquisition flipped the script: WW now writes GLP-1 prescriptions to its members. If clinical revenue is inflecting at 50%+ y/y and reaching, say, $200-300M by FY26, the multiple compresses fast. If clinical stalls at sub-$150M while legacy revenue collapses 15-20%/year, this is a melting ice cube and the equity prints zero.

**(3) Creditor overhang is the dominant near-term variable.** Goldman, Permira, Atlas don't run pizza joints — they're financial owners with an exit clock. Lockups expire in 2026; expect a registered secondary or strategic sale within 12-18 months. The path of least resistance: sell the clinical business to a pharma-aligned acquirer (Hims & Hers, Noom, even a GLP-1 manufacturer building a direct-to-consumer wedge) at a strategic multiple. Optimistic scenario: $300-500M clinical sale price = ~$30-50/share back to equity. Pessimistic: creditors dump into a thin tape and crush the stock.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Screens see "post-bankruptcy weight loss company" and recoil. What they miss:
- The **Sequence (now WeightWatchers Clinic) prescriber network** is one of the few moats in DTC obesity care — state-by-state telehealth licensure is non-trivial to replicate. A pharma player paying $400M for that infrastructure is not a stretch.
- The **brand still has 70%+ aided awareness** with overweight US adults — pharma marketers value that distribution.
- **Lockup expiry is a known catalyst date**, not a surprise. Smart money sometimes accumulates *into* lockup expiry to absorb the supply.

### CATALYSTS
- **Q1 2026 print (first full post-emergence quarter):** Clinical revenue trajectory, member-net-add inflection
- **Mid-2026:** Lockup expirations — overhang clearing event (positive or negative depending on absorption)
- **2026-2027:** Strategic sale of WeightWatchers Clinic to pharma/DTC acquirer
- **Sell-side coverage initiation:** likely 6-12 months post-emergence as creditors prepare exits

### MUNGER CHECKLIST
- **Circle of competence:** Consumer health + telehealth + post-BK creditor dynamics. Three layers, none of them simple. NEUTRAL.
- **Moat:** Brand awareness (yes) + telehealth prescriber network (modest) + recurring subscription base (eroding). Mixed.
- **Management:** Tara Comonte (CEO since 2024) is operationally credible; the noteholder board is finance-driven, not consumer-driven. The latter is actually a positive for an exit-focused thesis.
- **Margin of safety:** $110M market cap on $750M+ revenue = 0.15x sales. If clinical works, even a 1x sales multiple = 6-7x from here. If it doesn't, equity is a zero. Asymmetric, but truly binary. PARTIAL.
- **Price:** Cheap on absolute basis, fair-to-expensive if clinical disappoints. Munger would note that "cheap" in a structurally challenged business is often a trap.

**Bottom line:** Free option on a clinical pivot working, with creditor-driven catalysts on a 12-18 month clock. Sized as a 1-2% position, not a core long. Position sizing matters more than thesis precision here — this either works in a hurry or it doesn't.
