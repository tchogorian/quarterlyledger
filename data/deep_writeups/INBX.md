# {NUMBER}. INBX — Inhibrx Biosciences, Inc.
## SPINOFF | Sanofi-Inhibrx Split-Off Stub | Score: 58/100

### THE SETUP
INBX was spun out of Inhibrx in May 2024 as part of Sanofi's $1.7B acquisition of the parent's lead asset (INBRX-101). Public Inhibrx holders received Sanofi cash, a CVR, and shares in this new entity holding the residual pipeline (INBRX-109, INBRX-1061). Forced-seller dynamics from arbs and deal funds left an under-followed clinical-stage biotech with a real lead asset.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Inhibrx Biosciences is the residual pipeline company carved out of legacy Inhibrx, Inc. when Sanofi acquired the AAT-deficiency asset INBRX-101 in May 2024 for ~$1.7B in cash plus CVR. The split-off mechanic distributed shares of INBX to existing Inhibrx holders alongside the cash consideration. Lead asset is INBRX-109, a tetravalent DR5 agonist antibody being developed for conventional chondrosarcoma — a rare bone cancer with no approved systemic therapies. Secondary asset INBRX-1061 is in earlier development. Management is the same proven biotech operator team from the prior Inhibrx, which built INBRX-101 to a $1.7B exit. Market cap around $910M at $67.13 — already up roughly 5x in twelve months as the chondrosarcoma data narrative played out.

The bear case is biotech-binary. Single-arm Phase 2 data in a rare cancer can disappoint at the registrational endpoint; FDA's accelerated approval pathway for ultra-orphan indications is real but unpredictable. The current valuation already prices in optimistic Phase 2 readouts and a probable buyout, leaving compressed upside on success and meaningful downside on disappointment. Cash runway from the spin-off allocation is finite — depending on burn, INBX may need to raise capital before pivotal data, diluting holders at potentially unfavorable prices. No revenue, no commercial infrastructure, and limited sell-side coverage means any negative tape gets amplified.

The bull case rests on INBRX-109's mechanism and the orphan-disease economics. Conventional chondrosarcoma has no approved therapy; even modest activity at acceptable safety would clear FDA hurdles. The DR5 agonist class has a complicated history (multiple failures in solid tumors), but tetravalent format and target selection differentiate INBRX-109. A pivotal trial design announcement, accelerated approval pathway endorsement from FDA, or strategic interest from a large oncology buyer could each re-rate the equity. Rare-cancer assets with proof-of-concept data routinely command $1-3B acquisition values. Management's prior exit credibility is real currency in biotech M&A circles.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The split-off mechanics are the alpha here. INBX shares were distributed to a Inhibrx holder base that was overwhelmingly merger-arb funds focused on capturing the Sanofi cash and CVR — those holders systematically dump the spinoff stub regardless of fundamentals, creating the multi-month forced-selling window that drove the initial 5x rally as natural buyers stepped in. Look at the Form 10 (registration statement filed by INBX prior to spin) for the exact pipeline asset transfer terms and the cash allocation from parent. The CVR economics from the Sanofi deal are separate from INBX equity — don't double-count. Watch for Form 4 insider buying from the management team as a signal of confidence in upcoming readouts. Sell-side coverage remains thin: any new initiation could move the stock.
