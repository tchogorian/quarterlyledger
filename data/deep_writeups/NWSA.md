# {NUMBER}. NWSA — News Corporation (Class A)
## HOLDCO | ~34% NAV Discount, REA Stake Covers Mcap | Score: 71/100

### THE SETUP
News Corp's $14.4B market cap is essentially covered by its 61.4% stake in ASX-listed REA Group (worth ~$9.2B) plus ~$2B net cash. That implies the market values Dow Jones (WSJ, Barron's, MarketWatch, Factiva), HarperCollins, News UK (The Sun, Times of London), News Corp Australia, Foxtel, and Realtor.com at roughly $3B combined — a fraction of plausible standalone values. Persistent Murdoch dual-class control discount, but the recent succession resolution removes one overhang.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
News Corp is a multi-segment media holding company: Digital Real Estate Services (REA Group + Move/Realtor.com), Dow Jones (WSJ + financial info via Factiva), Book Publishing (HarperCollins), News Media (UK + Australia mastheads), and Subscription Video Services (Foxtel). Dow Jones in particular has transformed into a recurring high-margin digital subscription business with WSJ.com and the Factiva enterprise data feed; HarperCollins is one of the Big Five publishers; REA Group is the dominant Australian property portal with structural network effects. Realtor.com is the U.S. #2 to Zillow and runs separately under Move Inc.

The bear case: Murdoch family supervoting B-shares mean A-shareholders have no governance lever; complexity discount is structural and may not narrow without a corporate action; Foxtel is in long-term decline as Australian streaming intensifies; News UK and News Corp Australia print mastheads face the same secular headwinds as all newspapers; Realtor.com has been losing share to Zillow and is currency for any deal rather than a growth driver. AUD/USD translation introduces FX volatility into reported numbers. The post-Lachlan succession resolution removes one tail risk but doesn't itself catalyze a breakup — and the family has explicitly stated they value control over financial-engineering value.

The bull case is the look-through math. REA at 61.4% of ~$15B = ~$9.2B. Dow Jones, growing high-single-digits with ~30%+ adjusted EBITDA margins, comps to financial-info peers (Factset, Morningstar) at 15x+ EBITDA = $9-12B+. HarperCollins ~$2.5B. News UK + News Corp Australia ~$1.5-2B. Net cash ~$2B. Sum exceeds $24B vs $14.4B mcap. Active buyback program shrinks the gap mechanically. Catalysts include any spin (Dow Jones has been speculated repeatedly), a Foxtel sale (rumored multiple times), or a Realtor.com transaction. Average sell-side PT around $34 implies 35%+ upside.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K segment footnotes break out Dow Jones revenue between Professional Information Business (Factiva, Risk & Compliance — sticky enterprise contracts) and Consumer Subscriptions (WSJ, Barron's), with PIB carrying mid-teens recurring growth that screens lump into "media." REA Group is consolidated, so look-through to NWSA's economic interest requires backing out the minority interest on the balance sheet. The dual-class structure means buybacks are concentrated in NWSA (Class A) while NWS (Class B) trades at a persistent premium — pair trading possibilities. Foxtel's debt is non-recourse to News Corp parent — important for downside scenarios. Murdoch Family Trust documents and the recent court resolution (Reno, NV, 2024-2025) define the post-Rupert governance baseline.

### CATALYSTS (3 dated)
1. Foxtel sale or recap announcement (rumored, watch 1H 2026).
2. Dow Jones potential spin or minority IPO (no announced date; persistent street speculation).
3. FY2026 earnings (NWSA fiscal year ends June) — buyback pace and segment growth.
