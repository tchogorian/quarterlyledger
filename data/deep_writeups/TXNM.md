## TAKE-PRIVATE PENDING | $61.25 cash / 3.87% gross spread | Score: 78/100

### THE SETUP
TXNM Energy (formerly PNM Resources) is the parent of Public Service Company of New Mexico (PNM) and Texas-New Mexico Power (TNMP), a regulated electric utility serving roughly 800,000 customers across New Mexico and Texas. On May 19, 2025, Blackstone Infrastructure Partners agreed to acquire TXNM in an all-cash take-private at $61.25/share, valuing the company at approximately $11.5 billion including assumed debt. Shareholders approved with a reported 99.6% vote. With the stock at $58.97 against a $61.25 deal price, the gross spread is 3.87% and the implied annualized return for a Q3 2026 close is around 9%. This is the second take-private attempt for the company — Avangrid's bid was blocked by the New Mexico PRC in 2021 — which is the entire reason this spread exists.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The deal has cleared FERC, HSR, FCC, the NRC for the Palo Verde nuclear stake transfer is pending, and the Texas PUCT has signed off. The single binary risk is the New Mexico Public Regulation Commission (PRC). The PRC famously rejected the Iberdrola/Avangrid acquisition in 2021 on consumer-protection grounds despite a settlement, and the New Mexico Attorney General and Office of Consumer Advocate have already raised objections to the Blackstone transaction. Blackstone's commitments — rate freezes, capex commitments, jobs commitments — are designed to address the prior rejection, and the regulatory architecture (passive infrastructure capital, no operational control, ring-fenced subsidiaries) is specifically structured to be more palatable than a foreign strategic acquirer.

The risk is not that the deal closes at a lower price; merger agreements of this type rarely re-cut at the regulatory stage. The risk is that the PRC denies, Blackstone walks, and TXNM trades back to its standalone level (likely $40-45 based on pre-deal trading and peer multiples). That would represent a 25-30% drawdown against a 4-9% upside on close — a roughly 1:4 reward-to-risk ratio. Position sizing should account for this asymmetry.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Three things a quote screen misses. First, the New Mexico political backdrop: the state has a Democratic trifecta and an unusually activist consumer advocate office; private equity ownership of utilities is a national flashpoint after Hawaii Electric. Second, Blackstone's commitments package — they are reportedly offering a multi-year rate freeze, $2B+ in committed grid investment, and headquarters retention in Albuquerque — is materially richer than what Avangrid offered. Third, TXNM's regulated rate base growth (~9% annually driven by data center load in TX) gives Blackstone enough cushion to pay $61.25 even with concessions, which means an absolute walk is less likely than concession-driven re-trade attempts.

### CATALYSTS
- New Mexico PRC procedural schedule and hearing examiner recommendation (key data point in 2026)
- NRC approval of Palo Verde nuclear plant interest transfer
- Final PRC order — yes/no binary
- Blackstone concession package amendments in response to OCA testimony
