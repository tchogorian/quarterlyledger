# {NUMBER}. JNJ — Johnson & Johnson
## LITIGATION RESOLUTION | Talc MDL 2738 mediation begins April 13, 2026 | Score: 71/100

### THE SETUP
JNJ trades at ~$165 with a ~$397B market cap while ~66,000 active talc claimants press a single MDL forward — and on April 13, 2026 the court ordered the parties into formal mediation with veteran mediator Fouad Kurdi. After two failed Texas Two-Step bankruptcy attempts, this is the first time the company has been forced to negotiate at scale outside Chapter 11 protection. The asymmetry: a global resolution removes a decade-long overhang on a business that already throws off ~$25B+ of free cash flow.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Johnson & Johnson is now a focused Innovative Medicines + MedTech company following the 2023 Kenvue spin, with pharma franchises (Darzalex, Tremfya, Stelara biosimilar transition, Carvykti) plus surgical robotics (Ottava) and cardiovascular (Shockwave, Abiomed). The talc litigation — overwhelmingly ovarian-cancer claims tied to legacy Johnson's Baby Powder — has dominated the equity narrative since 2018. Two attempted bankruptcy ring-fencings via subsidiary LTL Management (the "Texas Two-Step") were rejected by the Third Circuit and a Texas bankruptcy court, leaving J&J without a forum to bind future claimants. April 2026's mediation order is the natural next step.

The bear case: even a "settlement" here is enormous. A $2.0B Missouri verdict in 2024 motivated talks, but plaintiff firms have been clear they expect $10B+, plausibly $15-25B, plus a future-claims trust funded over a decade. Pharma faces its own cliff — Stelara biosimilars in 2025-2026 cut $10B+ revenue, and Q1 2026 earnings on April 22 will be the first clean look at the post-Stelara run-rate. MedTech has had inconsistent organic growth versus Stryker and Boston Scientific. A bad mediation outcome combined with a Stelara air pocket could compress the multiple further before re-rating.

The bull case: the mediator was selected because both sides want a deal. Kurdi has handled mass-tort resolutions before and the court timeline pressures J&J to act before further large verdicts. A global settlement at even $20B is ~5% of market cap — well within manageable cash flow over five-to-seven years — and removes the principal reason JNJ trades at a discount to large-cap pharma peers. The pipeline (Tar-200 bladder, Rybrevant + Lazcluze in NSCLC, Carvykti expansion) and MedTech roll-up (Shockwave) underpin a credible mid-single-digit revenue path. Catalyst path: mediation status → Q1 earnings → deal headlines through Q3 2026.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 2024 10-K Note 21 (Litigation) discloses talc reserves materially below plaintiff demands and notes the company's position that ovarian cancer claims "lack scientific support." That gap is the negotiation. Also buried: J&J's Innovative Medicines segment carries Stelara biosimilar erosion explicitly forecast in MD&A, but management's "$57B by 2025" pharma target depends on Tremfya IBD label expansion already secured in 2024 plus Carvykti capacity ramp at the Raritan and Ghent facilities — physical-plant constraints, not demand. Capacity comes online through 2026, which is why pipeline revenue accelerates exactly when Stelara cliff is steepest. Most screens see the revenue dip, not the cost-side and capacity-side offsets disclosed in capex commentary.

### CATALYSTS

- Q2-Q3 2026 — Mediation outcome / settlement framework announcement
- 2H 2026 — Carvykti capacity expansion + Tar-200 FDA decision
