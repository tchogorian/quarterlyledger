# {NUMBER}. ALAB — Astera Labs, Inc.
## POST-LOCKUP | $18B mkt cap @ $106 | Score: 62/100

### THE SETUP
March-2024 IPO whose 180-day lockup terminated May 2024 — the post-IPO insider overhang has been worked through 10b5-1 trickle sales. What remains is a pure-play AI-server connectivity (PCIe / CXL / Ethernet retimers) franchise growing 115% in 2025 with 75%+ gross margins, now shorn of structural seller pressure.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Astera Labs designs purpose-built connectivity silicon — Aries (PCIe/CXL retimers), Taurus (Ethernet smart cable modules), Leo (CXL memory controllers), and Scorpio (PCIe Gen6 switches) — sold to hyperscaler and AI-server OEMs to scale GPU clusters. FY2025 (CY ended Dec 31, 2025) revenue was $852.5M, up 115% YoY; Q4 alone was $270.6M (+92% YoY) with non-GAAP gross margin 75.7% and non-GAAP operating margin 40.2%. Customers include Nvidia, AMD, Intel and Amazon (AWS, which received a warrant for ~$466M of ALAB stock — implying a long-term commercial commitment but a non-cash GM headwind of ~2 points starting Q2 2026). The IPO lockup ended May 9, 2024; subsequent insider sales have been measured 10b5-1 dispositions rather than block secondaries.

The bear case: extreme customer concentration on a small set of hyperscalers whose AI capex narrative drives the entire multiple — the company itself flags that concentrated customers cause "misaligned budgeting, cash flow management challenges, volatile operating results." A re-acceleration in Nvidia's NVLink moat or a hyperscaler insourcing cycle (Broadcom, Marvell ASICs) directly compresses Astera's TAM. The Amazon warrant is a structural reminder that hyperscalers extract economics from suppliers. Valuation is rich — at $106 the stock trades roughly 21x trailing sales and ~50–60x forward earnings; any hyperscaler capex pause re-rates the name 30–50%.

The bull case: Astera owns the connectivity-IP layer at exactly the moment GPU clusters must scale across racks/pods — PCIe Gen6 design wins ramp through 2026/2027 and Scorpio switches unlock a new revenue line. Stock fell from ~$165 peak to $106 despite revenue more than doubling — the multiple compression has done significant work. Net cash balance sheet (no debt; cash from IPO + ops). Catalyst path: Q1 2026 print May 2026, GTC follow-throughs, and any large Tier-1 hyperscaler design-win disclosure.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Amazon warrant is the most underappreciated disclosure: AWS earned the right to acquire ~$466M of ALAB equity tied to commercial milestones — a structural lock-in for the company's largest customer, but recognized as a GM contra-revenue (~2pts/qtr from Q2 2026). A screen reads this as margin deterioration; in fact it cements an ~8-figure-per-quarter run-rate customer through warrant vesting. Insider sales since the lockup expiry have been 10b5-1 plan-driven (CEO trust selling ~3K-share lots, not block dispositions) — clean float, no overhang. Also unappreciated: XScale acquisition spend showing up as opex pressure but expanding the addressable connectivity stack into Ethernet AI fabrics.
