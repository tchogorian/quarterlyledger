### ANALYST SUMMARY
Patrick Industries supplies decorative and functional components into the RV, marine, manufactured housing, and industrial end markets — laminates, cabinet doors, vinyl, fiberglass, plumbing, and aluminum extrusions. The company built a serial-acquisition flywheel under CEO Andy Nemeth and Todd Cleveland, executing 70+ deals over a decade to consolidate fragmented suppliers. RV wholesale shipments collapsed from a 600K+ peak in 2021 to roughly 300K in the 2024 trough, dragging Patrick's revenue and operating margins. The April 2026 InsideArbitrage report of merger-of-equals talks with LCII (the other consolidator in the space) is unconfirmed, but the strategic logic is real: combined the two would dominate RV component supply at ~$8B revenue.

The bear case is double-cyclical. RV demand is tied to consumer credit, gas prices, and discretionary income — all three look fragile in 2026. Marine is similarly exposed. Patrick's debt load from the acquisition spree is meaningful, and refinancing risk exists if rates stay higher for longer. The MoE rumor could be a head-fake that fails on antitrust grounds — RV component supply is concentrated enough that FTC review would be substantive, and either side could walk before signing definitives. If the deal evaporates, the stock likely retraces to mid-$90s.

The bull case is the cyclical recovery layered onto the MoE optionality. RV wholesale shipments are showing tentative recovery signs, dealer inventories have been worked down, and any normalization to even mid-cycle 400K shipments produces meaningful operating leverage given Patrick's fixed cost base. The MoE, if it happens, would unlock $200M+ in synergies. Even without the deal, Patrick at trough margins on trough volumes is a credible cyclical setup. Founder-operator alignment remains strong and capital allocation has been disciplined through prior cycles.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

Patrick's contribution margin on incremental RV shipment is roughly 20%+, meaning a return to mid-cycle volumes drops several dollars to EPS. The MoE rumor is not yet in any consensus estimate. Also invisible: Patrick's increasing mix toward higher-margin marine, powersports, and housing exposure (now ~40% of revenue) which structurally reduces RV cyclicality. The proxy will eventually disclose insider ownership and the management bench depth, both of which are stronger than the screen flags.
