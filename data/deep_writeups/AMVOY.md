# {NUMBER}. AMVOY — AUMOVIO SE (ADR)
## SPINOFF — FORCED SELLING | EUR ~3.7B / ~$4.3B mkt cap @ $21.50 | Score: 73/100

### THE SETUP
AUMOVIO is the automotive-electronics, ADAS, sensors, and software-defined-vehicle business of Continental AG, demerged Sep 18, 2025 and listed in Frankfurt (AUM.DE) with a US ADR (AMVOY). Continental shareholders received the spin and many — owning Continental for tires/rubber — are dumping the orphan auto-tech ADR; ~0.2x sales versus Aptiv at >1x.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
AUMOVIO comprises Continental's former Automotive group: User Experience (cockpits/displays), Autonomous Mobility (radar, cameras, ADAS domain controllers), Safety & Motion (brakes, body), and Architecture & Networking (high-performance compute, software-defined vehicle). FY2024 baseline revenue was ~EUR 19.6B with ~93,000 employees; LTM revenue through Q3'25 is EUR 18.96B with Q3'25 down ~9% YoY reflecting the global OEM destocking cycle. Spinoff balance sheet: ~EUR 1.5B cash plus a EUR 2.5B revolving credit facility. June 2025 Capital Market Day medium-term targets: revenue >EUR 24B, adjusted EBIT margin 6–8%, and ADAS market share 10–15% by 2027 (already at 10–15% order share). Order backlog ~EUR 19.3B.

Bear case: European auto suppliers face a triple compression — Chinese OEM share gain, EV demand softening, and a structural margin squeeze as Tier-1 software stacks get commodified. Q3'25 revenue down 9% reinforces the cyclical headwind. Software-defined-vehicle is a long-promised inflection that has consistently disappointed as OEMs in-house critical pieces (Tesla, BYD model). The ADR (AMVOY) is an unsponsored / lightly-sponsored vehicle with thin US liquidity and dual-orphan dynamics — Continental tire-focused holders sell, US investors lack a clear thesis, and the FRA listing carries the real volume. Index-rule forced selling continues if AUMOVIO is too small for DAX inclusion.

Bull case: at ~0.2x sales the market prices an outright distressed scenario; even a modest path to 6% EBIT margin on EUR 22B revenue implies ~EUR 1.3B of EBIT against a ~EUR 3.7B equity value — i.e. <3x normalized EBIT before any net-cash credit. Critical-content ADAS / sensor / SDV IP into every global OEM is precisely what those OEMs cannot replicate organically. Net-cash balance sheet enables M&A or buybacks. The classic spinoff playbook is in play: forced selling, ignored stub, eventual re-rating on first standalone investor day or DAX/SXXP index inclusion.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The ADR (AMVOY) trades thinly enough that a US-only screener never sees the much deeper Frankfurt liquidity (AUM.DE) — sophisticated investors arbitrage between the two. The EUR 19.3B order backlog provides ~1x revenue visibility — a metric absent from an income-statement-only screen. Capital Market Day ADAS share targets of 10–15% would make AUMOVIO one of the top three global ADAS suppliers, on par with Bosch and Mobileye, yet the equity is valued like a commodity exhaust supplier. The credit-line + cash position gives optionality for an opportunistic acquisition (e.g., a distressed European Tier-2) at the bottom of the cycle.
