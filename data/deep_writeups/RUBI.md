# {NUMBER}. RUBI — Rubico Inc.
## SPINOFF | $2.83M mkt cap; 2 Suezmax tankers ~157k DWT each | Score: 38/100
### THE SETUP
Rubico Inc. was spun out of TOP Ships (TOPS) on August 4, 2025 as a Nasdaq Capital Market listing carrying two 2020-built Suezmax crude tankers — the M/T Eco Malibu and M/T Eco West Coast, each approximately 157,000 deadweight tons, scrubber-equipped and eco-design. TOPS distributed 100% of Rubico shares pro rata to TOPS holders of record as of June 16, 2025. The shares were essentially given away to a TOPS holder base that had no use for them — most distributed shares were dumped immediately. A subsequent 1-for-10 reverse split reset the price optics and finished off whatever residual retail interest had survived. The result is a sub-$3M market cap nano-cap holding two modern tankers whose collective vessel-level NAV is multiples of that figure (industry comp pricing for 2020-built scrubber Suezmaxes implies roughly $60-70M per vessel before debt). The setup is the canonical "shipping nano-cap NAV gap" story — and like every such setup, the question is not whether the gap exists but whether public-market holders ever capture it.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
On a vessel-NAV basis, the discount to fair value is extreme. Two modern scrubber-fitted Suezmaxes, in any constructive crude-tanker market, generate meaningful TCE rates and free cash; a clean entity holding them at a sub-$3M market cap is, on paper, indefensibly cheap. The 2026-2030 IMO carbon-intensity regulations continue to advantage modern, eco-design tonnage over older vessels, which supports a structural premium for ships of this vintage. If Rubico were to be merged into a peer, sold, or distribute its vessels via a special dividend or wind-up, the gap would close quickly.

The reason the market refuses to close it on its own is the part that screens cannot show. Greek-shipping nano-caps have a long, well-documented history of value extraction by controlling shareholders via related-party deals, dilutive ATM offerings, reverse splits, and complex capital structures that systematically transfer value from public minorities to insiders. TOPS itself is the textbook of this pattern; Rubico inherited both the asset base and the governance structure. Without an unambiguous alignment-of-interest fix — independent board action, a tender at NAV, or an outright sale — the discount is not a margin of safety; it is the market pricing the probability that the next corporate action benefits insiders rather than holders.

The honest framing is a lottery ticket with negative expected operational governance and positive expected asset value. Sized very small, it can sit in a portfolio waiting for a catalyst — vessel sale, merger combination, special dividend, or a tanker market re-rating that forces management's hand. Sized as a real position, it exposes the holder to the well-known failure modes of micro-cap shipping spin-offs. The vessel asset is real; the corporate-governance risk is also real; both should be priced.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A screen will show ~$2.83M market cap, no analyst coverage, illiquid trading, and a recent reverse split — every flag a quant screen filters out. What screens cannot price: (1) the actual mark-to-market value of two 2020-built scrubber Suezmaxes at current secondhand prices vs. the company's vessel-level debt; (2) the related-party history at the parent that informs probability-weighting of governance outcomes; (3) the capital structure mechanics that allow ATM offerings or preferred issuance to dilute common holders; (4) the potential for forced action — a vessel sale at NAV, a special dividend, or a strategic combination with a peer — each of which would force price discovery; (5) the structural illiquidity that means even good news may not be tradable on size.

### CATALYSTS
- **First standalone TCE rate prints (2026)** — first independently disclosed earnings power for the two-vessel fleet.
- **Vessel sale or M&A combination announcement** — single most likely path to NAV realization.
- **Tanker market re-rating** in 2026-2027 driven by IMO carbon rules and OPEC+ supply dynamics.
