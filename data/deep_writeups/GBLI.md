# {NUMBER}. GBLI — Global Indemnity Group, LLC
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | ~0.7x BOOK VALUE | Score: 68/100

### THE SETUP
Specialty E&S insurer trading at roughly 0.7x book value while completing "Project Manifest" — a structural reorganization that de-stacks the insurance subs into separate divisions (Penn-America, Vacant Express, Collectibles, Specialty Products) and carves out tech (Kaleidoscope) and claims (Liberty) as standalone businesses, with Fox Paine as financial advisor and effective controlling shareholder.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Global Indemnity Group (Nasdaq: GBLI, recently moved from NYSE) is a specialty P&C holding company best known for its Penn-America excess & surplus lines book, plus a portfolio of niche programs (vacant property, collectibles, specialty commercial). Book value per share was reported at $49.88 as of September 30, 2024 against a stock price meaningfully below $35, putting the equity at roughly 70% of book — a discount that has persisted for years and is now compressing as Project Manifest separates the operating units, makes the tech and claims subsidiaries marketable, and crystallizes hidden segment value. Fox Paine & Company controls the supervoting Class B shares and remains both anchor shareholder and architect of the strategic plan.

The bear case is the standard E&S micro-cap discount: low float (Fox Paine owns the supervoting block), no analyst coverage, lumpy underwriting results in cat-exposed lines, and historical loss reserve volatility that has burned holders before. Specialty insurers can look cheap on book for years if reserves under-develop or if the tech and claims subsidiaries are priced for a sale that never materializes. The buyback has slowed dramatically — zero shares bought back in Q4 2025 — which removes one of the more reliable per-share value drivers. And Fox Paine's controlling position means minorities are along for the ride; any take-private at less than full book would leave minority holders with a haircut versus intrinsic.

The bull case is structural and self-help-driven. Project Manifest unbundled the operating divisions, increased consolidated surplus through the de-stacking, and made the technology (Kaleidoscope Insurance Technologies) and claims (Liberty Insurance Adjustment Agency) subsidiaries separately monetizable. The Nasdaq listing transfer is a sign of intent to broaden the investor base. Penn-America is operating in a hard E&S market with combined ratios well below 100%. Across the buyback authorization announced October 2022, GBLI has repurchased 1,357,082 shares for $33.98M — proof of capital-return intent even if the cadence has slowed. At 0.7x book in a hardening specialty market with multiple monetization levers, the asymmetry is favorable.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K's segment footnote on Project Manifest is the alpha: each of the four Penn-America divisions now has its own GAAP segment reporting, which means combined-ratio outliers (Vacant Express historically the laggard, Collectibles historically the gem) become visible to outsiders for the first time. Also note the Class B supervoting structure controlled by Fox Paine — read the 10-K's related-party section carefully because Fox Paine collects management/advisory fees that depress per-share earnings versus a clean cap structure. Finally, the Bermuda LLC structure of GBLI provides tax efficiencies that conventional U.S. insurance comps lack — book value comparisons to peers under-credit this.

### CATALYSTS

- 2026 buyback resumption / new authorization announcement
- Potential Kaleidoscope (insurtech) or Liberty (claims TPA) carve-out sale
- Continued hard-market combined-ratio outperformance in Penn-America
