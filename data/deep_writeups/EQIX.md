# {NUMBER}. EQIX — Equinix, Inc.
## ACTIVIST | Elliott Engagement + AI Capacity Re-Rating | Score: 71/100

### THE SETUP
Elliott Investment Management disclosed an initial 0.15% Equinix position in Q1 2025 and materially increased the stake in Q2 2025 (per 13F). Public reporting (CNBC August 2025, Bloomberg July 2025) suggests collaborative engagement focused on amplifying EQIX's AI/HPC positioning given its industry-leading interconnection density. Board-seat invitation by the May 2026 AGM is the consensus expectation.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Equinix is the world's largest interconnection-focused data center REIT, operating 260+ International Business Exchange (IBX) facilities across 70+ metros. The franchise rests on the network effect of having more carriers, clouds, and enterprise tenants in close physical proximity than any competitor — measured by the "Equinix Fabric" software-defined interconnect platform and the recurring revenue from cross-connects (high-margin, near-monopoly). With AI workloads driving demand for low-latency colocation and hybrid-cloud stitching, EQIX should be a primary beneficiary, but the stock has lagged pure AI infrastructure plays.

The bear case is twofold. First, EQIX's growth model depends on continuous greenfield IBX construction, and capex intensity has stepped up materially as the company chases AI/HPC capacity. Funding via debt and equity has compressed FFO/share growth and weighed on the multiple. Second, hyperscaler self-build represents a structural threat — Microsoft, Google, AWS, and Meta increasingly build their own facilities and bypass colocation for primary compute. EQIX's interconnection franchise insulates it somewhat, but the narrative of "data center = AI" doesn't perfectly fit a company whose core asset is multi-tenant retail colocation. Short reports (Hindenburg, May 2024) raised questions about accounting and depreciation policies that, while contested by the company, lingered with some investors.

The bull case is the Elliott catalyst plus AI-driven demand acceleration. Elliott has data-center expertise from Cyxtera and CoreWeave engagements and is pushing for capital allocation discipline, faster monetization of AI-related power capacity, and improved per-share growth metrics. A board seat by May 2026 would catalyze announcements: AI-specific product offerings, accelerated capacity sales, possible JV structures with hyperscalers or AI labs, and potentially returning capital. Take-private rumors have circulated periodically (Brookfield, GIP, KKR all logical buyers), and Elliott's involvement increases the probability of a process.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The interconnection moat is the entire investment case and screens don't capture it. EQIX's 480,000+ cross-connects generate ~17-20% of revenue at near-100% incremental margin, and each new tenant ecosystem-effect-strengthens the existing one. Read the 10-K's discussion of "Magnetism" — the company's term for the network-effect dynamic where customers cluster with their counterparties. The AI thesis hinges on whether AI inference (latency-sensitive, distributed) requires the kind of metro-edge density EQIX provides, vs. AI training (large-scale, centralized) which favors purpose-built hyperscaler campuses. Elliott engagement is collaborative per CNBC reporting, suggesting management is open to incremental optimization rather than radical change. xScale joint venture (with GIC) is the off-balance-sheet vehicle for hyperscaler-scale builds — economics there matter materially.

- Q1 and Q2 2026 earnings — AI/HPC bookings and capacity commentary
