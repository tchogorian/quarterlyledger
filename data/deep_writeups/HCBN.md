### THE SETUP
HCB Financial Corp., a small Michigan community bank holding company, agreed on March 18, 2026 to be acquired by Independent Bank Corporation (IBCP) for ~$72.81 per share in mixed cash and stock (1.590 IBCP shares + $17.51 cash). HCBN trades at $71.00 — a ~2.55% gross spread to a Q3 2026 close, juicy for a vanilla bank tuck-in but constrained by OTC illiquidity and IBCP stock-price drift risk on the unhedged equity portion.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
HCB Financial is the holding company for Hillsdale County Bank, a small Michigan community bank with a few branches concentrated in south-central Michigan. The franchise is exactly the type of sub-$1B-asset community bank that has seen consolidation pressure intensify with rate-cycle stress and rising compliance costs. Independent Bank Corporation (IBCP, ~$5B assets) is a rational consolidator — heavy in Michigan, runs cleaner cost ratios, and can take out HCB's core deposits while wringing duplicative back-office and IT spend. The deal headline of $72.81 per HCB share, totaling $70.2M, prices the bank at a modest premium to tangible book.

The bear case starts with the structure: HCBN holders receive 1.590 IBCP shares per HCB share plus $17.51 cash. That means roughly 76% of the consideration is exposed to IBCP's stock. If IBCP drops 10%, your implied take-out value drops to roughly $67.50 — wiping out the spread. There is no collar disclosed in the public release, which is unusual for community-bank stock deals and concentrates spot-price risk on holders. Add OTC liquidity (HCBN is thinly traded), a small absolute float, and the practical inability to short IBCP at retail-friendly borrow costs, and this is structurally hard to professionalize. Regulatory risk is genuinely low — Fed/OCC/state community-bank approvals are nearly automatic for a deal this size — but execution timing can drift one quarter on examiner scheduling.

The bull case is for a small fund or family-office investor willing to ratio-hedge IBCP. At 1.590:1 short-IBCP / long-HCBN, you isolate the cash component and the spread to deal close, generating a low-teens annualized IRR if Q3 2026 close holds. IBCP itself trades at reasonable bank metrics (~10x earnings, 1.2x TBV); the post-merger combined entity should accrete EPS by mid-single digits as cost synergies (likely 30-40% of HCB's expense base) realize through 2027.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Community-bank merger arbs of this size almost never appear in screener-driven datasets because HCBN reports through OTC Markets rather than EDGAR's clean structured-data pipeline. The 8-K filings on the IBCP side disclose a customary $2.8M termination fee and a fiduciary out — both standard for $70M deals. What's not in the screen: the proxy will likely reveal director and 10% holders signed voting agreements covering 30%+ of shares, making the shareholder vote a formality. Hillsdale County Bank's loan book is concentrated in agricultural and small-business lending in Michigan — an area where IBCP already has scale and underwriting models, supporting fast integration.
