# {NUMBER}. NSC — Norfolk Southern Corporation
## ACTIVIST / M&A ARB | UNP Bid $320; STB Risk | Score: 60/100

### THE SETUP
Union Pacific announced a $320/share mixed cash-stock acquisition of Norfolk Southern in July 2025 — the first-ever transcontinental Class I rail merger. NSC trades at $302.38 (5.83% gross spread, ~4.66% annualized at expected 2027-Q2 close). Both shareholder votes passed November 2025, but the Surface Transportation Board rejected the initial application January 16, 2026, leaving regulatory approval as the binary risk. Prior Ancora activist campaign already pushed operating ratios; the M&A path is the new story.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Norfolk Southern is the dominant Class I rail in the U.S. East, with a network spanning from the Mid-Atlantic to Memphis/New Orleans and into the Midwest. The 2023 East Palestine derailment forced a leadership and operating-philosophy change, eventually bringing in Ancora Holdings as an activist that won board seats in 2024 and pushed a Precision Scheduled Railroading-lite operational reset. Operating ratio improvements were beginning to materialize when Union Pacific announced the $320/share bid in July 2025, framing the deal as creating the first end-to-end transcontinental U.S. rail to compete with truck and to integrate intermodal flow.

The bear case is regulatory. The Surface Transportation Board rejected the initial filing on January 16, 2026 — a meaningful signal that the agency views the merger as creating unprecedented competitive concentration. Other Class I rails (BNSF, CSX, CPKC) have signaled opposition or demanded gateway/trackage-rights remedies that could materially erode synergies. The political climate around a transcontinental "single-rail" entity is fraught — shippers (especially ag and chemicals) have organized against the deal. If the STB ultimately rejects or imposes onerous conditions, NSC could re-rate downward toward standalone fair value (which itself is debated post-East Palestine and post-Ancora improvements).

The bull case: shareholder votes passed, both managements are committed, the strategic logic (intermodal share-shift from truck) is real, and the STB rejection of the initial application appears procedural rather than substantive — a refile with stronger remedies is expected. The 4.66% annualized spread is rich for a deal with passed shareholder votes if the regulatory path holds. Standalone NSC, even without the deal, has post-Ancora operational tailwinds and remains a high-quality duopoly East-coast rail asset. Downside is buffered by standalone earnings power.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The merger proxy discloses the exact cash/stock mix mechanics and any collar protection — critical for hedging UNP exposure. STB filings (publicly available on STB.gov) detail the proposed competitive remedies: trackage rights for competing rails over key bottleneck corridors, divestiture options, and Section 11321 conditions. NSC's 10-K discloses East Palestine remediation reserves and the related insurance recovery — a wildcard that could move standalone value. Ancora's 13D and proxy materials reveal the operational targets the board committed to even if the deal fails. Pension funded status and labor-contract reset timing (TCU, BLET, BMWED) are material to standalone earnings.
