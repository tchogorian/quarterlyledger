# {NUMBER}. LYFT — Lyft, Inc.
## ACTIVIST | Engine Capital — Operational Turn | Score: 67/100

### THE SETUP
Engine Capital filed in March 2025, nominated two directors in April, and withdrew them only after Lyft committed to capital returns. Engine remains in the stock and the dual-class share collapse — the actual structural unlock — has not yet happened. Trading at $14.95 vs. Uber at a much richer multiple, the mispricing comes from being the #2 in a duopoly at #5 valuation.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Lyft generated its first full year of GAAP profitability in 2024 and turned free cash flow positive on the back of operational discipline (route optimization, Express Drive, ad load) and a more rational competitive environment with Uber. Market cap is ~$5.95B. Engine Capital's ~1% stake led to a public letter campaign in Q1 2025 demanding strategic review, dual-class collapse, and capital return — and Lyft responded with the largest buyback authorization in its history.

The bear case is the autonomous-vehicle overhang: Waymo is scaling commercial robotaxi service in multiple US cities, Tesla's robotaxi product is launching in pilot markets, and as a #2 platform with no proprietary AV stack Lyft is structurally disadvantaged in any post-AV scenario. Even with current operational momentum, sentiment is anchored to a "rideshare commoditization in three years" thesis. Network effects favor the larger player; Lyft's driver supply remains the swing variable each quarter; and any insurance reform reversal could pressure margins quickly.

The bull case is multi-leg: (1) buyback execution at sub-1x sales is the highest-IRR capital deployment management has ever had; (2) AV partnerships with Mobileye and May Mobility position Lyft as a non-Waymo distribution partner for AV fleet operators that don't want to build a consumer app; (3) dual-class collapse would make Lyft a clean strategic target for Amazon, DoorDash, or even an international consolidator; (4) Engine remaining engaged is a continued governance ratchet.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The dual-class structure (founders Logan Green and John Zimmer hold high-vote stock) is the actual binary — without collapse, no strategic deal is realistically possible, and Engine's campaign was as much about that lock-up as about buybacks. Read the 14A: founder voting power and the sunset provisions matter. Also read the AV partnership disclosures (Mobileye, May Mobility, Mobileye-led pilots) — the Lyft-as-distribution thesis is in those press releases, not in any screen.

2. **2026** — board decision on dual-class share structure collapse
3. **Q2 2026** — Q2 earnings, AV partnership monetization update
