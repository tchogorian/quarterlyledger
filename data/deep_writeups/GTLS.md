# {NUMBER}. GTLS — Chart Industries, Inc.
## M&A ARBITRAGE | 4.9% Annualized Spread | Score: 78/100

### THE SETUP
Baker Hughes is acquiring Chart Industries for $210 cash per share, a $13.6B deal that already cleared a 98.9% shareholder vote in October 2025 and most material regulatory hurdles. With GTLS at $208.30, you collect a tight ~0.8% gross spread for what looks like a near-certain Q2 2026 close — a rates-plus risk-free income substitute, not a fat-pitch arb.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Chart Industries is a global engineered cryogenic process technology provider — heat exchangers, storage tanks, regasification, hydrogen liquefaction, and after-market services across LNG, industrial gas, and clean-energy markets. The company is the dominant pure-play in cryogenic equipment after its 2023 Howden acquisition built it into a $4B+ revenue process-technology platform. On July 28, 2025 Baker Hughes announced an all-cash $210/share offer, totaling $13.6B including assumed debt. The strategic logic: BKR's Industrial & Energy Technology segment swallows a complementary, recurring-revenue cryogenic franchise to round out its non-oilfield re-rating story.

The bear case here is mathematical, not fundamental. At a 0.82% gross spread to a $210 deal price, you are underwriting maybe a 4-5% IRR if Q2 2026 close hits, and zero or negative if the antitrust dominoes (China SAMR most notably) require divestitures or stretch into Q3. China is the swing variable; Baker Hughes has substantial Chinese LNG exposure that regulators may use as leverage. Standalone GTLS without the deal would likely re-rate down to $160-170 on a busted-deal scenario as Howden integration angst returns. Asymmetry is not in your favor at this spread.

The bull case is duration-of-cash-equivalents, not equity upside. Shareholder approval is in the bag (98.9%); US, EU, UK, Brazil, and most major reviews are cleared per BKR disclosures. The remaining holdouts are administrative timelines rather than substantive challenges given limited horizontal overlap (oilfield services vs. cryogenic process equipment). For a fund running an event book, GTLS is a high-confidence "park money" sleeve into Q2 2026 close, generating ~5% annualized with low correlation to broad equities.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
GTLS's merger proxy reveals the deal blew through one prior unsolicited approach in early 2025 at lower implied price; the board ran a 22-day go-shop that produced no superior bid, hardening the floor. Termination fee is $475M payable to BKR if Chart breaks; reverse termination fee is materially larger if BKR walks for antitrust. The proxy also discloses Howden earn-out payments still flowing — those crystallize on close, slightly reducing net cash. Most overlooked: BKR's Q3 2025 10-Q disclosed it had pre-funded $7B of acquisition financing via senior notes, removing financing risk that arb desks were modeling at 50bps of spread.
