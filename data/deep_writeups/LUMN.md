# {NUMBER}. LUMN — Lumen Technologies, Inc.
## POST-BANKRUPTCY | March 2024 Out-of-Court Recap | Score: 60/100

### THE SETUP
Lumen restructured ~$12.5B of debt out of court in March 2024 and pivoted, almost immediately, into AI infrastructure connectivity — winning $8.5B+ in new contracts including Microsoft. The market still prices it as a melting wireline incumbent at $8.57 and ~$8.8B market cap. The dislocation is the gap between a "BK-adjacent" tape and an actual capacity-sold-out fiber backbone.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Lumen owns the second-largest US long-haul fiber network behind AT&T, plus a national metro footprint and substantial real estate and conduit adjacent to hyperscaler data center clusters. The 2024 out-of-court exchange pushed the bulk of secured maturities to 2027+, restructured ~70% of the capital stack, and cleared the runway for the operational pivot the prior CEO Jeff Storey had begun. With ~$1.8B cash, ~1.03B shares outstanding, and $18–20B total debt, the equity is leveraged but no longer in imminent default risk.

The bear case writes itself: legacy voice and copper revenue is in secular decline, Quantum Fiber consumer build-out is capex-heavy with uncertain returns versus cable and fixed wireless, and the AI revenue announcements — while large in headline — are multi-year IRU style contracts where revenue recognition stretches, cash conversion is back-end-loaded, and counterparty concentration is rising. Distressed funds that took paper in the 2024 exchange are natural sellers as the equity recovers, capping any near-term re-rating. The 2027 maturity wall is closer than it looks.

The bull case is that Lumen is the cleanest non-AT&T pure-play on hyperscaler interconnect, a market that materially did not exist in 2023 forecasts and is now growing at AI-capex pace. The Microsoft, Meta, Google, AWS, and Oracle wins disclosed across 2024–25 represent 10+ year contracted backlog that is not yet in the income statement. A potential sale of the consumer Quantum Fiber business could accelerate the deleveraging timeline materially, and the equity has option value to a refinancing in 2026.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Read the 10-K segment footnotes: Lumen books AI infrastructure deals as multi-year sales-type leases / IRUs with most revenue recognized over the contract life, so the $8.5B in announced wins flows into reported revenue gradually rather than in one print — a screen looking at trailing revenue trends will miss the inflection entirely. The cap structure also includes ringfenced subsidiaries (Level 3 vs. legacy Qwest/CenturyLink) with different collateral packages — material for any debt-equity capital-arb thesis but invisible in any aggregate leverage screen.

### CATALYSTS
1. **2026** — first full year of meaningful AI contract revenue recognition
2. **2026** — potential sale or separation of Quantum Fiber consumer business
3. **2026–27** — refinancing window for 2027 maturities
