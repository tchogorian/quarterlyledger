# {NUMBER}. PHIN — PHINIA Inc.
## SPINOFF — QUALITY-AT-DISCOUNT | 9x Forward EPS / Aftermarket Cash Flow | Score: 61/100

### THE SETUP

PHINIA was spun out of BorgWarner (BWA) on July 3, 2023, taking with it the legacy fuel-systems and aftermarket business that BorgWarner wanted to separate as it pivoted its corporate identity to electrification and EV-pure-play. The spin-off was textbook orphaning: BorgWarner shareholders received PHINIA shares, EV-mandate funds dumped them within weeks, and ICE-narrative aversion suppressed the price even though the underlying business generates steady cash and recurring aftermarket revenue.

At $68.91, PHINIA carries a $2.96 billion market cap on roughly $3.5 billion in revenue and ~$130 million in trailing net income. The stock trades at approximately 9x forward EPS versus comparable industrial parts companies at 12-15x. The aftermarket segment — Delphi Technologies brand, ~30% of revenue — delivers structurally higher margins and recurring demand that is essentially insulated from the EV transition timeline.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

The "stranded ICE asset" narrative is overdone. Even in the most aggressive EV transition forecasts, internal-combustion engine vehicles remain on the road for 15-20+ years. Commercial vehicles (medium and heavy-duty trucks) are particularly slow to electrify because of energy-density requirements, and PHINIA's commercial-vehicle exposure is meaningful. Diesel will be powering trucks, off-highway equipment, and agricultural machinery well into the 2040s. The aftermarket business — which sells replacement parts for the existing global fleet — actually benefits from longer vehicle lives.

PHINIA's capital allocation since the spin has been disciplined. The company has run a steady buyback program, pays a 2-3% dividend, and has done bolt-on aftermarket M&A. Management is targeting incremental margin improvement through pricing discipline and cost-out actions inherited from the BorgWarner separation. The 2025 announcement of hydrogen and alternative-fuel injection-system contract wins is a credibility builder — PHINIA is not just a runoff business; it has technology applicability to next-generation fuels.

The valuation gap to industrial peers reflects the stranded-asset perception more than fundamentals. As the aftermarket cash flow story becomes clearer over multiple quarterly prints, and as buybacks reduce share count meaningfully (target is double-digit % over time at current cash generation), the multiple should compress toward peer levels. Even modest re-rating to 11-12x forward EPS implies meaningful upside before considering organic earnings growth.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The aftermarket revenue mix is the entire thesis. Most screens lump PHINIA in with cyclical auto suppliers and apply a cyclical multiple. But the aftermarket segment (Delphi, BWD, etc.) generates recurring high-margin demand that behaves more like a consumer staples business than a cyclical auto supplier. Splitting PHINIA into "OE fuel systems" (cyclical, lower-margin) and "aftermarket" (recurring, higher-margin) and applying segment-appropriate multiples produces a sum-of-parts well above current price.

There's also a strategic combination angle: another aftermarket parts company (Standard Motor Products, Dorman, even private equity rolls like First Brands) could view PHINIA's global aftermarket footprint as a natural fit. The CEO has been measured but not closed off to strategic options.

### CATALYSTS

- Quarterly aftermarket revenue and margin updates (the proof points)
- Buyback acceleration as cash builds
- Hydrogen / alternative-fuel injection-system wins (commercialization)
- Strategic combination with another aftermarket player
- Inclusion in industrial / dividend ETFs as discount narrative fades
