# {NUMBER}. BAYRY — Bayer AG (ADR)
## LITIGATION RESOLUTION | $25B mcap, $7.25B settlement clears | Score: 60/100

### THE SETUP
Missouri court granted preliminary approval March 5, 2026 to Bayer's $7.25B Roundup class action settlement, with final fairness hearing July 9, 2026 and opt-out deadline June 4, 2026. Resolution caps the bulk of ~65,000 remaining glyphosate claims and lifts a multi-year overhang that has crushed Bayer's multiple from ~12x to a distressed ~5x. The ADR trades at a fraction of pre-Monsanto-merger value despite owning a top-tier pharma franchise (Nubeqa, Kerendia) and the global #1 ag-chem business.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Bayer is a German life sciences conglomerate across three divisions: Pharmaceuticals (~€18B sales — Xarelto, Eylea, Nubeqa, Kerendia), Crop Science (~€23B — Roundup, seeds, traits, biologicals from the Monsanto acquisition), and Consumer Health (~€6B — Aspirin, Aleve, Bepanthen). FY2025 net loss €3.6B, weighed by litigation provisions and goodwill items. Crop Science Q3 2025 EBITDA before special items €172M (vs ~€36M PY), margin 4.5% — early signs of normalization after years of glyphosate price collapse and excess channel inventory. Pharma sales held flat with Nubeqa (prostate cancer) and Kerendia (CKD/HF) growing meaningfully. The litigation event: Missouri Judge Boyer preliminarily approved the $7.25B class settlement March 5, 2026 covering ~65K claims, with payments structured over 21 years; per-claimant payouts $10K–$165K. Final approval hearing July 9, 2026; opt-out deadline June 4, 2026.

Bear case: settlement is preliminary, not final — opt-outs by plaintiff law firms could materially reduce coverage and leave Bayer exposed to continued tort liability. Several state-court verdicts in 2025–26 ran into nine and ten figures (a recent $2B Missouri verdict tested the appeals). The 21-year payment schedule means future cash demands persist, and the settlement does not include latent-disease claims for users diagnosed after a cutoff. Bayer's leverage is heavy (~€33B net debt) and the dividend was cut to a token level. Pharma faces Xarelto and Eylea genericization/biosimilar pressure starting 2026–28, and the Crop Science recovery is contingent on glyphosate price normalization that has been promised for three years. CEO Bill Anderson's "Dynamic Shared Ownership" reorganization is unproven. The 10-K-equivalent (German annual report) flags ongoing PCB and dicamba litigation overhangs separate from Roundup.

Bull case: $7.25B is well within Bayer's pre-existing legal reserve (~$16B booked through 2024); incremental P&L impact is limited and the cash impact is amortized over 21 years. Removing Roundup uncertainty unlocks dividend reinstatement, allows asset sales (Consumer Health spin/IPO long-rumored), and re-rates the pharma franchise on standalone P/E. SOTP: Pharma ~€60–70B, Crop Science ~€40–50B, Consumer Health ~€20–25B = €120–145B EV vs ~€60B current EV after net debt — implies 50–100% upside if the conglomerate discount and litigation discount both compress. Cushion: even pre-settlement, Bayer generates €5B+ FCF and has €16B legal reserves. Pharma pipeline (acoramidis, asundexian re-do, finerenone label expansions) provides growth optionality. Activist pressure from Bluebell, Inclusive Capital and others continues to push for breakup.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The annual report discloses Bayer's pre-existing legal provisions of ~€16B for Roundup-related litigation — the $7.25B settlement is a use of, not an addition to, those reserves, so the income statement and balance sheet impact is largely neutral relative to consensus. What the screen also misses: Bill Anderson's "Dynamic Shared Ownership" delayered roughly 5,500 management positions through 2025 — a real cost-out that drops to OIBDA in 2026–27. The Crop Science Q3 EBITDA print (€172M vs €36M PY) signals the bottom is in for glyphosate pricing — a leading indicator the screen will not catch until the FY26 print. Bayer's pharma division has under-the-radar pipeline value: Nubeqa is on a ~€2B run-rate in prostate cancer, Kerendia label expansion into HFpEF is pending, and elinzanetant (menopause/VMS) launched in 2025 is a potential blockbuster.
