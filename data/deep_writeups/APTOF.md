### ANALYST SUMMARY
Aptose is a clinical-stage oncology biotech whose lead asset is tuspetinib (TUS), an oral kinase inhibitor for newly diagnosed acute myeloid leukemia (AML). The TUS+venetoclax+azacitidine triplet (TUSCANY study) reportedly delivered 90% CR/CRh across 40-120 mg doses with 78% MRD-negative remissions. The company has run on going-concern fumes for years, and Hanmi (already a strategic partner and ~17% holder) stepped in with a take-private at C$2.41/share — a 28% premium to the 30-day VWAP of C$1.88. Total deal value is approximately US$25M plus an US$11.1M bridge loan that Hanmi has already extended to keep the lights on through close.

Bear case: this is a clinical biotech with negative cash flow, going-concern doubts disclosed in the 10-K, and a single asset that has not generated revenue. If the cross-border arrangement hits a snag — Alberta court appeal window, residual SEC 13E-3 conditions, TSX final approval, or any dissenting-shareholder action — the stock has no fundamental floor. Standalone, APTOF was trading at C$1.88 pre-deal and would likely revisit a far lower number absent Hanmi capital. Cross-border plan-of-arrangement deals carry timing risk even after vote/court approval; the 13E-3/A was just amended April 1, 2026.

Bull case: this is a near-fully-cooked deal. ISS recommended FOR, no superior proposal emerged, vote passed, court order in hand, and Hanmi has the balance sheet and the strategic logic (Korean pharma extending into US AML pipeline). The annualized spread on ~14 days to close at 0.6% gross is roughly 8-15% IRR depending on settlement timing. Cushion is the bridge loan, Hanmi's existing equity stake, and the absence of any organized opposition. Risk/reward is binary-tight: collect 1-2% in two weeks, or take a 20-40% mark-down if the deal cracks late.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 13E-3/A filed April 1, 2026 reveals two things screens miss: (1) Hanmi already advanced an US$11.1M bridge loan, which means walking away costs Hanmi the bridge, not just the C$25M equity check — it's now economically committed. (2) The plan of arrangement is under Alberta law (Court of King's Bench), which is a fast, well-trodden path with predictable timing once the final order issues. Combined with no organized dissent and ISS support, residual deal risk is mostly settlement mechanics, not fundamental break risk. Standalone NAV is effectively zero — this is a pure deal-or-no-deal.
