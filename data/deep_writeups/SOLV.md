### THE SETUP

Solventum is the healthcare business of 3M Company, spun off on April 1, 2024. The company contains four segments: MedSurg (advanced wound care, sterilization, IV site management), Dental Solutions, Health Information Systems, and Purification & Filtration. At spin, SOLV inherited roughly $8.3B of debt — net leverage near 3.5x EBITDA — to fund a one-time cash payment back to 3M. Predictably, 3M shareholders (largely dividend/quality holders) dumped the levered, slow-growth healthcare cousin into the open market within weeks. The stock fell from a $70s open to the high $50s, and Trian Partners (Nelson Peltz) showed up with a ~5% stake by mid-2024.

The catalyst that re-rated the equity in 2025–2026 was the announced sale of Purification & Filtration to Thermo Fisher for ~$4.1B — the divisions accounted for low-growth, low-multiple revenue and disposing of them at a premium multiple lets management slash leverage and refocus on dental and medical surgical, where segment margins are 25%+. Combined with Trian's January 2025 open letter laying out a $140 by year-end 2027 target, SOLV has all the ingredients of a textbook spinoff-plus-activist trade: a forced-seller-driven discount, a clear deleveraging path, and an engaged activist with a credible plan.

### ANALYST SUMMARY (3p)

The bull case rests on three legs. First, **deleveraging**: $4.1B Thermo Fisher proceeds (after tax, call it ~$3.2–3.5B net) take leverage from ~3.5x to ~2.0x or below, freeing the balance sheet for buybacks and bolt-on M&A. Second, **margin expansion**: 3M's healthcare segment was historically a high-20s margin business; standalone dyssynergies (TSA costs, IT carve-out) compressed margins post-spin, but most of these roll off through 2026 — getting back to legacy levels alone is worth several turns of EBITDA. Third, **revenue re-acceleration**: management has guided to MedSurg organic growth recovery as supply chain and ERP issues normalize.

Trian's $140 PT implies SOLV trades at roughly 14x 2027E EBITDA on improved margins — not cheap, but not heroic for a focused medtech. At spot $70 the stock is at ~9x 2025E EBITDA, a clear discount to medtech peers (Baxter, Stryker, Becton trade 12–18x). Free cash flow guidance of ~$2B annually post-deleveraging supports the buyback narrative.

The bear case is execution: the company has missed organic-growth targets twice since the spin, the dental segment is exposed to discretionary consumer spending, and a Trian board push could become messy if engagement stalls. The activist accelerant is real but not infinite — Trian has shown patience at other names (Disney, Wendy's) but also escalation when needed.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

A standard quant screen sees: spinoff with declining margins, levered balance sheet, sub-3% organic growth, FCF conversion below peer medtech. It will not see (a) the Thermo Fisher deal proceeds and the resulting leverage delta, (b) Trian's nominating window opened December 2025 with potential 2026 board push, or (c) the structural re-rating mechanic where post-divestiture SOLV becomes a pure-play medtech worthy of a mid-teens multiple rather than the 9x conglomerate-style multiple it currently carries. The "Bad Cousin" optical narrative also masks the underlying business mix shift toward higher-growth dental and medical surgical.

### MUNGER CHECKLIST

- **Business quality**: Above average. MedSurg and dental are high-margin, recurring-revenue franchises with sticky hospital/dentist relationships. Health Info Systems has ARR characteristics. Purification was the weak link — being divested.
- **Management**: New CEO Bryan Hanson (ex-Zimmer Biomet) joined May 2024; track record of operational turnarounds. Board partly inherited from 3M; activist refresh likely.
- **Balance sheet**: Currently stretched (~3.5x net leverage), but Thermo Fisher proceeds bring this to ~2.0x — investment-grade comfortable.
- **Capital allocation**: Dividend established at modest level; buybacks expected post-deleveraging. Management has signaled disciplined capital deployment.
- **Margin of safety**: Trian PT $140 vs $70 spot = ~100% upside over 18–24 months if thesis plays out. Even base-case re-rating to 11x EBITDA on 2026 numbers gets to ~$95 — material upside with manageable downside given the asset value floor.
- **Risks**: Execution slip on margin guidance; dental cyclicality; activist friction with board; healthcare reimbursement headwinds.

The setup is one of the cleaner large-cap spinoff-plus-activist trades available — divestiture proceeds in hand, sophisticated activist on register, and a management team incentivized to deliver. Score reflects high conviction on direction with modest execution risk.
