# {NUMBER}. CRWV — CoreWeave, Inc.
## POST-LOCKUP | August 2025 lockup expired; stock down 46% from peak | Score: 56/100

### THE SETUP
CoreWeave IPO'd in March 2025 as the leading AI cloud infrastructure provider; the August 17, 2025 lockup expiration unleashed $1B+ of insider selling (notably from Magnetar) and the stock fell 46% from its June 2025 peak. With the technical overhang largely digested, the question is whether AI infrastructure demand can sustain a $61B market cap.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
CoreWeave is a specialized AI cloud provider operating roughly 30+ data centers stocked with NVIDIA H100, H200, and GB200 GPUs sold as bare-metal and Kubernetes-managed cloud. The customer base is concentrated among the largest AI workloads — Microsoft (the dominant customer), Meta, OpenAI, and a handful of frontier labs and enterprise generative-AI buyers. Revenue grew at triple-digit rates through 2024 and the first half of 2025 as the company aggressively expanded its NVIDIA fleet, financed by a mix of high-yield debt, asset-backed financings collateralized by GPU contracts, and equity from the March 2025 IPO. The Core Scientific acquisition (announced 2025) brings in additional power and data center capacity.

The bear case has multiple legs. Customer concentration is extreme — Microsoft alone is reportedly over 60% of revenue, and Microsoft has incentives to internalize compute over time. The capital structure is highly leveraged with billions in GPU-collateralized debt that depreciates as NVIDIA releases successive generations. Insider selling (Magnetar, founders, employees) post-lockup signaled near-term overvaluation. AI capex growth is unsustainable indefinitely; any pause in hyperscaler buildouts (Microsoft has already moderated some announcements) directly impairs CoreWeave's growth trajectory. NVIDIA itself competes via DGX Cloud and is a key supplier and shareholder. Convertible debt overhang remains. Regulatory scrutiny around concentrated AI compute could emerge.

The bull case is that AI workload demand structurally outstrips hyperscaler internal capacity for years, and CoreWeave has built a differentiated technical platform optimized for large-scale GPU training and inference. The Core Scientific deal materially expands power and site footprint at a time when grid interconnect is the binding constraint. Long-duration contracts (multi-year RPO) with credit-quality customers provide revenue visibility. A re-rating could come from confirmation of additional tier-1 hyperscaler contracts (Google, Amazon) which would diversify revenue and validate the platform. Insider selling overhang is largely cleared.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
CoreWeave's S-1 and subsequent 10-Q disclosures show that a substantial portion of debt is structured as delayed-draw term loans collateralized by specific GPU equipment and customer contracts (including the "DDTL 1.0" and "DDTL 2.0" facilities). This structure means deleveraging happens automatically as customer payments amortize, but it also means that any customer concentration shock (Microsoft renegotiation, OpenAI delay) directly hits debt service capacity. The remaining performance obligation (RPO) backlog is the key disclosed metric — it shows multi-year contracted revenue and is the cleanest read on demand visibility. Power purchase agreements and data center site leases are also disclosed in the 10-K and reveal where capacity is actually growing.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Q1 2026 earnings (expected May 2026) — RPO backlog and customer concentration disclosure
- Hyperscaler contract diversification announcements (Google, Amazon, or Apple)
- Core Scientific deal close and integration timeline
