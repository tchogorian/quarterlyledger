# {NUMBER}. ARM — Arm Holdings plc
## POST-LOCKUP | ~90% SoftBank Float Overhang | Score: 48/100

### THE SETUP
ARM IPO'd September 2023; SoftBank still owns ~90% of the float, all standard lockups have expired, and SoftBank has $20B in margin loans drawn against the position with a $22.5B OpenAI funding commitment. The setup isn't whether SoftBank sells — it's when, how much, and at what discount to the next print.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
ARM is the dominant CPU IP licensor — 99% of premium smartphones, expanding share in data center (AWS Graviton5, NVIDIA Vera, Microsoft Cobalt 200), automotive, and edge AI. Q3 FYE26 (December 2025) revenue grew 26% YoY to $1.24B — the fourth consecutive billion-dollar quarter. Royalty revenue hit a record $737M (+27% YoY); license & other revenue $505M (+25%). Arm Neoverse CPUs surpassed one billion cores deployed with expected ~50% share among top hyperscalers. 21 CSS (Compute Subsystem) licenses across 12 customers; all top four Android vendors ship CSS-powered devices. Market cap ~$137B at ~$130/share.

Bear case is structural: SoftBank owns ~87-90% (~923M ADS), expanded its margin loan facility from $13.5B to $20B with $8.5B drawn against the ARM position, and has a $22.5B OpenAI funding obligation due. That overhang means every rally invites a secondary, and every secondary prints at a discount. Beyond that: customer concentration (Apple, Qualcomm, MediaTek royalty rates are individually negotiated and disputed — Qualcomm litigation history); China royalty collection through the partially-controlled Arm China entity; and the valuation — ~70x trailing P/E on a business with $4-5B revenue. ARM is priced as a hyperscaler-AI proxy, not as a royalty company.

Bull case: AI server CPU is the most credible new royalty leg in 20 years. Hyperscalers building in-house CPUs (Graviton, Cobalt, Vera) all ride Neoverse, and the per-chip royalty in datacenter dwarfs mobile. Tesla Optimus and Rivian Autonomy are early "physical AI" production wins for custom Armv9 silicon. Royalty revenue compounds while incremental cost is near-zero — operating margins should fan out as the v9 mix grows. Catalyst path: FY2026 results May 2026, continued CSS adoption, and — paradoxically — a clean SoftBank secondary that sets a true free float reference price. Cushion is essentially the IP moat itself, not the multiple.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Two filing-level points: SoftBank Q3 disclosures (the November 2025 earnings) explicitly tied ARM as collateral to the margin facility and to the OpenAI commitment — that creates a non-discretionary supply pipeline, not a strategic one. And the v9 royalty rate uplift (multiples of v8) flows through reported royalty revenue with a multi-year lag because royalty is recognized when chips ship, not when they're licensed. So the $737M royalty quarter understates the pipeline; v9 royalties are still ramping into mass production. The screen sees a 70x stock; it doesn't see that the royalty mix is still mostly v8 today.
