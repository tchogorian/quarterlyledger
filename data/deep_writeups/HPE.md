# {NUMBER}. HPE — Hewlett Packard Enterprise Company
## ACTIVIST | Elliott Board Seat + Strategy Committee | Score: 71/100

### THE SETUP
Elliott Investment Management took a $1.5B+ stake in HPE in April 2025 and signed a cooperation agreement July 16, 2025 that put KLA Chairman Bob Calderoni on HPE's board and created a new Strategy Committee. With HPE at $26.44 trading roughly 9x earnings versus Cisco at 15-20x, the activist setup pairs an underappreciated AI server franchise and freshly-closed Juniper acquisition with explicit CEO accountability — Antonio Neri remains under pressure to deliver Juniper synergies or exit.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Hewlett Packard Enterprise is the enterprise infrastructure pure-play remaining after the 2015 split with HP Inc. — server (ProLiant), storage (Alletra), networking (Aruba and now Juniper), high-performance computing/AI (Cray-derived ProLiant Compute XD), and the GreenLake hybrid-cloud-as-a-service platform. The $14B Juniper Networks acquisition closed in 2025 after extended antitrust scrutiny, doubling HPE's networking franchise to ~$15B in revenue and creating a credible #2 enterprise networking player against Cisco. AI server momentum has been real: HPE has captured material H100/H200/Blackwell-based contracts including a multi-billion dollar GPU deal with X.AI in 2024.

The bear case: HPE is a low-quality compounder. Server hardware margins are persistently compressed by competition with Dell and ODMs (Supermicro, Quanta); AI server gross margins are even thinner because most of the BOM goes to Nvidia. Juniper integration risk is real — HPE doesn't have a great large-deal integration track record, and $600M synergy targets typically slip 18-24 months in tech. CEO Antonio Neri is a 30-year HPE/HP lifer; activist pressure plus a Strategy Committee plus a new board member is the classic precursor to involuntary CEO transition, but if the board defends Neri, Elliott may exit and the multiple compresses again. Hybrid cloud / GreenLake growth is good but small versus the legacy server/storage drag.

The bull case: trading at 9x earnings against Cisco's 15x, even closing half the gap is 30%+ upside. Elliott has a strong recent track record of installing operating discipline — its boards typically pursue cost programs of 8-12% of opex, which on HPE's $20B+ cost base is multi-billion-dollar EPS power. Juniper synergies are credible; networking margins (40%+ gross) significantly improve mix. AI server tailwinds continue into 2026/2027 as enterprise GPU adoption broadens beyond hyperscalers. Capital return — buybacks against a depressed multiple — compounds value creation.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
HPE's 2024 10-K segment disclosure shows server segment operating margin expanding into the low-teens with AI mix, and Intelligent Edge (Aruba networking) running mid-20s margins — significantly above the consolidated average. The Juniper acquisition adds another high-teens margin business. The 10-K also discloses long-dated GreenLake contracted backlog — multi-year recurring revenue committed but not yet on the income statement, providing visibility the trailing P/E doesn't reflect. Pension and post-retirement liabilities have shrunk meaningfully, freeing future cash for buybacks. Calderoni's prior board work at KLA, Citrix, and Sungard included CEO transitions — his presence on the Strategy Committee is not coincidental.

### CATALYSTS
- FY26 Juniper integration synergy milestones ($600M target)
- Q2/Q3 2026 earnings — AI server bookings and networking revenue
- 2026 annual meeting — Elliott push for additional governance changes
- Possible CEO transition announcement (open-ended)
