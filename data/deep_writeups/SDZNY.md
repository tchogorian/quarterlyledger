# {NUMBER}. SDZNY — Sandoz Group AG (ADR)
## SPINOFF | Novartis generics carve-out, Oct 2023 | Score: 71/100

### THE SETUP
Sandoz was spun out of Novartis on October 4, 2023 in a clean 1-for-1 distribution to Novartis shareholders, becoming an independent global generics-and-biosimilars pure-play listed primarily on the SIX Swiss Exchange (SDZ.SW) with a Level I unsponsored ADR (SDZNY) trading on OTCQX in the US. Each Novartis ADR holder received 0.2 Sandoz ADRs. The OTCQX listing meant the ticker is excluded from S&P 500, Russell, and most US institutional benchmarks, and most Novartis ADR investors who received the spin sold immediately to remain in pure innovative-pharma exposure. Two-and-a-half years post-spin, the stock has more than doubled from its initial trading range, yet still trades at a meaningful discount to global pharma peers.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The standalone investment case rests on biosimilars, not generics. Sandoz is the global #1 in biosimilars by revenue and was first-to-market in Europe with biosimilar versions of Humira (adalimumab — Hyrimoz), Enbrel (etanercept — Erelzi), and is launching biosimilar versions of Stelara (ustekinumab) across Europe and the US. The biosimilars TAM is estimated at $50-70B addressable market by 2030 as the patent cliff for major biologics ($100B+ in branded sales) lapses. Unlike commodity small-molecule generics — which suffer from instant 90% price erosion — biosimilars require significant manufacturing capability, regulatory navigation, and physician-detail effort. The result is a more defensible 3-5 player oligopoly per molecule with pricing erosion measured in 30-50% over years rather than weeks.

The spin economics are favorable. Sandoz exited Novartis with a clean balance sheet (net debt to EBITDA ~3x at spin, deleveraging to ~2x by 2026), a defined dividend policy targeting roughly 30% payout, and a multi-year capex program for biosimilar manufacturing in Slovenia and Austria already in flight. The "Boncioli plan" (CEO Richard Saynor's strategic refresh) explicitly targets mid-single-digit revenue growth and EBITDA margins ramping toward the mid-20s by 2028 from the high-teens at spin. Free cash flow conversion is improving as one-time spin/separation costs roll off.

The mispricing has three sources, all addressable. First, US benchmark exclusion — the OTCQX ADR will not change until Sandoz potentially files for a Level II/III ADR, which the company has hinted at but not committed to. Second, the bear narrative on "generics is bad business" mentally lumps Sandoz with Teva and Viatris despite the biosimilars mix being meaningfully different. Third, currency translation (CHF reporting against USD/EUR sales) creates noise that obscures underlying organic growth. Each can compress over 12-24 months as biosimilar revenue ramps demonstrably.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A US screen sees an OTCQX ticker with thin volume and skips. It cannot see that the primary listing on SIX is liquid (one of the largest healthcare names on the Swiss exchange) and that the ADR mostly tracks. It also cannot see the "spin discount + index exclusion + bad-narrative" triple-discount that has historically rewarded patient holders of European pharma carve-outs (Solvay, Akzo's Specialty Chemicals → Nouryon path). Pricing on a sum-of-parts of generics + biosimilars + cash-flow durability suggests fair value materially above current quote.

### CATALYSTS
- **Stelara biosimilar (Pyzchiva) US launch ramp through 2026:** the single largest near-term revenue growth driver.
- **FY2026 earnings (March 2027) and 2028 mid-term targets reaffirmation:** validates the margin walk to mid-20s EBITDA.
- **Possible US Level II/III ADR uplisting (12-24 month tail):** unlocks US institutional ownership and benchmark inclusion.
