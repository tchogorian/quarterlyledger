# {NUMBER}. APLS — Apellis Pharmaceuticals, Inc.
## M&A ARBITRAGE | $41 cash + up to $4 CVR @ $40.90 | Score: 72/100

### THE SETUP
Biogen agreed March 31, 2026 to acquire Apellis for $41/share cash plus a non-transferable CVR worth up to $4 (Syfovre 2027–31 sales triggers). Tender offer (SC TO-T filed April 14, 2026) initially expires May 13, 2026, with a Section 251(h) back-end merger — no shareholder vote required. At $40.90 the gross spread on the cash piece is ~0.24%; the CVR is the optionality.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Apellis is a commercial-stage biotech with two approved C3-targeted complement therapies: Syfovre (intravitreal pegcetacoplan) for geographic atrophy secondary to AMD, and Empaveli (subcutaneous pegcetacoplan) for PNH and now C3G/IC-MPGN. FY2025 results: net product revenue $689M (Syfovre $587M + Empaveli $102M); total revenue $1.0B including a one-time $275M Sobi (ex-US partner) milestone payment; net income $22.4M — first profitable year. Cash $466.2M at year-end. A $94M convertible matures September 2026. Syfovre delivered ~102,000 doses in 2025 and held ~60% GA market share; Empaveli ended with 267 cumulative C3G/IC-MPGN patient starts and >5% US market penetration. On March 31, 2026 Biogen announced the $5.6B acquisition; the SC TO-T was filed April 14, 2026 with a May 13, 2026 initial expiration. Morningside and certain insiders (~14%) signed support agreements. The Apellis Board unanimously recommends the offer.

Bear case: at $40.90 vs. $41.00 cash, the spread is essentially zero — risk/reward is asymmetric to the downside. Deal-break would re-rate APLS back to standalone fundamentals (probably mid-$20s given the convertible maturity and post-deal investor exodus), implying ~30%+ downside vs. ~0.3% upside on the cash piece. The CVR ($2 if 2027-30 sales hit $1.5B; another $2 if 2027-31 sales hit $2.0B) is "non-transferable" — it cannot be monetized in the secondary market and may be valued at zero by many institutional holders. Hitting $1.5B annual Syfovre + Empaveli sales by 2030 requires meaningful growth from $689M base, while complement competition (Iveric Bio's Izervay, Roche's pipeline) is intensifying.

Bull case: deal-break risk is genuinely low — Biogen and Apellis have zero marketed-product overlap (Biogen's neuro/MS franchise vs. Apellis's complement); HSR is routine; tender mechanics avoid shareholder vote and Section 251(h) back-end merger ensures swift close (Q2/Q3 2026). The 14% support stockholder pact provides a head-start toward the majority tender threshold. The CVR is real optionality — at $689M base with 17% YoY dose growth, a path to $1.5B by 2027–30 is plausible if Empaveli's nephrology launch executes. CVR worth ~$1–2 in present-value terms is the difference between a 0.3% deal arb and a 5–10% total return.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The CVR structure is the alpha. Syfovre LTM dose growth was ~17% and Empaveli's C3G launch is materially ahead of street models — a screener computes the deal IRR on the cash piece alone and concludes the trade is uninteresting, but the CVR's $2 + $2 ladder is back-end-loaded and matches a plausible product trajectory under Biogen's broader commercial reach. Also unappreciated: the $94M convertible maturing September 2026 was an overhang that the merger neatly resolves. The 14% support stockholder commitment effectively de-risks the tender minimum. Finally, no Biogen/Apellis product overlap means antitrust review is genuinely routine — closing-risk skew is far smaller than typical pharma deals.
