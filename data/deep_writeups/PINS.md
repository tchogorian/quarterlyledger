# {NUMBER}. PINS — Pinterest, Inc.
## ACTIVIST — CAPITAL RETURN | Elliott $1B Convert / $2B ASR | Score: 59/100

### THE SETUP

On March 3, 2026, Elliott Investment Management invested $1 billion in Pinterest convertible senior notes — 1.75% coupon, 2031 maturity, $22.72 conversion strike (a 30% premium to the prior close). Concurrently, Pinterest announced a $2 billion accelerated share repurchase program funded by the convert proceeds plus existing cash, and the board upsized total buyback authorization to $3.5 billion. Elliott has held a Pinterest position since 2022 and Marc Steinberg sits on the board.

The setup is unusual. At $20.12, the stock currently trades below Elliott's $22.72 conversion strike, meaning Elliott's convert is effectively underwriting a floor on Pinterest's own buyback program. If the buyback works and stock re-rates above $22.72, Elliott converts and shares in upside. If it doesn't work, Elliott collects 1.75% coupons on a senior debt instrument secured by Pinterest's $2B+ cash balance.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Pinterest's fundamentals are stronger than the stock price suggests. The platform has 500+ million monthly active users, a uniquely high-intent commercial discovery use case (people pin things they want to buy), and a multi-year AI-monetization roadmap. The Amazon ads partnership signed in 2024 has been ramping faster than expected, with third-party demand now augmenting Pinterest's direct ad sales force. Adjusted EBITDA margins have expanded from mid-teens in 2022 to mid-twenties in 2025, with management targeting 30%+ over the medium term.

The market has been skeptical for two reasons. First, ad spend on Pinterest has historically been viewed as a discretionary line that gets cut in macro tightening — though this proved mostly wrong in 2023-25. Second, Pinterest competes for ad dollars against Meta, Google, Amazon, and TikTok, all of which have larger user bases and more sophisticated ad tech. The bear case argues PINS will always be a "minority share" platform that can't earn a premium multiple.

Elliott's structure addresses both concerns elegantly. The $2B ASR creates immediate EPS accretion (~10% share count reduction at current prices). The $3.5B total authorization signals the board's confidence in cash generation. And Elliott's continued engagement via Steinberg's board seat ensures management stays focused on monetization execution rather than strategic distractions. The ASR being concentrated in H1 2026 means buyback activity will provide meaningful technical support to the stock.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The convert structure embeds an unusual signal. Elliott is not merely activist; they are paying for a 30% premium strike to acquire upside in a company they already help govern. That is a genuine endorsement of intrinsic value above $22.72 over the convert's life. Elliott does not throw away $1 billion on a thesis they don't believe in.

The Amazon partnership is also under-modeled by sell-side. The partnership generates a take rate on third-party ad spend flowing through Pinterest inventory, which scales with category coverage and is a relatively high-margin revenue stream that doesn't require Pinterest to expand its own ad-tech investment. Early traction has been strong, but the analyst community is mostly waiting for full-year 2026 disclosure to upgrade models.

AI commerce is the longer-tail thesis. Pinterest's image-recognition and visual-search capabilities are genuinely differentiated; if AI commerce evolves toward "show me something I want and let me buy it," Pinterest's user base behavior is uniquely aligned. This optionality is essentially free at current prices.

### CATALYSTS

- H1 2026 $2B ASR completion (technical support)
- Q1/Q2 2026 earnings (margin and revenue beats)
- Amazon ads partnership scale data
- AI commerce monetization disclosures
- Potential additional Elliott escalation if stock languishes

- Honest management: CEO Bill Ready has executed monetization roadmap credibly

- Reversion risk: Ad cyclicality is real; competitive intensity from Meta/TikTok persists
- Skip if: You don't believe Pinterest can defend its niche against larger ad platforms
