# {NUMBER}. REPL — Replimune Group, Inc.
## FDA BINARY (FAILED) | $165M Mcap / Second CRL April 10 2026 | Score: 32/100
### THE SETUP
On April 10, 2026, the FDA issued a second Complete Response Letter rejecting Replimune's RP1 (vusolimogene oncolytic immunotherapy) for advanced melanoma — the second CRL in roughly 12 months for the same indication. The stock collapsed more than 90%, taking the market cap from over $2 billion at the prior approval-expectation peak down to roughly $165 million at $2.15/share. The setup now is a busted FDA binary: the headline asset has been rejected twice, the lead indication is functionally dead in the near-term, and the equity has compressed to a level where investors are essentially valuing the company as a cash-floor-plus-pipeline-residual story. RP2 (uveal melanoma) and RP3 (HCC) remain in development, and there is roughly $200M of cash on the balance sheet against an annual burn that is now under intense management scrutiny. The question is whether REPL is a value trap on a permanent runway compression story or a deep-value pipeline restart at distressed pricing.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The honest read on Replimune post-CRL: this is a science story whose commercial timeline has been pushed back at minimum two years and possibly permanently for the lead indication. The melanoma RP1 program required a confirmatory Phase 3, which was already underway but won't read out until late 2027 at earliest. In the meantime, the cash burn has to be reset, the RP2/RP3 assets need to demonstrate independent value, and management has to decide whether to pursue strategic alternatives or try to grind through to a future approval cycle.

The cash-floor analysis is what makes this interesting at $2.15. Roughly $200M of cash plus the residual value of RP2 and RP3 platform IP (oncolytic HSV vectors with credible Phase 1/2 data in distinct tumor types) implies enterprise value below zero on most reasonable scenarios. The pipeline is not worthless — RP2 in uveal melanoma showed meaningful response data and uveal is a small enough patient population that a focused approval is at least conceivable. RP3 in HCC has more competition but credible mechanism. A strategic buyer (or even a license deal) would likely pay a premium to current EV simply for the platform.

The bear case is real and probably the base case. Biotech companies that suffer two CRLs on a lead asset are notoriously bad investments — management credibility is shot, the cash burn extends a year longer than guided, and a "going concern" footnote shows up well before the cash actually runs out, triggering forced equity raises at distressed prices. Any investor sizing REPL needs to assume at least one dilutive financing in the next 18 months. The "binary failed, now buy the wreckage" trade is mathematically attractive but operationally brutal — most of these names grind sideways or down for 18 months before any actual restart catalyst emerges.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A screen sees a 90%+ drawdown and either flags as garbage or as deep-value bounce candidate. What's invisible: (1) the CRL itself was reportedly tied to manufacturing/CMC and trial design questions rather than efficacy — meaning the underlying drug may still work, and a Type A meeting in H2 2026 could clarify a viable path; (2) the platform IP across RP1/RP2/RP3 is a credible oncolytic immunotherapy franchise that has strategic value to large pharma even if RP1 itself is dead; (3) cash runway depends on whether management can cut R&D spend by 40%+ — the next quarterly cash report is the real tell; and (4) institutional ownership has been gutted, leaving a thin insider/retail tape that can move violently on any positive data point.

### CATALYSTS
- **H2 2026 — FDA Type A meeting outcome:** Whether RP1 has any near-term regulatory path.
- **Q4 2026 — RP2 uveal melanoma Phase 3 interim:** The most credible standalone value driver.
- **2026-2027 — strategic alternatives announcement:** License/sale of platform or full take-out by larger oncology player.
