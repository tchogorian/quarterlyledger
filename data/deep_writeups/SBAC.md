# {NUMBER}. SBAC — SBA Communications Corporation
## TAKE-PRIVATE | Rumored $250/sh KKR/Brookfield bid vs. $223 | Score: 52/100

### THE SETUP
SBA Communications is the #3 US tower REIT (behind American Tower and Crown Castle), owning roughly 40,000 macro towers across the Americas and Africa. On April 2, 2026, Bloomberg reported SBAC was working with advisors to explore a sale after preliminary interest from KKR and Brookfield Infrastructure. No definitive agreement has been announced, no premium is on the table, and the company itself has not confirmed a formal process. Shares popped from the high-$190s to ~$223 on the leak and have hovered there ever since, implying the market gives roughly 60-65% probability to a deal getting done somewhere near the rumored $250 indication. This is "narrative arb," not a true merger arb — there is no signed deal, no walk price, no break fee, and no shareholder vote teed up. It belongs in the speculation bucket.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The strategic logic is real. Tower REITs are the platonic ideal of private infrastructure: 5-10 year contracted MLAs with auto-escalators, ~70% incremental margins on co-location, and underwriting that resembles a triple-net lease against investment-grade tenants (T-Mobile, Verizon, AT&T, Vodafone). KKR has been an aggressive buyer of telecom infra (Vantage Towers, Telecom Italia's NetCo), and Brookfield owns GD Towers in Germany via its infrastructure platform. SBAC's higher-leverage / higher-IRR posture (net debt to EBITDA in the high-6x range) is exactly the kind of asset PE can re-equitize and lever back up post-rate-cuts.

But the math is hard. At $250, the equity check is ~$27B; assume a takeout at 22-23x AFFO and EV pushes toward $52-55B — among the largest infrastructure LBOs ever. Financing such a deal requires a club, REIT-conversion mechanics, and cooperation from key tenants whose MLAs may have change-of-control language. American Tower and Crown Castle are out as strategic buyers (antitrust). The most realistic path is a consortium of KKR + Brookfield + a sovereign wealth co-investor.

The 12% gross spread reflects three live risks: (1) the talks fall apart and shares retrace to a pre-leak ~$195 print (~12-15% downside), (2) any signed deal closes at a price closer to $230-$240 rather than $250, and (3) a 12-15 month close timeline given FCC license transfers and country-by-country tower regulators in Brazil, South Africa, etc. Position sizing should reflect that this is an unconfirmed rumor, not a definitive merger agreement.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A screen will show "12% spread, 11% annualized" and rank this attractive. What it misses: there is no signed merger agreement. The "deal" is one Bloomberg sourced article. SBAC's board has acknowledged nothing publicly. There is no walk-away price, no go-shop, no fiduciary out — because there is no deal. The screen also misses the optionality the other way: if AFFO continues to compound at 6-8% with rate cuts in 2026, SBAC may walk away from any bid below $260-$270 and the stock re-rates organically.

### CATALYSTS
- **Q2 2026 earnings (early August 2026):** management commentary on strategic review, even a "no comment / process ongoing" formalization.
- **Definitive merger announcement (target: late Q2 / early Q3 2026):** the binary event — confirms or kills the trade.
- **FCC consent + sovereign reviews (12-15 months post-signing):** the real long-tail to close, modeling toward Q2 2027.
