# {NUMBER}. KLG — WK Kellogg Co
## M&A ARBITRAGE | Ferrero cash deal at $23; close 2H 2026 | Score: 73/100

### THE SETUP
WK Kellogg trades at $22.50 against a binding Ferrero cash offer of $23.00, leaving a ~2.2% gross spread on a deal that the market expects to close in the second half of 2026. The asymmetry: Ferrero (the privately held confectionery family behind Nutella, Kinder, Tic Tac, and Ferrero Rocher) acquiring a North American cereal pure-play has minimal antitrust overlap — Ferrero has essentially no cereal business, and the breakfast aisle is a separate retail planogram from confectionery. Spread compression to deal close is the base case; any topping bid is gravy.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
WK Kellogg is the North American cereal stub spun out of legacy Kellogg in October 2023, holding Frosted Flakes, Special K, Froot Loops, Corn Flakes, Raisin Bran, Mini-Wheats, Rice Krispies, Frosted Mini-Wheats, and the Kashi/Bear Naked natural lines. The post-spin thesis was operational: a slimmed-down independent NA cereal company with dedicated capital and a margin-recovery plan around supply-chain modernization (the "Network of the Future" plant investment program). Ferrero announced its acquisition in 2025 at $23/share — a meaningful premium to where KLG was trading and a validation of the brand portfolio's intrinsic value.

The bear case: cereal as a category is in volume decline globally as consumers shift to portable breakfast (yogurt, bars, on-the-go) and the narrative around added sugar in kids' cereals remains a long-term overhang. If the Ferrero deal breaks, KLG resets to roughly $14-16 standalone — its pre-deal trading range — a 35%+ drawdown. Antitrust risk is genuinely low, but a deal-break scenario from financing, family-owned-buyer reconsideration, or unexpected regulatory friction (state-level review, foreign-investment screening) cannot be dismissed entirely. The supply-chain modernization capex still in flight could also drive a profit air pocket if the deal extends.

The bull case: Ferrero is privately held with no equity-financing risk, has been actively building US presence (Nestle US confectionery acquisition in 2018), and has clearly been planning the cereal entry for years. The cash consideration is fully committed. The $23 floor combined with the 2H 2026 expected close offers an annualized spread in the high-single-digits — attractive on the merger-arb risk-reward spectrum. Optionality: if Mars (which already bought Kellanova) or another strategic decided cereal was strategic, a topping bid is conceivable, though Ferrero's binding agreement and matching-rights provisions make this unlikely. Quality-of-spread matters here.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 2024 10-K explicitly disclosed the multi-year "Network of the Future" supply-chain transformation — modernizing or consolidating cereal production across multiple US plants — with capex commitments running well into 2026. The merger proxy with Ferrero acknowledges this and structures Ferrero's purchase to assume the in-flight capex; this protects the deal from a "material adverse change" claim tied to one-time costs. Less obvious: KLG's 10-K segment data shows margin recovery already underway from post-spin lows — Ferrero is buying into improving fundamentals, not a falling knife. The proxy also discloses customary deal protections including a no-shop with fiduciary out, but no superior-proposal trigger has materialized as of the latest filings.
