### ANALYST SUMMARY
Great Lakes Dredge & Dock is the largest U.S. provider of dredging services and the only domestic player with the Jones Act-eligible fleet scale to bid on most U.S. Army Corps of Engineers and port-deepening contracts. Saltchuk — a private holding company that already owns TOTE, Foss Maritime, Tropical Shipping, and Northern Air Cargo — agreed on February 11, 2026 to acquire GLDD for $17 per share in cash, valuing equity at roughly $1.2B and enterprise value near $1.5B. The SC TO-T was filed March 4, 2026; the tender expired March 31, 2026 with conditions met; back-end merger and delisting expected Q2 2026.

The bear case at $17 is essentially zero return. Spread is 0%; if the back-end merger slips into Q3 due to a MARAD or CFIUS-style review of the Jones Act vessel transfer, the dollarized opportunity cost grows. There is also a thin tail risk that Saltchuk discovers a material adverse change in the offshore wind backlog — Great Lakes' subsea-rock installation vessel program for offshore wind has been an execution-risk story for years and a single dispute could create grounds for renegotiation, though the merger agreement has tight outs.

The bull case is mostly a no-loss waiting trade: hold for ~60 days, collect $17, redeploy. Optional upside would require a topping bid, which is highly unlikely given (a) Saltchuk's strategic fit and patient capital, (b) Jones Act limitations narrowing the universe of credible bidders to U.S. flag operators, and (c) shareholder approval already crystallized via tender. The realistic IRR is the risk-free rate equivalent over the close window.
