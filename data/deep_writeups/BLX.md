### ANALYST SUMMARY
Bladex is a Panama-headquartered specialized trade-finance bank serving Latin American corporates and financial institutions. Class A shares are owned by Latin American central banks; Class E shares trade on NYSE as BLX. Per FY24 results (filed in 2025 20-F): record annual net income $205.9M (+24% YoY) = $5.60 EPS; ROE 16.2% (+153 bps YoY); credit portfolio all-time high $11.2B (+18%); Tier 1 Basel III ratio 15.5%; regulatory capital adequacy ratio 13.6%; NIM 2.47% FY24 (2.44% in 4Q24); approx. $12B total assets. Quarterly dividend raised 25% to $0.625 = $2.50 annual. 2025 momentum continued: 2Q25 net profit $64.2M / EPS $1.73 / annualized ROE 18.5%; FY25 net income $226.9M = $6.11 EPS; raised dividend again toward $1.50 declared 4Q25.

Bear case: Latin American sovereign credit risk is the elephant — Bladex's portfolio spans Brazil, Mexico, Colombia, Chile, Argentina, Central America. Any regional crisis (Argentina default, Venezuelan-style sanctions spillover, Brazil fiscal blow-up) hits the loan book directly. NIM of 2.47% is thin and rate-sensitive — Fed cuts compress spreads. The bank's funding base depends on confidence-sensitive interbank and capital markets borrowing — a single confidence shock can trigger funding stress. Class structure (central bank Class A holders) means minority shareholders have limited governance influence.

Bull case: ROE inflection from ~14% in 2023 to 16-18% in 2024-2025 with capital adequacy intact = the rare profitable EM bank trading at modest multiples. At ~$36-42/share area in 2025-2026 with $6.11 FY25 EPS = ~6-7x earnings. Dividend yield 6-7% backed by $200M+ net income and growing. Bladex is the natural hedge to USD-funded LatAm trade — its niche is too narrow for global G-SIBs to compete, too sophisticated for purely domestic LatAm banks. Cushion: 15.5% Tier 1 ratio is well above regulatory minimum, Basel III compliant, and trade finance assets are short-duration (90-180 day rollovers) so credit shocks resolve faster than mortgage book stress.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Bladex has a structurally lower funding cost than commercial-bank peers because Class A central-bank shareholders provide implicit credibility — central banks of member countries are unlikely to allow a default. This is explicitly disclosed in the 20-F as a competitive advantage. The 2030 strategic plan (filed via 6-K) targets continued ROE expansion and credit portfolio growth, with management explicitly committing to disciplined capital allocation and progressive dividend hikes (now 25% raise + special dividend pattern). Trade finance margins also expand when interest rate volatility creates dislocations between LatAm and USD funding curves — exactly the regime since 2022.
