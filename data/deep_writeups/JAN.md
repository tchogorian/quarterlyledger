### THE SETUP
Janus Living is the only US-listed pure-play, fully RIDEA-structured senior-housing REIT — carved out of Healthpeak Properties (DOC) via IPO in March 2026. It owns a 34-community, 10,422-unit portfolio concentrated in Florida and Texas, with Healthpeak retaining ~85.3% of the post-IPO float and acting as external manager. The mismatch between newly-listed scarcity value (only RIDEA pure-play) and tiny float / no analyst coverage is the asymmetry.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Janus Living launched its IPO during the week of March 16, 2026 at $18-$20/share, raising approximately $703M (37M shares offered). At the midpoint, fully diluted market value implied roughly $4.8B. The current $25.86 print suggests the deal priced and traded above the indicated range. RIDEA structure means operating income flows from resident-paid private-pay services — there is no Medicare/Medicaid reimbursement risk. Healthpeak retains 85.3% via Class A-1 shares plus operating units and serves as external manager, giving Janus institutional asset-management infrastructure without standing up its own platform.

The bear case is real: thin float (15% public) suppresses liquidity and price discovery, no analyst coverage at launch, the externally-managed REIT structure historically trades at a discount to internally-managed peers, and senior housing still carries the COVID-era operating scar tissue (occupancy recovery is real but margins remain below 2019). Healthpeak's eventual sell-down is a known overhang — every secondary will pressure the stock. The $240M Atlanta acquisition demonstrates the external-growth thesis but also raises capital-deployment risk in a still-volatile cap-rate environment.

The bull thesis is structural scarcity: investors who want pure-play senior-housing exposure have only Janus, NHC, SBRA and Welltower (mixed model). RIDEA upside means operating recovery flows directly to shareholders rather than being capped at a NNN lease rate. The demographic tailwind is the longest in real estate — the 80+ population doubles by 2040. As Healthpeak sells down, free float seasons, index inclusion mechanics activate (S&P MidCap, Russell), and analyst initiations begin. The first standalone earnings print is the obvious catalyst — same-property NOI growth is the variable that will re-rate the multiple toward NHC/SBRA premium.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 85% Healthpeak overhang is well-known, but the structure of secondary share sales matters: any orderly distribution boosts float and triggers index-inclusion timing windows that mechanical buyers must respect. The external-management agreement with Healthpeak means GAAP G&A understates true cost (management fees flow through differently) — bears will use this against the stock; bulls will note the cost of standing up an internal platform would be higher in year one. The Atlanta-area $240M deal closed alongside the IPO, signaling external-growth runway; subsequent acquisition cap rates will be the most important fundamental data point in the first two earnings calls.
