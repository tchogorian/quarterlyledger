### ANALYST SUMMARY
HYT is a BlackRock-managed closed-end fund holding a portfolio of US corporate high-yield bonds, leveraged ~30% with a managed distribution policy. The fund trades around $9.30 against an indicative NAV near $9.65, a modest single-digit discount, with market cap roughly $1.3B before the rights raise. The current event is a 1-for-5 transferable rights offering: existing holders receive one right per five shares owned, exercisable at $8.69 — set to roughly 90% of NAV at pricing date. The offering is fully transferable, meaning rights will trade on the NYSE during the subscription period, creating a real market in the dilution itself.

The bear case: rights offerings are dilutive to NAV (you're issuing shares below NAV), and BlackRock collects fees on the bigger AUM base. Holders who don't exercise watch their NAV mark down by the discount-to-NAV times the dilution percentage. Discounts on BlackRock CEFs tend to widen during the offering window as forced selling from non-participants and arbitrageurs hits the tape. Underlying high-yield credit is fully priced; OAS spreads near cycle tights leave little cushion if defaults pick up in 2026. Saba Capital and 1607 Capital have been aggressive in BlackRock CEFs — but here BlackRock has pre-empted with a rights offering that locks in AUM rather than shrinks the fund.

The bull case is mechanical, not fundamental. Post-expiration, the technical pressure lifts and discount typically tightens 100-300 bps. Oversubscribers can effectively buy NAV at a 10% discount via the over-subscription privilege — a clean arbitrage if you have rights to exercise. The post-offering distribution announcement often re-prices the yield narrative: with $283M in fresh capital deployed at current HY yields ~7.5%, the fund's earned-income coverage improves. Discount mean-reversion plus a possible distribution increase gives a clean 5-10% total-return setup over a 3-6 month window. Not a fortune, but tight risk on a liquid CEF.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 424B5 prospectus supplement specifies the over-subscription mechanics and pro-rata allocation rules — read it. Key detail: only record-date holders can over-subscribe, and allocation is pro rata if oversubscribed. Watch the rights ticker (typically HYT.RT or similar) for value during the window — at expiration the rights value collapses to intrinsic. Also check the leverage line: BlackRock typically maintains target leverage ratios, so the rights raise will be paired with proportional new borrowings, multiplying the NAV-deployment effect. The fund's actual undistributed net investment income (UNII) and return-of-capital component of distributions are disclosed monthly — tight UNII coverage is a yellow flag for the post-offering distribution.
