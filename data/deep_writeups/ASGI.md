### ANALYST SUMMARY
ASGI is an abrdn-managed closed-end fund focused on global infrastructure equity income (utilities, midstream, telecom towers, transport). Per the most recent N-CSR (Dec 8, 2025), the fund delivered a 15.21% NAV return for 2025. Distribution is a managed income payout. The fund was originally launched in 2020 with a 15-year defined term ending July 2035 — that term provided a built-in NAV-realization catalyst that helped close the discount. Saba pushed for and benefited from discount-narrowing; with ASGI now trading at a premium, Saba reduced below 5% (1,423,673 shares per March 2025 13D/A). On March 16, 2026, the board announced a proposal to remove the term limit and convert to perpetual status, with a special meeting tentatively scheduled for May 27, 2026.

Bear case is straightforward: ASGI is ABOVE NAV. A CEF trading at a premium has negative expected value on the discount component (premiums mean-revert to discounts for sector CEFs), and removing the 2035 termination date strips out the embedded NAV-realization option that helped justify the premium. Most CEFs that go perpetual gradually re-rate back to a discount over 6-18 months as the structural NAV catalyst disappears. Saba's reduced stake means the activist pressure that created the premium is gone. Sector beta to interest-rate moves is high — long-duration infrastructure equities sell off on rate spikes.

Bull case: the 15.21% NAV return for 2025 is genuinely strong, distribution coverage looks reasonable, the abrdn complex is incentivized to keep the premium intact (it's one of the few CEFs in the family trading north of NAV), and infrastructure as an asset class benefits from continued AI-driven datacenter / power capex. If the term-removal vote fails, the 2035 catalyst is restored; if it passes, fee breakpoints kick in and reduce the net advisory fee. Either way the May 27 special meeting is binary.
