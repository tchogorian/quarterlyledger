### ANALYST SUMMARY
BMEZ is a $914M market-cap closed-end fund managed by BlackRock investing in global healthcare equities — biotech, medtech, pharma, healthcare services. NAV per share ~$16.20 vs. price $14.63 = 9.7% discount. The fund is structured as a "term trust" with a contractual termination date of January 31, 2027 (extendable by one year by board action), at which point it will liquidate or convert to open-end, returning NAV to holders. In January 2025, Saba Capital reached a cooperation agreement with BlackRock that triggered a 40% tender at 99.5% of NAV (commenced March 21, 2025); Saba tendered all its shares, withdrew its proposals, and accepted a 3-year standstill through the 2027 proxy season. The residual fund is now ~$914M with the same expiration clock and a wider-than-it-should-be discount.

Bear case: a 9.7% discount at face value is not a fat margin if you have to wait ~21 months to harvest it, and healthcare CEF NAVs can drop 15-20% in a sector drawdown — biotech XBI is a volatile underlying. The board can extend termination by one year (to Jan 2028), pushing the catalyst out and dragging IRR. Saba's standstill removes the most aggressive activist from the table. Distribution rate is funded partly from return of capital, masking true performance and creating a "managed distribution" trap where investors discover post-tender that NAV has been bleeding. If the board converts to open-end instead of liquidating, redemption queues + gating rules can delay full NAV capture.

Bull case: the term-trust expiration is a contractual, near-certain catalyst — at termination, NAV is paid in cash (or in-kind transferred). Hedging the underlying with XBI/IBB or a sector-neutral basket converts the trade into a pure 9.7% discount-capture over 21 months ≈ ~5.5% annualized hedged IRR with negligible market risk. Unhedged, you get healthcare beta + the discount close. Saba's 40% tender at 99.5% NAV is precedent: when the term arrives, BlackRock will not want a messy liquidation and will likely structure another orderly exit. Possible upside scenario: BlackRock proposes a second pre-term tender at high-90s% of NAV to shrink the fund further.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The cooperation agreement (Saba-BlackRock, Jan 21, 2025) contains a "most-favored-nation" provision in some BlackRock CEF settlements that auto-extends Saba's tender economics if BlackRock offers better terms to another holder — worth diligencing in the 8-K. The board's right to extend termination by one year requires a formal vote; in BlackRock's prior term-trust history (e.g., BSL, BHK), the extensions were exercised once and then funds liquidated cleanly. BMEZ's portfolio includes a chunky private/illiquid bucket (~10-15% in Level 3 biotech) that adds NAV-mark uncertainty — at termination, those will be sold at potentially below carrying value, so the realized return may be 50-150 bps below stated NAV. Distribution policy: ~7% managed distribution rate funded by ROC creates artificial yield.
