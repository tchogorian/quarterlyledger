# {NUMBER}. HQI — HireQuest, Inc.
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | Franchise Royalty Model + $20M Buyback | Score: 73/100

### THE SETUP
HireQuest is a 413-office staffing franchisor that booked $30.6M of royalty/license/interest revenue on $500M of system-wide franchisee sales in 2025 — a capital-light, high-margin franchise model wearing a low-multiple "staffing company" disguise. December 2025 brought a $20M buyback authorization plus the partial divestiture of executive-search brand MRINetwork, simplifying the story right as franchise temp-labor demand bottoms.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
HireQuest is the parent franchisor of HireQuest Direct (direct-dispatch industrial labor), Snelling, DriverQuest, and HireQuest Health staffing brands, plus historically MRINetwork executive search. The business model is materially different from operating staffing companies like Kelly Services or ManpowerGroup — HireQuest does not employ the temps; its 413 franchisee-owned offices employ ~75,000 temporary workers system-wide and remit royalties and license fees to HQI. That structure means HireQuest captures a low-double-digit percentage of system sales as recurring royalty revenue with corporate operating margins materially above operating-staffing peers. Capital intensity is near zero; the recurring revenue base scales with franchisee count and system sales rather than W-2 employee growth.

The bear case is cyclical and structural. Light-industrial temp-labor demand is sensitive to construction starts, manufacturing PMI, and warehouse activity — all of which softened through 2024-2025. Franchisees bear the front-line economic risk, but if franchisees fail or close, HQI's royalty stream contracts. The MRINetwork divestiture (January 1, 2026, contributing most non-contract staffing assets in exchange for 40% of MRI Operations) acknowledges that executive search franchising under the HQI umbrella underperformed and required restructuring. Acquisition history — Snelling, TEC Staffing, Ready Temporary Services — shows growth via tuck-in M&A; mis-priced deals or integration friction would compress returns. Float is small and liquidity is limited.

The bull case is the franchise economics finally being recognized. The $20M buyback (announced December 17, 2025) against a sub-$200M market cap is a large repurchase commitment and signals management confidence at depressed levels. As cyclical temp-labor demand recovers through 2026, royalty leverage (mostly fixed corporate cost base) drives operating-leverage EPS upside. Continued tuck-in acquisitions of small regional staffing brands at distressed multiples convert into franchised offices that compound the royalty base. The pure-franchisor multiple (think MTN, RCI Hospitality, Driven Brands) sits well above where HQI trades.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 2025 10-K reveals that revenue is "primarily royalties, license fees, and interest from franchisees" on system-wide sales of $500.2M — a structurally different financial profile from operating-staffing peers that screens lump it with. The MRI restructuring removes a low-margin, low-fit brand from the consolidated P&L while retaining 40% economics — accretive to corporate margins immediately. Workers' compensation reserves are a critical staffing-company tail liability; the 10-K's WC reserve commentary is the load-bearing footnote investors should pressure-test. The $20M buyback against a small float is a meaningful percentage of public float, mechanically supportive.

### CATALYSTS
- Q1 2026 earnings (first clean post-MRI quarter)
- Buyback program execution pace through 2026
- Franchisee count and system-sales metrics in next 10-K
- Possible additional tuck-in M&A announcements
