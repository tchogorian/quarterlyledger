# {NUMBER}. VFC — V.F. Corporation
## ACTIVIST — OPERATIONAL TURN | Engaged Capital + 2 board seats | Score: 71/100

### THE SETUP
V.F. Corporation owns a portfolio of apparel and footwear brands including Vans, The North Face, Timberland, Dickies, and Smartwool, with Supreme divested in 2024 for $1.5 billion. Once a $100+ stock and dividend aristocrat, VFC has been one of the largest-cap turnarounds in apparel: the dividend was cut, Vans collapsed from peak, leverage spiked, and the stock fell roughly 70% from its 2021 highs. Engaged Capital, the activist firm led by Glenn Welling, filed an SC 13D on January 19, 2024 and has run an active campaign since, ultimately winning two board seats with public backing from members of the founding family. CEO Bracken Darrell — recruited from Logitech in mid-2023 — is executing a "Reinvent" plan focused on cost cuts, debt paydown, and reigniting Vans. At $21.00 the market cap is approximately $8.2 billion.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The bull case is sum-of-parts plus an operational inflection. The North Face alone, on standalone apparel-brand multiples (15-20x EBITDA for premium outdoor brands like Moncler or Canada Goose at peak), would conservatively be worth $15-20 billion to a strategic acquirer. Add Timberland at low single-digit billions, Dickies at meaningful workwear value, and even a depressed Vans at $1-2 billion in liquidation/sale value, and the gross sum-of-parts comfortably clears $25-30 billion in enterprise value against current EV around $13-14 billion. The discount exists because Vans' decline has been worse and longer than expected, and consolidated EBITDA does not earn a sum-of-parts multiple.

The bear case is straightforward: Vans is a roughly $2.5 billion brand still in negative comps, and without Vans stabilization the consolidated story stays broken. Apparel cycles are unforgiving, the consumer is uneven, and brand turnarounds — particularly in youth-skewed footwear — have a poor base rate. Bracken Darrell has done the right things on cost and balance sheet (debt down, working capital cleaned up), but the inflection metric the market cares about is a quarter of positive Vans constant-currency growth, and that has not yet arrived.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Three under-screened factors. First, the founding-family backing of Engaged Capital is unusual and durable — it removes the risk of a counter-campaign and keeps governance pressure on management without being adversarial. Second, VFC owns its corporate campus and meaningful industrial real estate; sale-leaseback optionality is real and unmodeled. Third, the brand portfolio enables piecemeal divestitures: Timberland or Dickies could be sold to a strategic for cash applied to debt, accelerating deleveraging without forcing the issue on Vans. The market is pricing the conglomerate as if it must be held together, but the activist board presence increases the probability of a credible breakup path.

### CATALYSTS
- Vans constant-currency comp inflection (quarterly)
- Additional brand divestiture announcement (Timberland or Dickies most likely)
- Investor day or strategic update with refreshed long-term targets
- Real estate monetization announcement
- Margin recovery print — gross margin trajectory
- Dividend reinstatement or capital return signal

### MUNGER CHECKLIST
- Simple business: Yes — branded wholesale and DTC apparel
- Pricing power: Yes for The North Face, marginal for Timberland, weak for Vans currently
- Honest, capable management: Bracken Darrell has credibility from Logitech turnaround; activist directors provide accountability
- Reasonable price: At $21 with $25-30B sum-of-parts, the discount is wide; the question is whether the discount closes
- Within circle of competence: Apparel cycles and brand investing are accessible
- Margin of safety: Asymmetric — limited downside given balance sheet repair, North Face cash flow, and brand asset value, with optionality on Vans recovery and divestitures

The disciplined structure is to size for a multi-year hold, accept that Vans inflection timing is unknowable, and treat North Face as the floor asset. Engaged Capital's continued presence and the family's backing reduce governance risk. The trade is not the next quarter; it is the path to either a credible Vans recovery or a forced breakup — either of which prints meaningfully above $21.
