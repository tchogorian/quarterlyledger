# {NUMBER}. BATRA — Atlanta Braves Holdings, Inc. (Series A — Voting)
## SPINOFF — QUALITY-AT-DISCOUNT | $3.0B mcap, ~25% NAV discount | Score: 62/100

### THE SETUP
Atlanta Braves Holdings is the only public pure-play MLB franchise — split off from Liberty Media in July 2023, converting the old Braves tracking stock into a real-asset stub. BATRA is the Series A voting class. Comparable franchise sales (Orioles $1.7B in 2024, Twins talks ~$1.5B+) imply the team alone is worth $2.5–3B and the rapidly growing Battery Atlanta mixed-use development adds another $1–1.5B vs the $3B mcap. Malone-controlled, patient-capital structure with sale optionality.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Atlanta Braves Holdings owns 100% of the Atlanta Braves MLB club and 100% of The Battery Atlanta — the 1.5M-sqft mixed-use development around Truist Park. FY2025 total revenue $732M (+11%), split: Baseball $635M (+7%, driven by streaming-rights additions and contractual rate increases) and Mixed-Use Development $97M (+45%, accretive new acquisitions and tenant ramp). Adjusted OIBDA jumped 172% to $107.8M. Three share classes: BATRA (Series A, voting, ticker on Nasdaq), BATRB (Series B super-voting, OTC, controlled), BATRK (Series C, non-voting, Nasdaq, larger float). BATRA at ~$52.76 = ~$3.0B mcap; BATRA typically trades at a small premium to BATRK reflecting voting rights but the spread is rarely worth paying — in any sale or take-private both classes receive the same per-share consideration.

Bear case: MLB collective bargaining agreement expires December 2026 — work stoppage risk could compress 2027 revenue. Regional sports network economics keep deteriorating post-Diamond Sports bankruptcy; the Braves' RSN renewal is a binary cash flow event. Player payroll has escalated faster than revenue (CBT thresholds, free agent inflation), structurally compressing baseball OIBDA margins. The Battery Atlanta is a single-asset commercial real estate concentration in metro Atlanta — exposed to rate environment and tenant credit. Public sports franchise comp set is essentially N=1; valuation methods are non-standard, so generalist funds cannot easily underwrite. Liberty/Malone tracker overhang historically capped multiples; Liberty has not pre-committed to a sale.

Bull case: Forbes valued the Braves franchise at $2.95B in 2025; Sportico methodology suggests $3.0–3.5B; recent Orioles transaction at $1.7B in a smaller market is the cleanest comp and validates a $2.5B+ Atlanta floor. Battery Atlanta growing OIBDA 50%+ YoY with leasing momentum is at minimum a $1B asset on conservative cap rates. SOTP NAV = $3.5–4.5B vs $3.0B mcap = 17–33% discount. Charlie Ergen / billionaire-buyer playbook is alive: a sale to a strategic owner monetizes that gap immediately. BATRA voting rights matter only in a contested sale scenario — historically Liberty trackers convert at parity, so the small premium for BATRA usually compresses by deal close.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K discloses Truist Park land lease and Battery Atlanta development structure that are partly off-balance-sheet — the franchise is housed in Braves Holdings, LLC, with ~$725M of TeamCo-level debt that is non-recourse to ABH equity. That financial-engineering detail means the equity is more deeply NAV-cushioned than headline leverage suggests. Also: MLB's national media deal with ESPN/Fox/TNT structurally floors per-team broadcasting revenue regardless of local RSN outcomes — so the bear case on Diamond Sports is partly already absorbed. The new local broadcasting agreement (which prompted the Q4 contract-asset impairment) is now resolved with a streaming-rights addition that grew baseball revenue 7%. The Liberty/Malone history — Sirius XM, Live Nation, Formula One, Charter — shows holdings reliably get monetized eventually; mean holding period to event ~5–7 years post-spin.
