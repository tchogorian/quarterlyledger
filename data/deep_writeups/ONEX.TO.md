# {NUMBER}. ONEX.TO — Onex Corporation
## HOLDCO — NAV DISCOUNT | ~27% Discount to NAV | Score: 76/100

### THE SETUP
Canadian alternative-asset / private-equity holdco trading at ~$105.72 vs. an indicative $145 NAV — a 27% discount that management itself has called "anomalous." Aggressive buybacks at the discount, a CEO transition completed (Bobby Le Blanc replaced founder Gerry Schwartz), and $50B+ AUM platform with multiple monetization catalysts pending.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Onex is one of Canada's two flagship alternative-asset platforms (alongside Brookfield-adjacent vehicles), founded by Gerry Schwartz in 1984 and now managed by Bobby Le Blanc (CEO since 2024). The business is a hybrid: a fee-bearing asset-management franchise (Onex Partners private equity funds I-VI, ~$25B Onex Credit CLO/structured-credit platform, Gluskin Sheff high-net-worth wealth management) plus a $5-6B+ balance-sheet portfolio of legacy PE positions (Parkdean Resorts, Save-A-Lot, Convex Insurance, Wealth Enhancement Group, residual Ryan Specialty exposure). Total AUM exceeds $50B. The company explicitly publishes an "indicative NAV per share" each quarter.

The bear case is structural: Canadian holdcos persistently trade at 20-40% discounts to NAV due to founder-controlled multiple-voting share structures, illiquidity vs. U.S. peers, currency translation friction for U.S. investors, and the perennial PE-discount-skepticism — "if you mark your private positions to the public market, the discount disappears." Gluskin Sheff has had write-downs and AUM pressure in recent cycles. Onex Credit's CLO equity tranches are sensitive to credit-cycle stress. The PE realization machine is working but slow — $8B of realizations across funds in 2025 ($800M to Onex's balance sheet) is impressive but doesn't immediately re-rate the holdco multiple. Schwartz still controls voting, so the discount has structural floor.

The bull case is the buyback math: at a 27% discount, every share repurchased creates ~37 cents of NAV-per-share accretion for every dollar spent. Management has publicly identified the discount as unjustified and is sizing buybacks accordingly. Convex Insurance (specialty re/insurance startup founded with Onex backing) is approaching IPO/sale-window — a successful monetization at a 5-7x return on invested capital would be a defining catalyst. Onex Partners VI has fresh dry powder. Bobby Le Blanc as CEO removes the "founder overhang" narrative; rumors of a U.S. dual-listing would meaningfully expand the investor base. ADR (ONEXF) exists but is illiquid; the Canadian ONEX.TO listing is the cleaner vehicle.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Onex's quarterly "Investing Performance" supplement (filed on SEDAR) breaks out segment-level NAV: balance-sheet PE positions are marked at recent transaction values where available, and the wedge between management's indicative NAV ($145/share) and a conservatively-marked third-party NAV is roughly $15-20/share — meaning even on stress-marked NAV, the discount is still 15-20%. Convex Insurance's carrying value is disclosed separately and represents one of the largest single positions; its potential IPO is the single largest event-driven catalyst. Also missed by screens: Gerry Schwartz's voting control diminishes meaningfully under the post-transition governance — multiple-voting shares are subject to sunset triggers, which removes one of the structural discount factors over a multi-year horizon.

3. **2026-2027** — Possible U.S. dual-listing announcement (rumored); would expand investor base materially.
