### ANALYST SUMMARY
Brookfield Corp (BN) is the parent holdco of the Brookfield ecosystem. Following the December 2022 spin of asset-management arm BAM as a separately listed pure-play, BN retained a 75% economic interest in BAM (which has a $80.8B market cap; BN's stake worth ~$60.6B) and consolidated the on-balance-sheet "principal investments": insurance solutions via Brookfield Wealth Solutions (BNT) including Argo, Just Group and other annuity/reinsurance blocks; real estate equity stakes (private and BPY-style), PE/infra co-investments, and accrued carried interest receivables (~$10B+ unrealized). Mcap $105B, current price $46.59. Management's published "Plan Value" framework implies fair value ~$63/share by 2026 → 26% discount. Distributable earnings (DE) ~$3.50/share; trades at 13.2x DE vs. Apollo 14.6x, KKR 20x, Blackstone ~24x.

Bear case: the holdco discount is structural — multi-tiered listed entities (BAM, BEP, BIP, BBU, BWS) plus an opaque insurance wing make sum-of-parts valuation a guesswork exercise that has historically traded at persistent 20-30% discounts. GAAP P/E of ~98x scares quant screens. Insurance leverage at BNT (~$120B AUM) carries credit-spread duration risk that could blow up in a recession; commercial real estate stub remains an underwater chapter (BPY take-private was at a meaningful discount). Brookfield's "carry" claims rest on private fund vintages whose realizations depend on a transaction-market thaw — if 2026-2027 exit windows stay closed, the carried-interest pile-up is illiquid paper. Bruce Flatt is 60+; succession isn't guaranteed.

Bull case: the math is simple — 75% of BAM at current price = ~$60B / 2.27B BN shares = ~$26.50/share, but BAM should re-rate higher given its capital-light fee model (peer multiples imply $35-40/share for the BAM stake alone). Add ~$10B accrued carry (~$4.40/share), insurance solutions earnings power (~$1.5B annual DE → ~$10/share at 15x), and real-asset principal investments worth $20-30B on balance sheet, and "Plan Value" of $63 looks conservative. Catalysts: (1) DE growth +15-20% annualized through 2027; (2) carry realizations as 2014-2017 Brookfield fund vintages exit; (3) insurance float compounding at 12%+ ROEs; (4) potential corporate simplification eliminating discount.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Brookfield's "carried interest" line on the financials (~$10B unrealized as of latest disclosure) is a mark-to-model figure that Wall Street largely ignores — but it's real cash that crystallizes when private funds sell investments. The 2014-2017 BIF/BSREP vintages are entering peak harvest years 2026-2028; Brookfield Infrastructure Fund II already returned over 2x net. The 75% BAM ownership is a tax-efficient holding structure (BN's stake is held via a Bermuda intermediary), making any attempt to collapse the structure complicated but not impossible. Insiders Bruce Flatt, Connor Teskey and other partners own ~20% of BN directly and their economics are tied to DE per share growth — the 2024 long-term plan targets $6/share DE by 2030, a 3x from today. Read the latest letter to shareholders' "Plan Value" appendix; it spells out the SOTP line by line.
