# {NUMBER}. NPK — National Presto Industries, Inc.
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | Defense Backlog + Net Cash | Score: 72/100

### THE SETUP
National Presto is a quirky, family-controlled (Cohen family) micro-conglomerate combining defense ammunition manufacturing (AMTEC subsidiary, principally 40mm grenade rounds for the U.S. Army) with a legacy Presto-branded housewares business and a small adult-incontinence products unit. It carries large net cash, pays a giant variable special dividend most years, has near-zero sell-side coverage, and trades like the housewares brand even though defense is the main earnings engine.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
NPK's defense segment manufactures medium-caliber ammunition under multi-year programs of record with the U.S. Department of Defense. Backlog disclosed in the 10-K has expanded materially in the post-Ukraine restocking cycle, and the company has been awarded follow-on 40mm contracts that extend visibility several years out. The housewares segment (pressure cookers, fryers, small appliances) is mature and seasonal but throws off cash; the absorbent products segment is small. Capital structure is pristine — essentially debt-free with a meaningful cash hoard relative to the ~$700M-class market cap, which funds the annual special dividend that has been the headline shareholder-return mechanism for decades.

The bear case centers on lumpy defense revenue, single-customer concentration (the Army), and the housewares segment's secular decline as Instant Pot/competitor brands have eroded share. Margins in defense are subject to government cost-plus and FFP pricing dynamics that compress when input costs spike. Family control (Cohen heirs and trust) means there will be no activist-driven balance-sheet optimization, no buybacks at scale, and no spin-off of the defense unit despite its arguably much higher standalone multiple. Disclosure quality is famously thin — the company files plain-vanilla 10-Ks with limited segment color and does not host earnings calls.

The bull case: defense backlog growth in a multi-year DoD restocking environment, plus 40mm being a NATO-standard round with allied export demand, supports a multi-year earnings ramp. The "look-through" sum-of-parts (defense at peer multiples + housewares at scrap + cash) implies fair value materially above the current quote. The annual special dividend creates a natural total-return floor, and the family's track record of returning cash (rather than empire-building) is investor-friendly even without buybacks. Catalyst: any large new ammunition award or a rare strategic action.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K segment footnote shows defense-segment operating margin expansion that screens miss because they aggregate it with declining housewares. The pension footnote discloses an overfunded pension that flatters reported equity. Critically, the company holds a long-running litigation/indemnification reserve related to environmental remediation at AMTEC sites (Janesville, WI legacy) — the contingencies note is small but recurring and worth tracking. Insider ownership concentration via the Cohen family trusts is structural; proxy filings show consistent voting control with no dissent. The variable special dividend is announced in the Q4/annual release and not screen-visible until declared.

### CATALYSTS (3 dated)
1. Annual special dividend declaration (typically Feb/Mar 2026 with the 10-K release).
2. New 40mm or medium-caliber DoD contract announcements (rolling through FY2026).
3. FY2025 10-K filing — backlog and defense-segment margin disclosure.
