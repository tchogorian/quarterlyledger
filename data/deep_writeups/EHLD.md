### THE SETUP
Spun off from Euroseas (ESEA) on March 18, 2025 with two older feeder containerships, no debt, and a market cap below $30M. Six weeks post-spin, the board initiated a strategic alternatives review and then pivoted toward MR product tankers — classic forced-seller, orphaned micro-cap NAV arbitrage with multiple potential resolution paths.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
EuroHoldings is the textbook "ugly stepchild" spinoff: ESEA shareholders received fractional shares of a $25M micro-cap holding two aging feeder containerships (~2,500-3,000 TEU vintage). Index funds dumped, retail had no thesis, and Greek-shipping orphan optics scared off institutions. With the cash position and second-hand vessel values almost certainly exceeding the spin-off market cap, the discount-to-NAV is the entire trade. Float is tiny, liquidity is poor, and the entity is functionally controlled by the Pittas family — alignment is high but governance is family-run.

The bear case is straightforward: containership values are mean-reverting and the two vessels are old enough to face scrap-vs-trade decisions inside three years. Strategic-alternatives reviews from sub-$30M shipping shells frequently fizzle into self-dealing reverse mergers or perpetual delay. Greek-shipping micro-caps are notorious for value destruction at the corporate level (dilutive ATMs, related-party transactions, Marshall Islands governance with no recourse). The November 2025 acquisition of M/T Hellas Avatar (49,997 DWT product tanker) signals the family is using EHLD as a vehicle to scale rather than liquidate — meaning the NAV-realization catalyst could be deferred indefinitely.

The bull case rests on three legs: (1) NAV arbitrage at current prices is meaningful even using conservative second-hand vessel marks plus net cash; (2) the MR product-tanker pivot positions EHLD for the 2026 supply/demand inflection (Russian sanctions, fleet aging, slow newbuild orderbook); (3) strategic-alternatives review opens optionality for special dividend, sale to a peer (Castor, Globus, Performance Shipping), or roll-up. Asymmetry: vessel scrap value provides a downside floor; tanker re-rating plus NAV closure is multi-bagger.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
EHLD does not appear in most screens at all — it's a recently-spun Marshall Islands company with limited US filing history, no analyst coverage, and a market cap below most institutional minimums. The Hellas Avatar acquisition (announced November 17, 2025) fundamentally changes the asset mix from "two old containerships" to "containership + product tanker," and screening tools don't yet reflect the post-deal balance sheet. Critically, the strategic-alternatives review remains technically open per the April 30, 2025 release — meaning a wind-down or sale remains a live option even as management deploys capital. Pittas family ownership and Euroseas overlap create related-party risk but also strong cross-incentives.
