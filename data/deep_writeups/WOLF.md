# {NUMBER}. WOLF — Wolfspeed, Inc.
## POST-BANKRUPTCY | Mkt Cap ~$1.24B | Score: 67/100

### THE SETUP
Wolfspeed filed a prepackaged Chapter 11 on June 30, 2025 and emerged on September 29, 2025 as a recapitalized silicon-carbide pure-play. The reorganization cut roughly $4.6 billion of debt — about 70% of the prior obligations — pushed remaining maturities out to 2030, and reduced cash interest expense by approximately 60%. The legacy common was cancelled. Roughly 48 million new shares were issued, down dramatically from the ~156 million pre-BK float, with old equity holders receiving only ~1.3 million shares at a 0.0083 exchange ratio. Senior noteholders and backstop investors — including Apollo Global Management — own essentially all of the new equity. Post-emergence, the company carries about $2.07 billion of remaining debt against roughly $1.29 billion of cash. After a spectacular post-emergence pop (intraday gains widely reported at ~1,700%), the stock has settled around $25.56 with fresh-start accounting in place.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The post-bankruptcy WOLF is a structurally different company than the one that filed. The death-spiral thesis that dragged the prior equity to zero — too much debt, too much capex, no FCF visibility, dilution risk — has been surgically removed. What remains is the only US-domiciled silicon-carbide pure-play, with the Mohawk Valley 200mm SiC fab operational, approximately $2 billion of CHIPS Act funding intact, and a clean cap table. The fresh-start accounting restates the asset base, which makes prior-period comparisons largely meaningless and contributes to the screening blackout that typically follows post-BK emergences for 6-12 months.

The bull case rests on the SiC EV-adoption inflection in 2026-2028. Silicon carbide power electronics are increasingly the standard for 800V EV traction inverters, EV onboard chargers, fast-charging infrastructure and industrial power applications. Wolfspeed's competitive position is real — Mohawk Valley is the world's largest 200mm SiC device fab and the company controls upstream substrate supply through its Durham operations. The bear case is twofold: (1) Chinese SiC suppliers (BYD, SICC, TankeBlue) have flooded the market and compressed pricing, and (2) the post-BK shareholders (Apollo et al.) are not natural long-term holders and will distribute / sell over the next 12-18 months, creating overhang. That overhang is the central reason the stock is mispriced — it is a forced-seller dynamic, not a fundamental quality issue.

The setup follows the classic Greenblatt post-BK pattern: clean balance sheet, hated by fundamental investors who remember the prior wipeout, ignored by quants because of fresh-start accounting, and held by creditors who have to sell. As the distribution overhang clears and the SiC adoption cycle accelerates, the equity has a clear path to re-rating.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Fresh-start accounting makes nearly every quant ratio (P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/B) either nonsensical or misleading for at least 12 months. Most screens exclude post-emergence equities for 6-9 months until clean periods exist. Sell-side coverage has not re-initiated meaningfully. The CHIPS Act funding ($2B intact) is not always reflected in screener cash positions. Apollo's known distribution timeline is invisible to non-credit investors. Russell index reconstitution will eventually add passive demand once the float seasons.

### CATALYSTS
- Sequential gross-margin expansion as Mohawk Valley utilization ramps
- EV OEM SiC design-win disclosures (Tesla, Ford, GM, Hyundai)
- Strategic JV / equity investment announcement
- Russell index reconstitution (June 2026)
- Apollo distribution / overhang clearing
- China SiC pricing stabilization

- Honest, capable management: post-BK board refresh; new CEO discipline
- Margin of safety: moderate — clean balance sheet but cyclical demand
