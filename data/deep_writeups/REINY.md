# {NUMBER}. REINY — Reinet Investments S.C.A.
## HOLDCO | NAV Discount ~35% / 80% Cash Post-PIC | Score: 76/100
### THE SETUP
Reinet Investments is the Johann Rupert family's Luxembourg-domiciled investment holding vehicle, listed primarily on the Johannesburg Stock Exchange and Euronext Amsterdam, with REINY representing the thinly-traded US ADR. Until early 2026 the bulk of NAV was tied up in Pension Insurance Corporation (PIC) — the UK pension buyout specialist — and a residual British American Tobacco (BAT) position. On March 27, 2026, Reinet completed the GBP 2.9 billion sale of its PIC stake to Athora, following the prior multi-year exit from BAT (EUR 1.6 billion proceeds across 2024-25). The result: a balance sheet that is now approximately 80% cash (~$5.6B of total NAV around $7B equivalent), with the remainder in private fund commitments (Trilantic, TruArc, Prescient Asia funds) and small residuals. NAV per share was EUR 38.54 at June 30, 2025 and is estimated near EUR 47/share post-PIC close. The ADR trades at $30.70 against estimated NAV of ~$47/share equivalent — a ~35% discount on a portfolio that is now overwhelmingly liquid cash.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
This is the rare "event already happened, market hasn't fully repriced" holdco setup. Discounts to NAV in European holdcos are sticky — Reinet historically traded at 50%+ discount, narrowing to 30-35% as the portfolio became more liquid — but the post-PIC composition makes a persistent 35% discount logically hard to defend. When ~80% of your NAV is cash sitting in conservative instruments, the discount cannot be explained by "marks I don't trust" or "operating risk." It is essentially a discount on cash plus a small residual private-fund stake. The structural drivers are well-known: Luxembourg holdco tax inefficiencies, Rupert family majority control (creating capital allocation risk), low-liquidity ADR with no US analyst coverage, and the legitimate fear that the cash will be re-invested into something NAV-destructive rather than returned.

The bull edge is timing-specific. Cash is now visible on the balance sheet, monthly NAV publication is mandatory, and the Rupert family has historically supported buybacks at material discounts (Reinet has bought back stock periodically, and Compagnie Financière Richemont has done large take-out style returns). The plausible base case over 2026: either (a) special dividend / large tender announcement using a meaningful chunk of the PIC proceeds, narrowing the discount toward 20-25%; or (b) a take-private offer by the Rupert family at a ~10-15% premium, which still leaves 20%+ upside from current levels. Even the bear case (Rupert reinvests the cash into more illiquid funds at par) maintains the cash floor — the discount may persist, but downside is genuinely limited by the 80% cash position.

The honest caveat: REINY ADR liquidity is poor. The economically correct way to express this view is via the JSE-listed ordinary shares (RNI.SJ) or the Amsterdam line, where institutional flows can compress the discount. The ADR will follow but with lag and slippage. This is a 12-24 month thesis with a high-probability mid-teens IRR, not a tactical trade.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A screen will struggle to even pull NAV correctly because Reinet reports in EUR and quotes in ZAR/EUR while the ADR trades in USD. What's invisible from quote screens: (1) the post-PIC cash position dramatically de-risks the discount thesis — this is now 80% cash with a 35% discount, which is mathematically a buy-back-the-cash-at-65-cents trade; (2) the Rupert family's historical preference for capital return when discounts widen; (3) Trilantic and TruArc fund stakes are marked conservatively and have shown realizations above carry-forward marks; and (4) the absence of pension obligations or operating company drag means the discount is essentially pure-play structural, not fundamental.

### CATALYSTS
- **June 2026 — annual results and use-of-proceeds statement:** Likely capital-return announcement.
- **September 2026 — interim results / possible special dividend:** Pattern recognition from Richemont and prior Reinet returns.
- **2026-2027 — possible Rupert family take-private:** The discount-to-cash math invites a controlling-shareholder squeeze-out at a modest premium.
