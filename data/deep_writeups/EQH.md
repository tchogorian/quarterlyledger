### THE SETUP
On March 26, 2026, Equitable Holdings (EQH) and Corebridge Financial (CRBG) announced an all-stock merger of equals creating a ~$22B insurance and wealth firm. EQH receives 1.55516 NewCo shares per EQH share; CRBG receives 1.000. The market is trading EQH at $41.73 vs. an implied $41.61 deal value — a slight negative spread suggesting market expectation of a price bump or competing bid.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Equitable Holdings is the former AXA Equitable U.S. life insurance and retirement business, spun out of AXA via IPO in 2018. Its core franchises include Equitable Financial (variable annuities and life insurance), AllianceBernstein (publicly-traded asset manager AB), and Equitable Advisors (wealth management distribution). The CRBG combination — announced as a true MOE rather than an acquisition — would create a top-5 U.S. life and retirement player with combined scale in annuities, group retirement, individual life, and asset management. AIG remains a meaningful CRBG holder; the deal would help AIG ultimately monetize its CRBG stake into a more diversified vehicle.

The bear case starts with the negative spread itself: trading through deal price means downside if the deal closes at announced terms or breaks. Insurance MOEs carry meaningful execution risk — multi-state insurance regulator approvals (NY, CT, TX, IA), Fed/OCC for the holding combination, antitrust review, and shareholder votes from both sides. The all-stock structure exposes EQH holders to CRBG share-price drift before close. Variable annuity hedging programs, RILA reserves, and reinsurance side-car structures create complexity that integration teams underestimate. Activist agitation for a higher exchange ratio is possible but typically fails in agreed MOEs. Long timeline (Q4 2026 expected close) extends regulatory and market-condition risk.

The bull case is twofold: (1) a competing bidder for either side could emerge — life insurance consolidation has been steady (Brookfield, KKR, Apollo, Sixth Street all active), and EQH's wealth/AB stake has alternative-strategic value; (2) negotiated bumps are common when targets trade through, especially in MOEs where each side's board can renegotiate exchange ratios pre-vote. AB stake monetization, scale-driven cost synergies, and a re-rating of the combined entity to a higher ROE multiple are plausible.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 1.55516 exchange ratio implies a specific reference price relationship between EQH and CRBG that is not fixed-value — meaning the spread will move with both stocks. Read the merger agreement (when filed) for any walk-rights, top-up mechanics, and break-fee structure. Equitable's 61% ownership of AllianceBernstein (AB) is the embedded optionality the screen ignores: AB could be sold or spun separately, and the combined NewCo will need to address that strategic question. Insurance regulator approval timing in NY (DFS) and IA is the binding constraint, not antitrust. Watch for proxy advisor recommendations (ISS, Glass Lewis) — in MOEs these often determine whether one side's shareholders force a bump.

### CATALYSTS
- Joint proxy/S-4 filing expected Q2-Q3 2026 with detailed exchange-ratio analysis
- Shareholder votes from EQH and CRBG (likely Q3-Q4 2026)
- Insurance regulator approvals (NY DFS, multi-state) through Q4 2026
