# {NUMBER}. WS — Worthington Steel, Inc.
## SPINOFF | Mkt Cap ~$1.79B | Score: 68/100

### THE SETUP
Worthington Steel was spun off from Worthington Industries (now Worthington Enterprises, ticker WOR) on December 1, 2023, separating the steel-processing business from the consumer-facing tools, building products and ConX brands. At $35.28, WS carries a market cap of roughly $1.79 billion. The business has three growth vectors that the parent's mixed shareholder base never fully appreciated: (1) electrical-steel laminations for grid transformers, where US demand is structurally undersupplied with management citing roughly two-year transformer backlogs; (2) electrical-steel laminations for EV traction motors and onboard chargers; and (3) the recent acquisition of Sitem (Italy), which adds a European motor-lamination footprint and a strategic foothold with Tier-1 European OEMs. Capacity additions of approximately $85 million in Mexico and $85 million in Canada are scheduled to come online during 2026.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The Worthington Steel spin is a textbook small-cap orphan story. Worthington Enterprises holders bought into the consumer-products thesis — Coleman propane, ConX building products, water systems — and treated the cyclical steel-processing stub as something to be sold reflexively. The result is a sub-$2 billion market cap that is too small for the major dedicated steel funds (NUE, CLF, STLD all trade at >$10B and dominate generalist steel coverage), too cyclical for ESG / quality-growth mandates, and too obscure for most mid-cap value investors. Recent quarterly volatility from auto-cycle exposure has made the headline numbers lumpy and obscured the multi-year structural story underneath.

That structural story is electrical steel. Grain-oriented electrical steel (GOES) for transformers is in genuine supply shortage in North America — the combination of grid modernization, AI data-center build-out, and aging transformer fleets has produced a multi-year order book that domestic capacity cannot meet. Non-grain-oriented electrical steel (NOES) for EV traction motors is similarly tight, and Worthington Steel is one of a small handful of North American processors with the lamination capability. The 2026 Mexico and Canada capacity additions are explicitly aimed at this demand, and the Sitem acquisition extends the franchise into Europe at the same time European EV programs are accelerating. At roughly 14x earnings, the stock prices in cyclical steel-processing economics with essentially no premium for the strategic value of the electrical-steel franchise. The plausible strategic-acquirer set is small but credible — Cleveland-Cliffs has publicly identified electrical steel as a priority, and Nippon Steel (post-USS deal) would be a natural consolidator of upstream supply.

The risk is the auto cycle: Worthington Steel still derives a meaningful share of revenue from automotive flat-rolled steel processing, which is sensitive to OEM build rates and tariff dynamics. A 2026-2027 auto recession would compress earnings even as the electrical-steel franchise continues to grow. That cyclicality is real but at <2 years post-spin, the multiple has not yet reflected the structural mix shift toward higher-value electrical products.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Screens see the consolidated steel-processing margin profile and miss the mix shift toward electrical steel, which carries materially higher gross margins. They also miss the customer concentration in transformer manufacturers and EV OEMs that locks in multi-year contracts. The Sitem European footprint is too recent to appear in trailing financials. The two-year transformer backlog cited by management is not in any screener field. And the strategic-value optionality from a Cliffs or Nippon Steel approach is by definition unmodelable but real.

### CATALYSTS
- Quarterly electrical-steel revenue mix disclosures
- Mexico and Canada capacity ramp during 2026
- Sitem (Italy) integration and accretion
- Transformer-shortage policy commentary (DOE, FERC)
- Strategic M&A approach from Cliffs / Nippon Steel
- EV OEM design-win announcements
