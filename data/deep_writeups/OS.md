### THE SETUP
EPM SaaS LBO trading exactly at the $24.00 deal price after Hg Capital, General Atlantic, and Tidemark agreed to take OneStream private at $6.4B on January 6, 2026. ### ANALYST SUMMARY
OneStream is a corporate performance management (CPM/EPM) SaaS platform that competes with Anaplan, Workday Adaptive, and SAP for the financial close, consolidation, and planning workloads of mid-to-large enterprises. KKR took it public in mid-2024 but retained majority voting control through a dual-class structure, which is why the take-private deal could be approved by written consent in lieu of a stockholder meeting — a structurally clean exit. Hg Capital, with co-investors General Atlantic and Tidemark, agreed to $24.00/share or roughly $6.4B in total enterprise value, a modest premium to the pre-leak trading range.

The bear case for arbitrageurs is simple: at $24.00 there is no remaining spread. The IRR on this position is zero unless you bought before the announcement. Even on a downside basis, while close certainty is high, an outright break (HSR Second Request, geopolitical disruption, unexpected enforcement scrutiny) would send the stock back into the $15-18 zone where it traded pre-deal. So the trade is asymmetric the wrong way: zero upside, single-digit downside.

The bull case is narrow but real for cash-management portfolios: with KKR consent already in place and only HSR/regulatory approvals outstanding, this is essentially a money-market substitute with very high closing odds. If you can borrow the shares cheaply or pair the trade with another arb spread, OS represents low-volatility ballast for the H1 2026 close. The deal documents should be read carefully for the small step-up provisions and the no-shop language — both confirm management is fully aligned with the close.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Screens see "deal pending" and may flag annualized spread, but the spread here is literally zero — the market has fully discounted close certainty post-KKR consent. What a screen misses: the dual-class structure means there is no fiduciary out for the public minority because KKR can simply impose its will. Appraisal rights under Delaware law remain technically available, but historically appraisal trades for pre-IPO LBOs have not produced meaningful uplift. The interesting flow is in the BDC and credit fund space — the LBO debt syndication will be a benchmark for 2026 EPM/SaaS LBO pricing, and the buyer consortium's cost of capital matters more than OS's residual equity return.
