### ANALYST SUMMARY
Mallinckrodt emerged from its second Chapter 11 in November 2023, cutting ~$1.9B of funded debt and restructuring the opioid trust. On August 1, 2025 it merged with Endo Inc (also a 2024 post-BK company), and on November 10, 2025 it spun off Par Health (generics and sterile injectables). The reorganized branded specialty pharma was renamed Keenova Therapeutics and management has guided to NYSE relisting in the back half of 2026. The OTC tickers (MCKPF/MNKKQ/MNKTQ) currently price the equity at a tiny implied market cap relative to the underlying EBITDA, reflecting the institutional ownership ban on pink sheets, opioid stigma, and post-BK distribution-driven tax-loss selling.

The bear case is that "stranded security" theses are notoriously slow to resolve, the NYSE relisting timing can slip easily, and the pro-forma EBITDA guidance includes Endo merger synergies that have execution risk. The branded portfolio includes Acthar, Inomax, and Therakos — each with its own competitive and patent-litigation pressure — and the combined company is still a low-growth, asset-heavy specialty pharma with limited pipeline. Two BKs in five years suggests the asset base itself is impaired.

The bull case is pure mechanical: relisting flips the buyer base from retail-only to long-only institutional, which historically compresses post-BK stranded-security discounts substantially. With ~$700M+ EBITDA, a clean cap structure post second emergence, and a sponsor-class shareholder base aligned on relisting, the re-rating event is well-defined. Par Health spin establishment provides a separate equity for value to surface. Acthar revenue stabilization post the long-running litigation removes a key overhang.
