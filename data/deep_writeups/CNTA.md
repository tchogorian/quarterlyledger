This is a CVR pricing trade, not a deal-break trade.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Centessa is a UK-incorporated clinical-stage biotech whose lead asset is an orexin-receptor agonist for narcolepsy and other sleep-wake disorders. Lilly is paying ~$6.3B headline at the cash floor for a pre-revenue biotech because the orexin program has shown highly differentiated efficacy in narcolepsy type 1 — a category Lilly does not currently play in but where it sees long-term commercial fit alongside its neuroscience and metabolic franchises. The structure (UK scheme of arrangement, cash + CVR) is standard for cross-border biotech take-privates and provides high deal certainty on the cash component.

The bear case centers on the CVR. If the FDA approval, label, or sales milestones slip, the $9 CVR pays $0 and CNTA holders end up with $38 (down ~4% from current price, plus opportunity cost over a 6-month wait). UK scheme-of-arrangement timelines can stretch — court hearings, shareholder meetings, regulatory clearance from CMA and HSR. While Lilly/Centessa overlap is essentially zero, antitrust delay risk is non-zero in the current environment. A hostile FDA outcome on a pivotal Phase 3 readout between signing and close could give Lilly a MAC argument, though the deal value is small relative to Lilly's market cap.

The bull case is that the CVR milestones are well-defined and the orexin Phase 2 data is strong. If you believe the program is genuinely best-in-class for narcolepsy, two of the three milestones are likely achievable on a 24-36 month horizon, implying a probability-weighted CVR closer to $4-6, not $1.66. The cash floor caps downside at $38, the deal certainty is high (LLY balance sheet, no overlap), and the asymmetry favors CVR believers. A typical biotech CVR trades for 15-25% of headline value at signing; reaching that range on this deal would imply $40.5-40.25, putting current price near fair-CVR consensus.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The screen flags a -4% spread and looks past it. The actual trade is the CVR composition — which the proxy/scheme document (filed within 30-60 days of announcement) will detail with milestone definitions, payment triggers, and time-bar dates. Reading the CVR agreement reveals whether the $9 is one $9 payment or three $3 tranches (the latter is much more valuable on probability-weighted basis because partial wins are still wins). Also hidden: Lilly's typical CVR payment record on prior biotech acquisitions — a track record screen wouldn't pick up. Finally, the orexin field has multiple competing programs (Takeda, Alkermes); the CVR's commercial milestone may be the hardest to hit if competitive launches compress the addressable market.
