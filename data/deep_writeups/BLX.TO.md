# {NUMBER}. BLX.TO — Boralex Inc.
## TAKE-PRIVATE | C$37.25 cash deal, 1.66% gross spread | Score: 74/100

### THE SETUP
Brookfield Asset Management and La Caisse (CDPQ) signed a definitive agreement on March 25, 2026 to take Boralex private at C$37.25/share all-cash, valuing the Canadian renewable IPP at roughly US$9 billion. Stock at C$36.64 offers a clean 1.66% gross spread / ~2.94% annualized to a Q4 2026 close — a low-risk M&A arb anchored by a domestic acquirer (no Investment Canada Act blow-up risk).

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Boralex is a Quebec-headquartered renewable independent power producer with wind, solar, hydro and storage assets in Canada, France and the US, ~3 GW operating capacity plus a multi-GW development pipeline. The deal: Brookfield acquires the equity at C$37.25 cash with La Caisse rolling/contributing to retain a 30% partner stake in the privatized vehicle. Total enterprise value ~US$9B. Current price C$36.64 vs. C$37.25 deal price = 1.66% gross spread; annualized ~2.94% assuming a Q4 2026 close (roughly 7 months). Boralex's last public trading multiple was ~10x EV/EBITDA, in line with peer renewable IPPs. The transaction is structured as a plan of arrangement requiring 66.67% Boralex shareholder approval and Quebec Superior Court sanction.

Bear case: the spread is thin — 1.66% gross translates to single-digit annualized returns and offers little cushion if the deal slips two quarters or breaks. Investment Canada Act review, while less fraught for a Canadian acquirer, can still take 6-9 months for a strategic energy asset of this size. La Caisse already owns a stake which simplifies optics but means CDPQ governance must be re-papered. Multi-jurisdictional regulatory consents (FERC for US wind farms, French CRE/préfectoral consents for European wind/solar, Quebec's energy ministry) introduce schedule risk. A mild rate-cut surprise that re-rates IPPs could see Boralex shareholders agitating for a price bump à la Crown Castle / Innergex precedents. And a 1.66% spread offers no upside — if it widens to 4% on a delay, the IRR collapses.

Bull case: this is a domestic acquirer (Brookfield BAM is Canadian-incorporated; La Caisse is the Quebec pension), eliminating the Investment Canada "net benefit" hostility seen in foreign infrastructure takeouts. La Caisse rolling 30% means a key existing shareholder is signed-off — institutional vote support is already substantially de-risked. The C$37.25 price represents ~28% premium to undisturbed and was recommended unanimously by Boralex's special committee. Arbitrage upside: closes Q3 instead of Q4 → annualized return jumps to 4-5%; deal sweetener if a topping bid emerges (TotalEnergies, Engie or Iberdrola have all looked at Canadian wind assets).

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The deal documents disclose La Caisse's reinvestment as a "significant minority" rolled at deal price — meaning CDPQ is effectively underwriting the valuation with its own balance sheet. That signals Brookfield is not stretching. The merger agreement also reportedly includes a no-shop with a customary fiduciary out and a break fee in the ~3% range, which discourages a bidding war but doesn't preclude one. Cross-border arb angle: Boralex is Canadian-listed only (no NYSE listing), so US arbs need to clear FX exposure — most run a CAD/USD forward into the close date, locking in the spread in USD terms. Insurance arbs note the deal financing is internally funded at Brookfield (no external bridge), which removes the financing-out contingency that has tripped up several 2024-25 take-privates.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Boralex shareholder vote (special meeting expected June-July 2026)
- Investment Canada Act and Competition Act clearance (target Q3 2026)
- FERC and French regulatory consents → expected close Q4 2026
