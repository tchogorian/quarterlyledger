# {NUMBER}. NRP — Natural Resource Partners L.P.
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | Coal Royalty MLP, Debt Paydown Story | Score: 70/100

### THE SETUP
NRP is a coal royalty MLP that owns mineral rights and surface acreage; lessees mine, NRP collects royalties with no operating capex. Over the last several years management has used the cash gusher to pay down a once-precarious debt stack to near-zero, redeem the warrants/preferreds that diluted unitholders, and is now positioned to return capital aggressively. Trades at a single-digit free-cash-flow multiple with a depleting but durable royalty stream.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
NRP collects royalties on metallurgical and thermal coal mined from its lands (concentrated in Appalachia and the Illinois Basin), plus aggregates and a soda-ash interest via its stake in Sisecam Wyoming. The royalty model means essentially no maintenance capex — operators bear mining cost risk while NRP earns a percentage of revenue. Met-coal pricing post-2021 generated a multi-year cash windfall that management redirected toward debt reduction and warrant/preferred buyouts rather than distributions, transforming the capital structure from leveraged-and-fragile to clean-and-flexible.

The bear case is straightforward: this is coal in secular decline, ESG-screened out of most institutional mandates, and the asset base is depleting with finite mine lives. Met coal pricing is volatile and tied to global steel demand (China property weakness, EU steel weakness). MLP structure carries K-1 friction. Tisch family / Robertson Stephens-era heritage holders may eventually exit. Royalty rates and minimum payments can be renegotiated when leases roll. Environmental and reclamation liabilities — though contractually borne by lessees — can revert to NRP in lessee bankruptcy scenarios (a real risk given coal-operator bankruptcies in the prior cycle).

The bull case: with debt and preferreds substantially gone, distributable cash flow per unit is now uncapped and management has signaled a return to meaningful unitholder distributions. The soda-ash stake provides a non-coal cash stream tied to glass and lithium-battery demand. Aggregates royalties are growing. Insider alignment is strong; the GP has demonstrated discipline. If management commits to a sustained variable distribution policy, the unit should re-rate toward royalty-trust comparables (low-double-digit yield on a cleaner balance sheet). Tail catalyst: a strategic combination with another coal/aggregates royalty vehicle.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K royalty footnotes disclose the per-ton royalty rates and minimum-royalty floors by lessee — minimums create a downside floor most screens miss. The Sisecam Wyoming JV equity-method investment has a separate cash distribution stream that doesn't show up on the consolidated income statement cleanly. Recent 10-Q filings detail the warrant/preferred elimination and the resulting capital-structure simplification, which materially expands distributable cash per common unit. Reclamation surety bonds posted by lessees are disclosed in contingencies and protect NRP. Historical lessee bankruptcies (Foresight, Murray, Blackjewel) and how NRP recovered/redirected those leases are disclosed in MD&A and provide a track record of asset durability.
