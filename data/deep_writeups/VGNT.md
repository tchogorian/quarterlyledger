# {NUMBER}. VGNT — Versigent PLC
## SPINOFF — RECENT | $34.70 / ~$2.46B mkt cap | Score: 78/100

### THE SETUP
On April 1, 2026 — barely 18 days before this writing — Aptiv PLC (APTV) completed the spin-off of its Electrical Distribution Systems (EDS) segment as Versigent PLC, listed on NYSE. Aptiv had been positioning itself as a software-defined-vehicle and active-safety platform; the wiring-harness business, despite being the global #1 in vehicle electrical architecture, was treated as the dumb-pipe legacy stub. So Aptiv kept the high-multiple Signal & Power Solutions / Advanced Safety story, and the lower-multiple wiring business is now a stand-alone $2.46B equity with roughly $2.1B of new debt loaded on at distribution.

The combination — recency, lack of analyst coverage, forced selling by Aptiv-flavored shareholders (AI-vehicle and EV-software growth funds), and the optics of a leveraged industrial — is exactly the Joel Greenblatt template for spin mispricing. CEO Joe Liotine has run the segment since 2024 and is now a public-company CEO with full P&L authority for the first time.

### ANALYST SUMMARY (3p)

**Paragraph 1 — What you actually own.** Versigent makes wiring harnesses, connectors, and electrical-architecture systems for global automakers. It is part of a duopoly/oligopoly with Yazaki and Sumitomo Wiring Systems; together they have entrenched relationships and design-win cycles that span multiple model generations. Wiring is awarded ~3 years before SOP and stays on the platform for the vehicle's life. Engineering centers on four continents and manufacturing in 30+ countries means the moat is logistics + qualification + engineer hours, not glamorous IP. EVs increase wiring complexity (HV harnesses, more sensors, more powertrain wiring) per vehicle — so content per vehicle is structurally rising even if global SAAR doesn't grow.

**Paragraph 2 — Why it's mispriced today.** Three weeks post-spin is peak forced-selling. Index funds that owned APTV had to sell VGNT (it isn't yet in the indices the parent belongs to). ESG-tilted and growth-mandate funds have no interest in a leveraged auto-supplier stub. Quant flows that key off "low coverage + recent IPO/spin + leverage" classify VGNT as untouchable. Meanwhile, the parent's $2.1B debt load — chosen by APTV to lever-up the spin and pay itself a dividend — is scaring quality-focused investors who'd otherwise buy a duopoly business. That debt is real but is matched by EBITDA scale and a global cash-generative business; it isn't a melting ice cube.

**Paragraph 3 — How this resolves.** The standard spin path: forced selling exhausts within ~3 months, sell-side initiates by mid-2026, the first one or two standalone earnings prints establish a baseline, and management uses FCF to pay down the spin debt — each $100M of debt reduction directly accrues to equity holders. If Versigent prints in line with peer auto-supplier multiples (~6-8x EV/EBITDA) once leverage normalizes, the equity has meaningful upside from $34.70. Pattern recognition is doing a lot of work here: this looks like virtually every successful "boring spin" of the past 20 years.
