# {NUMBER}. DBD — Diebold Nixdorf, Incorporated
## POST-BANKRUPTCY | 4x since emergence | Score: 60/100

### THE SETUP
Diebold Nixdorf emerged from a prepack Chapter 11 / Dutch WHOA dual-track on August 11, 2023, with all legacy common cancelled and new equity issued to first-lien creditors. Stock has roughly quadrupled from initial post-emergence trade, included as the proof-of-concept for the post-BK creditor-dump-then-rip pattern.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Diebold Nixdorf is the world's #2 ATM and retail point-of-sale systems incumbent. The company filed Chapter 11 on June 1, 2023, after years of debt-fueled M&A (the legacy Diebold/Wincor Nixdorf merger) overwhelmed an underperforming services and software business. The prepackaged restructuring extinguished old common, issued ~37M new shares to senior secured creditors, and re-emerged on the NYSE on August 14, 2023, with a manageable debt stack and fresh-start accounting. Market cap is roughly $3.1B at $88; cleaned-up cost base, ongoing branch-automation tailwinds in EM banking, and disciplined debt paydown from FCF have driven the rerating.

The bear case is mostly about valuation discipline at this point. The deep-value, forced-creditor-selling phase of the post-BK trade is over; new shareholders are mostly fundamental holders. Cash usage remains in long-term decline in developed markets, so the ATM hardware refresh cycle is not a true growth story. Software/services attach is improving but still trails what a true SaaS-adjacent multiple would justify. At ~$88, the stock is no longer obviously cheap — generalist screens show a recovered industrial. Macro pressure on EM bank capex (where the growth lives) is the fundamental risk.

The bull case is the "second leg" of post-BK rerating: passive index inclusion (Russell, MSCI, S&P SmallCap), continued debt paydown converting EV gradually into equity, and a EU/UK bank IT replacement cycle catching up to North American refresh rates. Software and services backlog conversion is the underrated driver — recurring service contracts attach to most installed ATMs and are higher-margin than hardware. If management hits 2026 FCF guidance, the 4x bag may extend by another 30-50% from index-flow alone.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Fresh-start accounting under ASC 852 reset the goodwill, intangibles, and PP&E values on the post-emergence balance sheet, breaking GAAP comparability with the pre-petition entity. Most automated screens reading "TTM EPS" misread the bridge year. The 8-K and 10-K filed post-emergence detail the new $1.25B credit facility, ~37M new shares outstanding, and the warrants issued to certain creditor classes — these warrants represent latent dilution screens miss. The recurring services backlog disclosed in segment footnotes is the real asset.

### CATALYSTS
- Russell/MSCI passive index inclusion (next reconstitution)
- Continued debt paydown disclosed quarterly
- 2026 EU bank IT refresh cycle commentary
