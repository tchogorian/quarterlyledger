# {NUMBER}. TWNP — Twin Hospitality Group Inc.
## POST-BANKRUPTCY | DISTRESSED EQUITY STUB | Score: 28/100

### THE SETUP

Twin Hospitality Group is the casual-dining spinoff that FAT Brands separated on January 30, 2025, packaging Twin Peaks (the sports-lodge concept) and Smokey Bones (a barbecue chain in conversion to Twin Peaks units) into a standalone publicly traded entity. The spin priced effectively around $17.45 per share. Twelve months later the equity has collapsed roughly 99.5%, and the company filed Chapter 11 in April 2026 under the weight of a heavy debt stack inherited from the spin and deteriorating same-store sales across both brands. Stock now trades around $0.07 with a market cap of ~$4.1M. FAT Brands retained roughly 95% of the equity at the spin, so the publicly traded float is structurally tiny — a fact that drives the price action as much as the fundamentals do. This is a textbook pre-emergence distressed-equity stub: tiny market cap, almost certainly going to be wiped or near-wiped in a Chapter 11 plan, but with a real underlying brand (Twin Peaks has roughly 85 units, established franchise-system economics, and recognizable IP) that has option value if a debtor-in-possession process produces a clean balance sheet and a strategic buyer.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

The honest framing here is that current TWNP equity is overwhelmingly likely to be cancelled or massively diluted in any plan of reorganization. The standard Chapter 11 absolute-priority waterfall — secured lenders, unsecured creditors, then equity — almost never returns par to pre-petition equity in a restaurant restructuring of this leverage profile. What makes the situation marginally interesting rather than uninvestable is three-fold. First, the Twin Peaks brand itself has demonstrable franchise value: roughly 85 units, a recognizable concept, and a franchisee base that prefers continuity to liquidation. A sale of the brand to a strategic franchisor or a private-equity restaurant platform inside the 363 process is a realistic path. Second, the Smokey Bones conversion thesis — taking under-performing BBQ units and reflagging them as Twin Peaks — provides an operational story that a new owner could continue. Third, the FAT Brands residual stake and intercompany history create unusual deal mechanics: FAT itself is a complex levered entity whose strategic preferences will shape the plan. For a distressed-debt fund, the post-emergence equity (likely issued to the existing creditor class) is the more interesting instrument; for a public-equity investor in TWNP today, the pre-emergence stub is essentially a lottery ticket on a deviation from the standard waterfall — possible, but not the way to bet.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

Most screens miss three things on TWNP. First, the float is tiny because FAT Brands retained 95% — published share-count metrics significantly overstate effective trading liquidity, and price moves on micro-volume should not be read as real demand. Second, "market cap" of $4M is essentially a rounding error in any restructuring outcome — the operating debt stack is the real number that determines plan economics, not the equity cap. Third, screens treat the spin date (January 30, 2025) as a "new issuer" data point but miss that the spin transferred a significant debt burden onto a thinly capitalized entity, which is the proximate cause of the bankruptcy. A separate hidden item: the franchise-system contracts and trademarks of Twin Peaks may be ring-fenced from certain creditor claims depending on the corporate structure, which is the kind of detail that determines whether the brand sells whole or in pieces.

- Brand sale or 363 auction announcements
- Treatment of pre-petition equity in the proposed plan
- FAT Brands strategic decision on its residual stake
- Post-emergence equity issuance terms
