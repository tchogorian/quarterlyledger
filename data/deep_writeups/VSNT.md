# {NUMBER}. VSNT — Versant Media Group, Inc.
## SPINOFF (RECENT) | Mkt Cap ~$5.91B | Score: 64/100

### THE SETUP
On January 5, 2026, Comcast (CMCSA) completed the long-telegraphed spin of its cable-network portfolio into a new public company, Versant Media Group. The package handed to Versant shareholders includes USA Network, MSNBC (rebranded "MS NOW"), CNBC, Syfy, E!, Golf Channel, Oxygen and the digital assets Fandango and Rotten Tomatoes. CEO Mark Lazarus — formerly Chairman of NBCUniversal Media Group — runs the new entity. The spin opened down ~14% on day one, the textbook "forced-seller" dynamic of investors who wanted Comcast for broadband, theme parks and Sky and explicitly did not want a basket of declining linear-TV networks. At $40.86 the standalone trades at roughly 5x EBITDA and below 1x revenue despite generating ~$1.4B of net income in 2024 (per separation filings).

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The thesis is the classic Joel Greenblatt setup: an unloved, slow-decline asset spun out of a large parent, with indiscriminate index/ETF selling and zero analyst sponsorship at separation. The bear case is well known — pay-TV subscriber attrition runs roughly 8–10% annually and ad revenue at general-entertainment cable nets is in structural compression (see PARA, WBD multiples). Bulls counter that the assets still throw off >$1B of free cash flow, that more than 80% of revenue today is still profitable, and that the new company starts life with a clean balance sheet, an explicit M&A mandate from Lazarus, and rumored interest in CW, AMC Networks and Scripps assets. At sub-1x sales, Versant is essentially being valued as a runoff entity. Any combination of (a) buyback authorization, (b) a tuck-in roll-up of a competitor's networks at a similarly low multiple, (c) take-private interest from PE (Apollo and others have circled cable networks repeatedly), or (d) sports-rights renewals at NBC Sports/Golf/CNBC creates significant upside optionality with relatively bounded downside given the cash flow profile.

The structural risk is real and not academic — Lazarus has to cut costs faster than affiliate fees roll off, and any miss on the first standalone print will be punished hard. But the tape already reflects most of that pessimism.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Standalone Versant has zero historical trading data, so quant screens, momentum filters and most factor models will simply skip it for 60+ days. Index inclusion (S&P MidCap 400 or Russell 1000) won't happen for at least 1-2 quarters, creating a forced-buyer catalyst later. Sell-side coverage will lag — most analysts who cover Comcast cover the cable-broadband story and don't have content-network analysts on the same desk. Lazarus's stated M&A appetite is not yet in any model. And the Fandango/Rotten Tomatoes digital assets — which are profitable, growing and mostly hidden inside the consolidated narrative — could be carved out or strategically sold for material per-share value.

### CATALYSTS
- First standalone quarterly FCF print (likely May 2026)
- Buyback authorization announcement
- M&A: rumored interest in CW Network, AMC Networks, or E.W. Scripps cable assets
- Take-private interest from PE
- Index inclusion at the 6-12 month mark
- Affiliate-fee renewal cycle disclosures

### MUNGER CHECKLIST
- Understandable business: yes — cable-network bundle economics are well-trodden
- Durable advantage: weak — declining linear ecosystem, but sports/news brands have residual value
- Honest, capable management: Lazarus has decades of NBCU operating credibility
- Margin of safety: yes — ~5x EBITDA, sub-1x sales for >$1B FCF asset
- Reinvestment runway: limited organically, but M&A optionality real
