# {NUMBER}. CXT — Crane NXT, Co.
## SPINOFF | EV/EBITDA ~9x | Score: 71/100

### THE SETUP
Pure-play payment authentication and currency-printing technology spun from Crane Holdings April 2023. Trades at ~13x earnings versus 13-15x for industrial-tech peers despite a quasi-utility US Federal Reserve banknote contract. Generalist orphan creates a quiet compounder mispricing.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Crane NXT was carved out of Crane Holdings on April 4, 2023, separating the legacy Currency / Payment Innovations and Security & Authentication businesses from the diversified industrial parent (now Crane Company). The post-spin entity is a niche oligopoly: it prints US dollar substrate for the Federal Reserve, makes the BPS series of currency-handling machines for central banks and casinos, and runs a fast-growing brand-protection and authentication arm (OpSec/Crane Authentication) used by luxury, pharma, and government customers. Market cap is ~$3.0B at $41 on roughly 57M shares; the most recent quarter showed approximately 19.5% reported revenue growth with a non-GAAP EPS beat.

The bear case: cash usage is structurally declining in developed markets, which compresses the long-tail demand for payment validators in vending, retail, and gaming. The 2026 Federal Reserve print order range of 3.8-5.1 billion notes is wide and politically sensitive — tariff and policy crosscurrents could produce volatile order timing. M&A risk is real (the OpSec acquisition added integration complexity and goodwill). Management is relatively new as a standalone public-company team; capital allocation track record is short. Multiple expansion is not guaranteed if margins compress on mix shift toward lower-margin authentication services.

The bull case: this is an industrial-tech business priced like an industrial commodity. Authentication is a high-margin, growing TAM; currency is a slow-growth utility with extreme barriers to entry (the Fed contract is essentially a duopoly). Balance sheet is clean enough to support tuck-in M&A and buybacks, and the spin orphan effect tends to fade 18-30 months out as dedicated payment-tech analysts pick up coverage. Catalysts include quarterly Fed print order updates, authentication segment growth re-rating, and accretive M&A in security printing.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Form 10 spin filing detailed that Crane NXT inherited the long-running Federal Reserve banknote substrate contract via Crane Currency — a relationship dating to 1879 that no screen captures as moat. Authentication acquisitions (OpSec) brought micro-optic and holographic IP that is functionally irreplaceable on luxury goods packaging. Backlog and recurring service revenue are disclosed only in the segment footnotes; a screener sees "industrial machinery" and assigns a cyclical multiple to what is effectively a regulated quasi-utility plus a high-margin specialty optics business.

### CATALYSTS
- 2026 Federal Reserve print order quarterly updates (Q2 and Q3 2026)
- OpSec/Authentication segment organic growth disclosure (next 10-Q)
- Tuck-in M&A or buyback announcement on next earnings call
