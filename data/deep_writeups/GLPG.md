### THE SETUP
Galapagos trades at roughly $29 against an indicative ~$54/share in net cash and financial investments at year-end 2025 — a negative enterprise value of ~$25/share for what remains of a TYK2 inhibitor in Phase 3, a CD19 CAR-T with FDA RMAT designation, and Gilead collaboration residuals. The market is paying you to take the pipeline; new management has signaled appetite for capital return after abandoning the May 2025 cell-therapy spinoff plan.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Galapagos is a Belgian biotech (Euronext + Nasdaq ADR) that received a $5B equity-and-collaboration infusion from Gilead in 2019 and has since burned a portion of it through filgotinib's commercial disappointment and a costly cell-therapy build-out. As of FY2025 results, cash and financial investments stood near EUR 3.0B (~$54/share at current FX). Headline assets are GLPG3667 (oral TYK2 inhibitor in Phase 3 for SLE and dermatomyositis, with readouts in early-to-mid 2026) and GLPG5101 (CD19 CAR-T with FDA RMAT designation in relapsed/refractory mantle cell lymphoma). Management abandoned the cell-therapy spinoff in May 2025 and is winding down that unit.

The bear case is straightforward biotech entropy: cash burn against an empty commercial pipeline. The TYK2 class faces stiff competition from BMS's Sotyktu (deucravacitinib) and follow-on entrants from Takeda and Nimbus; even a positive Phase 3 in SLE would land into a crowded autoimmune market with delayed reimbursement. Cell-therapy wind-down costs and severance erode the cash pile faster than the published runway suggests. ADR holders bear FX risk on a euro-denominated cash hoard, and Belgian dividend withholding complicates capital return mechanics. Without a credible pipeline win the stock can drift toward a perpetual cash-discount.

The bull case rests on three asymmetric outcomes. First, a positive GLPG3667 Phase 3 readout could revalue the pipeline meaningfully against today's free option. Second, an asset-sale or partnership for the cell-therapy IP (manufacturing platform plus CAR-T data) with a strategic acquirer could crystallize hundreds of millions in residual value. Third — and most undervalued — new management has explicitly signaled openness to capital return; a large tender or special dividend at any price under cash-per-share is mechanically accretive. Gilead's continuing collaboration milestones provide tail upside.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The FY2025 release confirms net cash and financial investments near EUR 3.0B and explicitly references "evaluating capital return options." Screens still treat GLPG as a clinical biotech and apply a discount-to-cash for burn risk; they miss that the cell-therapy wind-down crystallizes an end-state cash balance investors can underwrite. The Gilead 2019 framework agreement also retains residual milestone economics on legacy assets — these line items are not in screener databases. Finally, Belgian "squeeze-out" thresholds (95%) and the family-influenced board composition make a Gilead-led re-take or third-party tender at a modest premium-to-cash a real, under-modeled outcome.

### CATALYSTS
- GLPG3667 Phase 3 SLE/dermatomyositis readouts: H1-mid 2026
- Cell therapy IP/asset sale announcement: 2026 (post wind-down)
- Capital return announcement (tender/special dividend/buyback): 2026
