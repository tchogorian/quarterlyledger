### THE SETUP
Match Group was deleted from the S&P 500 effective March 23, 2026, alongside Molina, Lamb Weston, and Paycom. Passive selling pressure has compressed the multiple to a level where multiple activists (Anson, Starboard) are circling. The technical overhang from forced index supply tends to dissipate within 4–8 weeks of effective date.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Match Group operates the leading global portfolio of dating apps — Tinder, Hinge, Match.com, OkCupid, PlentyOfFish, Meetic, Pairs, and a long tail of demographic-specific brands. The economic engine is Tinder, which throws off the majority of EBITDA, while Hinge has been the growth story for several years (high-teens to 20%+ MAU/payer growth). Total payers stabilized in the high-14M range during 2025, with monetization (RPP) carrying the topline. The company guides ~36% adjusted operating margin and is a meaningful FCF generator (>$800M annualized), most of which has been returned via buybacks under prior CEO Bernard Kim and now CEO Spencer Rascoff. The S&P deletion at the March 23, 2026 reweighting was the proximate cause of the most recent leg down to ~$31, valuing the equity at roughly $7.8B.

The bear case is real and not just sentiment: Tinder's payer base has been declining year-over-year for ten-plus quarters; younger Gen Z cohorts increasingly meet through Snap, Discord, BeReal, and short-video platforms rather than swipe apps; the "dating app fatigue" narrative is mainstream press; and AI-generated profiles plus pay-to-win mechanics have damaged the user experience. Hinge growth, while excellent, is partly a substitution from Tinder, not net-new household demand. App store fee pressure, EU DMA compliance costs, and a string of mid-cap acquisitions that quietly impaired (Hyperconnect) all weigh on the prior compounder narrative.

The bull case rests on Spencer Rascoff (Zillow founder) cleaning house, an explicit activist process from Anson and Starboard pushing for a sum-of-the-parts unlock or PE take-out, and pure technical exhaustion from index deletion. At 9–10x EBITDA on a steady-state Tinder plus a still-growing Hinge, MTCH screens cheaply versus other ad/subscription consumer-internet names. A LBO at $40–45 (the rumored bid range circulating in Q4 2025) is a >25% IRR for a sponsor given the FCF profile.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Match's 10-K segment disclosure breaks the company into "Tinder" and "Hinge" plus an "Evergreen & Emerging" bucket that pools Match.com, Meetic, OkCupid, PlentyOfFish, Pairs, Azar, and Hakuna. This last segment is what makes a sum-of-the-parts case credible: the legacy Match.com / Meetic web brands generate disproportionate cash relative to their growth profile and are precisely the kind of assets a PE buyer would carve out. Filings also disclose roughly $3.9B of long-term debt (mostly fixed-rate notes maturing 2027–2030), giving a sponsor a clean capital-structure path. Most overlooked: the buyback authorization remaining and the post-deletion share-count math — every passive seller that exits at sub-$32 accelerates per-share FCF accretion for the buyback.
