# {NUMBER}. PFE — Pfizer Inc.
## ACTIVIST — OPERATIONAL TURN | $1B Starboard Stake | Score: 52/100

### THE SETUP

In October 2024, Starboard Value (Jeff Smith) disclosed an approximately $1 billion stake in Pfizer — about 0.5% of the $156 billion market cap. The thesis was straightforward: post-COVID Pfizer had overpaid for Seagen ($43B), under-executed on R&D capital allocation, and let its stock price drift from $50+ in 2021 to under $30 by mid-2024. Starboard wanted CEO accountability, board refresh, and clearer portfolio rationalization.

Eighteen months later, the activist campaign has produced limited public wins. Starboard stopped short of nominating directors before the January 25, 2026 nomination deadline for the 2026 annual meeting — a signal that engagement is ongoing but not yet at the proxy-fight stage. The stock at $27.56 sits close to where Starboard built the position, and Pfizer has framed 2026 as a "transition year" with Seagen oncology ramp and loss-of-exclusivity (LOE) management.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

The Pfizer setup is harder than typical Starboard situations. Big pharma is structurally complex — Starboard's bread-and-butter (mid-cap industrial/tech with clear cost-side opportunities) does not translate cleanly to an enterprise where pipeline value, patent cliffs, and oncology launches dominate fundamentals. CEO Albert Bourla has hung onto the COVID-era credibility built by the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine, even as the post-COVID hangover has been brutal: Comirnaty and Paxlovid revenues collapsed faster than Pfizer's capital deployment plans assumed.

The Seagen acquisition is the central question. At $43 billion, Pfizer paid roughly 28x sales for the antibody-drug conjugate (ADC) franchise — a price that requires near-flawless commercial execution to justify. Padcev (urothelial cancer) is the lead asset and has performed in line with deal model, but Tukysa, Adcetris, and the pipeline ADCs need to deliver to make the math work. If Seagen ramps as guided through 2027, Pfizer's earnings power could approach $4.50 EPS, which on a 12x multiple would imply $54 stock — roughly 2x current.

Starboard's leverage is the threat of escalation. Even without nominating directors in 2026, they can revisit at 2027 nominations if engagement stalls, and they can publicly criticize capital allocation. That ongoing pressure has likely contributed to Pfizer's renewed focus on cost discipline — the company announced incremental cost cuts in late 2025 — but the pace is slower than Starboard would prefer.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The Haleon stake is a hidden asset. Pfizer holds roughly 32% of Haleon (consumer health spinout from GSK/Pfizer), worth approximately $13-14 billion at current Haleon prices. This is non-core and is being gradually monetized; full monetization would generate ~$2.30 per Pfizer share in cash. Add Seagen's standalone value at the deal price ($7.50/share equivalent), and the residual "stub" Pfizer pharma business trades at a low single-digit multiple of earnings.

The other invisible factor: obesity pipeline. Pfizer's oral GLP-1 program (danuglipron and successors) had setbacks in 2024-25, but the next-generation program could re-enter the conversation in 2026-27. Even modest success in obesity could re-rate the multiple meaningfully given the category's market cap implications.
