# {NUMBER}. DYNX — Dynamix Corporation
## SPAC LIQUIDATION | $50M Breakup Fee Augments Trust | Score: 73/100

### THE SETUP
Dynamix terminated its proposed merger with Ether Machine and pocketed a $50M breakup fee, augmenting its trust well above the $10.35 stub price. Investors now own a SPAC with extra cash, a fresh November 22, 2026 deadline to find a new combination, and a redemption right at trust value — heads you make a few percent on liquidation, tails you ride a re-targeted deal.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Dynamix is a roughly $60M-market-cap SPAC. Its 8-K disclosed the termination of the previously announced business combination with Ether Machine, paired with a $50M breakup fee paid to Dynamix and an extension of the outside merger date to November 22, 2026. The structural setup is the textbook SPAC arbitrage: trust per share now exceeds the public stub price because the $50M cash inflow is dropped into trust on top of the ~$60M baseline. At $10.35 with a fattened trust, every share gives the holder a redemption put at NAV at the next vote — a near-riskless asymmetry, modulo trust-level expenses.

The bear case is mostly time decay and dilution risk. Sponsor warrants and founder shares dilute downside if a second deal is announced and consummated; SPACs with one failed deal often pivot to lower-quality combinations under deadline pressure, eroding redemption discipline. Even a "good" deal carries tracking risk vs. trust value, and the sponsor's economic incentive is to consummate any deal rather than liquidate. Working capital draw on the trust between now and Nov 22, 2026 is small but not zero, and any extension vote could rebase the timeline (and incremental redemptions could shrink float to uneconomic levels).

The bull case is purely arithmetic: a SPAC trading at or below trust, with extra cash injected from a breakup fee, is one of the cleanest fixed-income-equivalent setups in equity markets. If management finds no deal by Nov 22, 2026, holders redeem at trust value (likely $10.50–$10.85+ per share depending on interest accrual and breakup-fee allocation). If management finds a deal, holders can still redeem at the vote — keeping warrants for free upside. Estimated annualized return to the deadline is in the high single digits to low teens with negligible downside, which is rare in any market regime.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 8-K is the alpha — a market-cap screen still shows ~$60M with a $10.35 close, missing that the trust line has been augmented by the $50M breakup fee. The Ether Machine breakup fee is unusually large for a sub-$100M SPAC and shifts pro-rata trust value materially above stock. Critically, the new 11/22/2026 outside date is not subject to a fresh extension vote (the breakup-fee inflow appears to have funded the extension), so unlike most SPACs there is no near-term shareholder-approval friction or per-share trust drag from extension contributions — a meaningful structural improvement vs. the standard SPAC arbitrage.

### CATALYSTS
- November 22, 2026 — outside merger deadline / liquidation vote
- New business combination announcement (any time before deadline)
- Trust value disclosure in next 10-Q (confirms breakup-fee-augmented per-share trust)
