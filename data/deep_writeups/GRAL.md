### THE SETUP
Grail was spun from Illumina in June 2024 under FTC/EU regulatory pressure with $1B+ in parent-funded cash. Two years in, the Galleri multi-cancer early-detection (MCED) test remains a real $50B+ TAM opportunity, but a February 2026 Phase 3 disappointment punctured the near-term reimbursement narrative. At ~$38 with $1.3B market cap, this is now a balance-sheet-protected option on whether MCED screening reaches Medicare coverage before cash runs out.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Grail develops Galleri, a methylation-based liquid-biopsy test designed to detect signals of more than 50 cancer types from a single blood draw. The company was acquired by Illumina in 2021, then forced to divest after the FTC and EU competition authorities ruled against the deal; spinoff was completed June 25, 2024 with Illumina contributing >$1B at separation to fund 4-5 years of operating burn. Galleri is sold today as a self-pay test (~$949) and through employer/health-system pilots; the long thesis has always been Medicare/CMS coverage following positive pivotal data.

The bear case sharpened materially in February 2026 when Galleri's Phase 3 readout disappointed on key endpoints, undermining the timeline to a positive National Coverage Determination. Without Medicare coverage Galleri remains a self-pay product with limited volume; the cash runway, while real, is finite, and any equity raise at sub-$40 dilutes the optionality holders are paying for. Strategic-acquirer interest cooled after the readout — Roche, Exact Sciences, and large pharma have less reason to pay a premium for an asset whose pivotal trial underperformed. The "spinoff value trap" pattern (forced divestiture, no natural buyer base, cash-burning clinical asset) fits cleanly.

The bull case requires patience and balance-sheet survival. The MCED TAM is genuinely large; even a partially positive subgroup analysis or a CMS coverage decision tied to high-risk populations (heavy smokers, hereditary cancer syndromes) could reignite the equity. Strategic interest may resurface at lower prices — diagnostics M&A historically hunts for pivotal-trial dislocations. Population-health pilots with the UK NHS and large U.S. employers continue to generate real-world data; a follow-up study with revised endpoints could land in 2027. The Illumina-funded balance sheet meaningfully delays the dilution clock.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Form 10 spinoff documents disclose the specific Illumina cash contribution and the transition-services-agreement economics that subsidize Grail's near-term opex — screens treat Grail as a standalone biotech and overstate near-term burn. The February 2026 Phase 3 readout details (subgroup performance, sensitivity by cancer stage) matter enormously for Medicare's framing; a screen captures only the binary "miss." Galleri's reimbursement code structure (CPT vs. PLA codes) and the existing private-payer coverage footprint with select Blue Cross plans are also not screen-visible. Finally, Grail's IP estate around methylation signatures has standalone value to any genomics platform company.

- Follow-on Phase 3 / real-world evidence updates: 2026-2027
