### ANALYST SUMMARY
AlphaTime Acquisition Corp (CIK 0001889106, file 001-41584) is a Cayman Islands SPAC that IPO'd in early 2023 targeting Asian operating businesses. It signed a Business Combination Agreement with HCYC Group Company Limited — a Hong Kong insurance brokerage — at a base $75M equity valuation (7.5M PubCo shares to HCYC holders + up to 1.5M earnout shares contingent on $5M 2024 / $10M 2025 net income). Shareholders approved the merger on December 5, 2025; Form F-4 registration became effective; combined company will list on Nasdaq as "HCYC Holding Company" under ticker HCYC. After redemptions of 6,135 shares ($79,481), the trust held $4,762,484 against 2,545,432 ordinary shares outstanding. Recent extension (March 27, 2026) pushed deadline to Dec 4, 2026 via up to eight one-month $15K extensions.

Bear case: trust per ordinary share is roughly $1.87 — not $10. The $10.40 market price is essentially priced as if liquidation = $10 (the original SPAC convention) when in fact the redemption math has destroyed that floor. The stock spiked to $104 in December 2025 on the merger vote / registration effectiveness — classic SPAC retail melt-up that has since collapsed. HCYC is a small Hong Kong insurance broker, not a hyper-growth business; once the post-deSPAC dust settles, comparable HK insurance brokers trade at 8-15x earnings. With $5-10M earnout-tied net income, fair value of the combined entity is conservatively $50-100M — call it $5-7/share fully diluted. Any further redemptions before close further erode trust per share.

Bull case: the deal IS approved and structurally moving forward. HCYC has Asia growth optionality, and as a deSPAC'd Nasdaq listing it gives Asian investors a US listed wrapper. The earnout structure (1.5M shares at $5M/$10M NI thresholds) provides built-in upside if HCYC delivers. Small float post-close (sub 10M shares) makes it highly volatile, which can cut both ways. If retail interest returns post-close on "Asia insurance plays," the shares can spike again.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Three filing-level facts the SPAC screener misses: (1) Trust per share is $1.87, not $10 — the standard SPAC arb floor doesn't exist here. The $10.40 price is post-merger-approval optimism, not arb floor. (2) The Dec 5, 2025 vote approved the merger; we are in the "wait for closing mechanics" window, not the "deal-or-liquidate" window. The 8-K of April 2, 2026 confirms ongoing extension payments and HCYC progress. Treat ATMC as a deSPAC-pending merger arb with negative-skew risk, NOT a yield-to-trust trade.
