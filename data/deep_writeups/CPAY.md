# {NUMBER}. CPAY — Corpay, Inc.
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | B2B payments rebrand from FleetCor | Score: 70/100

### THE SETUP
FleetCor rebranded to Corpay in 2024 to reflect its pivot away from legacy fleet card economics toward higher-growth corporate payments (cross-border B2B, AP automation, lodging). The market still values it as a card issuer, not a software-led payments platform — creating a structural multiple gap with Wex, Toast, Bill.com, and other "fintech" peers.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Corpay operates three commercial-payments segments: Vehicle Payments (legacy fuel cards under Comdata, Fleetcor brand), Corporate Payments (cross-border FX and AP automation, anchored by the Cambridge Global Payments and AFEX acquisitions), and Lodging Payments (CLC Lodging for project-based crew hotel stays). The company has long been one of the highest-margin operators in payments, with EBITDA margins consistently in the 50%+ range and ROIC well above cost of capital. CEO Ron Clarke has been a serial capital allocator, executing dozens of bolt-on M&A deals while running an aggressive buyback program. The shift toward Corporate Payments (now the fastest-growing segment) reflects a deliberate de-emphasis of fuel card economics that face long-tail EV headwinds.

The bear case centers on three risks. First, regulatory scrutiny: the FTC and state AGs have circled FleetCor for years over fee transparency in fuel card programs, with a 2024 FTC settlement ($35M+) and ongoing class actions. Second, the EV transition will eventually compress fuel card take rates as commercial fleets electrify. Third, FX volatility in cross-border payments can whipsaw quarterly revenue. The Corpay rebrand has not yet translated to a multiple re-rating because Corporate Payments is still under one-third of total revenue. There is also balance-sheet leverage from acquisition spending.

The bull case is that the market is mispricing the segment mix shift. Corporate Payments is growing organically in the high teens, and management has explicitly opened the door to spinning or selling the legacy Vehicle business. Activists Elliott and others have publicly engaged on portfolio simplification. A pure-play Corpay corporate payments business would re-rate to the Bill.com / Toast cohort, while the Vehicle business has strategic value to private equity. Even absent a separation, the buyback program continues to compound per-share earnings at a low-teens rate.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K Vehicle Payments segment buries customer concentration in its largest fleet-card programs, which are partnership/co-branded deals (e.g., with Speedway and Shell) — these have low-single-digit attrition and are far stickier than the fee-transparency lawsuits suggest. The Corporate Payments segment masks the embedded margin profile of the FX trading book; Corpay earns spread on cross-border transactions in addition to platform fees, providing optionality on currency volatility. Capital-light: working capital float ($1B+) earns interest income at higher rates, a quietly powerful tailwind not visible in headline organic growth.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Q1 and Q2 2026 earnings (May and August 2026) — Corporate Payments organic growth disclosure
- Resolution or further development on the FTC/state AG fuel card matters
- Potential Vehicle Payments separation announcement or strategic alternatives review
