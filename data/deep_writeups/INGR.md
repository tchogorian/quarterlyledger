# {NUMBER}. INGR — Ingredion Incorporated
## CYCLICAL TROUGH | RECORD EPS / WEAK FY26 GUIDE | Score: 64/100

### THE SETUP
Ingredion just delivered record 2025 EPS of $11.18 yet the stock trades at a forward P/E in single digits because Q4 missed and the FY26 guide is a low-single-digit grow-over a record year. A cyclical sweetener trough plus an Argo (Indianapolis) corn-wet-mill operational disruption are masking the underlying mix shift to specialty Texture & Healthful Solutions, where management is reinvesting buyback dollars and pricing at a premium.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Ingredion is a $7.2B-revenue ingredient maker that converts corn, tapioca, potato and pulses into starches, sweeteners and specialty proteins for global food, beverage and industrial customers. The 2025 print showed full-year diluted EPS of $11.18 and adjusted diluted EPS of $11.13 with gross margin above 25%, while the company returned $435M to shareholders through dividends and 1.8M-share repurchases. The strategic story is the Texture & Healthful Solutions segment plus LATAM Food & Industrial Ingredients, where pricing power, clean-label trends and protein fortification produce mid-teens operating margins, far above legacy bulk sweetener economics.

The bear sees a tired mid-cap commodity processor: Q4 2025 missed both revenue and EPS estimates, the Argo facility disruption hit the largest US/Canada plant, sweetener demand softened (high-fructose corn syrup is a long-term secular loser to sucralose, stevia and reformulation), and the FY26 guide of $11.00–$11.80 EPS implies essentially flat earnings off a record. Corn-cost volatility, FX headwinds in Pakistan/Argentina/Mexico, and the slow capital-cycle nature of the business cap multiple expansion. Activist screens turn up nothing — no 13D, no proxy fight, no break-up catalyst.

The bull frames it as a defensive, high-yield, undervalued compounder transforming for growth: the specialty mix is now a majority of operating income, capital allocation is disciplined (debt under 2x EBITDA, growing buyback), and FY26 cash from operations is guided at $820M–$940M against a sub-$8B market cap. Catalysts include Argo back to nameplate run-rate, Texture & Healthful Solutions volume acceleration, continued LATAM pricing, and the eventual cyclical re-rate when sweetener pricing turns. Without an activist or break-up, this is a slow-money compounder, not an event-driven trade.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 2026 proxy and 2025 Investor Day re-cut segment reporting around "Texture & Healthful Solutions" — that segment's incremental margins on specialty starches are double what bulk sweeteners earn, but consolidated reporting buries the mix shift. The Argo disruption is operational (not structural), so the FY26 guide bakes in a self-inflicted headwind that should reverse. Buybacks accelerated through 2025 and management hinted at a higher pace once Argo normalizes. There is no activist on the file, but the SOTP — specialty starches/proteins valued at peer multiples vs. legacy sweeteners at 5x EBITDA — implies meaningful upside if a strategic ever pushes for a separation.
