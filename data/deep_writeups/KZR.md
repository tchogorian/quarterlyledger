# {NUMBER}. KZR — Kezar Life Sciences, Inc.
## M&A ARBITRAGE | -5.1% gross spread (CVR play) | Score: 58/100

### THE SETUP
Aurinia Pharmaceuticals announced March 30, 2026 it would acquire Kezar Life Sciences for $6.955 cash + one Contingent Value Right per share (~$51M headline). Kezar trades at $7.33 — 5% above the cash component — meaning the market is implicitly pricing the CVR at ~$0.40. With Tang Capital's 9% stake locked up supportive and the SC TO-T already filed April 13, 2026, this is a pure CVR optionality bet.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Kezar is a clinical-stage biotech that effectively wound down its lupus nephritis (LN) program for zetomipzomib in late 2024 after Phase 2 PALIZADE was halted on safety. After exploring strategic alternatives through 2025, the company landed on a sale to Aurinia (which sells Lupkynis for LN and would gain zetomipzomib as a pipeline option, plus Kezar's residual cash). The deal: $6.955 cash up front + a CVR tied to (1) net proceeds from any disposition of zetomipzomib, (2) Everest Medicines milestone payments, and (3) excess cash above a defined $50M closing threshold. Tender close is targeted for Q2 2026.

The bear case centers on the CVR tail. Zetomipzomib's safety signal makes any out-license a charity case — most acquirers will pay nominal upfront with milestones. Everest milestones depend on regulatory progress in China that may not materialize. And the cash threshold is tight — Kezar's actual closing balance after wind-down costs, severance, D&O tail insurance, and banker fees may dip below $50M, meaning the cash-excess CVR component pays zero. Buying KZR at $7.33 means you've already paid $0.375 above the guaranteed cash for these CVR lottery tickets — a negative arb in the base case if the CVR pays under $0.30.

The bull case is that the CVR has real option value. Zetomipzomib retains a Phase 2 dataset in some indications, Everest paid Kezar $7M up front for China rights and milestones could exceed $200M aggregate, and the wind-down was planned with the cash threshold in mind. If even one CVR component clears, total deliverable easily exceeds $7.30. Tang Capital — sophisticated biotech wind-down specialists — supporting the deal at 9% suggests the CVR was priced reasonably. Annualized economics improve materially if closing is faster than guided.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Read the SC TO-T's CVR Agreement carefully. The $50M cash threshold is calculated net of "permitted expenses" — a defined term that includes deal expenses, severance, tail insurance, accrued litigation reserves, and "wind-down expenses" managed by Aurinia. That gives the buyer significant discretion to depress the cash component of the CVR. Tang Capital's tender support agreement (likely pegged to the $6.955 + CVR package) is the clearest signal: Kevin Tang specializes in late-stage biotech wind-downs and reportedly invested in Kezar specifically for this kind of structured exit. The CVR's expiration date and Aurinia's obligation to "use commercially reasonable efforts" on zetomipzomib disposition are the two material legal provisions to confirm.
