### ANALYST SUMMARY
Duke is a $93B-market-cap regulated electric and gas utility serving the Carolinas, Florida, Indiana, Ohio, and Kentucky. The CONVERTIBLE MATURITY angle is mechanical: $1.725B 4.125% notes mature April 15, 2026; in March 2026 Duke issued $1B in new convertibles to partially refinance, with the balance covered by senior unsecured debt and revolver capacity. Convertible refinancings of this size temporarily depress the stock as arbitrage desks delta-hedge the new issue (short equity to neutralize convexity) — that pressure has been the main reason DUK has lagged the broader utility complex over the past 60 days despite supportive fundamentals.

The bear case is straightforward. Duke trades at ~19x forward earnings, a premium to peers like Southern Co. and Dominion, and at $120 the dividend yield is ~3.5%, below the 10-year Treasury. Regulatory risk is real: the North Carolina Utilities Commission's pending decisions on multi-year rate plans, and the Florida Public Service Commission's storm-cost recovery, are both upside skewed but unpredictable. Capex obligations have grown to ~$73B over five years, and equity issuance to fund the build is dilutive at current multiples. AI/data-center load growth narratives are widely held — any disappointment in actual MW additions in 2026/27 would deflate the entire IPP-tinged utility trade.

The bull case rests on three legs: (1) the April 15, 2026 maturity completes and short-vol/arb pressure unwinds, releasing 30–50bps of artificial discount; (2) Duke is one of the top three named beneficiaries of Carolina/Indiana hyperscaler data center colocation, with management guiding ~9–10% load growth in served data-center subsegments; (3) constructive regulatory outcomes in NC and FL through 2026 lift the allowed ROE base. Combined, a 5–8% total return (dividend + multiple normalization) over the next four months, with downside cushioned by the bond-proxy nature of the equity.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A momentum screen sees DUK as a sleepy utility — it cannot detect the technical convertible-arb overhang. The new $1B March 2026 convertible's delta-hedge dynamics matter materially: convertible arb desks routinely short ~70–85% of the underlying delta to neutralize, and on a $1B issue with a ~30 delta that is roughly $200–300M of incremental short pressure that unwinds as the new bond seasons. Combined with the April 15 maturity unwind on the existing 4.125% bonds (where the embedded equity option is deep ITM and arbs are long-stock/short-bond), the cleanup creates a clean April–May technical reset window invisible to fundamental screens.
