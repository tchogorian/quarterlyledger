# {NUMBER}. Q — Qnity Electronics, Inc.
## SPINOFF | DUPONT ELECTRONICS CARVE-OUT | Score: 74/100

### THE SETUP

Qnity Electronics is the November 1, 2025 spin-off of DuPont de Nemours' electronics and semiconductor materials business — a pure-play "picks-and-shovels" supplier to the AI accelerator and advanced-node semiconductor build-out. Trading near $136 with market cap ~$28.6B, Qnity is the higher-multiple, faster-growing stub that DuPont separated from its slower industrial chemicals parent. The classic spin-off setup: generalist DuPont holders received Q shares and many sold (because Q didn't fit their mandate or because of index-rebalancing flow), creating short-term price pressure on a structurally superior business.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

The Qnity portfolio is concentrated in two adjacent franchises: Semiconductor Technologies (consumables, advanced lithography materials, CMP slurries, photoresists, dielectrics) and Interconnect Solutions (thermal management, EMI shielding, advanced packaging materials). Both directly serve the leading-edge logic, memory, and packaging nodes — meaning Qnity sells consumables that get used every time TSMC, Samsung, Intel, SK Hynix, or Micron run a wafer or build an advanced package. Margins are correspondingly attractive: 25%+ EBITDA margin profile with mid-teens revenue growth tied to AI accelerator and advanced-node CapEx cycles.

The peer set tells the valuation story. Entegris (ENTG), CMC Materials (CCMP, now part of ENTG), and Cabot (CBT) — comparable semiconductor materials franchises — trade at 15–20x EBITDA and 25–30x earnings. Qnity at $136 still trades closer to legacy DuPont specialty chemical multiples than to pure-play semi materials peers. Oppenheimer initiated at Outperform with a $100 price target (note: the spin opened higher than expected and the stock has rallied — the relative valuation framework still implies upside as Qnity reports standalone earnings and the market re-rates it to peer multiples).

The structural thesis: spin-offs of high-growth divisions from large industrial parents have a long history of multi-year outperformance, particularly when (a) the spin-co has materially better growth and margins than the parent, (b) initial selling pressure from index-mismatched holders creates a discount, and (c) the spin-co has clean balance sheet and standalone management incentives. Qnity ticks all three boxes — and benefits from the additional tailwind of being directly levered to the AI and advanced-node investment supercycle that is the dominant capex theme of 2024–2026.

S&P 500 inclusion has already been announced, which creates a forced-buying catalyst as index funds rebalance. First standalone earnings will be the next major print — and a clean Q4 2025 with raised 2026 guidance could be the catalyst for a meaningful re-rating toward peer multiples.

The risk: semiconductor materials demand is correlated with leading-edge wafer-start cycles. A pause in AI capex (e.g., Nvidia order air-pocket, hyperscaler digestion) would compress growth. The spin valuation already prices in significant optimism — Qnity is no longer a "neglected stub" at $136. Pricing has moved well past the initial spin-distribution discount, so the easy money has likely already been made.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

A screen sees a recently-listed materials company with limited financial history. It does not see: the spin-off mechanics that created initial price pressure; the peer-multiple gap to ENTG/CBT; the AI/advanced-node leverage embedded in the consumables franchise; the imminent S&P 500 forced inclusion; or the clean balance sheet and standalone capital allocation flexibility. Most generalist screens still classify Qnity under chemicals rather than semiconductor capital equipment / materials — the wrong peer set.
