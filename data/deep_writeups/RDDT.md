# {NUMBER}. RDDT — Reddit, Inc.
## POST-LOCKUP | $28B Mcap / Secondary Supply | Score: 62/100
### THE SETUP
Reddit IPO'd in March 2024 at $34/share. The standard 180-day lockup was actually structured with an early-release mechanic that allowed certain holders to sell post Q2 2024 earnings — meaning the bulk of insider supply hit the tape by September 2024 rather than later in the year. Eighteen months on from IPO, the equity is at $158, having round-tripped from $230+ peaks earlier in 2025 as advertising guidance moderated and ongoing employee/VC distributions kept secondary supply flowing into the market. The setup here isn't the lockup expiry itself (long since passed) but rather the residual post-lockup tape dynamics: heavy insider sale days continue to create air pockets, and a structurally over-supplied float intersects with a fundamental advertising and AI-licensing inflection that the market is still re-rating. With $28B market cap and an active Q1 2026 print due late April, RDDT sits at a junction between dislocation lows and the next earnings catalyst.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The Reddit thesis post-lockup is not a deep-value setup — it is a quality-platform-at-fair-price story complicated by persistent secondary supply. The fundamental tape is real: advertising revenue has been growing 60%+ year-over-year through most of 2025, the AI/search licensing deals (Google, OpenAI) are cementing Reddit's role as the premier human-conversation training and citation source, and operating leverage is significant given the platform's relatively flat cost base. The "DAUq" (daily active uniques) and ARPU expansion math has begun to work as advertisers absorb that Reddit is the third-largest unique audience in the US that they can't reach anywhere else.

The bear case is cleanly structural rather than fundamental. Insiders and VCs continue to distribute, with multiple secondary blocks pricing through 2025; passive index demand has absorbed most of it, but on heavy days the tape gaps lower. The stock's trajectory from $230 to $158 reflects de-rating on multiple expansion rather than fundamental deterioration — and at current levels, RDDT trades at a still-rich ~12-14x forward sales, leaving little room for advertising softness or AI license deal slippage.

The honest analyst view: Reddit is a real business with a real moat, but the valuation reflects most of the obvious bull case. The post-lockup thesis really only works on dislocation days when forced selling produces sub-$140 prints, at which point the AI-data-monetization optionality starts looking asymmetric. Q1 2026 earnings late April will be the key tell — advertising growth above 50% with continued ARPU expansion would re-confirm the thesis; deceleration would extend the de-rating.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A screen sees a high-multiple, recently IPO'd consumer internet name and either flags it as expensive or as growth-quality. What it misses: (1) the AI/search licensing line is structurally accretive at near-100% incremental margins and is barely two years into monetization; (2) Reddit's data is uniquely valuable for LLM grounding because of its conversational, human-attributed format — this is not commodity training data; (3) post-lockup secondary-supply dynamics produce repeatable dislocation lows that don't show up in volatility metrics; and (4) Reddit's international advertising business is barely monetized — US ARPU is multiples of international, providing a multi-year geographic expansion runway.

### CATALYSTS
- **Late April 2026 — Q1 2026 earnings:** Advertising growth and AI licensing line item visibility.
- **2H 2026 — additional AI data licensing deals:** Anthropic, Meta, or sovereign LLM providers as incremental contracts.
- **Q3 2026 — international ARPU breakout:** First quarter where international ad revenue grows materially faster than US.
