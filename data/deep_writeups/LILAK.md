### ANALYST SUMMARY
Liberty Latin America is a multi-country Caribbean and Latam telecom holdco assembled by John Malone's Liberty empire. The asset base spans Cable & Wireless Caribbean (mobile + B2B in 24 markets), C&W Panama (mobile leader), VTR Chile (cable, JV with America Movil), Liberty Puerto Rico (post-Sprint integration), Cabletica (Costa Rica), and a small Liberty Networks subsea fiber JV. The Class C shares (LILAK) are non-voting and trade at the deepest discount in the complex. Market cap ~$3.48B vs management/sell-side indicative SOTP near $16 per share — a 45% discount.

The bear case is real: hurricane risk concentrates physical assets in storm corridors; Puerto Rico has been a multi-year integration drag; Chile is a hyper-competitive cable/wireless market with VTR losing share; segment-by-segment leverage is high; and Liberty trackers historically trade at structural discounts that rarely close cleanly. Reported numbers are a tangle of multi-currency, multi-segment, JV-accounted entities — most generalist investors simply skip it. Refinancing cliffs in 2027-2028 are a real concern if rates stay elevated.

The bull case rests on three things converging in 2026: (1) Puerto Rico free cash flow ramp now that the Sprint customer migration is essentially done and competitor WOM filed bankruptcy; (2) management explicitly stating they are "focused on unlocking the SOTP discount" — translation: they will sell Panama, Chile, or PR if the price is right; (3) buybacks at 45% NAV discount are extraordinarily accretive. Q3 2025 rebased Adj OIBDA +7% confirms the inflection.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
LILAK is not one company — it's six businesses in different currencies with different leverage profiles, and equity screens treat the consolidated number as one thing. The Panama segment alone, on a stand-alone EV/EBITDA basis at regional comp multiples (~6-7x), is worth more than LILAK's entire current market cap. The C&W Caribbean segment trades inside the holdco at an implied 3x EBITDA — distressed multiples for a #1 mobile operator across 24 island markets with no real competition in most of them. The Chile JV with America Movil is on Liberty's balance sheet at zero economic profit but has an obvious exit ramp (sell to Movil). None of this shows up in P/E or EV/EBITDA screens because the consolidated leverage masks segment quality.
