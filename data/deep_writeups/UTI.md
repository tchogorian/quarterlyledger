# {NUMBER}. UTI — Universal Technical Institute, Inc.
## ACTIVIST | Coliseum 7.3% / insider activist | Score: 74/100

### THE SETUP
Universal Technical Institute is a for-profit post-secondary trade school operator focused on transportation (auto, diesel, motorcycle, marine), skilled trades, and — since the 2022 acquisition of MIAT and the 2024 acquisition of Concorde Career Colleges — healthcare. The company runs roughly 15 campuses and serves about 25,000 students. On December 8, 2025, Coliseum Capital Management (Christopher Shackelton) filed an SC 13D disclosing a 7.3% stake. The unusual element: Shackelton already serves as Chairman of UTI's board. The "activist" filing is more accurately a conviction-buying disclosure by a board insider who chose to publicly mark his accumulation. Shackelton bought additional shares December 1-5, 2025 at $23.66-$25.38; the stock has since rerated to $38.25 — market cap roughly $2.1 billion.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The thesis sits at the intersection of two macro tailwinds and one company-specific transformation. Macro: the chronic skilled-trades shortage in the U.S. is structural — boomers retiring out of HVAC, diesel mechanics, line work — while AI-driven white-collar disruption is making vocational training more attractive on a relative basis. UTI's enrollment has grown for nine consecutive quarters. Company-specific: the "North Star" strategy launched in 2022 pivoted UTI from a pure transportation play to a diversified vocational platform, and the Concorde acquisition added healthcare tuition revenue with materially better unit economics than the legacy mechanical programs. The October 2025 announcement of a Dallas campus expansion confirmed management is leaning into capacity additions in metros with verified demand.

The valuation case rests on multiple expansion. UTI trades around 12x forward EBITDA versus the for-profit education comp set (Strategic Education, Adtalem, Grand Canyon) at 14-16x. Closing the discount alone implies a 20-30% rerate. Add Concorde synergy realization and continued enrollment growth, and a two-year double from current levels is plausible — though the easy money has been made; the stock is up roughly 60% from Coliseum's December 2025 buys.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
For-profit education carries political risk that screens cannot price. The Department of Education's gainful-employment rule, Title IV funding eligibility, and 90/10 rule changes have repeatedly compressed the entire sector's multiples. UTI has run cleanly under multiple administrations and its outcome metrics are strong, but a future regulatory hostility cycle would be the primary downside. Second, the Coliseum-as-Chairman dynamic deserves attention: it eliminates the activist-vs-board friction that normally creates the catalyst, but it also means there is no external pressure mechanism if execution slips. Insider-activist alignment is bullish for governance but reduces the optionality on a sale process.

### CATALYSTS
- Q2/Q3 fiscal 2026 enrollment data (UTI fiscal year ends September)
- Concorde integration milestone disclosures and synergy capture
- Tuition pricing actions in fiscal 2026
- Additional M&A — vocational sector remains fragmented
- Potential capital return (buyback or dividend initiation given ~$200M cash position)

### MUNGER CHECKLIST
- Simple business: Yes — sell a 12-18 month vocational credential, collect tuition (mostly Title IV funded)
- Pricing power: Yes, modest — graduate placement rates allow above-inflation tuition increases
- Honest, capable management: Insider-activist Chairman aligned with shareholders, Q1 enrollment beats credible
- Reasonable price: At $38.25 versus Coliseum's $24 cost basis, the valuation has compressed sharply; sector-relative discount remains
- Within circle of competence: For-profit education is a regulated sector requiring policy literacy
- Margin of safety: Reduced from December 2025 levels — at 12x EBITDA the discount to peers is the cushion, not absolute valuation

A late-cycle entry into a thesis that has worked. The Coliseum 13D was the buy signal at $24; at $38 the question is whether enrollment growth and Concorde synergies justify multiple expansion to peer levels. The tailwinds are real, the management is aligned, and the political risk is the unmodeled tail. A starter position with adds on regulatory-fear pullbacks is the disciplined approach.
