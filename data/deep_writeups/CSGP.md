### THE SETUP
CoStar trades at $39.72, less than half its 2024 peak near $90, after Homes.com's roughly $3B cumulative spend has produced only ~$80M of annual revenue. D.E. ### ANALYST SUMMARY
CoStar is the dominant subscription data and analytics franchise for U.S. commercial real estate (CoStar Suite, LoopNet, Apartments.com), with high-90s gross retention and SaaS-like economics in its core. Screen catches it because the stock collapsed roughly 55% from peak while the underlying CRE data business kept growing high-teens. Market cap is $16.8B, EV is similar net of cash and debt, and the screen flags the activist filing on Feb 4, 2026 by D.E. Shaw with a ~1.5% economic stake plus an open letter and presentation. Third Point also engaged but exited April 10, 2026 saying CoStar had moved enough.

Bear case from filings: Homes.com is a multi-year, multi-billion-dollar capital allocation experiment competing with Zillow and Realtor.com that is still cash-burning at scale, and CEO Andy Florance has resisted hard limits. Core CRE growth has decelerated, the Matterport acquisition diluted margins, and SG&A has ballooned. If Homes.com cannot reach contribution-margin breakeven, it is a permanent drag and management credibility takes another hit. Founder-CEO control and a staggered defense playbook can blunt the activist outcome.

Bull case: D.E. Shaw is anchoring on $300M+ of immediate cost takeout plus a credible monetization or wind-down path on Homes.com; even modest discipline plus mid-teens core CRE growth supports a re-rating. The board added two D.E. Shaw / Third Point names plus a third independent, named a new chair, and stood up a Capital Allocation Committee — all classic pre-vote concessions. If June vote pressure forces CEO transition or a take-private discussion, the asymmetry from $40 is meaningful given peers trade at 8–12x revenue versus CSGP near 4x.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The activist letter and presentation lay out segment-level Homes.com unit economics that the 10-K rolls up into "Residential" — D.E. Shaw's deck pegs cumulative Homes.com investment at over $3B against ~$80M revenue, a roughly 38x lifetime spend-to-ARR ratio that no SaaS investor would tolerate. Filings also reveal Florance's voting control via concentrated insider shares is not majority, meaning a real proxy outcome is mathematically possible. The Capital Allocation Committee charter is the under-the-radar concession: it institutionalizes a board check on Homes.com burn that did not exist before February 2026.
