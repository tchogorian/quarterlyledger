### THE SETUP
The first leg of the GE breakup, spun January 2023, still trades at a meaningful multiple discount to medical-device peers (SYK, MDT, PHG) despite #1 share in MR/CT imaging, a $19B+ backlog, and the highest-margin recurring contrast-agent franchise (PDx) in the industry. Indiscriminate post-spin selling pressure has compressed but not closed; standalone management is delivering the margin expansion they promised at spin.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
GE HealthCare (Nasdaq: GEHC) was spun from General Electric on January 4, 2023 as the first major step in the GE three-way breakup. The company is the global leader in MR and CT imaging, a top-three player in ultrasound and patient monitoring, and operates the high-margin PDx (Pharmaceutical Diagnostics) contrast-agent business — a quasi-consumable franchise tied to imaging procedure volumes. Three years post-spin, GEHC has delivered consistent organic growth, expanding standalone margins, growing its services backlog, and rolling out AI-enabled imaging products that drive both share gains and software-style attach economics. Yet the multiple gap to medtech peers persists.

The bear case rests on three concerns: hospital capex sentiment, China exposure, and the dilutive M&A risk inherent to a newly independent platform. Hospital systems have alternated between capex pauses and catch-up spend cycles since COVID, and large imaging equipment is a deferrable purchase. China — historically a 10%+ exposure for GE imaging — has been pressured by anti-corruption probes and local-procurement preferences. And spin-off companies inevitably face M&A pressure as standalone management seeks to fill out portfolio gaps; GEHC's recent bolt-ons in software and AI imaging have been digestible but the temptation to do something larger remains. Finally, "spin-off discount closes" thesis has been live for three years already — at some point that thesis is just a narrative.

The bull case is improving operating leverage on a backlog-locked revenue stream. Backlog of more than $19B with improving book-to-bill provides multi-year revenue visibility. AI-enabled imaging is a real share-gain story. PDx is structurally high-margin and recurring. Standalone management has consistently delivered on margin guidance. At a discount to SYK/MDT/PHG despite arguably superior franchise positioning in imaging and contrast media, the re-rating math is mechanical: even partial multiple compression closure against peers is meaningful upside, and underlying earnings are growing in the high-single digits. The capital-allocation playbook — modest buybacks plus tuck-in software M&A — is shareholder-friendly.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K's segment commentary on PDx is the hidden gem: contrast-agent sales scale roughly with imaging procedure volumes globally, generating recurring high-margin revenue that screens conflate with cyclical equipment sales. Also read the backlog disclosure carefully — services backlog (multi-year imaging service contracts) carries margin profiles much closer to software than to capital equipment, and continues to grow. The China segment commentary is critical: GEHC has been actively re-shoring/diversifying production and the China revenue line has stabilized. Finally, the spin tax separation agreement with GE / GE Aerospace / GE Vernova has tax-efficiency implications that linger and matter for any cross-spin transactions.

### CATALYSTS
- Quarterly book-to-bill and backlog updates
- AI-imaging product launches (theranostics, AI Reading)
- 2026 margin-expansion guide vs. delivery
- Potential bolt-on M&A in imaging software
- Continued China stabilization commentary
