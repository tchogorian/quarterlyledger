### THE SETUP
Mubadala Capital + TWG Global agreed Feb 9, 2026 to take Clear Channel Outdoor private at $2.43/share all-cash ($6.2B EV, 71% premium to unaffected). Stock at $2.38 = 2.1% gross spread / ~5% annualized to expected close. The only material gating item is CFIUS for the UAE acquirer.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Clear Channel Outdoor is a global out-of-home advertising operator with U.S. (Americas + Airports) and historically European footprints. The Feb 9, 2026 transaction values CCO at $6.2B EV with shareholders receiving $2.43/share cash. Equity from Mubadala/TWG; preferred from Apollo; debt led by JPMorgan + Apollo. Go-shop expired late March 2026 with no superior bid (per InsideArbitrage), narrowing risk to regulatory closure. Expected close window: late 2026 to early 2027 pending CFIUS, FCC license transfers, and stockholder vote. Spread at $2.38 = ~2.1% gross / mid-single-digit annualized — typical for a deal with a foreign-buyer/CFIUS gating factor.

The bear case turns on three risks. First, CFIUS: Mubadala is an Abu Dhabi sovereign wealth fund and CCO owns U.S. airport outdoor advertising assets — those airport contracts touch passenger movement data and are physical infrastructure inside secured federal facilities. CFIUS could impose mitigation (data security, U.S.-citizen-only board members) or, in a worst case, block. Second, financing: Apollo preferred + bank debt is committed but conditioned on no MAC; any 2026 advertising recession (CCO is highly cyclical) gives lenders an exit ramp. Third, the stock is sub-$3 and trades wide bid/ask — execution friction is meaningful, and a deal break sends shares back to ~$1.40 unaffected (-40%).

The bull case is short-dated arbitrage with limited downside catalysts already removed. Go-shop expired without a topping bid — eliminating one source of uncertainty (and confirming the strike price is fair). The 71% premium provides political cover for board approval and reduces stockholder-vote risk to near-zero. Mubadala has prior CFIUS experience and would have pre-cleared the deal structure with advisors before signing. Annualized 5% spread on a deal with a defined close is acceptable for a special-sit allocator alongside other small-cap merger arb. The fact that no shareholder activist or Delaware appraisal action has materialized since Feb 9 is a positive tell.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The DEFM14A merger proxy (filed by CCO post-announcement) discloses the "fair value" board projections that supported the $2.43 price — typically containing a base case AOI/EBITDA forecast, plus management's standalone DCF which would imply >$3+ in success scenarios. This becomes material in the rare event of a Delaware appraisal challenge. Additionally, the credit agreements at the holdco and OpCo level have change-of-control puts that must be tendered or refinanced — buyer financing structure addresses this, but the timing of bond refi is a closing condition. Watch for CCO's Q1 2026 earnings (any meaningful AOI miss strengthens the buyer's MAC argument) and FCC filings for license transfer at airport assets.
