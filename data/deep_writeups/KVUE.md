### THE SETUP
Starboard's Jeff Smith joined the Kenvue board in March 2025; eight months later, Kimberly-Clark agreed to acquire the consumer-health spinoff for ~$48.7B EV ($3.50 cash + 0.14625 KMB shares per Kenvue share). With shareholders already approved (Jan 29, 2026) and HSR cleared, the 19% spread is essentially priced regulatory wait + KMB stock decline — a true arb dressed as a defensive consumer name.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Kenvue is the J&J consumer-health spinoff (Tylenol, Listerine, Band-Aid, Neutrogena, Aveeno) that came public in May 2023. The business is high-quality but post-spin operating execution disappointed: market share losses in skin care, Tylenol category overhang from acetaminophen-autism press cycles, and a sluggish allergy season repeatedly missed Street numbers. Starboard built its position in late 2024, secured a board seat in March 2025, and pushed for a strategic alternatives review that culminated in the November 3, 2025 announcement of a sale to Kimberly-Clark at an implied ~$21/share. Shares closed at $17.61 on report date, leaving a 19.3% gross spread.

The bear case is collar/regulatory: the deal is a mixed cash-stock structure, so KMB's own equity drift drives implied value. KMB stock fell after announcement on integration skepticism (32% pro-forma leverage, $50B asset combination, channel concentration), and any further KMB weakness shrinks the math directly. EU/UK regulators are still working through filings on a global consumer-health combination, and a remedy package or extended timeline could push close into late Q4 2026. Talc litigation overhang technically sits with J&J under indemnity but headline risk lingers, and a deal break — however unlikely — would expose Kenvue to a re-rate toward standalone $14-15.

The bull case is that all the gating items are de-risked: shareholders voted yes, US antitrust cleared, no horizontal product overlap, and Kimberly-Clark's $4B+ synergy story gives the buyer maximum incentive to close. Annualized 51% spread over an ~9 month window is wide for a strategic buyer with cleared US antitrust. Even if KMB stock drops another 10%, breakeven is still positive at current Kenvue prices.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The merger agreement contains a fixed exchange ratio (0.14625 KMB) with no collar — meaning Kenvue holders bear 100% of KMB equity drift between signing and close. A pairs trade (long KVUE / short 0.14625 KMB per share) isolates the pure spread/timing arb at roughly the same 19% gross. Also buried: the $1.65B termination fee payable by KMB in an antitrust break, which functions as a floor on Kenvue downside in the antitrust-fail scenario. J&J's talc indemnity (carved out at separation) means the litigation headlines that drag KVUE in retail flow have effectively zero economic impact at Kenvue level.
