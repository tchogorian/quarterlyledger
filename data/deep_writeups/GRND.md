# {NUMBER}. GRND — Grindr Inc.
## POST-LOCKUP | SPAC de-merger 2022, founder + sponsor still control | Score: 60/100

### THE SETUP
Grindr came public via SPAC (Tiga Acquisition Corp) in November 2022 at a fully-diluted enterprise valuation that has since been validated by genuine subscriber growth and free cash flow. The float remains thin and dominated by the original San Vicente Acquisition consortium plus a handful of pre-merger holders, creating recurring secondary-offering and lock-up-expiry overhangs that keep institutional ownership low relative to the underlying earnings power.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Grindr is the largest geosocial network targeted at gay, bi, trans, and queer users, with a paying subscriber base monetized via Grindr XTRA, Unlimited, and ad revenue. Operating leverage is the central story: the platform requires modest engineering and content moderation spend relative to revenue, and ARPU expansion via subscription-tier reshuffling has outpaced sub-count growth. The company has guided to material EBITDA margin expansion and continues to invest in product (AI assistants, travel feature) without breaking the cost discipline established post-SPAC.

The bear case starts with the controlled-company structure. San Vicente Acquisition (the consortium that bought Grindr from Kunlun in 2020) and pre-merger PIPE investors collectively retain a controlling block; secondary offerings have repeatedly been used to monetize, and each one creates an air pocket. Regulatory and reputational risk is real — privacy and HIV-status data exposures have driven prior FTC scrutiny; CFIUS and overseas regulators continue to examine Chinese-origin user-data legacy issues. Competition from Hinge, Sniffies, and free alternatives compresses ARPU expansion long-term. The stock has already re-rated from sub-$10 to current levels, narrowing the margin of safety.

The bull case is sustained operating leverage plus capital return. Grindr generates substantial free cash flow on a small fixed-cost base; debt has been paid down and the company has authorized buybacks. Subscriber penetration of the addressable user base remains low — high-single-digit percent — leaving runway for ARPU and conversion gains. Strategic value to a larger dating-app or entertainment platform (Match, Bumble, an ad-tech consolidator) is non-trivial; the controlled structure also opens the door to a take-private at a premium if the public-market discount widens. AI-driven product features could re-accelerate engagement.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K and recent secondary prospectuses disclose the specific San Vicente consortium ownership block and the lock-up release schedule — most screens still tag Grindr as broadly held when the public float is materially smaller than reported share count suggests. The company also carries a tax asset and capitalized software basis from the SPAC merger that distorts GAAP earnings versus economic FCF. Subscriber cohort retention data (disclosed only in investor-day decks, not 10-K) shows long-tail LTV materially higher than peer dating apps, which is invisible to a P/E or P/S screen. Finally, the controlled-company election allows governance practices that an institutional screener would penalize but which create take-private optionality.

### CATALYSTS
- Next secondary offering / lock-up release event (recurring overhang)
- Q1 and Q2 2026 earnings: subscription growth and ARPU update
- Strategic M&A or take-private discussion: speculative, unscheduled
