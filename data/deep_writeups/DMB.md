Saba Capital filed a 13D/A on December 30, 2025, disclosing 1,546,121 shares (8.4%) of BNY Mellon Muni Infra CEF, signaling intent to engage on board nominees and a value-enhancing merger. Fund trades at a 9.3% discount to NAV; part of Saba's coordinated BNY muni cluster campaign.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
DMB is a closed-end municipal bond fund focused on infrastructure-related muni issues, managed by BNY Mellon. Net asset value is approximately $12.10 per share against a market price of $10.98, a 9.3% discount that has persisted as muni CEFs have underperformed open-end alternatives in the rising-rate cycle. The fund holds ~$200M of investment-grade infrastructure muni paper. Saba Capital, run by Boaz Weinstein, has been the most active CEF activist of the past decade, repeatedly forcing tender offers, board changes, and fund mergers across the BNY Mellon, Nuveen, and BlackRock muni complexes.

The bear case is that muni CEFs are a notoriously slow activist battleground. Boards routinely adopt poison pills, classified board structures, and majority-of-the-minority tender provisions that blunt activist economics. Saba's 8.4% stake is meaningful but well short of the threshold to win a contested vote without significant retail support. The fund's underlying muni portfolio is also rate-sensitive — if long rates rise, NAV erosion can match or exceed any discount narrowing, leaving the activist trade flat in absolute terms. Tax-exempt income holders may also resist a merger that disrupts a stable distribution stream.

The bull case is pattern recognition: Saba has won enough of these campaigns (BlackRock muni cluster 2024, Nuveen muni cluster 2023) that boards now negotiate earlier. A fund-merger outcome typically narrows the discount by 400-700 bps within 6-12 months of resolution, providing a high-probability mid-teens total return when combined with the underlying ~5% tax-equivalent yield. The 2026 annual meeting is the key forcing function. A coordinated cluster campaign across multiple BNY muni CEFs increases the leverage on the BNY board.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Fund proxy filings disclose the byzantine governance terms muni CEF boards routinely use — Maryland law allows for control-share statutes that disenfranchise large holders above 10%, which is precisely why Saba sits at 8.4%. The N-CSR semi-annual reports show portfolio duration, leverage ratio (typically 30-40% via tender option bonds), and unrealized loss carry-forwards that materially affect any liquidation math. Screeners see "muni fund, 9% discount" and miss the activist mechanic entirely.
