# {NUMBER}. ROP — Roper Technologies, Inc.
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | $7.9B 2025 revenue, 56.7% Application Software | Score: 78/100
### THE SETUP
Roper Technologies is the quietest of the great American compounders — a vertical-software roll-up with three reportable segments (Application Software, Network Software, Technology Enabled Products) that has spent two decades migrating from industrial niches into mission-critical SaaS. 2025 revenue was $7,902.5M, up 12.3% (5% organic, 7% from acquisitions). Application Software alone was $4,483.0M (56.7% of total) and grew 16% (10% acquired, 5% organic). The 2025 capital deployment: $3.3B into vertical-software bolt-ons including the ~$1.85B CentralReach acquisition and Subsplash. The portfolio is the moat — Aderant (legal), CentralReach (ABA therapy), Clinisys (lab informatics), Data Innovations (lab middleware), Deltek (project ERP), Frontline (K-12 admin), PowerPlan (utility tax), Procare (childcare), Strata (healthcare planning), Transact/CBORD (campus payments), Vertafore (insurance agency). Each business is a small-vertical monopoly with high gross retention and limited cyclicality. The "hidden" qualifier is more about audience than obscurity: ROP is well-followed but rarely held by quality-compounder skeptics who dismiss it as "Constellation Software with American multiples."

### ANALYST SUMMARY
The Roper machine is simple to describe and hard to copy. Identify a small or mid-cap software business serving a narrow vertical with low customer churn and pricing power; pay a premium multiple; integrate lightly; redirect free cash flow into the next acquisition. The result over time is an asset-light, cash-generative portfolio whose organic growth (mid-single digits in 2025) compounds on top of acquired growth (high single digits in 2025), with margins that expand as software displaces lower-multiple industrial revenue. The 2018-2025 portfolio reshaping — divesting industrial businesses, acquiring vertical software — is now substantively complete; ROP is essentially a software conglomerate that retains a small Technology Enabled Products tail.

The bear case is well-understood and largely about price. ROP rarely trades cheaply on near-term earnings, and the per-deal multiples in vertical software have crept up across the cycle as private-equity dry powder competes for the same assets. Organic growth at 5% is respectable but not exciting; if M&A capacity is constrained (by leverage, by valuation, or by deal scarcity), the algorithm slows. Goodwill and intangible amortization make GAAP earnings noisy; investors must rely on adjusted/free-cash-flow framings that the company controls. And the model depends on disciplined underwriting at scale — at $3.3B of annual deployment, even one mispriced acquisition is meaningful.

The reason ROP earns the "hidden compounder" tag despite being a household name in quality circles is its tendency to be overshadowed by flashier compounders (Constellation, Brookfield, MSCI). Patient holders have been rewarded by a long-duration combination of organic growth, accretive M&A, debt-financed cash returns to shareholders via reinvestment, and serial multiple expansion. The 2025 print — 12.3% revenue growth, software now ~85%+ of mix, $3.3B deployed — reaffirms the recipe. The key question for any new buyer is purely the price paid: ROP's compounding mechanics deserve a premium, but the right entry point typically requires patience or a market drawdown.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Screens see ROP as expensive on near-term P/E, modest on dividend yield, and mid-teens grower on revenue. What screens miss: (1) the cash-conversion quality — ROP's free-cash-flow margin runs well above its GAAP earnings margin because of intangible amortization from past deals; (2) the durability of customer cohorts in each acquired niche (e.g., Vertafore in insurance agencies, Aderant in BigLaw practice management); (3) the acquisition pipeline — ROP's relationships with vertical-software founders are a structural sourcing advantage; (4) the quality of recurring revenue, which is heavily subscription-based with high retention; (5) the optionality of price increases as inflation-protected, mission-critical tools that customers cannot easily switch off.

### CATALYSTS
- **Q1/Q2 2026 earnings prints** — first reads with full CentralReach contribution and updated organic-growth disclosure.
- **Next major acquisition (likely 2026)** — ROP typically deploys $2-4B per year; the next single ~$1B+ deal will reset earnings power.
- **Capital structure update** — leverage capacity refresh after 2025's deployment dictates 2026 M&A pace.
