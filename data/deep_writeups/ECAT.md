### ANALYST SUMMARY
ECAT is a $1.45B BlackRock multi-asset ESG-tilted closed-end term trust. Saba has been accumulating since 2023 and as of February 18, 2026 owns 22,451,311 shares (22.57%) at a cost basis around $334M, making this Saba's largest BlackRock CEF position. Saba has filed a PREC14A nominating eight trustees — explicitly seeking board majority — with conditional withdrawal of one nominee (Lawrence Epstein) if the board does not expand beyond nine seats. The June 9, 2026 AGM is the dispositive event. Saba's demands: NAV-based tender, conversion to an open-end fund, and (if board control achieved) potentially removing BlackRock as adviser.

The bear case is that BlackRock has greater retail-shareholder mobilization than any other CEF sponsor, has been hardening proxy-defense playbooks since the BIGZ/BMEZ campaigns, and that ISS/Glass Lewis have not consistently sided with Saba on full-slate board takeovers. ECAT is a "term trust" already (currently scheduled to terminate around 2030–2033), which BlackRock will argue obviates the need for activist intervention — the discount, sponsor will say, will close naturally as the wind-down date approaches. The current discount of ~5.4% on the spot tape is below Saba's typical 10%+ trigger, weakening the public case.

The bull case is layered. (1) Outright Saba win → board majority → NAV tender + likely open-ending = 5–15% gap closure on the entire position, plus discount compression on remaining shares. (2) Negotiated settlement (most likely) — BlackRock's pattern with BIGZ/BMEZ was a 30–40% NAV tender with standstill, capturing 60–80% of the discount-closure economics for activist holders. (3) Loss at ballot but discount narrows anyway as term-trust wind-down approaches and discount-management-program triggers (>10% avg discount tender) activate. The $14.63 entry vs. $15.47 NAV is a hard mark with multiple paths to value crystallization.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A standard CEF discount screen sees a modest ~5.4% gap and might pass. The PREC14A's structural detail is the alpha: Saba's 8-nominee slate seeks an actual board majority (not a minority slate), which legally enables — within months of a winning vote — a forced NAV tender and removal of BlackRock as adviser. The conditional Epstein withdrawal mechanism is a lawyer-crafted concession to ISS, signaling Saba's seriousness about getting institutional proxy advisor support. BlackRock's prior BIGZ tender (where the firm caved at ~32% of shares at NAV) is the explicit settlement template — the market is mispricing the probability of a similar negotiated outcome.
