# {NUMBER}. ULY — Urgent.ly Inc.
## TENDER OFFER | $5.50 cash / 2.23% gross spread | Score: 62/100

### THE SETUP
Urgent.ly is a connected-mobility and roadside-assistance platform that dispatches service providers (towing, lockouts, jump-starts, tire changes) on behalf of OEMs, insurance carriers, and auto clubs. The company went public via a 2023 SPAC merger and has struggled with covenant pressure on its debt facility throughout 2024-2025. On March 13, 2026, Agero — a privately held, much larger roadside-assistance competitor backed by Cross Country Group — agreed to acquire Urgent.ly through subsidiary Medford Hawk for $5.50 per share in cash, valuing the equity at approximately $156 million. Agero filed its SC TO-T tender offer March 30, 2026, and amended it April 17, 2026. The current price of $5.38 implies a 2.23% gross spread and roughly 22% annualized to a late-May 2026 close.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
This is a small-cap strategic roll-up where a thinly-traded, levered target is being absorbed by its larger private competitor. The strategic logic is straightforward: Agero is the dominant U.S. roadside dispatcher, and acquiring Urgent.ly removes its primary tech-enabled competitor while picking up OEM contracts and a software stack that complements Agero's call-center heritage. The price — $5.50 — reflects ULY's distressed posture; the stock traded as high as $20+ post-SPAC and has been a chronic disappointment.

The 22% annualized spread on a small deal is doing real work. Three live risks. First, ULY's credit facility has been amended multiple times and a covenant trip during the tender period would likely give Agero a walkaway. Second, Agero is private and its financing sources are not transparently disclosed in deal documents the way a public acquirer's would be. Third, the minimum tender condition on a thinly-held name with active short-side activity could fail or extend the tender. None of these are showstoppers, but cumulatively they justify a wider spread than a typical strategic deal.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The strategic backdrop: the U.S. roadside-assistance market is consolidating rapidly. Agero/Cross Country had been circling Urgent.ly informally for at least 18 months, and the $5.50 print is not an opening bid — it follows the company's own strategic review. The deal is best understood as a rescue: ULY's standalone alternative was a debt restructuring that would have wiped equity. From the seller's side, the board likely had no realistic counterparty because the credit facility's restrictions limited go-shop dynamics. From the buyer's side, Agero gets the asset cheaply but inherits OEM-contract assignment risk. Customer consents on enterprise SaaS-type roadside contracts are real and unmodeled.

### CATALYSTS
- Tender offer expiration (currently set for late May 2026)
- HSR clearance — likely a non-issue given size
- Minimum tender threshold disclosure
- Any further TO/A amendments or extensions
- Credit facility covenant compliance through close

### MUNGER CHECKLIST
- Simple business: Yes — dispatch software with a call center
- Pricing power: No, ULY is a price-taker against carriers and OEMs
- Honest, capable management: Going away
- Reasonable price: At $5.38 you are buying $0.12 of spread for binary close risk
- Within circle of competence: Standard tender mechanics with small-cap distress overlay
- Margin of safety: Minimal — if the deal breaks, ULY likely trades to $1-2 on debt fears

This is a "fast nickel" trade. The 22% annualized headline is misleading because the asymmetry is brutal: roughly $0.12 upside versus $3-4 of downside if Agero walks on a covenant trip. Position size accordingly. The right way to play this, if at all, is small and only after the minimum tender condition is met and the offer goes effective — at which point the spread compresses but the binary risk vanishes.
