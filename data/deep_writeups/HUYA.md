# {NUMBER}. HUYA — HUYA Inc.
## HOLDCO | Net-Cash Stub / ~21% NAV Discount | Score: 65/100

### THE SETUP
HUYA trades at ~$3.18 with roughly $3.85/ADS in net cash (RMB 6.25B at March 2025). Tencent owns 67% economic / 95% voting. The live-streaming business is essentially a free option, and Tencent has a documented playbook of taking subsidiaries private — providing a structural floor.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
HUYA is China's #1 game live-streaming platform, controlled by Tencent following Tencent's tender offer years ago. The company sits on RMB ~6.25B of cash, short-term, and long-term deposits (about $862M, or roughly $3.85 per ADS). Against a $3.18 share price and ~$710M market cap, the operating business carries a negative enterprise value. HUYA paid a $0.66/ADS special dividend in 2024 and has signaled additional capital return programs. Float is small (only ~33% non-Tencent), and trading volume is thin, but the math is unambiguously cheap on a sum-of-parts basis.

The bear case is real and not theoretical. Chinese gaming regulation has been episodically hostile (license freezes, minor-protection rules) and the live-streaming monetization model depends on virtual-gift consumption that fell low-double-digits year over year. The VIE structure means US holders own offshore shells with contractual claims into the operating company — a structure that has survived but never been fully tested. Tencent control creates two-sided risk: they can extract value via related-party transactions, or they can take HUYA private at an unfair price (though "unfair" relative to net cash sets a hard floor). DouYu merger optionality was killed by SAMR in 2021.

The bull case is "heads I win small, tails I don't lose much." If Tencent declares another special dividend, public holders capture a high-single-digit yield directly. If Tencent takes HUYA private, the floor is net cash plus some control premium — almost certainly above current. If Chinese consumer/gaming sentiment recovers, the operating stub re-rates from zero to something. Even the Korean/SEA esports vertical Tencent is building out gives HUYA latent strategic value. With the operating business still gross-margin-positive at the platform level, and net cash covering the entire market cap, the asymmetry is genuinely attractive for sized-appropriately China exposure.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The cash quality matters: HUYA's deposits are split across Chinese onshore banks (RMB) and offshore USD accounts. Repatriation friction is real — China's SAFE rules cap dividends out of operating subs to retained earnings of the WFOE. The 2024 special dividend required Tencent's vote, signaling alignment, but also revealing the gating mechanism. The 20-F discloses related-party transactions with Tencent for game-content licensing — these are the conduit by which Tencent could either enrich or extract from minorities. Watch the "amounts due to/from related parties" line. Also note: HUYA's headline cash includes RMB time deposits earning 2-3% — not a sleepy zero-yield pile.

- Tencent privatization bid (history with Sogou, Bitauto)
- Q4 2025/Q1 2026 results: capital return program update
