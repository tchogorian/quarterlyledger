# {NUMBER}. RHLD — Resolute Holdings Management, Inc.
## SPINOFF | $1.27B mkt cap, 2.5% of CMPO LTM Adj EBITDA fee | Score: 62/100
### THE SETUP
On February 28, 2025, CompoSecure (CMPO) completed the spin-off of Resolute Holdings Management as a separately listed Nasdaq company (one share of RHLD per twelve CMPO held). The vehicle is led by David Cote (former Honeywell CEO, Executive Chairman) and Tom Knott (CEO, prior CompoSecure operator), and was structured as an alternative-asset platform with one initial managed business — CompoSecure itself. The economics flow from a Management Agreement under which CompoSecure Holdings, L.L.C. pays Resolute "a quarterly management fee in cash equal to 2.5% of CompoSecure's Latest Twelve Months' Adjusted EBITDA." The taxable distribution, the tiny float, and the absence of revenue at spin produced indiscriminate selling that drove the price into the low $20s; by April 2026 the stock has run roughly six- to twelve-fold, depending on the entry print, to ~$147 — analysts who passed on it at $24 now anchor on "too expensive." The mispricing has migrated from neglect to narrative: the new market is debating whether RHLD deserves an asset-manager multiple on a single fee stream while it tries to sign a second managed company.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
RHLD is a publicly traded operating-services manager whose initial fee stream is a fixed percentage of CompoSecure's adjusted EBITDA. The structure is deliberately Brookfield- or 3G-flavored: a small, low-overhead holdco that earns recurring fees for capital-allocation oversight, M&A sourcing, and operational guidance, with the explicit intent to add additional managed companies over time. Each new managed acquisition — whether financed by the platform itself, by partners, or by separate vehicles — would compound the fee base without commensurate G&A growth. That is the bull case: an early-stage compounder with an operator-CEO of unusual pedigree.

The skeptical reading is just as straightforward. Today, RHLD has one client. The fee stream tracks CompoSecure's metal-card EBITDA, which has its own customer-concentration questions (Robinhood, American Express co-branded products) and a maturing premium-card category. At ~$1.27B market cap on a fee equal to 2.5% of CMPO LTM Adjusted EBITDA, the implied multiple on current-run-rate revenue is steep and largely discounts a successful second deal. Management has been clear that the model requires acquisitions, but those announcements remain prospective.

The asymmetry here is no longer the pure post-spin dislocation — that trade has been made. What remains is optionality on a credible operator successfully replicating the CompoSecure structure. If Cote and Knott sign one or two additional managed businesses on similar economics within twenty-four months, the platform thesis crystallizes; if not, the multiple compresses toward the underlying CMPO fee. Position sizing should reflect that bimodality, and that the share count is small enough that any large prospective capital raise to fund acquisitions will be price-sensitive.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
A screen will show ~$1.27B equity, minimal revenue, no GAAP earnings to speak of, and a ticker that looks expensive on every conventional metric. What screens cannot see: (1) the structural permanence of the management agreement — the fee is contractual, not at-will; (2) the pedigree of the operating team and the network it gives Cote and Knott to source mid-cap take-privates and orphan divisions; (3) the small float that creates both upside slippage and downside air-pockets; and (4) the precedent value — CompoSecure's first managed-acquisition would re-rate the model, while extended silence does the opposite.

### CATALYSTS
- **2026-Q1/Q2 earnings prints** — first full year of management fee accruals; demonstrates the unit economics of a single-client fee model.
- **Announcement of a second managed business** (any time 2026) — single most important catalyst for the platform thesis.
- **Capital raise or structured-vehicle launch** to fund a managed-company acquisition — likely required and likely dilutive but would validate the model.
