# {NUMBER}. COHR — Coherent Corp
## INDEX REBALANCE | S&P 500 March 23, 2026 + Nvidia $2B Anchor | Score: 73/100

### THE SETUP
Coherent was added to the S&P 500 on March 23, 2026, and announced concurrently a $2B investment from Nvidia to expand optical transceiver capacity for AI data-center networking. The combination of forced index buying and a marquee strategic anchor delivered a step-change in both the bid and the long-term demand visibility for the optical-component business.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Coherent (the post-merger entity formed from II-VI's acquisition of legacy Coherent Inc.) is a vertically integrated photonics and compound-semiconductor company. Its three reported segments span communications (datacom and telecom optical transceivers, indium-phosphide and silicon-photonics components), industrial lasers (cutting, welding, marking), and electronic devices/specialty products (silicon carbide for EVs and power, and various sensing components). The communications segment is now the AI-narrative engine: 800G and 1.6T transceivers used in hyperscale data centers represent rapidly growing units shipped per AI training cluster.

The bear case starts with the optical-transceiver competitive set: Lumentum, Innolight, Eoptolink, and Marvell-via-Inphi all chase the same hyperscaler design wins. Pricing on 800G transceivers normalizes once supply catches up to demand, compressing gross margins. The Nvidia $2B commitment ties significant future capacity to a single customer — a concentration that cuts both ways. The industrial laser business has been weak through the late-cycle downturn in machine tools and consumer electronics manufacturing. Silicon-carbide ramp at the company's Pennsylvania facility has had execution challenges and high capex. Net debt remains elevated post-II-VI/Coherent merger, and the company has not yet returned to a clean leverage profile.

The bull case is that COHR is the rare vertically integrated optical player — owning the InP epitaxial growth, the wafer fab, the laser chip, the transceiver assembly, and select silicon-carbide capacity. That vertical integration is genuinely scarce and hard to replicate. The Nvidia anchor signals that the largest accelerator supplier in the world wants COHR's capacity locked up. Index inclusion locks in passive ownership. Industrial laser is at trough and has operating leverage on recovery. SiC is a multi-year option. Long-term EBITDA can step up materially if the communications mix continues to shift toward AI datacom.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Nvidia $2B is structured as a capacity-investment commitment, not pure equity — the disclosure mechanics in the 8-K and subsequent 10-Q notes will reveal whether it's a prepayment, equity-linked instrument, or capacity reservation fee, and the accounting treatment differs materially. A screen also misses the segment cross-subsidization: industrial laser cash flow funds communications capex, so a sharper-than-expected industrial recovery would meaningfully accelerate balance-sheet repair. Backlog disclosure (in earnings call Q&A rather than press releases) is where AI mix actually shows up. Finally, the silicon-carbide unit's profitability profile is buried inside the broader electronic devices segment — pulling it apart requires segment-data triangulation across several quarters.
