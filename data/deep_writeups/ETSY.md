# {NUMBER}. ETSY — Etsy, Inc.
## CONVERTIBLE MATURITY | $650M Sept 2026 Wall — Refi Overhang | Score: 53/100

### THE SETUP
Etsy has $650M of 0.125% convertible notes maturing September 1, 2026. The conversion price (~$330) is dramatically out of the money against a sub-$60 stock, meaning the notes will almost certainly be settled in cash or refinanced. The market is pricing the equity overhang and refinancing risk; resolution removes a significant uncertainty.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Etsy operates the leading marketplace for handmade, vintage, and craft items, with secondary brands Reverb (musical instruments), Depop (Gen-Z resale), and Elo7 (divested). After a pandemic-era surge that doubled GMS and lifted the stock above $300, the business has spent three years digesting that pull-forward: GMS has roughly stagnated, active buyer counts have plateaued, take-rate increases have masked underlying weakness, and competition from Amazon Handmade, Temu, Shein, and TikTok Shop has compressed seller economics.

The bear case is that the marketplace's core proposition (unique, handmade goods from independent makers) has been diluted by mass-produced and resold inventory, eroding both buyer trust and seller community. Take-rate increases have generated revenue growth at the cost of seller goodwill; multiple high-profile seller strikes and protests have surfaced. The convertible refinancing — while manageable given roughly $1B of cash and ongoing FCF — will likely be done at materially higher coupon rates than the 0.125% legacy notes, creating a real interest-expense headwind into 2027. Buyback capacity will be constrained until the maturity is resolved. Secular threats from Chinese cross-border platforms targeting the same value-conscious craft buyer are real and growing.

The bull case rests on three legs: (1) refinancing resolution removes the overhang and could unlock buyback resumption; (2) Etsy generates substantial FCF and could simply pay down the notes from balance sheet cash, eliminating any dilution risk; (3) management's focus on "consideration" (search relevance, quality enforcement, AI-driven curation) could rekindle GMS growth if execution lands. Insider ownership is modest but management has historically been disciplined on capital allocation. At current prices, the stock trades at a high single-digit FCF multiple — undemanding if any growth returns.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 0.125% coupon on the 2026 converts is a relic of the 2021 ZIRP era — refinancing at 5-6% on $650M means roughly $30-35M of incremental annual interest expense, which is non-trivial against a company generating low-hundreds-of-millions of net income. The company also has $1B of 0.25% converts due 2028, meaning the same refi pressure recurs in two years. Read the 10-K's debt footnote carefully: the convertible structure includes capped calls that effectively raise the strike price and reduce dilution risk — this is investor-friendly but limits flexibility. International GMS as a percentage of total has stagnated; the Depop bet for Gen-Z growth has not delivered the inflection management expected. Watch quarterly active buyer counts as the leading indicator; revenue can be inflated by take-rate but buyer count cannot.

### CATALYSTS
- Refinancing announcement expected H1 2026 (likely Q2 2026)
- Q1 2026 earnings late April/early May 2026 — buyer count, GMS commentary
- Sept 1, 2026 maturity date — final resolution of overhang
