The fund has long traded at discounts to NAV due to Nuveen's perennial CEF complex overhang, leverage costs, and limited natural buyer base for a single-state fund. Saba Capital's reported 10%+ stake (filed 2025-11-05) puts it among the largest holders, with documented partial selling of $1.2M of shares on Nov 4–5, 2025 — read as position trimming ahead of a merger arbitrage payoff rather than abandonment of the campaign.

The merger structure may be NAV-for-NAV but the receiving fund itself trades at a discount — so the "narrowing" is partial. Distribution rate cuts at Nuveen funds in 2024–2025 hurt investor sentiment and could continue. If interest rates rise again, leverage spread compresses further.

The bull case is Saba's well-documented playbook: merger pressure, tender offers, or outright liquidation crystallizes NAV. The Feb 2026 NXJ merger is a template; if NQP follows, the discount closes from 9.4% toward 2-4% (typical post-merger residual). Saba's November 2025 SC 13D/G activity shows they are actively managing position size around the catalyst. Worst case, Nuveen offers a tender at 95-98% of NAV to placate Saba — still meaningful pickup from current levels. Plus a stable monthly tax-exempt distribution while you wait.
