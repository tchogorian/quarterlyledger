# {NUMBER}. FLYYQ — Spirit Aviation Holdings, Inc. (Spirit Airlines)
## POST-BANKRUPTCY | Chapter 22 stub | Score: 28/100

### THE SETUP
$6M market cap stub equity on an airline that emerged from Chapter 11 in March 2025 and re-filed five months later. CNBC reports liquidation possible "as early as this week" (April 15, 2026). Pure lottery ticket: liquidation ($0) versus a Frontier/Allegiant scoop-up of slot portfolio (multi-bagger).

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Spirit's first Chapter 11 (Nov 2024-March 2025) wiped out $795M of senior secured debt; bondholders took the equity, the stock relisted on NYSE American as FLYY in April 2025, and management promised a "right-sized" 200-aircraft fleet ULCC. Five months later, an August 2025 fuel spike combined with weak leisure demand re-broke the model. The company filed Chapter 22 on 8/29/2025, the stock was de-listed and now trades OTC as FLYYQ at ~$0.215 — implying creditors recover ~$273M cash against ~$1.19B of debt with the stub holding the residual.

Bear case: Chapter 22s historically wipe equity. The April 15, 2026 CNBC piece sourced from advisors warns liquidation could be triggered within days as DIP lenders lose patience. Spirit's brand has been gutted — 2025 traffic data shows -22% YoY; pilot attrition to UAL/DAL eroded the operational base. The 28.3M share count means even a constructive plan (debt-for-equity at lower haircut) likely cancels old equity per Section 1129's absolute priority rule. Realistic probability of zero recovery: 70-80%.

Bull/catalyst: Frontier (ULCC) twice tried to merge with Spirit (2022 ULCC deal blocked) and reportedly approached during the first Ch11. With JetBlue prohibited (DOJ), Frontier remains the only logical acquirer of Spirit's slot portfolio at FLL/MCO/LAS — slots that have $200-400M in standalone secondary-market value. A 363 sale to Frontier could include a "consent fee" to common, or a plan that retains stub. At $0.215, even a $1-2 takeout is a 5-10x. Allegiant has also been mentioned. Catalyst window: weeks, not months.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Chapter 11 docket reveals the U.S. Trustee filed objections to the proposed DIP financing structure on grounds it impairs unsecured creditors and equity inappropriately — this is unusual and could force Spirit's advisors to consider a 363 sale instead of a straight cramdown plan, which historically preserves more value for the stub. Additionally, Spirit's Latin/Caribbean route authorities (Bogota, San Pedro Sula, Lima) are scarce and not freely transferable in liquidation; an acquirer must take them in a 363 sale, providing slight strategic value to the remaining estate. The fuel hedge book was unwound at the first emergence — Spirit is fully exposed to spot crude at $90.

### CATALYSTS
- Imminent (April 2026) DIP financing decision / liquidation determination
- Potential Frontier or Allegiant 363 bid
- Plan of reorganization filing with stub treatment specified
