### ANALYST SUMMARY
GE Vernova (NYSE: GEV) was spun from GE on April 2, 2024 as the second leg of the GE three-way breakup. It is a pure-play energy infrastructure company across three segments: Power (gas turbines, steam, nuclear services — the crown jewel), Electrification (grid equipment, transformers, switchgear, software), and Wind (onshore + offshore — the historical loss-maker, now stabilizing). At spin, consensus underwrote modest growth and a multi-year wind drag. Twelve months later, the AI data center build-out has converted GEV's gas-turbine and grid-equipment franchises into the supply-constrained choke point for new electrical capacity in the U.S. and Europe, sending the stock from ~$141 at spin to near $1,000.

The bear case starts with the obvious: a 7x move in 18 months means a lot of good news is priced in. The forward EV/EBITDA multiple is now demanding — investors are paying for sustained pricing power, continued wind margin recovery, and a multi-year capex super-cycle that is partially policy-dependent (IRA continuity, permitting reform, hyperscaler capex pace). Wind remains a structural laggard with cost and warranty issues that have surprised to the downside historically. Gas-turbine slot scarcity is real but eventually competitors (Siemens Energy, Mitsubishi Heavy) add capacity. And any AI-capex deceleration would compress the multiple violently — the stock is a derivative of hyperscaler intent.

The bull case is order-book mechanics. Power and Electrification orders are being booked at materially higher prices than legacy backlog, which means revenue and margin growth from already-signed contracts will continue for several years even without new bookings. Hyperscalers are reserving turbine slots into 2028, locking in pricing. Wind losses are narrowing as new orders are being signed at price points that actually generate margin (vs. legacy backlog that bled cash). Capital return ramp (buyback + initiated dividend) is accelerating. Even on conservative forward EBITDA growth, the math supports continued upside on margin expansion and free-cash-flow conversion alone — multiple does not need to expand further.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K filed January 29, 2026 walks through Power orders growth in detail; the meaningful disclosure is the new-orders gross margin embedded in backlog vs. the run-off margin of legacy backlog — that delta is what drives forward earnings power without new orders. Also read the Wind segment commentary on contract cancellations and pricing discipline; Vernova has walked away from low-margin wind orders rather than chase volume, a structural mindset shift from legacy GE Renewable Energy. The Electrification segment's transformer and switchgear capacity additions matter — capex is being deployed but the orderbook is growing faster, sustaining backlog cover. Finally the post-spin tax matters disclosure interacts with GE Aerospace and GEHC — read the indemnification terms before sizing any cross-spin trade.
