### THE SETUP
Clearwater agreed Dec 21, 2025 to be taken private by a Permira / Warburg Pincus consortium (with Francisco Partners and Temasek participating) at $24.55 cash per share, an $8.4B deal at a 47% premium. Go-shop ended Jan 23, 2026 with no superior bid; shareholder vote is May 6, 2026; spot at $24.11 implies a 1.83% gross spread or about 9.1% annualized.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Clearwater Analytics is a SaaS platform for institutional investment accounting, performance, compliance, and risk reporting — used by insurers, asset managers, corporates, and government entities to handle multi-asset portfolio data. The model is high-retention, multi-tenant, and produces SaaS-grade economics. Screen catches it because of the announced LBO take-private at $24.55 cash. Spread of 1.83% gross / 9.1% annualized reflects a low-friction, financing-secured deal with a defined May 6, 2026 vote and Q2 2026 expected close. PE consortium includes a sovereign co-invest (Temasek) which is positive for financing certainty.

Bear case: even an essentially-clean PE LBO can stall on foreign regulatory clearances (EU, UK, possibly Asian) or on shareholder class-action objections that delay the close into late Q3 or Q4. CWAN's go-shop produced no superior bidder — bears can argue the sale price under-values the franchise given peer SaaS multiples have re-rated since announcement. If the deal breaks for any reason, the unaffected price was likely closer to $17 (pre-announcement low to mid teens), so downside is meaningful — call it 25-30%. Sponsor LBO break risk is low but not zero in a turbulent rates environment if debt financing has to be re-cut.

Bull case: this is a textbook clean PE take-private. Sponsor consortium with deep equity backing, sovereign co-invest, committed financing, no antitrust overlap (multiple PE firms, no horizontal concern), board-recommended deal, completed go-shop, shareholder vote scheduled. The 13E-3 amendments in April 2026 indicate process is on rails. At a 1.83% gross spread and 9.1% annualized to Q2 close, this is one of the lower-risk arb spreads available — especially attractive when paired against a portfolio of higher-risk merger arbs to balance downside. For a non-arb investor, this is essentially a cash equivalent maturing in late Q2.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 13E-3 filings disclose the special-committee process, financial advisor fairness opinion, and projection set used to justify the $24.55 price — which gives a window into management's own forward case for organic growth and margin trajectory that public guidance had been more conservative on. The go-shop window closing Jan 23, 2026 with zero superior offers is informative about strategic alternatives — strategic acquirers (FactSet, S&P, MSCI) chose not to bid, which tells you the price is at or above what synergy buyers would pay. The Permira/Warburg consortium structure with Temasek co-invest indicates pre-arranged equity certainty — financing risk is essentially zero.
