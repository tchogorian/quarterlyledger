# {NUMBER}. FONR — FONAR Corporation
## TAKE-PRIVATE PENDING | $19 cash MBO | Score: 60/100

### THE SETUP
Damadian-family MBO at $19/share representing a 31.5% premium; vote May 28, 2026; tight 1.06% spread / 2.55% annualized reflects high deal certainty. The hidden risk: dual-class structure pays Class A non-voting only $10.50, creating litigation risk from disenfranchised holders.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
FONAR invented the original MRI scanner; today the operating business is HMCA, which manages 35+ diagnostic imaging centers across NY/FL with the legacy "Stand-Up MRI" device franchise. The Damadian family (founder Raymond's son Timothy is CEO) has long sought to take the $120M-cap company private. The 12/23/2025 deal at $19/common ($10.50/Class A non-voting preferred) is financed by $35M senior debt from OceanFirst, a $10M additional facility, and $45M family/insider equity. Vote scheduled May 28, 2026 at 11am NY; close projected Q3 2026.

Bear case: Conflicted MBOs at small-cap value names invite litigation. The $19 price represents only 6-7x trailing FCF and ~7x EV/EBITDA — well below imaging-center M&A comps of 9-11x. The Class A treatment ($10.50) is particularly aggressive and could draw a Delaware appraisal action that delays closing. OceanFirst financing tied to bank-debt covenants exposes the deal to rate-environment shifts. A failed vote sends FONR back to ~$14.50, a -22% downside.

Bull/catalyst: Special-meeting record dates and the 13E-3/A filing (April 16, 2026) suggest the family controls or has rolled enough voting power that approval is mechanical. The independent-directors' "go-shop" reportedly drew zero competing bids, suggesting the price clears the market test. Tight spread of 1.06% with a defined ~3-month close window provides clean positive carry. For arbitrageurs comfortable with MBO conflicts, this offers a near-binary play with the discipline of a special meeting calendar.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The DEFM14A discloses that FONAR's Special Committee retained Houlihan Lokey for the fairness opinion, which valued the common between $17-$22 — placing $19 squarely in range but not at the top. More telling: the proxy notes the Damadian family already owns or controls ~50.1% of voting power via Class B/C super-voting shares (10 votes per share), making the public minority vote essentially advisory. The differential treatment of Class A holders ($10.50 vs $19 common) reflects the Class A's economic-but-not-voting structure laid out in the original 1995 charter. Litigation has been filed in Delaware (Frances v. FONAR et al.); historically these cases settle for "mootness fees" without altering deal terms but can delay close by 30-60 days.

### CATALYSTS
- Special meeting May 28, 2026 (vote)
- Q3 2026 deal close (projected July-September)
- Resolution of any Delaware shareholder litigation
