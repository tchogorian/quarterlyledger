### THE SETUP
ITOCHU is the highest-quality of the five Japanese sogo shosha Berkshire Hathaway has been quietly accumulating since 2020. Berkshire crossed 10% in March 2026 (10.07%, up from 9.54% — disclosed via a National Indemnity vehicle) and Buffett has signaled he is "still buying." Morningstar's fair value implies 23–36% upside across the trading houses, with ITOCHU best-in-class on ROE and most insulated from commodity cycles thanks to FamilyMart and a consumer-tilted portfolio.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
ITOCHU operates eight divisions — Textile, Machinery, Metals & Minerals, Energy & Chemicals, Food (FamilyMart 50%+, Dole), General Products & Realty, ICT & Financial, and "The 8th Company" (consumer focus). It is the only top-tier sogo shosha with under-indexed resource exposure, generating earnings stability that peers Mitsubishi/Mitsui/Sumitomo/Marubeni cannot match. Berkshire's stake is now 10.07% (up from 9.54% — disclosed in a March 2026 ITOCHU release) and the five-house aggregate Berkshire holding has topped $30B. Total shareholder yield (dividend + buyback) is roughly 4.32% with a 1.4 trillion-yen buyback authorization across 2024-25.

The bear narrative: trading houses are opaque conglomerates, Japanese governance lags Western standards even after the Tokyo Stock Exchange "PBR<1" reform campaign, JPY/USD volatility introduces FX risk for ADR holders, and the SOTP discount has persisted for years — there is no obvious catalyst to close it. ADR liquidity (ITOCY) is thinner than the Tokyo line. Resource exposure, though under-indexed, is still material — a global commodity downturn would compress earnings. Management's payout ratio is around 26% TTM; capital return is meaningful but not aggressive.

The bull thesis stacks: (1) Berkshire endorsement at 10%+ with stated intent to keep buying; (2) Morningstar fair value implying 23-36% upside; (3) FamilyMart take-private (2020) showed management's willingness to monetize SOTP; (4) Dole and other consumer carve-outs are plausible follow-ons; (5) the TSE PBR-under-1 campaign is forcing larger buybacks across the sector; (6) yen normalization would mechanically lift the ADR. ITOCHU is a quality compounder at a HoldCo discount with a Hall-of-Fame anchor shareholder.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
ADR investors miss the disclosure that Berkshire holds via National Indemnity — the March 2, 2026 ITOCHU press release confirms the 10.07% threshold breach and the regulatory filing path. Most US screens map ITOCY to "diversified financials" or "trading conglomerate," missing that FamilyMart (Japan's #2 convenience chain at 16,000+ stores) is consolidated inside the Food division and would re-rate dramatically as a standalone retail comparable. The sogo-shosha business model — earning from minority stakes, captive financing, off-take rights, and trading margin — does not appear cleanly in standard ROIC / ROE screens, which under-state economic returns by capitalizing the wrong base.
