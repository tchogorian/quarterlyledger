# {NUMBER}. ONTF — ON24, Inc.
## TAKE-PRIVATE | $8.10 All-Cash from Cvent/Blackstone | Score: 60/100

### THE SETUP
ON24 announced on January 15, 2026 a definitive all-cash sale to Cvent (Blackstone-owned) at $8.10/share, $350M total. Stock trades exactly at the deal price — zero spread to capture, but the March 26 vote and 1H 2026 close make this a near-zero-risk parking spot rather than an arb opportunity.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
ON24 is a webinar / digital-engagement SaaS platform that IPO'd in early 2021 at peak SaaS multiples (~$50/share), benefited from the COVID-era virtual-events boom, and then de-rated viciously as enterprise customers consolidated webinar/event platforms post-pandemic. Annual recurring revenue has been declining for several years as net-revenue-retention dropped below 90% and the company struggled to differentiate against Zoom Webinars, Microsoft Teams Live Events, and Cvent itself. Activist Ondine Capital pushed for strategic review in 2024-2025; the result is the Cvent take-private at $8.10.

The bear case for sitting in ONTF here: there is literally no spread. Stock at $8.10, deal at $8.10. The only return profile is (a) zero, if the deal closes on schedule, or (b) significantly negative if the deal breaks. Cvent is a strategic + Blackstone-financed buyer, so financing risk is low, but HSR review and shareholder-vote outcomes are still execution risks. With zero spread, the asymmetry is awful — you are taking deal-break risk (estimated 5-10% probability of $5-6 trough re-rating, or -25-30% drawdown) for no upside. The only rational use of this position is as a brief cash-equivalent parking spot with March 26 vote and 1H26 close as the unwinding date.

The bull case is narrow: ONTF holds significant cash on the balance sheet, and a topping bid is theoretically possible — but Ondine Capital agreed to vote in favor, and the go-shop period (if any) has expired without a competing bidder emerging. Realistically there is no bull case to add capital here at $8.10. The pre-deal interest is over. For investors who already owned ONTF below $5, this is the exit; for new capital, this is not a setup.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The DEFM14A merger proxy details Ondine Capital's voting agreement (locks up the activist's stake to vote yes), the go-shop provisions (already expired with no superior proposal), and the termination fee schedule (typically 3-4% of equity value, which would be ~$10-14M). Buried in the proxy: ON24's net cash position is meaningful relative to the $350M deal value — the implied enterprise value is much smaller than headline equity value, which is why Cvent could justify the price even for a declining-ARR business. Also missed by screens: ON24's customer base includes pharma and life-sciences accounts with regulated-event compliance requirements that Cvent specifically wanted to acquire — this is a strategic combination, not a financial cost-take-out, which lowers deal-break probability versus pure-PE LBOs.
