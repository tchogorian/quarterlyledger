# {NUMBER}. LLYVA — Liberty Live Holdings, Inc. (Series A)
## HOLDCO — NAV DISCOUNT (post-split-off) | 30% LYV stake stub | Score: 71/100

### THE SETUP
Liberty Media's Live Nation tracking stock was converted into a real asset-backed split-off on December 15, 2025. Forced selling from year-end ETF rebalancers and tax-loss sellers created a structural discount to look-through Live Nation NAV — one that historically compresses 12-18 months post-spin in the Malone playbook.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Liberty Live Holdings is now a standalone public company holding 69.6 million shares of Live Nation Entertainment (LYV) — roughly a 30% economic interest in the world's dominant concert and ticketing platform — plus 100% of Quint (luxury sports/entertainment hospitality and F1 partner) and a Meyer Shank Racing minority. LLYVA is the Series A (voting) class, $92.31 reference price, ~$8.59B market cap. The structure replaces the prior Liberty Live tracking stock with a real asset-backed entity, eliminating the historical 25-35% tracker discount that plagued FWONA-era valuations.

The bear case starts with leverage: Liberty attributed roughly $1-1.5B of margin loan and exchangeables to the holdco at the split, so the equity stub is levered to LYV's stock price. Live Nation itself faces an ongoing DOJ Ticketmaster antitrust suit that could force structural separation. Quint is a small, lumpy hospitality business worth maybe $300-500M depending on F1 contract renewals. Two-class share structures historically attract a governance discount. And the embedded NAV math depends on LYV continuing to trade above ~$130 — any LYV correction translates 1.5x to LLYVA equity given the look-through leverage.

The bull case: 69.6M LYV shares at recent prices (~$155) imply ~$10.8B of look-through equity, against an LLYVA + LLYVK combined market cap of ~$14.4B less ~$1.3B holdco debt — meaning the stub is trading at a meaningful discount once you back out attributable debt. Malone's Liberty playbook over 30 years has rewarded patience: discounts close via buybacks, special dividends, or eventual roll-up into the operating company. Maffei has done it with FWONA, LSXMA, QVCA, BATRA. Buybacks at material discount to NAV are extraordinarily accretive, and Liberty has consistently authorized and used them.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The split-off prospectus (S-1/A and 424B3 filings filed December 2025) details the exact attribution of debt and the indemnification structure between Liberty Media and Liberty Live — most screens treat the post-spin entity as a fresh equity with no history. Crucially, Liberty Live inherited a clean credit profile and unused buyback capacity that was authorized but not deployed pre-split (ETFs that owned it as a tracker had to sell mechanically at year-end, creating a flush — Liberty management can repurchase into that flush). The Quint asset, buried in the SOTP, includes long-term hospitality contracts with Formula 1 that are not separately disclosed in LYV financials — call it a $300-500M optionality piece most analysts zero out.
