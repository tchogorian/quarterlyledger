### ANALYST SUMMARY

abrdn Global Dynamic Dividend Fund (NYSE: AGD), managed by abrdn Inc. (Aberdeen), is a closed-end global equity fund seeking high current dividend income with secondary capital growth. Saba Capital filed an SC 13D on July 25, 2024 with a ~5.0% stake. abrdn responded by raising the distribution rate 70% effective August 30, 2024 and adopting a 12% Managed Distribution Policy (paid monthly at 1% of average daily NAV). The result: AGD flipped from a chronic 10–14% discount to a sustained PREMIUM, currently 6.9% (market $12.25 vs NAV $11.46 per the latest research datapoint; Morningstar/CEF data shows AGD oscillating between 1–7% premium through Q1 2026). The DEF 14A filed April 7, 2026 covers AGD's 2026 annual meeting — two Class III Trustees up for election (Nancy Yao nominated; Todd Reit continuing as Chair) — routine governance, no Saba dissident slate.

The bear case is the policy itself. A 12% Managed Distribution Policy is mathematically unsustainable on a global equity portfolio yielding ~3% organically. The shortfall is funded by realized gains and, crucially, return of capital (ROC) — the fund is paying out NAV. This works in bull markets when capital appreciation funds distributions; in flat or down markets, NAV declines, the absolute distribution (1% of NAV monthly) shrinks, and the premium evaporates. Historical CEF research is brutally consistent: funds trading at premiums driven by managed distributions revert to discounts within 18–36 months when distribution sustainability becomes questioned. Saba's typical playbook is to exit at premiums — the SC 13D/A filed October 15, 2024 may have been a partial exit; a further reduction is highly likely in 2026.

The bull case requires either (a) the abrdn 12% MDP being sustained for years through favorable equity returns, OR (b) abrdn announcing a STRUCTURAL fix (open-ending the fund, large tender at NAV, ETF conversion). The 2026 proxy contains no such proposal. abrdn has historically resisted open-ending its CEF lineup. The 12% distribution rate is genuinely attractive vs peer equity CEFs (8–10% typical), and global-equity exposure with dividend tilt is a defensible portfolio holding. If you already own AGD, the income stream and premium are real cash returns. But for a NEW buy at 6.9% premium, this is paying a fee to hold what is essentially an active global-equity fund.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

All three were Saba targets and all three adopted aggressive managed distribution policies in mid-2024. AOD has similar premium dynamics. The fact that abrdn manages these three funds as a unit means a single sponsor decision (e.g., reducing distribution policy in 2027) would affect all three premiums simultaneously — concentrated sponsor risk. Saba's October 15, 2024 SC 13D/A was the most recent activist filing — they have not filed again, suggesting either monetization or quiet observation. The trustee elections are staggered (Class I/II/III) — Saba would need a multi-year campaign to flip a board majority, making activist re-engagement at AGD unlikely while the premium holds. Abrdn's Philadelphia-based US fund team (1900 Market Street) has been managing aggressive CEF distribution policies across multiple closed-end funds as a defensive moat.
