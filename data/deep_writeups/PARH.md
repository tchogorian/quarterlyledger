Initially trades on grey/pink markets pending exchange listing; double-removed from natural holders.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Par Health is the classic Greenblatt/Mihaljevic spinoff stub: a generics-and-injectables business carved out of two recently-emerged-from-bankruptcy parent companies (Mallinckrodt and Endo) which themselves merged into Keenova in late 2025. The shares were distributed to Mallinckrodt holders, who in turn received their shares as creditor distributions through the bankruptcy process. This means the natural holder base is distressed-debt funds, hedge funds with credit mandates, and event-driven funds — none of whom want to hold a small-cap generic-pharma equity once it spins. The forced-selling pressure is real and likely persistent through index inclusion or exchange uplisting in 2026.

The bear case is the generics business itself. Generic pricing has been deflationary for a decade, sterile injectable manufacturing carries chronic FDA inspection risk (483s, warning letters, plant shutdowns), and the combined SKU rationalization will require management bandwidth and capex. The opioid-tainted heritage of both Mallinckrodt and Endo could create reputational drag with institutional buyers even if the operations are clean. There is no analyst coverage, no normalized historical financials given fresh-start accounting and the spin's complex carve-out, and the grey-market trading window can produce wild price action with minimal volume.

The bull case is structural and policy-driven. US-domiciled API capacity has new strategic value under drug-shortage policy and tariff-protected manufacturing tailwinds (BIOSECURE, FDA shortage-list incentives). The combined business is a top-15 US generics maker — sub-scale enough to be acquired but large enough to be relevant. Once exchange-listed in 2026 and eligible for institutional ownership, the discount to fundamental value should compress. Distressed fund forced selling is the single best inefficiency in event-driven investing.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
No screener will flag PARH for months — it has no listed exchange, no consensus estimates, no standardized GICS code, and the fresh-start accounting renders trailing financials meaningless. The Form 10 (or equivalent disclosure document) filed in late 2025 contains carve-out historicals showing combined adjusted EBITDA, capex run rate, and working capital seasonality — these are the only meaningful numbers. The capital structure post-spin is also non-obvious: PARH likely received some allocated debt from the parent restructurings, which materially changes the equity story. The ~200 SKU portfolio includes several injectable products on the FDA shortage list, which carry pricing power that screens never capture.
