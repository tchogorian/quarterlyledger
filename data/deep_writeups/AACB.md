### ANALYST SUMMARY

Artius II Acquisition (Nasdaq: AACB) is a Cayman-Islands blank-check shell led by Boon Sim, Founder/Managing Partner of Artius Capital, targeting tech-enabled, software, or fintech businesses. The IPO closed February 14, 2025 with 22,000,000 Units at $10.00, generating $220M IPO proceeds plus private placements that brought trust assets to roughly $228M. The 10-K (filed March 19, 2026) discloses a $1.2M working capital deficit and the auditor's "substantial doubt about our ability to continue as a going concern" language standard for late-cycle SPACs. Definitions section confirms the "Completion Window means (i) August 14, 2026 (or February 14, 2027 if we have executed a definitive agreement…) or (ii) such other time period…pursuant to an amendment to the Articles." The market cap (~$285M reported) reflects warrants/rights stub on top of the share trust value.

The bear case is structural to all post-2022 SPACs: founder Boon Sim and the sponsor have economic incentive to push ANY deal across the line because their Class B founder shares are worthless on liquidation. Public shareholders have redemption rights at trust value, but the rights and contingent rights attached to public Units are forfeited if you redeem ("If you elect to exercise your redemption rights…you will be deemed to have tendered your Contingent Rights for no additional consideration"). Liquidity in AACB common is thin; an extension vote could dilute trust per share through sponsor share-class tweaks. SPACs that complete combinations have historically delivered ~70% one-year losses to non-redeemers.

The bull case is straightforward arbitrage: at $10.25 you're capturing yield-to-liquidation of roughly 1.5–4% over 4–10 months (depending on whether the August deadline triggers wind-up or the February extension activates), with optionality on a deal-pop pre-announcement. Trust assets sit in T-bills and accrue interest. If no deal materializes by August 14, 2026, the trust distributes pro-rata. The non-redemption optionality, combined with the contingent-rights kicker (one-tenth of a Class A share per right), creates a low-double-digit IRR floor for share-only buyers who plan to redeem.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Unit structure is unusual: each Unit (AACBU) contains one Class A share PLUS one "Contingent Right" PLUS one "right to receive one-tenth of one Class A ordinary share." The contingent rights only have value in a successful business combination — they're forfeited on redemption. This means the AACB common (no rights/no contingent right) trades cheaper than the implicit trust per share would suggest, and the market is splitting trust value between three securities. Pure arbitrageurs should buy AACB shares and skip the rights/units; deal-pop speculators should buy AACBU. Boon Sim's prior SPAC (Artius Acquisition / Origin Materials) traded down ~85% post-merger, so the sponsor's deal-selection track record is an active bear datapoint.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)

- **Definitive agreement announcement** — would create a 5–15% deal-pop pre-vote and unlock the contingent-rights value
- **Extension proxy / sponsor concessions** — if deal slips, watch for sponsor giving back founder shares or contributing to trust to sweeten an extension vote
