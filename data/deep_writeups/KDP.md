# {NUMBER}. KDP — Keurig Dr Pepper Inc.
## ACTIVIST — CAPITAL RETURN | Starboard engaged Oct 2025; JDE Peet integration | Score: 67/100

### THE SETUP
KDP trades at $26.53 (~$36B market cap) after a sharp drawdown following the JDE Peet's acquisition announcement, which the market initially read as a poorly-timed coffee doubling-down. Starboard Value's Jeff Smith went public October 18, 2025, and the February 2026 board nomination deadline passed without a slate — strongly suggesting amicable engagement on synergy disclosure, portfolio review, and possibly a coffee/cold-beverage separation. With sum-of-parts estimates above $35, the asymmetry is real if Starboard secures meaningful structural action.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Keurig Dr Pepper is the product of the 2018 Keurig + Dr Pepper Snapple merger — single-serve coffee (Keurig brewers and K-Cups), carbonated soft drinks (Dr Pepper, 7UP, Canada Dry, Sunkist, Squirt, A&W), premium waters (Core, Evian distribution), and energy/fuel (Bloom, C4 distribution, Ghost stake). The 2025 announced acquisition of JDE Peet's (Peet's Coffee, Jacobs, Douwe Egberts, L'OR) doubles down on coffee at a moment when single-serve volume has been pressured. CEO Tim Cofer (former Mondelez/Kellanova) inherited the deal and faces a complex integration plus an activist demanding that he articulate the synergy bridge.

The bear case: coffee is a scale game with structural headwinds — single-serve K-Cup volume has been declining as consumers shift to cold brew, drip, and away-from-home consumption returns. JDE Peet's is a heavily levered European business with its own portfolio issues and FX exposure. The deal adds debt at a moment when the cold-beverage business is also seeing competitive pressure (Celsius/Alani/Olipop in energy/functional). Standalone Dr Pepper has been the bright spot, having overtaken Pepsi as #2 cola in the US, but cracks could appear if pricing power fades. If Starboard's engagement fizzles into governance tweaks rather than structural action, the stock stays range-bound.

The bull case: Starboard's Costa Coffee/Whitbread playbook is directly applicable — push for a clean separation of coffee (KDP + JDE Peet) from cold beverages (Dr Pepper, waters, energy distribution). A two-company outcome could unlock 25-35% upside versus current prices using comp multiples (Coca-Cola, Monster on the cold side; Nestle/JDE peers on coffee). Even short of a break-up, $300M+ of articulated synergies on JDE Peet plus accelerated buyback would re-rate the stock. The fact that Starboard didn't file a slate by February 2026 deadline is bullish — it implies private commitments from the board.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 2024 10-K segment disclosures separate U.S. Refreshment Beverages, U.S. Coffee, International, and Latin America — making a clean break-up structurally simple, with U.S. Refreshment Beverages alone generating ~$3B of segment operating income on ~50% gross margins versus U.S. Coffee margins compressing. The proxy details Tim Cofer's compensation explicitly tied to "transformation actions" — language unusual for a soft-drinks CEO and consistent with strategic optionality. Buried in MD&A: management quietly disclosed the JDE Peet's deal expects "synergies primarily in procurement and SG&A" — Starboard's argument is that this number is materially understated, and the activist will hold management to publishing a higher target.
