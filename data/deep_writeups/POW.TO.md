# {NUMBER}. POW.TO — Power Corporation of Canada
## HOLDCO — NAV DISCOUNT | ~20% DISCOUNT | Score: 76/100

### THE SETUP

Power Corporation is the Desmarais family's Montreal-based holding company, structured as a multi-decade compounder built around insurance, asset management, and a now-emerging digital-wealth franchise. Trading near C$73, with NAV indicatively around C$92, POW currently trades at roughly a 20% discount to NAV — meaningfully tighter than the historical 30% but still wide enough to offer a margin-of-safety entry into a portfolio of high-quality regulated financials. Market cap is ~US$31.7B. The asset stack: 66.9% of Great-West Lifeco (TSX:GWO), 62.1% of IGM Financial (TSX:IGM), 83.6% of privately-held Wealthsimple, and indirect stakes in Europe's GBL, Pargesa, Sagard alternative-asset platform, and Power Sustainable.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

The Desmarais family's prior "triple-decker" structure (Power Financial wrapped inside Power Corp) was simplified in 2020, removing one layer of holdco discount. Yet the discount has remained sticky for three reasons: (1) Great-West Life is a conservative, lower-multiple insurer that drags blended valuations; (2) family voting control via multiple voting shares limits any forced break-up optionality; and (3) the most valuable hidden asset — Wealthsimple — is private and conservatively marked. Wealthsimple is the embedded growth call option: Canada's leading digital broker and wealth manager, profitable in 2024, with a private valuation of ~C$15B (POW's 83.6% look-through ~C$12.5B). Comparable public fintechs (Robinhood at ~25x earnings, Interactive Brokers at premium multiples) suggest Wealthsimple alone could be worth C$15–25B in an IPO window.

POW's capital allocation under Jeffrey Orr (and now successor leadership) has tilted aggressively toward buybacks at NAV-discounted prices, which mechanically grows look-through NAV per share. The dividend yields ~5%+, the holdco simplification continues, and Sagard's alt-asset AUM is scaling — adding a fee-stream that didn't exist a decade ago. The combination of (a) buying $1 of NAV for $0.80, (b) a 5% dividend, (c) underlying GWL/IGM earnings growth, and (d) an embedded Wealthsimple monetization catalyst, makes POW one of the cleaner risk-adjusted compounders in Canadian large-cap.

The pushback: Canadian holdco discounts are stubbornly persistent (Onex, Fairfax, Brookfield holdcos all carry them), the family will never relinquish control, and Great-West Life's exposure to UK/Irish/Putnam/Empower competes in a brutal asset-management margin environment. Wealthsimple monetization could be years away — or never (the family may prefer to compound it privately).

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

A screen sees a Canadian financial holdco yielding ~5%, with mid-single-digit earnings growth and a P/E around 10x. It does not see: (a) Wealthsimple — invisible at book value but worth multiples of carrying cost; (b) the buyback cadence that mechanically narrows discount; (c) Sagard as a future fee-engine; (d) the Desmarais family's century-long capital allocation track record (Paul Desmarais Sr. built one of the great Canadian compounder franchises). The look-through quality of the underlying assets is materially better than the SOTP arithmetic suggests.
