### ANALYST SUMMARY
Coterra is a diversified U.S. independent E&P with assets in the Permian Delaware, Anadarko, and Marcellus, formed via the Cabot–Cimarex merger. It generates substantial free cash flow at mid-cycle commodity prices and pays a fixed-plus-variable dividend. The screen catches it because of the announced merger with Devon Energy, a $26B all-stock deal at 0.70 DVN per CTRA share. At a $30.89 spot price the gross spread is just 0.23%, annualizing to roughly 1.36% — essentially priced as a near-certain close with the only meaningful risk being DVN's share-price volatility between now and Q2 close.

Bear case: this is not a true arbitrage — it is a synthetic long DVN. If WTI rolls back to the mid-$60s or natural gas weakens, DVN's stock can drop 15–25% and the implied CTRA value follows, easily wiping out years of expected merger return. Stock-deal arbs have no collar protection here. There is also integration risk: combining two large E&Ps with overlapping basins always produces synergy slippage and asset-sale leakage. If proxy advisors flag governance or social issues at one of the two shareholder votes, the deal could face an embarrassing close vote even with HSR done.

Bull case: HSR clearance on April 7, 2026 removes the only material regulatory obstacle, and the basins are complementary not horizontally overlapping. Devon and Coterra both have aligned management visions on shareholder returns, and the pro forma entity will be one of the largest U.S. independents with scale benefits in midstream contracts and service costs. For investors who want DVN exposure anyway, owning CTRA captures the 0.70 ratio plus collects any narrowing of the spread before close. The best risk-adjusted play is a paired short of DVN against long CTRA to lock the 23 bps and clip the dividend differential.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 0.70 fixed exchange ratio means CTRA holders are fully exposed to DVN's commodity beta with no walk-away or collar. CTRA's Marcellus dry-gas position is the hidden gem the merger model under-credits — Devon historically had no gas exposure, and at $4+ Henry Hub the Marcellus throws off cash flow that materially de-risks DVN's oil-heavy book. The merger proxy will quantify run-rate synergies (likely $400–600M) that the current CTRA price has not fully discounted. Watch the proxy for the special-committee process — if any board member dissented or asked for a higher ratio, that is a signal of upside negotiating leverage that could come back if DVN trades up materially before vote.
