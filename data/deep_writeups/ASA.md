### THE SETUP
Saba Capital owns ~29.86% of ASA as of Feb 2026 (up from 17.24% in Aug 2025), two directors already resigned under a August 2025 standstill, and the discount remains ~10% even as gold/miners have rallied. ### ANALYST SUMMARY
ASA is a Bermuda-domiciled closed-end fund that invests in gold and precious-metals mining equities — basically a leveraged proxy on miners with ~$700M+ NAV. Top holdings are typically the major gold miners (Newmont, Agnico, Barrick) plus mid-tier developers; full holdings disclosed in N-CSR/N-CSRS. The fund has chronically underperformed the Arca Gold Miners benchmark (-36% over 10 years per Saba's prior proxy) and has historically traded at a 10-20% discount to NAV. Saba won board seats in 2024 (Bruce, Desai, Donovan, Hoene, Kazarian); under standstill in Aug 2025, two directors (Hoene as chair, Donovan) resigned, with Karen Caldwell appointed. By Feb 2026 Saba had pushed to 29.86% — a stake that economically rivals the residual long-term holders.

Bear case: ASA is a sector fund tied to gold miners — if gold reverses, NAV drops faster than the discount narrows, and the activist setup becomes a value trap. The Bermuda domicile complicates US-style tender mechanics, and the fund has prior history of adopting a poison pill that Saba ultimately got struck under the Investment Company Act. Saba has won board seats but still hasn't forced a tender or liquidation; a multi-year siege with limited NAV monetization is a real outcome. Expense ratios are higher than ETF alternatives (GDX/GDXJ).

Bull case: at ~30% Saba ownership the calculus tips. Either (a) ASA agrees to a meaningful tender / Dutch auction at ~95-98% of NAV to relieve pressure, immediately closing most of the 10% discount, or (b) Saba escalates to seek conversion/liquidation. Either path is +8-12% before any gold-price move. With gold at multi-year highs, NAV momentum provides a tailwind even if the discount is sticky. Cushion is the underlying portfolio of liquid mining equities — true "look-through" liquidity rather than illiquid private assets.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The legal history matters: Saba previously sued ASA over the Dec 2023 poison pill, and a court ruled the rights plan violated the Investment Company Act of 1940. That precedent means the board cannot easily redeploy a defensive structure to block Saba — Saba has both the votes and the legal precedent. The 2025 standstill that produced two resignations was a tactical retreat by the board; the fact that Saba kept buying through the standstill window (17.24% → 29.86%) signals it views the standstill as a stepping stone, not a settlement. The DEF 14A filed Sep 26, 2025 is the prior annual proxy — the next annual meeting is the live battleground.
