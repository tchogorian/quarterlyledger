### ANALYST SUMMARY
Janus Henderson is a $7.9B-market-cap global asset manager (US/UK dual-listed; FCA-regulated) being taken private at $52 cash by Trian Fund Management (existing 20.6% holder) and General Catalyst. Initial deal announced December 22, 2025 at $49/share / $7.4B. After Victory Capital launched an $8.6B counter, Trian raised to $52 cash on March 24, 2026; Victory walked away. Shareholders approved the deal with 99.7% support on April 16, 2026 — representing 83% of total shares outstanding voting in favor. Expected close is mid-2026 (Q2/Q3), pending UK FCA, US SEC and client mandate consents. A $1/share quarterly dividend kicks in if the deal does not close by June 30, 2026 — a meaningful tick-rate sweetener.

The bear case is the spread itself — at ~85 bps gross with the vote already passed, the absolute return is small and the calendar-time arb is unattractive unless leveraged. Asset-management mandate transfers require client consent on a contract-by-contract basis — large institutional clients (sovereign wealth funds, pension consultants) sometimes use take-private events to re-bid mandates, creating real AUM-leakage risk. FCA review of UK regulated entities can drag. If a major client defects mid-deal, Trian could in theory re-trade, though the merger agreement structure typically locks the price.

The bull thesis is high deal certainty with a tick-rate floor: 99.7% approval is essentially a referendum vote, Trian is a 20.6% existing holder with deep PE pockets and "only actionable proposal" language explicitly stated, the $1/share quarterly dividend post-June 30 protects against close-date slippage, and the Victory walk-away removed the last interloper risk. For arb desks running modest leverage, the annualized spread (2.5-5% depending on close timing) is acceptable for an event with regulatory-only risk and no antitrust concern. Tail-risk upside from a re-emergent bidder is small but non-zero.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The $1/share quarterly dividend trigger if the deal does not close by June 30, 2026 is a structural protection most arb screens miss — it converts calendar-time risk into cash yield. Trian's "only actionable proposal" language in its March 24 press release is signal that the special committee's process is closed and Victory will not return. Client-consent risk (the actual remaining variable) cannot be measured from public filings — investors must monitor Form ADV amendments and read sub-advisory contract language to understand which mandates require affirmative consent vs. passive consent. UK Takeover Code adds procedural certainty (no last-minute material adverse change disputes typical of US deals).
