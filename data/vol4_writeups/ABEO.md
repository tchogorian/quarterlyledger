### ANALYST SUMMARY
Abeona is now a commercial-stage gene therapy company. The lead asset, ZEVASKYN (prademagene zamikeracel, "pz-cel"), is an autologous, gene-modified cell therapy for recessive dystrophic epidermolysis bullosa (RDEB) — an ultra-rare, devastating skin-blistering genetic disease. The FDA approved ZEVASKYN on April 28, 2025; commercial launch began in Q4 2025 with the first commercial patient treated in December. Total 2025 revenue was $5.8M ($3.4M license + $2.4M product), reflecting only one commercial patient. The screen captures the name on $166M net cash / 54% of market cap and a 5.4 TTM P/E driven entirely by the $152.4M PRV sale gain — not recurring operations. Underlying operating burn remained meaningful (mid-double-digit millions annually).

The bear case: ZEVASKYN is a complex autologous therapy requiring biopsy, gene modification ex vivo, and graft application at qualified treatment centers. Each treatment is logistics-heavy and surgical — slow ramp is the base case. The addressable US RDEB population is roughly 750–1,000 patients, with maybe 200–300 with severe enough wounds to warrant the procedure (only ~30 patients were initially identified by the company). Reimbursement at the multi-million-dollar list price requires payer education for what is a one-time-per-wound therapy. Competition exists — Krystal Bio's Vyjuvek (a topical gene therapy approved 2023) addresses the same disease with very different administration. Real underlying loss without PRV is ~$80M annually; cash funds operations into 2027 base case.

The bull case: the entire enterprise value is roughly $140M ($307M cap minus $166M net cash) for an FDA-approved gene therapy in an ultra-rare disease with multi-million-dollar pricing power. If Abeona treats just 20–30 patients per year at peak, run-rate revenue would be $50–100M+ — and the launch infrastructure (treatment center activations) is the gating item, not approval or efficacy. Beyond ZEVASKYN, the AAV204 capsid platform offers preclinical optionality. The PRV sale already de-risked cash through the launch ramp window; another 1–2 years of treatment center activations and patient throughput data could justify a multi-hundred-million dollar revaluation if the launch executes.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 5.4 P/E is non-recurring — it's the PRV sale. Strip out the $152.4M one-time gain and Abeona is structurally loss-making until ZEVASKYN reaches commercial scale. The screen also doesn't show that "approved gene therapy with $191M cash" is fundamentally different from a Phase 2 binary: the binary is now operational (can Abeona activate treatment centers and execute the surgical workflow at scale?) rather than scientific (does the therapy work?). Treatment center activation pace and Q1/Q2 2026 patient counts are the actual leading indicators — not press releases. Underlying cash burn (~$80M/yr) means runway is roughly 24 months absent further milestones; that's the real clock.
