### ANALYST SUMMARY

Cannae was spun from Fidelity National Financial in 2017 as Bill Foley's standalone investment vehicle. Foley's track record (FNF, Black Knight, MSG-style M&A acumen) gives Cannae an unusual proprietary-deal pipeline. The portfolio has rotated dramatically — from a public-stake bag (D&B, Ceridian/Dayforce, Alight) into private operating businesses, with Black Knight Football Club (BKFC) now the cornerstone. BKFC owns 100% of AFC Bournemouth (Premier League), 100% of FC Lorient (Ligue 1), majority of Moreirense (Portugal), with strategic affiliations to Auckland FC, Orlando City SC, Kyoto Sanga. TTM revenue at BKFC ~$267m. Cannae self-publishes monthly SOTP and has been buying back stock aggressively below NAV.

The bear case centers on related-party governance and asset opacity. Trasimene Capital — a Foley-affiliated management company — collects fees on Cannae assets, a structure activist Carronade explicitly criticized. Multiple private buckets (BKFC, Restaurant Group, JANA, consumer brands) are hard for outside analysts to mark, creating a "trust me" valuation discount. European football economics are notoriously cash-burn-heavy; Premier League survival is not guaranteed. The 2025 AGM saw the management pay proposal rejected — a positive for shareholders but a sign of structural conflict. Liquidity is thin (~$680m mcap) and float-shrinkage further reduces tradability.

The bull case is multi-layered. ~$500m returned to shareholders in 21 months and the float retired by ~34% — a true buyback machine below NAV. The Restaurant Group is under strategic review (read: likely sale). BKFC is the asymmetric upside — recent European football transactions (Chelsea minority sale at $3B+ EV; Manchester United partial sale; Premier League franchise valuations rising sharply) suggest a single comp event could multi-bag the implied football value. Carronade winning board seats means more shareholder-aligned capital allocation. With ~$200m+ in cash and Treasuries plus public stakes, the implied private-asset value is meaningfully discounted.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The February 23, 2026 SOTP refresh confirmed the BKFC mark at $249m invested for ~42% economics — implying group EV around $590m. AFC Bournemouth's 2024-25 Premier League finish + new TV deal ramp could re-rate BKFC alone by 30-50% within 18 months. The Trasimene management agreement is worth reading in proxy footnotes — Carronade's activist letters argue it transfers ~$10-15m/yr of value to Foley affiliates. The 2025 AGM "no" vote on pay is a rare governance win at a Foley vehicle. Note that JANA Partners' Cannae stake is itself an interesting kicker — Cannae owns equity in an activist firm. Watkins beverage and Minden Mill bourbon are early-stage consumer bets; Sightline Payments was written down materially — a reminder that not every Foley deal works.
