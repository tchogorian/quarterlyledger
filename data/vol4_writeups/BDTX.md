### ANALYST SUMMARY
Black Diamond is a clinical-stage precision oncology company built around its MasterKey allosteric kinase inhibitor platform. After an October 2024 restructuring that cut headcount and refocused the pipeline, the entire investment case is now BDTX-1535 — a fourth-generation EGFR inhibitor designed for non-classical EGFR mutations and osimertinib-resistant disease. The screen flags it because the GAAP TTM net income (~$22M) is non-operating: it reflects upfront and milestone recognition from the Servier BDTX-4933 deal, not commercial revenue. The $109.9M net cash represents two-thirds of market cap, and the company guided runway into 2H 2028 as of the Q3 2025 print ($135.5M cash, equivalents, and investments).

The bear case is straightforward biotech binary risk. BDTX-1535 has shown encouraging early Phase 2 numbers, but durability of response (DOR) and progression-free survival (PFS) — the data that actually drive registration — won't read out until Q2 2026. The EGFR-mutant NSCLC space is crowded: J&J's Rybrevant (amivantamab) is approved and combined with lazertinib is the new standard of care challenger; Takeda's Exkivity was withdrawn from market in 2023 (a sobering precedent); sunvozertinib and furmonertinib are advancing as well. A miss on durability or competitive labeling tilts the equity toward cash value with optionality discount.

The bull case rests on three pillars. First, BDTX-1535 attacks a differentiated niche — non-classical / compound EGFR mutations and osimertinib-resistance — where current therapies underperform. Second, FDA feedback in Q4 2025 on a pivotal path could unlock a registrational design in 1H 2026, accelerating the value-recognition timeline. Third, downside is mathematically protected: $110M net cash on a $167M cap = $1.66/share of cash floor, so a clean clinical wash leaves ~30% downside before management discretion erodes it. The Servier BDTX-4933 milestone tail provides additional non-dilutive optionality.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 7.3 P/E is a mirage. TTM net income is dominated by Servier BDTX-4933 license revenue recognition — there is no recurring product revenue and operating cash burn is meaningful (the runway-extending impact of the Servier deal pushed cash from "into Q4 2026" guidance to "into 2H 2028"). The screen also misses that BDTX-1535 is NOT positioned in EGFR exon 20 insertion (where Rybrevant and the now-withdrawn Exkivity sit) — the company explicitly targets PACC/compound mutations and osimertinib-resistance, a smaller but less crowded niche. Finally, the restructuring shed the rest of the pipeline, so a single Phase 2 readout dictates equity outcome.
