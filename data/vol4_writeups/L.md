### THE SETUP
The Tisch family's quiet compounder. Loews owns 92% of CNA Financial (~$12B at market), 100% of Boardwalk Pipeline (interstate gas pipelines, ~$11-13B private), 100% of Loews Hotels (Universal Orlando partner, riding the Epic Universe ramp), 100% of Altium Packaging, and ~$2-3B of HoldCo cash with no parent debt. Year-end 2025 book value/share (ex-AOCI) was $95.89; SOTP NAV is well above $140. The Tisches retired 8.9M shares for $782m in 2025 — ~4% of the float in one year. Slow, patient, and underestimated.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Loews is a Tisch-family conglomerate (descendants of Larry & Bob Tisch) with the smallest holdco discount of the five names but the highest quality of disclosed assets. CNA is a mid-sized P&C and specialty insurer trading roughly at book — CNA pays Loews ~$700m/yr in dividends including periodic specials. Boardwalk Pipeline is a pure-play interstate natural-gas system (~14,000 miles, FERC-regulated) that Loews took private in 2018 at a controversial low; it now generates ~$1B of EBITDA and at FERC-pipe multiples of 11-13x is worth $11-13B. Loews Hotels operates ~26 hotels including the high-margin Universal Orlando JV — Epic Universe opened in 2025, materially lifting RevPAR and ADR for the JV. Altium Packaging is a plastic-bottle and rigid-packaging manufacturer.

The bear case is the perpetual Tisch-discount and slow-monetization risk. CNA is sub-scale in a hardening but consolidation-prone P&C market — it could be a structural laggard vs Travelers/Chubb. Boardwalk operates in a regulated, low-growth segment with rate-case overhang and energy-transition headlines. Hotels are cyclical. The Tisches almost never sell — Boardwalk re-IPO talk has circulated for years without action. Book value/share growth has been steady but not explosive (~6-9%/yr). The 25-30% discount has persisted for decades; mean reversion is slow. Insurance-driven NAV is sensitive to catastrophe losses (CNA had elevated cat-loss years recently).

The bull case is compounding-machine math. Year-end 2025 BVPS (ex-AOCI) was $95.89, up from $88.18 a year earlier — ~9% growth. Loews bought back 8.9M shares for $782m in 2025 — accretive to BVPS by mid-single-digits annually. CNA's $700m+ dividend stream funds those buybacks. Boardwalk would IPO at a premium when rates fall — even a partial 20-30% IPO at infrastructure multiples would crystallize $2-3B of currently-hidden value. Loews Hotels is the surprise upside — Universal Epic Universe (opened May 2025) is driving record Orlando attendance and RevPAR; Loews has six on-site Universal hotels with management agreements. The discount is narrower than peers because the assets are higher-quality and disclosure is cleaner.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The Boardwalk Pipeline carrying value on Loews' balance sheet is dramatically below its mark-to-market private-pipeline equivalent. Loews disclosed in its 2025 10-K (filed Feb 10, 2026) that Boardwalk delivered "record" results in 2025. Universal Epic Universe (opened May 22, 2025) is Loews Hotels' biggest organic catalyst in a decade — the Loews/Universal JV has six hotels at the resort with another opening in 2026. CNA paid a $1.00/share special dividend in early 2026 (in addition to the regular $0.46 quarterly) — this is the cash funnel back to Loews. Larry Tisch's grandson, Andrew Tisch, and brother James Tisch (CEO) and Jonathan Tisch run the firm; family owns ~22% of shares and ~25% of votes. The annual letter is a Buffett-like read on insurance and energy — read it. Note: parent-level debt of ~$1.8B is modest vs HoldCo cash of $2.5B+, so net cash positive.
