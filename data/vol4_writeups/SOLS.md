### ANALYST SUMMARY
Solstice Advanced Materials operates two segments: Refrigerants & Applied Solutions (~72% of net sales) and Electronic & Specialty Materials (~28%). The R&AS segment makes the Solstice family of HFO low-GWP refrigerants (1234yf for automotive AC, ze and yf blends for stationary cooling, foam blowing agents) plus Genetron HFC legacy and applied solutions. ES&M makes electronic materials for advanced-node semiconductors (specialty fluids, photoresist materials, wet etchants) and Spectra advanced fibers. Full-year 2025 net sales were $3.9B (+3% YoY); Q4 was $987M (+8%) with refrigerants +10% and electronic materials +4%. Q4 net income was $41M. Solstice issued $1B of 5.625% senior notes due 2033 to fund a one-time dividend back to Honeywell, leaving a manageable but real ~3x EBITDA leverage.

Bear case: the 1234yf refrigerant story has a clock on it. The first composition-of-matter patents are already expiring; Chinese producers (Zhejiang Huanxin, Sino-Resource) and Arkema have been challenging continuously; even with EPO upholding the Solstice process patent, generic competition will pressure pricing into the late 2020s. R&AS at 72% of revenue means margin compression there hits the whole company. Electronic Materials is small (~$1.1B) and tied to leading-edge semi capex which is cyclical. The carve-out debt ($1B at 5.625%) plus the spin dividend creates real interest expense. Honeywell's cost-allocation disciplines may not survive intact at the standalone level.

Bull/catalyst: refrigerants are levered to two structural megatrends: data-center liquid cooling (every hyperscaler is moving from air to liquid; Solstice has IP-protected dielectric fluids) and EV thermal management (1234yf is the auto-AC standard worldwide). The IP portfolio has 1,400+ issued/pending patents with composition-and-use protection extending into the 2030s and 2040s — the bear-case "patent cliff" is real but staggered, not sudden. Electronic Materials touches every advanced-node semiconductor (TSMC, Samsung, Intel, Micron) and grew 5% in a soft semi year. ~25%+ EBITDA margins, $3.9B revenue at ~$12.9B mkt cap = ~3.3x sales / ~13x EBITDA — cheap for a specialty chemicals leader. BMO initiated Outperform at $70 PT; broader generalist coverage is still missing. S&P MidCap eligibility once 6-month seasoning completes (~late April 2026).

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-12B/A and 10-K reveal that Solstice's IP portfolio is structured as 350 composition-of-matter cases plus 1,000+ process and use patents — meaning even after composition patents expire, the manufacturing process and end-use applications remain protected into the mid-2030s and beyond. The February 2026 EPO ruling upheld the foundational process patent, removing the largest near-term legal overhang. Filings also confirm Spectra advanced fibers (used in body armor, ropes, ballistic composites) is reported inside ES&M but is functionally a defense/industrial business with totally different cycle dynamics — sell-side chemicals analysts mis-classify it. Lastly, the $1B note coupon of 5.625% due 2033 is meaningfully cheaper than where SOLS could refinance today on a standalone basis — embedded value.
