### THE SETUP
The largest cement, ready-mix and roofing supplier in North America was just spun out of a Swiss parent whose European holders sold on receipt because they wanted EU/EM exposure, not U.S. construction. AMRZ generated $1.5B of free cash flow in 2025, posted 1.1x net leverage at year-end, and management proposed a $1B buyback. At ~9x EBITDA vs. U.S. pure-play peers (MLM, VMC, EXP, SUM) at 13-18x, the dual-listing index orphan discount is the largest mispricing in the spin bucket.

### SPINOFF MECHANICS
- Parent: Holcim Ltd. (HCMLY) — kept the rest-of-world cement business
- Spin date: June 23, 2025 — ~10 months seasoned
- Distribution ratio: 1 AMRZ share per 1 Holcim share via dividend-in-kind to holders of record June 20, 2025

- Forced selling: European Holcim holders dumped USD-denominated shares because mandates require EU/EM exposure; dual-listing creates orphan status (not in S&P 500, not in Stoxx); initial sell-side coverage was lukewarm with hold ratings; CHF-denominated holders also faced FX hedging friction

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Amrize is the consolidated U.S. and Canadian operations of the former Holcim — Lafarge, Holcim cement, ready-mix, aggregates, plus the building envelope/roofing business assembled from years of bolt-on acquisitions (Firestone Building Products, Malarkey, Duro-Last). 2024 revenue was $11.7B, adjusted EBITDA $3.2B (27% margin), free cash flow $1.7B, with 13%/16%/15% three-year CAGRs respectively. The company emerged with a $3.4B debt facility plus $2B revolver and $2B commercial-paper program; year-end 2025 net debt was $3.35B against $5.27B gross debt and $1.9B cash, for a net leverage ratio of 1.1x — investment-grade rated BBB+/Baa1 stable. March 2026 mid-term targets call for 5-8% sales growth and 8-11% EBITDA growth through 2028.

Bear case: cement is a brutally cyclical, energy-intensive commodity with regional pricing wars when capacity overshoots. The U.S. residential construction cycle is rolling over and the IIJA infrastructure spend has been slower to deploy than promised. Roofing margins in particular are exposed to asphalt and oil prices. AMRZ also inherits legacy environmental liabilities — cement is one of the largest CO2-emitting industries and carbon pricing in California, Washington, and a future federal regime would compress margins. Capex to maintain 1,000+ sites is meaningful and any large acquisition risks the BBB+ rating.

Bull/catalyst: $2T/yr U.S. construction spend with $1.2T of IIJA + CHIPS + IRA tailwinds is the most favorable cement demand backdrop in 50 years. AMRZ holds #1 U.S. cement position with regional moats (cement is uneconomical to truck >300 miles, so each plant has near-monopoly pricing in its rim). Cement shortages on the Gulf and Mountain regions translate directly to pricing power. The proposed $1B buyback represents 3% of cap and signals confidence. Standalone US-focused capital allocation, US analyst initiations (vs sleepy Swiss coverage), and eventual S&P MidCap or 500 inclusion all close the multiple gap. CRH trades at 11x EBITDA, MLM/VMC at 14-18x — half-way to peers means $80+ on AMRZ.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K confirms AMRZ ended 2025 with net debt of $3.35B and $1.5B of FCF — implying an unlevered FCF yield over 6% and rapid optionality to repurchase stock or do M&A. Net interest expense fell from $512M to $413M YoY — pre-spin allocation was punitively high and the standalone structure is already cheaper to fund. The 1,000-site footprint includes proprietary aggregates reserves whose book value has not been re-marked to current replacement cost in decades — embedded inflation accounting alpha that pure-play peers MLM and VMC have already monetized via re-rating. Building-envelope (roofing) is now ~25% of profit and margin profile resembles ROOF/IBP rather than a cement bag, which sell-side cement analysts systematically under-value.
