# {NUMBER}. MAGN — Magnera Corporation
## SPINOFF (from Berry Global / Glatfelter merger) | Spin Date: 2024-11-05 | Score: 70/100 | POSITION SIZE: $2,000

### THE SETUP
Magnera is the Reverse Morris Trust child of Berry Global's Health-Hygiene-Specialties Global Nonwovens & Films business merging with Glatfelter — creating the world's largest nonwovens producer at a sub-$400M micro-cap orphan. Berry shareholders received tiny stubs (~10% of their Berry value), couldn't add to a sub-$1B no-yield position, and dumped indiscriminately. Result: ~$1.7B of net debt loaded onto $370M EBITDA looks scary on screens, but it trades at <3x EBITDA with a synergy/refi self-help story the market hasn't underwritten.

### SPINOFF MECHANICS
- Parent: Berry Global Group (BERY) merging with Glatfelter Corporation (GLT)
- Spin date: 2024-11-04 / 2024-11-05 (~5.5 months ago)
- Distribution structure: Reverse Morris Trust — Berry shareholders received MAGN shares pro rata, then MAGN merged with Glatfelter; final ownership split roughly 90% former Berry HHS shareholders / 10% former Glatfelter
- Initial trading: opened around $19-20 on 11/5/24; currently ~$11 (roughly halved)
- Key reasons for forced selling: Berry shareholders received tiny pro-rata MAGN stubs that were small relative to their BERY position and below institutional minimums; Glatfelter had a stigma from prior operational issues; high leverage (~4-5x) scared quality investors; no sell-side coverage from major banks; micro-cap size ($380M) keeps it off most institutional screens; index ejection (Glatfelter was in Russell indices, MAGN had to re-qualify)

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Magnera produces engineered materials and nonwovens used primarily in hygiene products (baby diapers, adult incontinence, feminine care), wipes, healthcare/medical, food packaging, and specialty industrial applications. The combined company runs roughly 45 facilities globally with leadership positions in spunmelt nonwovens, airlaid (wipes substrate), and specialty papers. Pro-forma revenue is approximately $3.0B with EBITDA around $370M — about 12% margins. The merger thesis was stated $55M+ of cost synergies over 2-3 years, plus working capital release and footprint rationalization. Customers include the global hygiene majors — Procter & Gamble, Kimberly-Clark, Essity — which is both a moat (qualified supplier on multi-year programs) and a concentration risk.

The bear case is meaningful: leverage is high at roughly 4.5-5x net debt / EBITDA, refinancing risk exists with a chunk of debt at high coupons coming due in 2027-2029, and Glatfelter's pre-merger track record of operational issues (Spezialpapiere fire, cost overruns) leaves a stigma. Customer concentration with P&G and Kimberly-Clark (collectively likely >25% of revenue) creates pricing pressure as those customers continually re-bid contracts. Nonwovens is commoditized at the low end and faces low-cost Asian competition. EBITDA margins in the low teens are not impressive vs. specialty packaging peers in the high teens. A delay in synergy realization or a working-capital surprise would tip the leverage ratio into ratings-downgrade territory.

The bull case rests on three legs: (1) cost synergies — only ~30% realized at spin, with the bulk of plant rationalization and procurement savings landing in 2025-26 and dropping straight to EBITDA; (2) deleveraging — every $50M of debt paydown materially shrinks equity risk and unlocks multiple expansion at this leverage level; (3) refi optionality — current high-coupon debt (8%+ on portions) could refinance 200+ bps lower in a stable rate environment, adding meaningful FCF. EV/EBITDA below 6x vs. specialty packaging peers (BERY itself, SEE, AMCR) at 9-11x leaves significant re-rating room. With ~$50M of EBITDA improvement and a 1-turn multiple expansion, equity could double from current levels.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Form 10/S-4 details: (1) Capital structure — Berry pushed approximately $1.5-1.7B of debt down into MAGN at separation (the classic "RMT debt dump") in the form of senior secured notes and term loans; the cash transfer back to Berry was the entire economic point of the structure for BERY shareholders and explains the leverage. (2) Customer contracts with the hygiene majors are typically 2-3 year MSAs with quarterly volume true-ups; pricing typically passes through resin (polypropylene) costs with a 1-quarter lag, which protects margins on the way up but can squeeze on the way down. (3) Transition Services Agreement with Berry covers shared services for up to 24 months — G&A run-rate will normalize higher in 2025-26 as MAGN stands up standalone systems. (4) Glatfelter's prior environmental remediation liabilities (legacy paper mills) transferred into MAGN — disclosed in risk factors at modest amounts but worth monitoring.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Quarterly synergy realization updates (target: $55M+ run-rate by end of 2026)
- Refinancing of post-spin debt at lower coupons (likely opportunistic in 2025-26)
- Divestiture of non-core mills (specialty paper assets ex-Glatfelter)
- First analyst initiations from packaging coverage teams at major banks
- Working capital release in 2025 driving FCF inflection
- Possible activist involvement given micro-cap + cheap valuation + clear self-help
