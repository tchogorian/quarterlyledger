### ANALYST SUMMARY
Compugen is an Israeli computational antibody discovery platform — its proprietary algorithms predict drug targets from genomic data, and the company validates and out-licenses them. The crown jewel is COM701/rilvegostomig: a TIGIT/PD-1 bispecific antibody licensed to AstraZeneca, now the most advanced anti-TIGIT asset in industry, with 11 Phase 3 trials across NSCLC, GI, and endometrial cancers. A second asset, COM902 (anti-TIGIT mAb), is partnered with Gilead. The screen captures Compugen on $142.7M net cash / 52% of market cap and a 7.6 P/E — the GAAP profitability comes from the December 2025 royalty monetization with HealthCare Royalty, not recurring operations. As of December 31, 2025, cash was $145.6M and management guides runway into 2029.

The bear case: TIGIT as a target has been a graveyard. Roche's tiragolumab failed multiple Phase 3s; Merck's vibostolimab data has been mixed; Gilead's domvanalimab is ongoing but watched with skepticism. AstraZeneca's rilvegostomig is the bispecific bet, but the broader class signal-to-noise is poor and a high-profile Phase 3 miss would compress the platform multiple immediately. Secondary: Compugen has limited control over partner-driven Phase 3 timing, the royalty stream Compugen retained is now diluted (sold a portion), and the company is a foreign private issuer (Israel) with semi-annual reporting cadence under the 20-F regime, plus regional geopolitical overlay.

The bull case is structurally compelling. The $65M HCRx payment is non-dilutive and validates the rilvegostomig royalty stream's NPV at well above current EV. Enterprise value of ~$129M values the residual royalty (mid-single-digit tiered), retained $195M milestone potential, the entire discovery platform, and the Gilead-partnered COM902 program at less than the price of a single Phase 3 monetization tranche. The 11 Phase 3 trials create a portfolio of binary readouts in 2026–2028; even one positive registrational outcome (especially in biliary tract or NSCLC) would re-rate the equity meaningfully. Cash to 2029 removes financing overhang, a critical de-risker for small biotech.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 7.6 P/E is entirely from the December 2025 royalty monetization upfront — no recurring product revenue exists. The $195M of retained milestones from AstraZeneca is contingent and not on the balance sheet, but represents the real value driver. The screen also can't see that Compugen is a foreign private issuer reporting under IFRS — the cash figure and "net cash" calculation differ slightly from US GAAP screen conventions. Most importantly, the rilvegostomig royalty rate is mid-single-digit tiered (Compugen sold a portion to HCRx but retained "the majority"); the precise royalty rate Compugen still owns is not fully disclosed, which is the single most important undisclosed number for valuation.
