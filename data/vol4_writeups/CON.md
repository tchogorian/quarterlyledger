### THE SETUP
Concentra is the largest occupational-health platform in the US (549 medical centers + 156 onsite employer clinics) — a high-margin, recession-resistant B2B niche that was buried inside Select Medical's hospital-multiple discount. The separation was unusual: a partial IPO in July 2024 followed by the November 2024 distribution of SEM's remaining ~80% stake, creating two waves of forced selling. SEM holders own SEM for inpatient rehab and acute care, not workers'-comp clinics — so the spun shares went straight to the bid.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Concentra operates the largest network of occupational-health centers in the US — 549 medical centers across 41 states plus 156 onsite clinics located inside employer facilities (manufacturing plants, distribution centers, corporate campuses). The business is dominated by two recurring revenue streams: workers'-compensation injury care (treating job injuries, billed to employers' workers'-comp carriers) and employer services (DOT physicals for commercial drivers, drug screens, pre-employment physicals, vaccinations, etc.). Approximately 75-80% of revenue comes from employer-paid services rather than insurance reimbursement, which means pricing is negotiated B2B on annual contracts rather than dictated by Medicare/commercial payer rate schedules. Revenue is approximately $1.8B with adjusted EBITDA margins in the high teens. The company serves over 200,000 employer clients with no single client above ~3% of revenue.

The bear case: workers'-comp medical spending has been in secular decline as US workplace injuries (especially in industrial sectors that are shrinking as a share of GDP) have trended down for decades. DOT physicals are stable but not a growth market. Same-clinic visit growth has been low-single-digit for years; the growth story depends on M&A roll-up of independent operators and onsite-clinic expansion, both of which require execution. The company carries meaningful debt from the spin (~$1.5B in term loans/notes) — leverage is around 3.5x EBITDA, manageable but limits dividend growth. Carve-out IPO comp structures often unwind in the first 12-18 months as management turns over.

The bull case: this is a utility-like, hyper-recurring revenue business at a healthcare-services-discount multiple. DOT physical mandates and workers'-comp regulatory requirements create non-discretionary, regulation-driven demand. Margin profile is mid-teens EBITDA with modest capex (~3% of revenue) — strong FCF conversion. The TAM for onsite clinics is vast and growing as large employers consolidate health benefits with single vendors. CON is the only public pure-play in occupational health, making it the de facto consolidator in a highly fragmented market (~10,000+ independent occ-health practices). At 8-9x EV/EBITDA vs healthcare-services peers (USPH, ACHC, ENSG) at 11-13x, the discount is real and the catalyst path (M&A, onsite wins, first investor day) is concrete.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Form 10/S-1 details: (1) Capital structure — Concentra raised approximately $585M in IPO proceeds in July 2024, of which roughly $523M was used to repay intercompany debt to SEM (effectively a dividend recap), then issued ~$1.5B of senior notes and term loans pre-distribution, also largely upstreamed to SEM; this is a classic "parent strips cash from spin" mechanic that explains why the leverage is what it is. (2) Long-term agreements with SEM include shared services (IT, certain HR, real estate) for up to 24 months at cost-plus pricing; the headline G&A run-rate will normalize as Concentra stands up standalone functions. (3) Workers'-comp regulatory framework is state-by-state — about 25 states have "employer choice" of provider (favorable to CON) vs ~25 with "employee choice" (less favorable); this granularity is in the risk factors and matters for the M&A geographic strategy. (4) Top employer clients include Fortune-100 manufacturers and logistics companies; 200,000+ clients with no single client >3% — true diversification. (5) Management holds meaningful equity post-spin and the comp plan is heavily weighted to EBITDA growth and same-center volume.
