### ANALYST SUMMARY
First Industrial owns ~67M square feet of industrial real estate concentrated in 15 major U.S. logistics markets (Southern California, South Florida, Dallas, Northern New Jersey, Seattle). Pure-play distribution/warehouse REIT — no office, retail, or specialty exposure. At $63.75 the equity is ~$8.7B; with ~$2.4B net debt the EV is ~$11B. Trades at ~6.0% implied cap rate vs. industrial peers (Prologis, Rexford, Terreno) at 4.5-5.5% — the spread implies either FR's cash flows are lower-quality, or it's mispriced. Litt's NAV math says mispriced. Industrial REIT sentiment cratered in 2024-2025 on (a) post-COVID warehouse oversupply, (b) Amazon's pullback on incremental space, (c) tariff/onshoring uncertainty, (d) capital-rotation away from REITs into AI / data center comps.

**Bear case:** The industrial NAV discount is rational, not anomalous. Sublease space across major logistics markets sits at multi-year highs. New supply continued to deliver through 2024-2025 even as demand slowed; rent growth in core markets has decelerated from ~10% (2022) to ~3-4% (2025) and could go negative in oversupplied submarkets. FR's portfolio, while quality, is not the trophy-grade Class-A that Prologis owns; its same-store NOI growth lags. Litt's track record is real but his last few campaigns (Aimco, Apartment Income REIT) ended in board-level frustration without deal outcomes. Take-private interest from Blackstone/Brookfield is more selective in 2026 than in 2021 — REIT M&A volumes are down significantly. Even if Litt wins his board seat, six months is a tight deadline for a strategic process.

**Bull case:** Industrial real estate is the right asset class long-term — onshoring, e-commerce penetration still rising, last-mile demand structural. The supply wave is digestible: starts have collapsed in 2024-2025 (~50% off peak), implying tighter market by 2027. FR's portfolio is concentrated in supply-constrained coastal markets where land scarcity matters most. The NAV discount opens the door to either (a) accretive asset sales at private market cap rates funding buybacks, (b) full take-private by Blackstone (which already owns ~$70B of industrial via BREIT), or (c) merger with a peer (Terreno, Rexford). Path to re-rate: (1) Litt board win May/June 2026, (2) $500M+ asset disposition announcement at sub-5% cap rate proves NAV math, (3) take-private bid emerges by Q4 2026.
