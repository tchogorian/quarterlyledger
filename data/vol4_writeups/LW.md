### ANALYST SUMMARY
Lamb Weston supplies frozen potato products (fries, hash browns, formed potato) to roughly 100+ countries — McDonald's is the largest customer (mid-teens of revenue), with Yum, restaurant chains, and retail rounding out the mix. The industry is a global duopoly with McCain, with Aviko and Simplot as smaller competitors. 2024-2025 was a perfect storm: a mismanaged ERP cutover that produced fill-rate misses with key customers, a global potato oversupply that crushed pricing, and weak QSR traffic globally. Trades at <8x normalized EBITDA versus packaged-food peers at 11-13x and prior LW peak multiples of 13-15x. The activist target was governance and operational discipline — too much capacity built into a soft cycle, weak cost controls.

Bear case: Frozen potato is more cyclical than the multiple suggests — global potato yields normalized after a strong 2024 crop, supply remains long, and 2026 contract pricing is pressured. McDonald's customer concentration is a structural risk if any service issue lingers or if QSR same-store traffic weakens further. Capacity additions (the new Idaho line, China expansion) hit just as demand softened. Net debt sits over $4B post-buybacks, limiting dividend/buyback flexibility. ERP/fill-rate damage to relationships may take longer than 2-3 quarters to fully repair, and Craps is unproven in US foodservice. If the turnaround stalls AND a sale process fails, the stock can stay range-bound at $40-50 indefinitely.

Bull case: Three lined-up catalysts. (1) Supply rationalization is happening — McCain has cut shifts, LW has cut output, and 2026 contract pricing should firm by Q3/Q4 fiscal 2026. (2) Craps brings Budweiser-APAC operating playbook (zero-based budgeting, cost discipline, customer service KPIs) — the kind of incremental margin recovery that re-rates a turnaround story. (3) JANA's standing threat to push for a sale is the floor — strategic suitors (Conagra, Mondelez, even a McCain combination) and PE (Apollo, KKR) all reportedly sniffed in 2024-25. Re-rate to 10-11x normalized EBITDA on $1.4-1.6B EBITDA equals $70-90 per share. Median analyst PT support exists.
