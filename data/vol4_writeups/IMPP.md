### THE SETUP
A debt-free Greek tanker/drybulk owner trading at roughly book value of cash. Imperial Petroleum closed FY25 with $179.1M of cash plus time deposits, zero bank debt, and just declared a $10M open-market buyback in February 2026 — all on a market cap that has at times sat *below* the cash line. The catalyst stack: a 26-vessel fleet on the water by year-end 2026, no covenants to choke capital returns, and a controlling shareholder who has finally pivoted from dilution to repurchase.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Imperial Petroleum (NASDAQ: IMPP), an Athens-based ship-owning company controlled by CEO Harry Vafias, transports petroleum products, crude oil and dry bulk cargo. The fleet stood at 19 vessels by year-end 2025 (seven MR product tankers, two Suezmax tankers, and ten drybulk carriers) with one Post Panamax delivery in January 2026 lifting the count to 20 — and a contracted pipeline to reach 26 vessels by year-end. FY25 produced revenue of $161.0M (up 9.2% YoY), net income of $50.0M, EBITDA of $71.0M and operating cash flow of $80.8M. The screen flags net cash > market cap (~$179M vs ~$158M, ratio 1.13x). At a P/E of ~3.4x reported earnings and ~3.0x adjusted, IMPP is among the cheapest profitable cash-rich names in the public market — even after a brutal 2024–25 share dilution that took weighted-average count from 30M to 38M.

The bear case is real and well-documented in the 6-K. Tanker rates are violently cyclical and "ongoing attacks by Houthis in the Red Sea and Gulf of Aden" remain a live risk explicitly named by management. Vafias-affiliated entities provide the company's commercial management, vessel management and ship brokering — and the press release shows $5.9M of related-party voyage/operating/management fees in FY25, with another related-party "Investment in related party" of $13.0M sitting on the balance sheet. The float is dual-class with Series B preferred granting outsized control to Vafias; minority holders have been diluted repeatedly via warrants, ATM offerings and a $60M registered direct in 2025. The drybulk pivot also added depreciation, lifting D&A from $17.0M to $25.9M and pulling reported net income flat YoY despite the revenue growth.

The bull case rests on three pillars. First: $179M cash + zero debt = downside protection that is genuinely hard to vaporize even in a tanker downturn. Second: management explicitly disclosed *current* cash of "about $198 million" as of the March 6, 2026 press release — i.e., cash is *building*, not bleeding. Third: capital allocation finally turned shareholder-friendly. The $10M buyback (251,625 shares already repurchased for $0.9M as of disclosure) plus declared 8.75% Series A preferred dividends signal that further common dividends or buybacks are plausible if the share price keeps trading at a discount to NAV. Six more vessel deliveries in 2026 = scaled earnings power into a tanker market that, per the CEO, was "firm particularly in the second half of 2025."

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The "cash" line is now mostly time deposits ($173.3M) — only $5.8M is actual cash and equivalents — because management has been laddering proceeds to harvest interest income ($7.3M in FY25). That's positive for return-on-cash but means the company is one bad rate cycle from giving back its non-operating earnings buffer. Second hidden item: the $13.0M "Investment in related party" generates a $760K dividend stream and is *not* truly liquid. Third — and the genuine alpha for filing-readers — Vafias's earlier vessel acquisitions used *seller financing* booked at imputed-interest discounts, which is why FY25 has a $2.3M "Interest expense – related party" line despite zero bank debt. All of this was repaid in 2025; going forward, the income statement should look cleaner. Finally, the buyback authorization is small ($10M) relative to the cash pile — it is a price-support gesture, not a recapitalization.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Six contracted vessel deliveries through 2026 lifting earnings power on the existing fleet utilization base of ~91.8% in Q4 25.
- Continued execution of the $10M repurchase program (only $0.9M used as of March 6, 2026 disclosure).
- Q1 2026 6-K — first quarter as a fully-debt-free 20+ vessel operator; tanker rates and any incremental dividend declaration the key items.
- Geopolitical wild card: any escalation/de-escalation in Red Sea routing impacts ton-mile demand directly.
