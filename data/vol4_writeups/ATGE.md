### ANALYST SUMMARY
Adtalem operates four core institutions — Chamberlain University (the largest US nursing program by graduation), Walden University (online; the asset being acquired), Ross University School of Medicine and Ross University School of Veterinary Medicine, and American University of the Caribbean — focused exclusively on healthcare/professional education end markets. Post the 2022 ATI divestiture, the business is high-quality, cash-generative, and ride the structural tailwind of US nursing and clinician shortages. Q2 fiscal 2026 EPS up 35% year-on-year demonstrates the standalone earnings power. Trades around 14-15x earnings — historically deserved a premium valuation given 90%+ of degrees are in growing healthcare verticals; activist thesis is that capital allocation discipline (buybacks over a questionable acquisition) drives the next leg of multiple expansion.

Bear case: For-profit higher education is permanently politically exposed — Department of Education rule-making, 90/10 rule, gainful employment regulations, and federal investigation overhang at Walden are real risks even outside the acquisition. If management closes the Walden deal over activist objections, integration costs, federal investigation contingent liabilities, and brand-impairment risk all materialize. The for-profit education category multiple ceiling has historically been low. If the strong Q2 earnings momentum decelerates (enrollment softens, Chamberlain growth normalizes), the standalone re-rate thesis weakens. Activists are 1% holders — limited proxy leverage if management dismisses them.

Bull case: A clean asymmetric capital-allocation event. (1) If Adtalem walks from Walden using the federal-investigation MAC clause, the $1.5B is redeployed into buybacks at a ~$3.5B market cap — that is roughly 40%+ of float repurchasable, mathematically transformative for EPS and the multiple. The $750M authorization in December 2025 already signaled board willingness. (2) If Adtalem closes Walden cleanly, the deal does add scale in online programs and credit accretion (~$25/share NPV per the activist analysis) — even if the market does not fully credit it day-one, execution evidence over 12-18 months drives re-rating. (3) Q3/Q4 fiscal 2026 results plus the Investor Day in February 2026 provide near-term catalysts. Re-rate to 17-18x on $7-8 of forward EPS equals $120-140 per share — 20-40% upside.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The federal investigation into Walden is the legal exit ramp that screens cannot price. Most acquisition agreements contain a Material Adverse Change (MAC) clause, and a federal regulatory investigation that materially impacts the target's business is a textbook MAC trigger — Engine and Hawk Ridge specifically cited the investigation as Adtalem's contractually clean off-ramp. A screen sees an education company doing a $1.5B deal; the filing-level alpha is that the activists are not asking management to break a contract, they are pointing at a contractually-built exit door. Additionally, the December 2025 $750M buyback authorization within weeks of the activist letter is tacit board acknowledgement that capital-return is on the table — this is the leading indicator that a Walden deal abandonment scenario is being seriously considered internally.
