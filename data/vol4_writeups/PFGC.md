### ANALYST SUMMARY
Performance Food Group is a $60B+ revenue broadline foodservice distributor (Vistar, Performance Foodservice, Convenience segments) serving restaurants, vending, c-stores, and theaters. Operates a national footprint after acquiring Reinhart and Core-Mark. Foodservice distribution is a scale game — route density, private label penetration, and customer mix drive margin spread. PFGC trades at roughly 10x forward EBITDA versus Sysco (13-14x) and US Foods (~11x) despite comparable growth and identical end markets. Sachem's thesis is that combining #2 USFD and #3 PFGC creates a credible #2 to Sysco, with $400-600M+ identified synergies (procurement, route consolidation, private label) — Ferguson, who knows both boards from prior investments, is uniquely positioned to broker.

Bear case: FTC approval is the binary. A USFD/PFGC combination would create a clear duopoly with Sysco in broadline foodservice — even a more permissive FTC may demand large divestitures in overlapping metros, and a deal could be blocked outright. Standalone PFGC carries $5B+ of net debt; if the macro softens (restaurant traffic weak through 2025), EBITDA could disappoint and the multiple compress further. Independent restaurants — the highest-margin customer cohort — have been losing share to chains. If Sachem walks or settles for cosmetic governance changes, the merger optionality decays quickly.

Bull case: Two independent payoff paths. (1) Definitive USFD+PFGC merger announcement at $115-130/share, even with required divestitures, would reprice PFGC to a deal-weighted ~$110+ today versus $93.50 spot — 18-25% upside in months, with downside cushioned by standalone fair value. (2) If FTC blocks, PFGC is a clean PE/strategic LBO target at ~$110-125 (cash flows support 7-8x leverage). Even no-deal, standalone re-rate to 12x EBITDA on margin improvement is ~$115. Median sell-side PT $116.50 already discounts these scenarios partially. Ferguson's board presence is the highest-conviction governance signal in the activist universe right now.
