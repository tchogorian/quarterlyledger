### THE SETUP
A profitable, growing Chinese auto-parts maker trading at a P/E of ~3.9 with 45% of its market cap in net cash, on track to a record $730M revenue year — yet the market values the entire equity at $129M because "China + auto + small cap" is three strikes. Q3 2025 EPS grew 77.8% YoY. The company already paid a $0.80 special dividend in 2024, proving the cash is real and repatriable.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
CAAS is a Hubei-based supplier of power steering systems (hydraulic, electric, and the hybrid "iRCB" intelligent electro-hydraulic circulating ball steering for heavy vehicles) operating through eight Sino-foreign joint ventures. Customers include FAW, Dongfeng, BYD, Beiqi Foton, Chery, Stellantis and Ford. Screen flagged it for net cash of ~$58M against a $129M market cap (45%), TTM net income of $33.5M and a P/E under 4. Q3 2025 net sales were $193.2M (+17.7% YoY); nine-month sales hit $536.5M (+16.1%) and management raised full-year 2025 guidance to $730M. Cash and short-term investments were $167.3M (~$5.54/share) at quarter-end. North America +77.3%, Brazil +30.5%. By April 22, 2026 the company files audited 2025 numbers.

The bear case is the China discount, which exists for reasons: VIE-adjacent corporate structure (Sino-foreign JVs with minority partners on the operating subsidiaries); RMB-to-USD repatriation risk via PRC capital controls; opaque governance for US holders; auditing/PCAOB overhang on US-listed China names; customer concentration in a viciously competitive Chinese auto market where local OEMs compete on price; EV transition risk to legacy hydraulic steering content; and geopolitical risk of US-China tariffs/delistings. Sales to Chery dropped in 2025 even as the rest of the book grew. The dividend was a one-off "special," not recurring. A $25M dividend is a small thank-you on a $58M net cash hoard sitting in PRC subsidiaries.

The bull case: at ~3.9x earnings, the equity is essentially priced to liquidate. Strip the cash and the operating business trades at ~2x earnings on a growing book. Management is executing the EV transition in real time — winning a first R-EPS (rack-assist electric power steering) order from a major European OEM with annual sales expected to exceed $100M starting 2027, and bringing a 20 N·m eRCB intelligent steering motor to mass production in mid-December 2025. Brazil and North America growth diversifies away from China. The 2024 special dividend proved cash can leave China. Catalyst: full-year 2025 audited results April 22, 2026 — a clean print + repeat special dividend would force a re-rating.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The screen sees "China auto P/E 4" and reflexively assumes value trap. The filings show something more interesting: this isn't a melting hydraulic steering company; it's actively winning EV-platform business at premium European OEMs (R-EPS deal, Stellantis-driven Brazil growth) and just raised guidance — most "value trap" autos are guiding down. The eight-JV structure also matters: it means CAAS doesn't 100% own the operating cash, but minority partners are mostly Chinese SOEs with aligned interests, and the parent has an established mechanism (the 2024 special dividend) to upstream capital. The 77.8% Q3 EPS growth is being ignored because investors stopped reading Chinese small caps in 2021.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)

- Repeat special cash dividend (precedent: $0.80 in 2024 = $25M)
- Mass production ramp of 20 N·m eRCB and first R-EPS shipments to European OEM
