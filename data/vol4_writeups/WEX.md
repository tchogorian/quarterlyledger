### ANALYST SUMMARY
WEX runs three segments: Mobility (fleet fuel cards — ~60% of revenue, the crown jewel), Corporate Payments (B2B accounts payable automation), and Benefits (HSA/FSA administration). The fleet card franchise generates ~50% EBITDA margins with sticky enterprise contracts (Wells Fargo, the U.S. government, large logistics fleets). At $173.50 the stock trades at ~10x forward EPS and ~8x EBITDA — a discount to FLEETCOR/Corpay (~13x) and to its own pre-2023 multiple (~17-20x). The mispricing is a reaction to (a) interchange fee compression litigation/regulation, (b) EV transition fears for fleet fuel revenue, (c) declining consumer fuel prices reducing per-transaction interchange.

**Bear case:** WEX's fleet card moat erodes structurally as commercial fleets electrify. Charging at depot/home circumvents the point-of-sale model entirely; even if WEX builds an EV charging network product, unit economics are worse. Interchange fee caps (Visa/Mastercard settlement, potential Durbin extension to credit) compress the spread permanently. Corporate Payments segment has never lived up to its acquisition premium (eNett, Optal). Health benefits is a low-margin admin business. Activists in payments have a mixed record (Elliott at Salesforce-style outcomes are rare); a proxy fight ending in a board settlement may not move the underlying earnings power.

**Bull case:** Fleet electrification is a 15-year curve, not a 3-year one — gas-powered commercial fleets dominate through 2030+. WEX has signed EV charging partnerships and is positioned to ride either fuel type. Sub-10x earnings on a high-quality, recurring-revenue fintech is anomalous; PE buyout precedent (FLEETCOR's Stonepeak/Apollo flirtation, Edenred's continued accretion) makes WEX a legitimate take-private candidate at $230-260. Impactive's nominees bring fintech/payments operating experience the current board lacks. Path to re-rate: (1) ISS/Glass Lewis recommend in Impactive's favor → settlement granting 1-2 seats, (2) accelerated buyback announcement, (3) strategic review or PE bid.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K discloses WEX's float income — interest earned on customer balances — has become a meaningful EBITDA contributor in the high-rate environment. As rates fall in 2026-2027, that tailwind reverses; the company has been quiet about how much of recent earnings is rate-driven vs. core fee growth. Second alpha point: the Benefits segment houses an HSA custodial deposit base of several billion that is essentially a free-deposit bank — this is structurally undervalued inside a payments multiple and could be spun or sold to a HSA Bank or HealthEquity for premium. Third: Impactive's April 14 presentation reportedly highlights specific underperformance vs. self-set guidance — the kind of "promises broken" data that typically wins ISS support. Finally, board reduction from 10 to 9 is a tell that the board fears the proxy math.
