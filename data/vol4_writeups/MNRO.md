### ANALYST SUMMARY
Monro operates ~1,200 company-owned auto-service stores across 32 states under Monro, Mr. Tire, Tire Choice, Car-X and similar banners. The business is tires (~50% of mix) plus oil change, brakes, alignment and undercar service. Comp sales have been negative for multiple consecutive quarters as consumers defer discretionary maintenance and tire OEMs lose pricing leverage. Stock at $17.87 trades at roughly 0.5x revenue and ~6-7x trough EBITDA — vs. Driven Brands at higher multiples and Mavis Tire (private) reportedly transacting at 10x+. Icahn's rationale: real estate-heavy footprint plus 1,200 service bays in a fragmented market are worth materially more in private hands than the public-market quote.

**Bear case:** Monro's comp problem isn't cyclical — it's structural. Fleet electrification erodes oil-change revenue. Tire DTC channels (Discount Tire, Costco, online) are taking share. Labor inflation crushes a mechanic-dependent model where Monro has historically struggled with technician retention and ticket conversion. The 145-store closure looks defensive, not strategic. Poison pill signals a board willing to fight rather than engage — and Icahn's recent track record (Hertz, Occidental, IEP itself) shows what happens when he picks losing battles in melting-ice-cube businesses. Microcap liquidity ($540M cap) means any forced exit by Icahn craters the stock. Dividend was already reduced; further cuts possible.

**Bull case:** Tire replacement is non-discretionary on a 3-5 year cycle — comps will normalize as deferred work returns. Sale-leaseback of owned real estate could fund a $200M+ buyback at this depressed multiple. Mavis or Bridgestone retail are natural strategic acquirers; PE (Roark, Leonard Green) has consolidated this space repeatedly. Even a modest re-rating to 8x normalized EBITDA implies $25-30/share. The poison pill is a negotiating posture, not a permanent block — Icahn's mere presence forces a strategic review. Path to re-rate: (1) board settlement granting 1-2 seats, (2) sale-leaseback announcement, (3) sale process by year-end 2026.
