### ANALYST SUMMARY
Starz is the standalone owner of the Starz premium service — a hybrid linear + OTT subscription network whose franchise hits include Outlander, Power and its spin-offs, and a slate of female-and-underrepresented-audience originals. After years of failed separation attempts the May 2025 spin finally severed it from Lionsgate's studio. The post-spin balance sheet carries roughly $625M of gross debt ($300M Term Loan A + $325M of 5.5% senior unsecured notes) against ~$66M of opening cash. Year-end 2025 leverage came in at 2.9x, ahead of the 3.1x target, and management is guiding to ~2.7x by YE 2026 inside a covenant ceiling of 4.5x stepping down to 3.5x by 2028.

Bear case: the linear half is a melting ice cube. Cord-cutting steepens, MVPD economics keep deteriorating, and even strong content can't fully offset the secular bleed — Q4 revenue was $322.8M (still down YoY) and Starz posted an operating loss. Content amortization is lumpy and obscures true cash earnings; renewing flagship franchises (Outlander, Power universe) gets more expensive as talent leverage rises. Management has said it will stop disclosing quarterly subs starting next quarter, removing the cleanest growth tell. If the OTT pivot stalls, the equity is small and over-levered.

Bull/catalyst: 12.7M U.S. OTT subs (+890K YoY, +370K sequential), 17.6M total U.S. subs, OIBDA growth of >100% in Q4 ($45M to ~$56M), full-year OIBDA of $204M beating guide, and 2026 guidance of $80-120M unlevered FCF show the digital-first model is working. CEO Jeffrey Hirsch has positioned Starz as a streaming roll-up vehicle and the standalone structure makes it a clean target for Paramount, WBD, AMC Networks, or PE. At ~$340M equity / ~$900M EV vs $204M OIBDA, Starz trades around 4.5x EV/OIBDA — half of any plausible private-market take-out.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K reveals the new credit agreement is meaningfully friendlier than what existed inside Lionsgate: a $300M senior secured Term Loan A plus $150M revolver at SOFR+300, four-year tenor, covenants that loosen with deleveraging. That gives Hirsch optionality to do tuck-in M&A or self-tender if the equity stays below $20. The 5.5% senior notes are pre-payable and cheaper than Starz could refinance today — an embedded option. Filings also confirm Starz is now a Canadian domicile, which lowers the effective tax rate and complicates any U.S. acquirer's structuring (suggesting a sale would likely come with a control premium to overcome friction).
