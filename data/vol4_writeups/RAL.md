### THE SETUP
The slower-growth, more cyclical half of Fortive — Tektronix, Keithley, Gems, Setra, Qualitrol — orphaned by FTV's quality-growth holders the day it began trading, then carpet-bombed in February 2026 after a $1.4B EA Elektro-Automatik goodwill impairment dropped the stock 32% to all-time lows around $37. With a fresh $200M buyback authorization, insider buying, and 20%+ EBITDA margins still intact, the setup is classic Joel Greenblatt: a forced-selling cliff into a cyclical trough.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Ralliant comprises Fortive's old Precision Technologies portfolio split into two segments — Test & Measurement (~56% of sales: Tektronix, Keithley, Sonix, EA Elektro-Automatik) and Sensors & Safety Systems (~44%: Qualitrol, Gems, Setra, Hengstler-Dynapar, Anderson-Negele, Dover Motion, Pacific Scientific Energetic Materials). Q3 2025 revenue was $529M flat YoY and adjusted EBITDA margin was 20.4% (down 610 bps on mix and tariffs). The board approved a $200M repurchase authorization at separation and 2026 guidance points to 18-20% adjusted EBITDA margins. Mkt cap ~$5.35B; the company carries the carve-out debt typical of an FTV spin (notes issued to fund a one-time dividend back to Fortive).

Bear case: this is the cyclical, capital-intensive half that FTV was trying to hand off. Test & measurement is tied to semiconductor capex (oscilloscopes, power supplies for fab/lab use) and aerospace/defense spend. Tariffs are biting cost. The $1.4B EA Elektro-Automatik goodwill write-down in Q4 2025 confirmed the asset was overpaid for under FTV — a real signal that capital allocation discipline pre-spin was lax. EBITDA margins compressing from 26%+ to 20% rebases the multiple. If 2026 revenue actually falls mid-single-digits as guided, EPS estimates have further to come down.

Bull/catalyst: brands like Tektronix and Keithley are #1/#2 in their niches with 50+ years of share — these are toll-bridge instruments engineers buy reflexively. ~25% recurring revenue from calibration, software, and service. Standalone, RAL can do its own buybacks ($200M = ~3.7% of cap), bolt-on M&A in sensing, and run leaner without FTV's overhead allocation. Insider open-market buying in March 2026 (director +2,350 shares) signals management views current pricing as a gift. VIC writeups peg fair value $58-68 vs. $47 today on ~13x FCF when KEYS, COHU, AME trade 17-20x. New CEO and a 2026 investor day articulating long-term margin recovery to 22-24% would close the gap.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-12B/A and Q4 2025 release reveal that the EA Elektro-Automatik impairment was non-cash — adjusted EBITDA and free cash flow were unchanged. Yet the stock dropped 32% on the print, implying a screen that flags net loss / negative book momentum will dump RAL while the cash machine is unaffected. The Q3 release also disclosed that Sensors & Safety Systems carries SETRA and PACIFIC SCIENTIFIC ENERGETIC MATERIALS COMPANY — the latter is the Pentagon's primary supplier of warhead initiation devices, an arms-cycle beneficiary that no FTV holder ever owned for that exposure. Standalone reporting will surface the defense angle and likely re-rate the SSS segment specifically.
