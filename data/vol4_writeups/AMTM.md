**Tier: SPINOFF — QUALITY-AT-DISCOUNT**

### THE SETUP
A $14B-revenue, $47B-backlog government services giant trading at a 3-4 turn EBITDA discount to LDOS/SAIC purely because (a) it was born from a Reverse Morris Trust merger that confused the screens, (b) PE-sponsor selldowns hung over the float, and (c) net leverage opened at ~4.0x. Management has already pulled leverage to 3.4x in Q1 FY26 and is targeting <3x by year-end — with a Moody's upgrade already lopping 25 bps off Term Loan B interest. Truist target $42 implies ~50% upside as the discount unwinds.

### SPINOFF MECHANICS
- Parent: Jacobs Solutions (NYSE: J)
- Spin date: September 30, 2024 — ~19 months old
- Distribution: Jacobs distributed ~80.95% of CMS common stock pro rata to J holders; structure = Reverse Morris Trust merging Jacobs CMS + Cyber & Intelligence with PE-owned legacy Amentum (Lindsay Goldberg / American Securities). At close: J shareholders 51.0%, PE sponsors ~37.5%, Jacobs retained 7.5%, plus 4.5% contingent escrow tied to FY24 operating-profit targets.
- Pre-spin cash payment: CMS paid Jacobs ~$911M, funded by new Amentum debt — driving the opening ~4.0x leverage.
- Initial trading vs current: opened ~$28; recent ~$27.58 (still re-rating).
- Forced selling: (1) Jacobs index funds dumped a non-engineering, government-services stub that didn't fit J's mandate; (2) PE secondary overhang from two sponsors with a clock; (3) defense-services PMs who anchor on LDOS/SAIC's cleaner balance sheets passed on the leverage.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Amentum is the post-merger combination of Jacobs' Critical Mission Solutions plus parts of its Cyber & Intelligence unit with the legacy Amentum business — yielding the second-largest pure-play federal services contractor behind Leidos. End-markets are DoD platform sustainment, DoE environmental management (Hanford, Savannah River), intelligence community digital modernization, NASA space ops, and overseas base operations. Q1 FY26 revenue was $3.2B with adjusted EBITDA of $263M (8.1% margin) and adjusted diluted EPS of $0.54 (+6% y/y). Backlog hit $47.2B (~3.3 years of revenue), and book-to-bill remained >1.0x. The strategic logic of the spin was clean: Jacobs wanted to be a pure higher-margin engineering & consulting firm; CMS+C&I fit better merged with Amentum where scale unlocks larger IDIQ awards. PE sponsors gained a public exit path.

Bear case: federal services is a low-margin (~8%), low-growth, contract-recompete business where a single lost re-bid (e.g., Hanford Mission Essential Services Contract) can vaporize a year of EBITDA. The opening 4.0x leverage limits buyback / M&A optionality and means a defense-budget hiccup hits equity hardest. Continuing-resolution noise and a recent shutdown drove a Q1 revenue miss that the company had to talk through. The PE overhang is real — two sponsors have clear motive to monetize, and every secondary is a price ceiling. Government-services peers trade at 11-13x EBITDA partly because they earn it with cleaner balance sheets and longer track records as standalones; AMTM's history as a standalone public company is barely a year, and pro-forma "core" margins are still being argued. Reverse Morris Trust deals also carry tax-protection covenants (no >50% change-of-control window) that constrain strategic flexibility for ~2 years post-spin.

Bull case: the leverage delta is the entire story. From ~4.0x to 3.4x in a year, with management guiding <3x by FY26 year-end. Each turn of deleveraging at peer multiples is roughly $10-12 of stock. The Moody's upgrade has already lowered cost of debt. Backlog of $47B against $13-14B revenue is one of the best book-to-bill setups in federal services. Catalysts are tight and dated: quarterly leverage updates, the inevitable PE secondary that "clears the deck," and a likely S&P MidCap promotion once the float settles and sponsor stakes drop below thresholds. Truist's $42 PT (implying ~50% upside from $27.58) assumes simply closing half the gap to LDOS on EV/EBITDA. Government services is also one of the most administration-agnostic federal spending buckets — DoE cleanup, IC modernization, base ops survive every budget regime.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

Form 10 / S-4 specifics screens miss: (1) The $911M pre-spin cash payment from CMS to Jacobs is the entire reason the balance sheet looks scary — it was financing-driven, not operational. (2) The 4.5% contingent share escrow tied to FY24 operating-profit targets means real share count is fluid; pro-forma EPS denominators differ across data feeds. (3) Reverse Morris Trust tax-protection covenants prohibit any transaction that would cause J shareholders to own <50% of AMTM for two years — explicitly blocking change-of-control until late 2026. (4) Transition Services Agreement with Jacobs covers IT, accounting, treasury and HR, with stand-up costs running through "separation expense" lines — these obscure ~$50-80M of recurring opex once normalized. (5) The PE sponsors hold registration rights and have a clear playbook: marketed secondaries every 2-3 quarters until they're out. Every secondary is a discrete catalyst — the price-discovery moments are PRINTED, then the overhang lifts.
