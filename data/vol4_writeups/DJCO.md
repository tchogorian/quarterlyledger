### THE SETUP
Munger's old holdco trades at a $749M market cap with a marketable securities portfolio worth ~$443M (mid-2025 mark) and a growing court-software business (Journal Technologies) compounding revenue +12% YoY in Q1 FY26. ### ANALYST SUMMARY
Daily Journal is two businesses stitched into one entity. (1) The Traditional segment publishes legal and business newspapers in California (declining, low-margin, but cash-positive). (2) Journal Technologies sells eSuite — case management, e-filing, and jury management software to courts, prosecutors, and public defenders across the US, Canada, and Australia. Q1 FY2026 (Oct–Dec 2025) put consolidated revenue at $19.5M (+10% YoY), with Journal Tech at $15.2M (+12%). Steven Myhill-Jones is Chairman and CEO post-Munger. The screen flagged the name on $477M net cash / 64% of market cap and a 8.0 P/E — both numbers driven by a securities portfolio originally constructed by Charlie Munger.

The bear case has three legs. First, the investment portfolio is heavily concentrated in financials — Wells Fargo (~48%), Bank of America (~40%), Alibaba ADR (~10%), US Bancorp (~2%) per the Q4 2025 13F (~$277M reported value at filing date) — and quarterly mark-to-market drives wild GAAP swings (Q1 FY26 showed an $11.7M unrealized loss flipping a $10.9M underlying profit into an $8M reported loss). Second, Journal Technologies revenue is concentrated in long-cycle government contracts; implementation timelines slip, deferred revenue can be lumpy, and competition from Tyler Technologies and Thomson Reuters is real. Third, post-Munger capital allocation discipline is unproven — the portfolio rebalancing risk is non-trivial.

The bull case is the rare combination of a real, growing software business inside a holdco trading well below sum-of-parts. Journal Technologies is recurring-revenue government software (sticky, high switching costs, mission-critical for courts) growing double digits. The Munger portfolio, even after recent drawdowns, still carries hundreds of millions in unrealized gains — a meaningful deferred-tax shield. At $749M cap minus $443M portfolio (June 2025 mark), the implied operating value is ~$300M for a software business doing $60M+ revenue with secular tailwinds (court system digital modernization). Catalysts: continued Journal Tech bookings, possible buybacks (DJCO has historically been disciplined), and portfolio mark-to-market reversals.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The "8.0 P/E" is meaningless because TTM earnings are dominated by securities mark-to-market — when bank stocks rally, EPS spikes; when they sell off, EPS collapses; the underlying operating earnings are far more stable but much smaller. The screen also doesn't capture the deferred tax liability on the unrealized portfolio gains (~$303.9M of pretax unrealized gains at June 2025 means a meaningful tax overhang on full liquidation). Most importantly, no screen captures the cultural inheritance: DJCO is one of the few public companies where management has demonstrated for decades they will hold concentrated positions through volatility rather than diworsify. That's the actual moat.
