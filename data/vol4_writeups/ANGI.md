# {NUMBER}. ANGI — Angi Inc.
## SPINOFF (from IAC Inc., IAC)  |  Spin Date: 2025-04-01  |  Score: 65/100  |  POSITION SIZE: $1,250

**Tier: SPINOFF — FORCED SELLING (turnaround optionality)**

### THE SETUP
Classic small-cap spin set-up: IAC dumped Angi after years of trying to fix HomeAdvisor + Angi's List, dual-class structure collapsed to one share/one vote, and IAC's index-fund holders sold without diligence. With ~$387M of cash on a ~$320M market cap, the enterprise value is approximately zero — you get the marketplace business, the brand, and the operational reset for free. New CEO Jeff Kip + Joey Levin as Executive Chairman provide focused leadership. Already repurchased ~10% of shares post-spin. 2026 expected return to revenue growth and $145-150M adjusted EBITDA.

### SPINOFF MECHANICS
- Parent: IAC Inc. (NASDAQ: IAC)
- Spin date: April 1, 2025 (record date March 25, 2025) — ~12 months old
- Distribution ratio: ~0.5251 shares of Angi Class A per IAC share
- Initial trading vs current: opened ~$8.50; current ~$7.40 (down modestly post-spin as the reset plays out)
- Structural change at spin: dual-class structure eliminated → one share / one vote; cleaner governance for activists or strategic buyers
- Forced selling reasons: (1) IAC was held by hedge funds owning the *holdco-discount* trade — Angi was a side-bet "free option" they didn't model; (2) IAC dropping it was an explicit signal that even Diller had given up on the marketplace fix, sending a "left for dead" signal that drove redemptions; (3) micro-cap (<$500M) status pushed it below institutional minimums; (4) limited float plus prior years of operational pain made it un-coverable by sell-side; (5) loss-making at the operating line scared off momentum funds.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Angi is the home-services marketplace combining HomeAdvisor and Angie's List, finally separated from IAC's holdco complexity. The business: lead generation and matching between homeowners with home-improvement projects (everything from plumbers to roofers) and contractor "pros" who pay subscription + per-lead fees. The history is brutal — five-plus years of declining revenue, churn-heavy pro retention, lead-quality complaints, and a series of strategic resets at IAC. New management under CEO Jeff Kip with Joey Levin as Executive Chairman has explicitly pivoted from "lead volume" to "jobs done well" — killing unprofitable cohorts, raising lead quality bars, and accepting near-term revenue decline (-12% to -16% in 2025) for sustainable margin recovery. 2025 guidance: $135-150M adjusted EBITDA, $55-100M operating income. 2026 guide: low-single-digit revenue growth (1-3%), $145-150M adjusted EBITDA. Key cap-structure fact: ~$387M cash at the end of Q1 2025, ~$416M total liquidity, market cap ~$320M — enterprise value is approximately ZERO or negative. Already executed a ~10% buyback in 2025.

Bear case: home-services lead generation is structurally challenged. Angi has been losing share to Google's Local Services Ads, Yelp, Thumbtack, and direct-to-pro platforms for half a decade. AI-driven search disruption (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews) potentially commodifies the matching function — Angi's brand is no defense if homeowners get answers from an LLM. Pro churn is the death-knell metric and there's no compelling evidence it's permanently fixed. Operating losses persisted into spin; the EBITDA guidance includes meaningful add-backs and depends on continued cost cuts that have a floor. Joey Levin signaled "I'm done with this" by spinning it — the smartest operator in IAC's universe gave up. Micro-cap means no analyst coverage, no liquidity, no path to multiple expansion without a transaction. The cash pile invites bad capital allocation — Angi has a history of value-destructive M&A.

Bull case: the EV-near-zero math is the entire trade. ~$300M+ of net cash on a ~$320M market cap means you're paying nothing for a business that should generate $145M+ adjusted EBITDA in 2026 — implying single-digit-million enterprise value against $145M EBITDA, an absurd disconnect if even half the operational reset sticks. The buyback authorization is already aggressive and shrinking the float meaningfully. New CEO + cleaner governance + no IAC drag = first time in years the team can focus. Optionality on AI-driven matching efficiency is essentially free — if AI improves the lead-quality / job-completion rate, the whole flywheel reignites. Strategic transaction is plausible: a private equity buyer looking at "broken consumer marketplace + cash + sub-1x revenue" would pay double the current price; a Yelp / Thumbtack / Google could be acquirers. Sentiment reset alone — going from "left for dead" to "stable + cash-rich + EBITDA-growing" — is worth multiple turns of expansion.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

Form 10 / 8-K / proxy specifics: (1) The dual-class collapse to one-share/one-vote at spin is the SINGLE most underappreciated detail — every prior activist push was blocked by IAC's super-voting; that defense is GONE, making strategic transactions immediately viable. (2) Joey Levin moved from IAC CEO to Angi Executive Chairman — when one of the smartest holdco operators puts on an executive hat at the orphan, the signal value is enormous. (3) Cash distributed at separation gives Angi ~$387M cash; the parent took the haircut, not the spinco. (4) Tax-free distribution under Section 355 means no overhang from tax liability. (5) Buyback already aggressive — 10% of shares retired in months — implies management views EV as undervalued. (6) Pro-retention metrics are the load-bearing KPI; quarterly disclosure of cohort retention is the gating signal for thesis confirmation. (7) No meaningful debt at separation — pristine balance sheet is rare for a "broken" spinco.

- Continued aggressive buyback (each ~5% chunk shrinks float and lifts per-share economics)
- Strategic transaction announcement — PE take-private or strategic acquirer (now possible with single-class structure)
