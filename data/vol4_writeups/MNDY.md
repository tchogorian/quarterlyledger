### THE SETUP
A best-in-class work-management SaaS that grew revenue 27% to $1.232B in 2025, generated $322.7M of free cash flow, and sits on $1.5B of cash — 44% of its $3.4B market cap. This is a deep-value manual but MNDY is here for one reason: when a high-quality SaaS compounder gets de-rated to the point that nearly half the price is cash, it stops being a "growth stock" and starts being a Munger-style cash fortress with a free option on continued execution.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
monday.com sells a horizontal "Work OS" — visual workflows, dashboards, CRM, dev, service modules — to ~250K+ paid customers globally. FY2025 revenue $1.232B (+27%), Q4 $334M (+25%). Customers >$50K ARR: 4,281 (+34% YoY); >$100K ARR: 1,756 (+45%); >$500K ARR: 87 (+74%). NDR was 116% in both >$50K and >$100K cohorts. Adjusted FCF $322.7M (26% margin). 14% non-GAAP operating margin in 2025. Cash and equivalents ~$1.5B with no debt. The screen flagged it for cash >40% of mcap; the 2026 guide is $1.452-$1.462B revenue (18-19% growth) and $275-$290M adjusted FCF. The Q4 print + soft guide drove the de-rating that made this name screen-eligible at all.

The bear case is competitive intensity and growth deceleration. Asana, Atlassian, ClickUp, Smartsheet, Notion and Microsoft Loop all overlap — and AI-native upstarts threaten the entire workflow-builder category. Management already embedded weak no-touch SMB channel performance into 2026 guidance, plus 100-200bp FX headwinds from shekel appreciation (55% of staff in Israel). 18-19% growth from a 27% base is a real slowdown. Israel HQ creates geopolitical, FX, and talent risks. Multiple compression continued through 2025 as the market re-priced unprofitable-to-marginally-profitable SaaS. SBC remains material (typical SaaS dilution dynamics) — the "cash fortress" optic understates dilution if shares grow ~3-4%/yr.

The bull case: at $3.4B mcap minus $1.5B cash = ~$1.9B EV on $1.232B revenue and $322M adjusted FCF. That's ~1.5x EV/sales and ~6x EV/FCF — for a 25%+ grower with 116% net retention. Compare that to peers trading at 5-10x sales. Founders Roy Mann and Eran Zinman are still operating. Vibe (AI app-building) just became the fastest product to $1M ARR in company history. Enterprise traction (>$500K customers +74%) suggests upmarket motion is working. Catalysts: AI/Vibe monetization scaling, large buyback authorization on the cash pile, or a multiple recovery if 2026 guide proves conservative. Downside is cushioned by the FCF + cash balance.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The screen captures "cash 44% of mcap" but misses that nearly all of that cash is operating cash flow generated, not raised — meaning MNDY has crossed the threshold most "Rule of 40" SaaS companies never reach: it is self-funding and accreting cash at >$300M/yr. The filings also show enterprise momentum (87 customers >$500K ARR, +74%) that mid-market software companies rarely achieve without acquisition. And monday.com runs a multi-product strategy (Work OS + CRM + Dev + Service + Vibe) where each module is a separate land-and-expand vector — the screen gives no credit for cross-sell optionality. Finally, the Israeli founder-operator model (similar to Wix, Fiverr, Check Point) historically outperforms US-HQ SaaS on capital efficiency.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Buyback authorization (cash pile is now embarrassingly large for a 14%-margin company)
- Vibe / AI product monetization milestones (already fastest to $1M ARR)
- 2026 quarterly beats vs. conservative 18-19% guide → multiple re-rating
