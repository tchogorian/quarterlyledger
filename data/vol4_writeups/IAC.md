### ANALYST SUMMARY

IAC is Barry Diller's serial-spinout machine — having previously birthed and separated Match Group, Expedia, LendingTree, Vimeo, Angi, and others. CEO Joey Levin runs the parent. Post-March 2025 Angi spin, IAC is now a focused holdco with: (1) a ~25% MGM Resorts stake (acquired in 2020-22 at much lower prices, now ~$2.5B); (2) People Inc. (the rebranded Dotdash Meredith) — owner of People, Better Homes & Gardens, Investopedia, Allrecipes, Verywell — generating Q4 '25 digital revenue of $355m (+14% YoY) with full-year adj. EBITDA guide of $310-340m; (3) Care.com, which swung Q4 EBITDA to $19m (+135%); (4) smaller bets in Vivian Health and the Daily Beast. ~$700m of net cash sits at the parent for opportunistic capital allocation.

The bear case is the chronic conglomerate discount and a digital-ad cycle. People Inc. is the swing factor — print revenue fell 23% in Q4 '25 and the digital-ad business is sensitive to a recession. Care.com is rebuilding but still small. The MGM stake's "unlock" thesis depends on Diller actually monetizing — and he tends to wait years. Print-magazine assets inside People Inc. depress reported multiples vs pure-digital comps. Spin candidates (People Inc.) are not yet committed. Diller is in his 80s — succession is real risk despite Levin running ops.

The bull case is mathematical. MGM stake ($2.5B) + net cash ($0.7B) = $3.2B vs the entire $3.4B market cap — which means the rest of IAC (a top-3 US digital publisher generating $300m+ EBITDA) trades at essentially zero. People Inc. comparables (NYT at ~25x, Ziff Davis at ~7x, Future PLC at ~8x) imply a $2-3B fair value alone. IAC has spun out 10+ companies; People Inc. is the obvious next candidate once digital growth reliably outpaces print decline. Levin retired ~10% of float in 2025 ($37m in Q4 alone) and is buying back below SOTP. MGM-Osaka opens 2030 — long-tail re-rating optionality.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

IAC bought 1m additional MGM shares in Q4 2025 for $40m — Levin still adding to the position despite the parent trading below MGM-stake-plus-cash. The Dotdash-Meredith integration is officially "complete" as of late 2025 and management has subtly opened the door to a People Inc. spin in the 2026 letter. The $800m senior notes refinancing at People Inc. in Q1 2025 cleaned up the capital structure ahead of a potential separation. Care.com's swing to ~$70m+ run-rate EBITDA makes it a legitimate sale candidate to a strategic (e.g., a benefits platform). Diller's last shareholder letter explicitly framed IAC as "a portfolio of underappreciated assets" — Diller-speak for "we're going to surface value." Note: the 10-K (filed Feb 20, 2026) discloses Diller still controls Class B supervotes, so any breakup requires his consent — but his entire career is breakups.
