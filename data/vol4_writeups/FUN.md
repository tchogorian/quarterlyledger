### ANALYST SUMMARY
Six Flags Entertainment is the post-merger entity combining legacy Cedar Fair and Six Flags (closed July 2024). It owns 42 amusement parks, water parks, and resort properties across North America — including flagship Cedar Point, Knott's Berry Farm, Magic Mountain, and Six Flags Great Adventure. The merger thesis was $200M of synergies, scale benefits in vendor purchasing, and combined seasons-pass cross-selling. Reality: integration has been painful, attendance disappointing in 2025 summer season, ~$5.2B post-merger debt load is the elephant in the room, and the stock has roundtripped to below the merger close. JANA targeted FUN because regional theme parks remain a stable cash-flow asset class historically attractive to PE (Apollo previously owned Six Flags, Blackstone owns Merlin Entertainments, KKR owns multiple regional parks globally).

Bear case: $5.2B of debt at ~6.5% blended cost = $340M+ annual interest, leaving thin equity cushion if 2026 attendance disappoints again. Weather risk is real (heat domes, hurricane season). Consumer discretionary stress at low-end demographic (Six Flags Cleveland, NJ, Texas). PE buyers — particularly Apollo (former owner) — were burned on Six Flags' last LBO and may not pay up. Strategic buyers (Disney, Comcast/Universal) don't need 42 regional parks. Without a buyer, JANA campaign devolves into operational fix that takes years and the debt load compounds.

Bull case: Industry consolidation logic is powerful — combined SIX/FUN footprint covers ~50M+ unique annual visitors and the season pass database is one of the most valuable assets in regional entertainment. PE sponsors love stable park EBITDA (recurring season passes, in-park spend, real estate option value). Rate-cut cycle helps the LBO math meaningfully. Travis Kelce ambassador + new marketing playbook can drive 5-10% attendance lift on cultural relevance alone. At 8x 2026E EBITDA the stock is $30; at 10x it's $40. Even absent sale, operational reset under JANA-friendly board gets to mid-$20s.

Apollo's prior ownership is the hidden asset of the bull case: they know the asset cold and could move fast if invited.
