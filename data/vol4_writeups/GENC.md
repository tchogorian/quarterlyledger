## CASH FORTRESS  |  Score: 60/100  |  POSITION SIZE: $2,000 (of $100K)

### THE SETUP
A 60-year-old, family-controlled Florida maker of asphalt-plant equipment with $136M of cash and marketable securities (cash 68% of market cap), zero debt, and a backlog that just rebuilt to $57.4M as of Q1 fiscal 2026 from a December trough of $23.6M. The bear case (IIJA expiration September 30, 2026) is real but priced in; the bull case is a profitable cyclical with a fortress balance sheet, a chairman transition, and a board with no excuse not to return capital — except its long-stated refusal to do so.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Gencor Industries (NYSE-American: GENC) designs and manufactures hot-mix asphalt plants, soil-remediation equipment and related combustion systems, sold to highway contractors in the U.S. and Canada. Fiscal 2025 (year ended September 30, 2025) net revenue was $115.4M (+2.0% YoY), gross profit $31.7M (27.5% margin), operating income $14.0M, and net income $15.7M ($1.07 diluted EPS). The screen flag — net cash $147M against market cap $216M (68% ratio) — is conservative: the 10-K states "the Company had $26,587,000 in cash and cash equivalents, and $109,714,000 in marketable securities" plus "working capital was $197.7 million." Marketable securities are managed by a third-party investment firm and include corporate bonds, ETFs, mutual funds, equities and government securities. 318 full-time employees, no major union exposure outside Marquette, Iowa. The company is controlled by the Elliott family: Class B holders elect 75% of the board, and EJ Elliott alone owns 87.9% of Class B (plus 13.3% of Common); his son Marc Elliott (now Chairman effective January 1, 2026) holds another 8.3% of Class B.

The bear case is well-laid-out in the 10-K. First, "the Company has identified material weaknesses in its internal control over financial reporting" — an explicit ICFR failure as of September 30, 2025. That is an audit-committee level red flag in any size company. Second, the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act "is scheduled to expire on September 30, 2026"; backlog already collapsed from $72.2M (Sept 2024) to $28.2M (Sept 2025) before partially recovering to $57.4M in Q1 fiscal 2026. Third, the Company "does not expect to pay cash dividends for the foreseeable future" and the Class B/Class A structure means the Elliott family fully controls capital allocation — minorities have no leverage. Fourth, marketable-securities exposure to equities and ETFs introduces realized/unrealized P&L volatility ($2.0M+ historically) into reported earnings.

The bull case: $136M of liquid securities + $26.6M cash on $216M of market cap means the *operating business* is being valued at roughly $80M for a company that did $14M of operating income and $15.7M of net income — a sub-6x EV/EBIT for a profitable, debt-free industrial. IIJA reauthorization is the dominant catalyst; even partial reauthorization would re-rate the multiple. The new chairman (Marc Elliott) has the latitude to re-think capital return policy after his father's December 2025 retirement from the board. Even without a dividend, the cash hoard compounds at Treasury yields and provides downside protection through the cyclical valley.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K bears two filing-specific items most screens miss. (1) Customer concentration: in fiscal 2024 "one customer accounted for 11.3% of net revenue" — concentration that briefly disappeared in fiscal 2025 (no >10% customer) but is structural to a market with a small number of large highway contractors. (2) Revenue is recognized "over time when the performance obligation is satisfied by transferring control of the equipment" with "revenues and costs recognized in proportion to actual labor costs incurred" — a percentage-of-completion methodology that creates earnings volatility and makes the material-weakness ICFR finding more concerning than at a typical reseller. Third, the marketable-securities portfolio is *actively managed by an external manager* — meaning a chunk of the "cash" is exposed to equity beta and credit risk. Insider ownership: Elliott family + officers + Foundation = 17.3% of Common and 100% of Class B — outside holders cannot force capital return.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- IIJA reauthorization or successor-bill negotiations through Q3 calendar 2026 — the single largest event for the entire customer base.
- Q2 fiscal 2026 10-Q (~April-May 2026) — backlog trajectory after the $57.4M Q1 print.
- Material-weakness remediation update; failure to remediate could attract activist attention.
- Marc Elliott's first full year as Chairman — any change in dividend or buyback posture would be a substantial re-rating event.
