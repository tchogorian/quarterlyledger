### ANALYST SUMMARY
GDEV is a mobile games holding company headquartered in Limassol, Cyprus, which evolved from Nexters in June 2023 after relocating away from Russia. Its primary asset is Hero Wars (cumulative $1.7B revenue, 185M+ installs, ~93% of 2025 revenue). Screen flagged it for net cash ~$112M against $289M mcap (39%), TTM net income ~$69M, and a P/E of 4.2. Q3 2025 revenue $98M (-12% YoY); 9-month revenue $315M vs. $323M prior year. But profit improved sharply: 9-month profit went from ~$24M to ~$55M as marketing pulled back to a smaller, higher-value paying audience. EBITDA $64M for first nine months. February 2025: special dividend of $3.31/share (~$60M). Remains debt-free.

The bear case is structural. Hero Wars bookings down 4% in 2024, down 13% in 2025; monthly paying users down 18% in 2025. The franchise is harvesting, not growing — and 93% revenue concentration in one game is a single-asset risk on a depleting asset. The Russian-heritage problem is not fully behind the company: even after relocation, sanctions risk, key-person risk, and reputational discounts remain. The special dividend was explicitly described as one-time — management said no recurring dividends are anticipated. The earlier 2023 buyback authorization (up to 20M shares at $2) has been more theatrical than executed. New game pipeline is thin — Pixel Gun 2 is the headline launch, but mobile game launches have notoriously low hit rates and 2026 timing creates execution risk. Cash gets dividended out faster than it's reinvested.

The bull case: even after the $60M special dividend, ~$112M cash remains (39% of mcap). Ex-cash, the operating business trades at ~2.5x earnings on a profitable, FCF-positive game studio that just expanded margins by being more disciplined on UA spend. Pixel Gun is a real franchise — Pixel Gun 3D has 300M+ installs and $230M+ in lifetime bookings; a successful sequel launch in early 2026 could re-rate the equity meaningfully. Multi-platform launch (iOS, Android, Steam, then console) widens monetization beyond pure mobile. Catalyst: Pixel Gun 2 launch + potential second special dividend if cash continues to accumulate. Cyprus jurisdiction is now stable; sanctions tail-risk has shrunk meaningfully since 2022.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The screen sees "P/E 4, 39% cash" and concludes "cheap." It can't see that Hero Wars is in managed decline — the company is deliberately running it for cash, not growth. That's actually a feature, not a bug, if you trust management to return the cash. The 2025 special dividend ($60M) was the proof point: this is not a Russian-style "trapped cash" story, it's a Cyprus holdco that distributes when it can. The filings also reveal that 2025 profit grew sharply ($55M vs. $24M YTD) by cutting unprofitable user-acquisition spend — a sign of mature, disciplined management. Pixel Gun 2 is hidden upside the screen ignores entirely; a hit shifts the franchise mix away from sole Hero Wars dependency.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Pixel Gun 2 launch (early 2026 — iOS, Android, Steam)
- Possible second special dividend if cash continues to accumulate
- Stabilization or sequential growth in Hero Wars bookings (would re-rate the multiple)
