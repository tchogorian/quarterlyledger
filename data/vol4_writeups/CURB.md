# {NUMBER}. CURB — Curbline Properties Corp.
## SPINOFF (from SITE Centers) | Spin Date: 2024-10-01 | Score: 78/100 | POSITION SIZE: $2,500

### THE SETUP
CURB is the rarest of REIT spinoffs — the parent (SITC) kept the troubled legacy strip-center portfolio and spun the high-quality "convenience real estate" piece OUT with roughly $500M of net cash. SITC dividend/value holders received small-cap CURB shares with no dividend history, no index inclusion, and no sell-side coverage, and most dumped them in October-November 2024. What's left is a pure-play roll-up vehicle with dry powder equal to ~17% of market cap chasing a $70k-property TAM.

### SPINOFF MECHANICS
- Parent: SITE Centers Corp. (SITC)
- Spin date: 2024-10-01 (~6.5 months ago)
- Distribution ratio: 2 CURB shares per 1 SITC share
- Initial trading: opened around $22 on 10/1/24; currently $28.23
- Key reasons for forced selling: SITC was a yield/value REIT held by dividend funds; CURB launched with a token initial dividend, no index membership, sub-$3B mcap, and a "growth REIT" profile that was the polar opposite of its parent's holder base

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Curbline owns and acquires "convenience" strip centers — small (typically <50,000 sq ft) unanchored or shadow-anchored centers on high-traffic corridors, leased to service tenants (quick-serve restaurants, salons, medical, financial services). At spin it owned 79 properties, almost entirely in wealthy Sun Belt and coastal suburban submarkets contributed by SITC. The company was launched with roughly $500M of net cash and an undrawn credit facility — capitalized for offense rather than survival. Initial occupancy was in the mid-90s with positive leasing spreads. Management (David Lukes, Conor Fennerty — formerly the SITC team) explicitly framed the spin as a vehicle to roll up a fragmented niche that traditional shopping-center REITs ignore because the asset size is too small to move the needle.

The bear case is straightforward: at ~23x AFFO and a ~3.5% initial yield, CURB optically screens as expensive vs. peers like Kimco (KIM) or Regency (REG) at high-teens AFFO multiples. Cap rates on convenience centers have compressed as private buyers have figured out the niche, so accretion math depends on disciplined sourcing. Tenant base skews to small-business operators that can be cyclical, and a ~$3B mcap can quickly raise more equity (dilutive if not deployed at spreads). Sponsorship risk: insiders are essentially the same SITC management whose track record at the parent is mixed.

The bull case rewards the optionality. Adjusted for ~$500M net cash, the operating multiple is closer to high-teens AFFO — in line with peers but on a higher-growth, lower-leverage vehicle. Each $200-300M of acquisitions at 7-8% cap rates funded by 5-6% cost of capital drives meaningful AFFO/share growth; management telegraphed an annualized acquisition pace of ~$500M+. Once seasoning passes, RMZ/MSCI REIT index inclusion creates a passive bid. A first material dividend hike alongside a 2025 investor day would re-rate the equity toward the "growth REIT" cohort (CUBE, EXR, REXR style multiples).

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Form 10 details that matter: (1) the spin was structured as a tax-free distribution AFTER SITC sold roughly $1.7B of "non-Curbline" assets and pushed the proceeds into CURB as cash — meaning CURB launched debt-free with the proceeds of SITC's 2023-24 disposition program, an unusual and very founder-friendly capitalization. (2) Tenant base in the contributed portfolio is heavily service-oriented (food service, beauty, medical, financial), with the top-10 tenants under 20% of ABR — far less concentration than typical strip-center REITs. (3) There is a transition services agreement with SITC for accounting and IT (limited duration, ~12 months) but no long-term operational dependency — CURB took the operating bench with it. (4) Management compensation is heavily skewed to AFFO-per-share growth and TSR, aligning with the deployment thesis.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)

- First material dividend increase, likely with Q4 2025 earnings
- RMZ / MSCI US REIT Index inclusion once 12-month seasoning passes (Oct 2025)
