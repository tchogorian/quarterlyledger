### ANALYST SUMMARY
Riot operates two business lines today: (1) bitcoin mining at Rockdale (Texas) and other sites — historically the entire revenue base, exposed to BTC price + halving cycle + difficulty, and (2) data center hosting — newly emergent, anchored by the AMD lease. Riot's strategic asset is power: 1.7GW total contracted with the ERCOT grid, including 1GW of expansion capacity at Corsicana, Texas — built originally for mining but architecturally convertible to liquid-cooled HPC. At $18.11 the equity is ~$6.9B; net of cash and BTC holdings the EV is ~$5-6B against 1.7GW. AI data center comps trade at $20-40M/MW (CoreWeave, Iris Energy AI segment, Applied Digital). Even at $15M/MW, capacity alone is worth $25B — 4-5x the current EV. The market is refusing to pay because (a) lease conversion is unproven at scale, (b) capex per MW for HPC is much higher than for mining, (c) Riot's mining cash flow is volatile.

**Bear case:** AI data center economics require liquid cooling, high-redundancy power, fiber to multiple carriers, and Tier III+ certification — none of which Riot's mining shells were built for. Conversion capex could be $5-10M/MW, requiring multi-billion in financing that dilutes equity heavily. Hyperscalers (Microsoft, Google, Meta) prefer building their own; Riot's natural customers are Tier-2 AI compute (CoreWeave, Lambda, neo-clouds) — those tenants are themselves financially fragile. AMD lease is real but small relative to capacity; one swallow doesn't make a summer. Bitcoin price downturn (e.g., post-halving difficulty squeeze) crushes interim cash flow. Starboard's history with technical pivots (Box, Salesforce) is good; with energy/infrastructure pivots, less proven.

**Bull case:** The grid-power scarcity is real and getting worse — ERCOT interconnect queues are 5+ years out for new builds. Riot's Corsicana site is one of a handful of multi-hundred-MW shovel-ready sites in North America. Even one additional hyperscaler lease (Microsoft, Oracle, Meta rumors all circulating) at $250-400M annual rent transforms the equity story from "miner" to "data-center landlord with optionality." Path to re-rate: (1) second hyperscaler announcement → multiple expansion from miner-comp to data-center-comp, (2) potential REIT spin of HPC assets at premium valuation, (3) sale of mining business to Marathon/CleanSpark to fund full conversion. Starboard's letter explicitly invokes $1.6B EBITDA potential — math implies $40-60 stock if executed.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K discloses Riot's power purchase agreements include a controversial demand-response program with ERCOT — Riot gets paid to curtail mining during grid stress, generating ~$100M+ in some quarters. That ERCOT relationship is the real moat: it took years to negotiate and would transfer to any HPC tenant. Second alpha point: Corsicana's substation interconnect is already energized for 1GW — moving from "permitted" to "energized" cuts 24-36 months off any HPC competitor. Third: the AMD lease is structured as a hosting deal, not a real estate lease — meaning Riot retains operational responsibility (and margin) on power management, which is a higher-margin model than pure colocation. Fourth: Starboard's February letter reportedly cited specific MWs of stranded capacity that should be repurposed — a level of operating detail that suggests deep insider engagement, not a drive-by activist.
