# {NUMBER}. YALA — Yalla Group Limited
## CASH FORTRESS  |  Score: 71/100  |  POSITION SIZE: $3,500 (of $100K)

### THE SETUP
A genuinely profitable Middle East voice-chat and casual-gaming platform — Cayman holdco, Dubai-headquartered, NYSE-listed — with $754.6M of cash and short-term investments against $1.11B market cap (68% net cash) and a fresh $150M share-repurchase authorization announced March 9, 2026. Net margin 43.3% on $341.9M of revenue. The market is treating it like a melting Chinese ADR; the filings show a UAE-tax-domiciled, cash-generative platform with active capital return.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Yalla Group (NYSE: YALA) operates Yalla (voice-centric group chat), Yalla Ludo (casual gaming with in-game voice), YallaChat, WeMuslim, and a broadening portfolio of mid-core/hard-core titles across the Middle East and North Africa, principally Saudi Arabia and the UAE. FY25 revenue was $341.9M (+0.7% YoY), with chatting services $216.4M and games services $124.0M (the games line accelerated +9.1% YoY). Net income was $148.1M (+10.4%), net margin 43.3%; non-GAAP net income $158.5M. Average MAUs grew 8.2% YoY to 44.8M in Q4 25, while paying users *fell* from 12.3M to 10.4M because of fewer third-party-payment promotional events. The balance sheet at year-end 2025: $527.0M cash + $1.6M restricted + $84.8M term deposits + $141.3M short-term investments = $754.6M total liquid; total liabilities just $94.2M (most of which is deferred revenue). Cumulative buybacks under the prior program reached 15.7M ADSs for $106M; in 2025 alone, $56.6M was returned. A new $150M, 24-month authorization began March 9, 2026.

The bear case starts with regulatory geography. The 20-F is explicit: VoIP services in the UAE can technically only operate in narrow circumstances under a "Closed Group Network," and provision of unlicensed VoIP services is "a criminal offense that may be penalized by a fine between AED50,000 (US$13,615) and AED1,000,000 (US$272,294) and/or imprisonment of up to two years." Regulators rely on local telecom carriers blocking unlicensed services rather than prosecuting users — a fragile equilibrium. Founder/CEO Tao Yang controls the Class B block (24.7M shares) with 10:1 votes; minority holders cannot dictate strategy. Revenue growth is a rounding error (0.7%) and Q1 26 guidance ($75M-$82M) implies a YoY decline given Ramadan timing. Paying-user attrition (-15% YoY) is a structural concern even as MAUs grow. The China-incorporated parent and Cayman holding-company structure introduce China-VIE-style perception risk despite operations being UAE-centric.

The bull case: this is a profitable, cash-rich operating business at sub-cash on operating earnings power. EV (mcap minus net cash, ex-buyback impact) is ~$360M against $148M of net income — a sub-3x EV/earnings multiple on a 43%-net-margin platform with eight years of operating history in the world's fastest-growing youth demographic. Management is *actually* returning cash: $56.6M repurchased in 2025 + $150M newly authorized = $206M of demonstrated/promised capital return on a $1.11B market cap (~18%). Saudi Arabia presence is being formalized with a Riyadh office and a Saudi eLeague partnership — local strategic positioning that is hard to replicate. UAE corporate-tax rate cuts already pulled the FY25 effective tax rate from 9.4% to 2.6%.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Three filing-level items. (1) Deferred revenue dropped from $58.1M to $55.2M (current) but a new $1.8M non-current deferred revenue line appeared — a small but novel monetization shift. (2) "Long-term investments" of $82.0M sit alongside the $754.6M of "near-cash" — these are wealth-management-product positions whose mark-to-market drives the lumpy "Investment (loss) income" line ($3.3M income FY25 vs. $2.8M loss FY24). The actual cash story is therefore slightly less liquid than the screen flag suggests. (3) Share-based compensation fell sharply from $14.7M to $10.3M YoY — a discipline signal that supports the dilution-management story behind the buyback program. (4) The company decided to *cancel* all 6.23M ADSs repurchased in 2025 — meaning these are *retired*, not held in treasury for re-issuance, which permanently lifts EPS and stops the "buyback only to fund SBC" complaint.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)

- Q1 2026 results (~May 2026) — first quarter post-Ramadan, plus launch of "desert-themed SLG title" in Q2 26.
- Saudi eLeague 2026 traction and any monetization tied to the Saudi Esports Federation partnership.
- Continued tax-rate compression as the UAE preferential-rate subsidiary regime matures (tax expense already $4.0M FY25 vs. $13.9M FY24).
