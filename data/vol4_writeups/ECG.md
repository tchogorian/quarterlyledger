### THE SETUP
ECG is the specialty electrical/mechanical/utility-construction arm spun out of MDU Resources (a regulated-utility holdco). MDU's shareholder base — utility income funds — sold the cyclical construction stub aggressively on receipt. What they unloaded is a debt-light contractor with $2.8B+ backlog, accelerating data-center mix, and direct exposure to grid-modernization and IIJA spending — i.e., the classic "boring parent dumps high-growth stub onto income investors who won't touch it" setup.

- Parent: MDU Resources Group (MDU)
- Spin date: 2024-10-31 / 2024-11-01 (~5.5 months ago)
- Distribution ratio: 1 ECG share per 4 MDU shares
- Initial trading: opened around $59 on 11/1/24; currently ~$129 (more than doubled)
- Key reasons for forced selling: MDU is a utility/dividend holdco — institutional and retail income holders sold cyclical construction reflexively; ECG launched with no dividend; sub-scale (~$3B mcap at spin) relative to peers (MTZ, PRIM, EME) kept it off most institutional construction screens for the first month

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Everus Construction Group operates two segments: Electrical & Mechanical (E&M) — commercial, institutional, industrial, and data-center construction services — and Transmission & Distribution (T&D) — overhead/underground utility line work and substations. MDU built the business via 30+ years of bolt-on acquisitions; revenue at spin was approximately $3.0B with mid-single-digit EBITDA margins and a backlog growing toward $2.8B. Critically, the data-center work mix has expanded rapidly inside E&M as hyperscaler buildouts ramped through 2024 — this segment carries margins above the legacy commercial mix and provides multi-year contracted visibility. The company spun with minimal debt (no parent debt push-down), modest pension obligations, and a clean balance sheet enabling tuck-in M&A.

The bear case: this is fundamentally a cyclical specialty contractor exposed to project-level execution risk, surety bonding, and labor cost inflation. EBITDA margins in the 5-7% range are structurally lower than IT services or software; a single mis-bid project can wipe out a quarter. The stock has already more than doubled since spin, so the easy "forced-selling discount" trade is largely played out — at ~$129 vs. $59 at open, the multiple is no longer screaming cheap. Data-center capex is at cyclical highs and a hyperscaler pause (or AI capex digestion) would compress backlog growth quickly. Comparable peers like MTZ, PRIM, and EME have run with the same theme.

The bull case: even after the run, ECG trades at a discount to peers like Quanta (PWR), MasTec (MTZ), and Primoris (PRIM) on EV/EBITDA — 12-13x vs peers at 14-17x, with arguably better growth visibility given the data-center mix. Backlog book-to-bill above 1.0x signals continued momentum. Management has guided to mid-single-digit margin expansion as data-center mix rises and as the company sheds lower-margin legacy work. With essentially no debt, ECG can pursue M&A in a fragmented contractor universe at cycle-low private multiples. Index inclusion (S&P MidCap 400, Russell 1000 reweights) once 12-month seasoning passes adds a passive bid. Insider buying post-spin and increasing sell-side initiation are early signs of institutional discovery.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Form 10 details: (1) Capital structure — ECG launched with essentially no parent-allocated long-term debt and just a working-capital revolver; this is unusual for a spin and reflects MDU keeping its utility leverage. The clean balance sheet plus ~$200M+ of cash means ECG has dry powder for tuck-in acquisitions in a fragmented contractor universe. (2) Customer concentration disclosed in the Form 10 shows no single customer above ~10% of revenue, but the top-10 includes hyperscalers and major utility customers — meaning data-center mix is real and contracted, not just project-by-project bidding. (3) Transition Services Agreement with MDU is short-dated (~12-18 months) for IT, payroll, treasury — minimal long-term parent dependency. (4) The Form 10 explicitly references "sponsor M&A" as a use of capital and management compensation is heavily weighted toward EBITDA growth and TSR — strong alignment with the deployment thesis.
