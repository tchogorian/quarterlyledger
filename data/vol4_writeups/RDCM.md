### THE SETUP
A 5G/cloud-native network assurance vendor that just delivered 17.2% revenue growth, record operating margins, $109.9M in cash with no debt — and trades at roughly half its market cap in net cash. Customer concentration is the moat (multi-year Tier-1 carrier contracts) and the risk (top three = ~86% of revenue). When a Tier-1 telecom embeds your software in their 5G core, you don't get ripped out cheaply.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
RADCOM is an Israel-headquartered software company that sells "service assurance" — the systems that monitor whether AT&T's, Rakuten Mobile's and Verizon's mobile networks are actually delivering performance to subscribers. The screen flagged it because cash and short-term deposits of ~$109.9M sit against a ~$218M market cap (49% of mcap), the company is profitable (TTM net income ~$12M), and it just printed a record FY2025 with $71.5M revenue (+17.2%). Gross margin reached ~76.8%. Six consecutive years of growth. 2026 guidance: 8–12% revenue growth (~$78.6M midpoint). It's a small-cap software company priced like a melting ice cube but acting like a steady compounder.

The bear case starts with concentration: ~86% of 2025 revenue came from the top three customers (AT&T and Rakuten Mobile are public; Verizon and Vodafone have been historical names). Lose one, lose roughly a quarter to a third of revenue overnight — and these are deals that ramp slowly, so the offsetting wins take 18–36 months. Lumpy bookings make any single year of guidance vulnerable to a slipped contract. RADCOM is also Israel-based: geopolitical disruption, currency (shekel) exposure, and a thin float (~$220M mcap, modest daily volume) all amplify volatility. Slowing growth guidance (8–12% vs. 17% delivered) suggests management sees a deceleration into 2026 absent a new Tier-1 win.

The bull case: the cash cushion makes you nearly indifferent to a one-year revenue hiccup. Stripping cash, the operating business trades around $108M against $12M net income — roughly 9x ex-cash earnings on a software franchise with 76% gross margins, a five-year contract pipeline, and a real shot at adding a new Tier-1 in 2026 (management's stated focus). 5G monetization, AI-driven assurance and Open RAN expansion are tailwinds. Catalysts include incremental Rakuten Symphony wins, expansion at existing customers as they roll out 5G standalone cores, and potential capital return — the cash hoard is large relative to operating needs. Downside is cushioned by the cash; upside is operating leverage.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 86% top-three concentration looks scary in a screen — but the same filings reveal these are multi-year, deeply embedded software contracts inside Tier-1 5G core deployments, not transactional sales. AT&T has been a customer for years; Rakuten Mobile chose RADCOM as its assurance vendor at the beginning of its greenfield mobile build and stayed as it crossed 10M subscribers in December 2025. Switching costs in network assurance are extreme: the software ingests live network telemetry across thousands of nodes, and rip-and-replace creates blind spots no carrier accepts mid-rollout. The screen also can't see the operating margin trajectory — record margins in 2025 imply most incremental revenue is dropping to the bottom line, which compounds quickly on a sub-$80M revenue base.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- New Tier-1 carrier signing (management has flagged this as the key 2026 driver)
- Expansion deals at AT&T / Rakuten as 5G standalone deployments scale
- Capital allocation announcement — buyback expansion or special dividend on $109.9M cash pile
