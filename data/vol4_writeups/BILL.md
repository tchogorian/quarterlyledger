### ANALYST SUMMARY
BILL Holdings provides AP/AR automation and embedded payments for ~480,000 small businesses, plus the Divvy spend management platform and Invoice2go. The core thesis was always "QuickBooks for payments" — automate B2B paper checks, take a clip on every transaction. Revenue growth has decelerated from 70%+ in 2021 to mid-teens, gross dollar volume growth slowed, and the float-income tailwind from rates is reversing. Starboard targeted BILL precisely because the company executed the playbook of tech-bubble overspend (Divvy/Invoice2go acquisitions at peak), got fat on float income, and now trades at trough multiples while still generating ~$200M+ FCF and sitting on ~$1.6B cash.

Bear case: Embedded finance landscape is brutal — Stripe, Adyen, Brex, Ramp all expanding into BILL's lane; banks (JPM, BofA) pushing free AP automation to retain SMB deposits. Take-rate compression is the existential risk: even with volumes up, revenue per dollar of TPV is structurally falling. Float income headwind from Fed cuts directly hits high-margin revenue. Strategic buyer pool is narrow — Intuit (regulatory issues), SAP (just bought a competitor), Visa/MA (too small). Sale process could fail to clear at premium and Starboard ends up with operational reset that takes 2+ years.

Bull case: Cash-rich balance sheet ($1.6B+ cash, manageable convert debt) means even at zero growth this prints meaningful FCF yield. Starboard's pressure has already triggered cost discipline — opex growth slowing visibly. PE buyers (Vista, Thoma, Silver Lake) are well-positioned for take-private at $55-65 given the cash + recurring revenue stream. Even absent a sale, simply removing growth-at-any-cost spending and returning capital re-rates the multiple. Re-rate path to $50-60 in 12 months on capital return alone; $65-75 on take-out.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

Float income — buried in "interest income on customer funds" — was ~$170M in FY24 and falling fast as Fed cuts hit; this disclosure is the silent earnings shoe-drop.
