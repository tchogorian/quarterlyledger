# {NUMBER}. ORMP — Oramed Pharmaceuticals Inc.
## SUB-CASH OPERATING  |  Score: 58/100  |  POSITION SIZE: $2,000 (of $100K)

### THE SETUP
A failed-Phase-3 oral-insulin biotech that has quietly transformed into a specialty financier. Oramed's "profitability" is an artifact: $89M of FY25 financial income from its Scilex notes, warrants and royalty deal — not from drug development. Net cash is ~80% of market cap, the company just paid a special dividend funded by Scilex repayments, and it is actively returning capital while shareholders still treat it as a binary biotech.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Oramed Pharmaceuticals (NASDAQ: ORMP) is an Israeli holding company with two distinct lives. Drug-development life: oral insulin candidate ORMD-0801 missed both primary and secondary endpoints in the ORA-D-013-1 Phase 3 trial. The company plans a 60-patient U.S. trial in H2 2026 to validate "responder" subpopulations and has handed clinical management to a JV (with HTIT, though HTIT failed initial closing conditions) and to Lifeward (POD platform transferred January 2026). Investment-vehicle life: in 2023 Oramed lent $101.875M (Tranche A) to Scilex Holding; later added $25M Tranche B plus warrants and a 4% royalty on ZTLido. As of March 26, 2026, Scilex had repaid $69.2M of Tranche A and $13.0M of Tranche B; per company communications cumulative returns reached approximately $118M on the original $99.5M cost basis. FY25 revenue of $2.0M (versus zero in FY24), operating loss of $14.1M, but financial income of $89.5M produced reported net income of $64.0M.

The bear case is direct. The drug pipeline failed and is being de-prioritized in favor of small responder-subgroup trials; per the 10-K, "we expect to discontinue oral insulin clinical activities for T2D." The Scilex creditor exposure is concentrated and risky — Scilex itself has disclosed "substantial doubt about its ability to continue as a going concern" in its filings. The 10-K explicitly warns "Scilex is unable to service its obligations under the Note and defaults on such Note, it could have a material adverse effect on our business." Operating cash burn of $9.1M in FY25 will continue. Working capital fell from $137.5M to $114.2M YoY — the cash pile is shrinking even as the company harvests Scilex repayments, because of the dividend payable and continued investments in Alpha Tau, Lifeward, and real-estate ventures (Hapisga, Profit Sharing Loan).

The bull case rests on the realisation pattern. Scilex has *actually paid* — $118M on $99.5M cost basis already, and Oramed retains roughly $27M of remaining notes and a $12M convertible note (convertible at $36/share at Oramed's option), plus the 4% ZTLido royalty perpetuity, plus warrants. That residual stack is essentially "free" if you're paying market cap below cash. The company has aggressively repurchased shares: 899,609 in 2025 at $2.39 average and a separate 1,155,367 from HTIT at $2.23 — both at significant discounts to NAV. Board-authorized $20M Buy-Back Program was extended to June 2026. A January 2026 cash dividend was announced after the Scilex Option Agreement payoff.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K's "Investments at fair value" section is where the alpha lives. Oramed elected the fair-value option for the Scilex notes "in order to reduce operational complexity of bifurcating embedded derivatives" — meaning the carrying value of those notes already reflects management's expected repayment scenarios, weighted across multiple paths. Translated: the $89M financial income line is part real cash interest and part mark-to-fair-value re-rating as Scilex actually pays. The company has also become a hidden real-estate financier — September 2024 Profit Sharing Loan Agreement and March 2025 Hapisga loan are property project finance, not biotech. April 2025 Alpha Tau equity stake and November 2025 $3M Lifeward secured note are further capital allocations away from the original drug thesis. Insider activity: a flurry of Form 4 filings in March 2026 (Section 16 transactions on March 16, 18 and 31, 2026) signals active trading by officers/directors — review these before sizing.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Remaining Scilex Tranche B amortization payments through October 2026 — additional ~$30M of expected cash inflow if paid on schedule.
- Special-dividend follow-on: management has shown willingness to declare cash distributions when Scilex repays.
- 60-patient responder-subgroup U.S. trial start in H2 2026 (binary biotech optionality, but *the cash already pays you to wait*).
- Continued buyback execution under the $20M program (extended to June 2026).
