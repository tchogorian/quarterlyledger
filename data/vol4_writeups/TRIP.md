### ANALYST SUMMARY
Tripadvisor operates three segments: Brand Tripadvisor (legacy hotel meta-search/reviews), Viator (experiences booking — fastest growing OTA category globally), and TheFork (European restaurant booking, ~70k restaurants). Viator is the crown jewel — high-growth, asset-light, taking share from GetYourGuide and Klook. TheFork is the European #1 in restaurant bookings. Starboard targeted TRIP because the public market values the consolidated entity at ~1x revenue when peer experience platforms (Airbnb Experiences embed, Klook private rounds) trade at 4-6x, and standalone TheFork comps (Booking's OpenTable peer set) suggest $700M-$1B value alone.

Bear case: Liberty TripAdvisor (LTRPA) holds a ~28% supervoting stake that effectively blocks any change-of-control without Greg Maffei's blessing — historically Liberty has held assets long after they should have been monetized. Hotel meta-search is in secular decline as Booking and Expedia squeeze ad pricing and Google grabs share with free hotel modules. Viator faces tightening competition from Airbnb Experiences relaunch and Klook's IPO war chest. TheFork has never generated material EBITDA. A messy Liberty unwind could push any sale 18-24 months out, during which core revenue keeps eroding.

Bull case: Starboard's settlement is the unlock. Two directors are in, two more on deck, and a formal TheFork review means a process is committed — not aspirational. Liberty's own structure is rumored to be undergoing simplification (Maffei stepped back from Liberty Media chair), which could remove the supervoting overhang. Viator alone, growing 20%+ at scale, is worth $1.5-2B in a strategic sale to Booking, Expedia, or Airbnb. If TheFork sells for $500M-$700M and Viator goes for $1.5-2B, residual hotel meta is essentially free option value at current prices. Path to $18-22 over 12-18 months.
