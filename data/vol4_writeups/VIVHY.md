### ANALYST SUMMARY

Post-split Vivendi is a near-pure listed-stake holdco. The crown jewel is ~10% net economic exposure to Universal Music Group (UMG) — the world's largest recorded-music company, currently worth EUR ~38B at UMG.AS. Telecom Italia is a smaller residual stake being progressively trimmed. Gameloft (mobile gaming) is the only meaningful unlisted asset, marked conservatively. The Bolloré family controls ~29.3% via Compagnie de l'Odet. Vivendi has self-published monthly NAV since December 2024 to combat info asymmetry, a corporate-governance positive.

The bear case is the Bolloré overhang and process risk. Bolloré orchestrated the 2024 split partly to escape "de facto control" rules under French market law. The Court of Cassation in November 2025 overturned the Paris Court of Appeal finding that Bolloré controls Vivendi — a setback for minority shareholders who had hoped for a mandatory tender at fair value. The case has been remanded; oral arguments come May 22, 2026. UMG itself has fallen ~10% YTD 2026 on streaming-growth deceleration, dragging Vivendi NAV. Central run-rate ~EUR 85m/yr is a permanent stub-cost. Gameloft is hard to mark and could disappoint. Liquidity is moderate; VIVHY ADR is OTC pink with wide spreads.

The bull case is binary catalysts paired with mark-to-market hard NAV. If the May 2026 hearing — or any subsequent appeal — re-confirms control, Bolloré is forced to launch a mandatory cash offer at a "fair price" that arbitrators argue should approximate NAV. That alone implies a near-double from current levels. Even without a tender, continued asset sales (Vivendi has trimmed Telefonica and Telecom Italia in 2024-25) plus the self-published NAV format compress the discount. UMG's strategic value (Spotify renegotiation, AI music licensing, possible US listing in 2026) provides NAV-growth optionality. The current ~50% discount to a transparent, mark-to-market portfolio is the type of asymmetric setup Asset Value Investors (AVI) has been writing checks for.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The May 22, 2026 Court of Cassation remand hearing is a hard date — minority shareholders Phitrust and Ciam have been activist on this since 2024, with the AMF originally siding with them (decision later voided). In early 2026, Compagnie de l'Odet (Bolloré's own holdco) sold ~6m UMG shares at EUR 27.50 — a quiet way to extract value before any forced bid. Vivendi's monthly NAV publication is unique among European holdcos and has narrowed the discount somewhat since launch but still leaves 50% — historically high. The Gameloft division is the wild card: rumors of a sale or carve-out have circulated. Note: VIV.PA is the primary line; VIVHY ADR is OTC pink and quotes can drift relative to Paris.
