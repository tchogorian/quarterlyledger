# {NUMBER}. UTMD — Utah Medical Products, Inc.
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER  |  Score: 77/100  |  POSITION SIZE: $3,000 (of $100K)

### THE SETUP
A boring, micro-cap niche medical-device maker with 57%+ gross margins, $85.8M cash on a $223M market cap (38%), zero debt, no meaningful stock-based compensation, a CEO-Chairman who has run the place since 1992, and a buyback machine that retired ~4.5% of shares in 2025. Revenue and earnings dipped in 2025 — the screen entry point. Quality you can't usually buy at this multiple.

### ANALYST SUMMARY
UTMD makes specialty obstetric and gynecologic medical devices: intrauterine pressure catheters (IUPCs), the Filshie Clip female sterilization device, neonatal blood-pressure monitoring (Deltran), electrosurgical disposables, vacuum-assisted delivery systems, and obstetric vacuum cups. Manufacturing is in Utah, Ireland, England and Australia. Screen flagged it for $85.5M net cash (38% of mcap), TTM net income ~$11.3M, and profitability with low SBC. FY2025 revenue $38.5M (-5.8%), GAAP EPS $3.483 (-12.1%), gross margin maintained above 57%. In 2025 the company repurchased 148,935 shares for $8.36M (avg $56.10) — about 4.5% of shares — and paid $4.0M in dividends. The 2025 weakness was driven by a PendoTECH OEM customer slowdown and a Chinese distributor cancellation that produced a written-off receivable.

The bear case is concentration and growth. PendoTECH (a single OEM) and a now-burnt Chinese distributor relationship caused the 2025 revenue dip. A $38.5M-revenue medical device company has limited operating leverage, no high-growth product line, and faces gradual ASP/competitive erosion in legacy categories like Filshie Clip (which itself faced FDA-related labeling controversy historically). Kevin Cornwell is 80+ and has been CEO since 1992 — there is real key-person and succession risk; the 1998 change-of-control agreement is a tell. The float is tiny (~$220M mcap, low daily volume), making both entry and exit costly. International exposure (Ireland, UK, Australia, China) creates FX drag. There is no realistic 2x re-rating catalyst; this is a "compound at high single digits + buyback" story, not a multibagger.

The bull case: this is a textbook Munger company hiding in a screen. 57%+ gross margin specialty medical, family-controlled (Cornwell ~5.2%, ~190K shares ~$11M+ insider stake), zero debt, $85.8M cash growing despite buybacks, ~zero stock-based compensation (almost unheard of), and a 30+ year CEO who treats shareholders like partners. Strip the cash and you pay ~$137M for ~$11M of net income — ~12x earnings on a 57%-GM annuity that has paid uninterrupted dividends and bought back stock for years. The 2025 dip is cyclical (one OEM, one distributor) not structural — a normalization in 2026 plus the smaller share count creates EPS upside.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The screen sees "P/E ~20" and gets bored. The 10-K reveals one of the rarest combinations in micro-cap healthcare: a CEO who has been in the seat since 1992, owns ~5% of the float, takes a $152K base salary, no stock options awarded, and runs the cash like a private owner — buying back 4.5% of shares in a single year ($8.4M) at $56.10 average while the company simultaneously paid $4M in dividends and grew the cash balance. There is no SBC dilution silently eating returns. The filings also show that 2025 weakness is identifiable and idiosyncratic (one OEM + one distributor write-off), not a market-share story. Manufacturing across four countries (Utah, Ireland, UK, Australia) creates FX drag but also tariff insulation. The Filshie Clip and IUPC franchises throw off cash with minimal reinvestment.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- 2026 normalization off the PendoTECH/Chinese distributor trough (easy comps)
- Continued buyback at depressed prices (4.5% retired in 2025; pace likely continues)
- Special dividend or accelerated buyback if cash hits $90M+ on a $223M cap
