# {NUMBER}. PERF — Perfect Corp.
## SUB-CASH OPERATING  |  Score: 64/100  |  POSITION SIZE: $2,500 (of $100K)

### THE SETUP
A profitable Taiwan-based AI/AR beauty SaaS — IPO'd 2022 via SPAC — trading at $172M of cash and time deposits against a $177M market cap. As of March 18, 2026, the founder (CEO Alice H. Chang) and parent CyberLink filed a preliminary non-binding take-private offer at $1.95 per share. That is the asymmetry: you are buying a profitable, growing business at sub-cash with a live insider bid sitting on the table.

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Perfect Corp. (NYSE: PERF) builds AI- and AR-powered virtual try-on, skin-analysis and image-generation tools sold to beauty/fashion brands (B2B) and to consumers via the YouCam app suite (B2C subscriptions). FY25 revenue was $69.2M (+14.9% YoY), driven by $61.1M of "AI- and AR- cloud solutions and subscription" revenue (+13.5%). Net income was $4.6M; operating cash flow was $13.3M. The screen flag — net cash $161.6M against market cap $177M (91% ratio) — is grounded in $126.0M cash and equivalents plus $36.3M six-month time deposits and $10.2M U.S. Treasuries (per the Q4 release). 908,000 active YouCam subscribers and 859 brand clients (135 "Key Customers" generating >$50K trailing twelve months). Spun out of Taiwan Stock Exchange-listed CyberLink, which remains a major shareholder and lessor.

The bear case begins with the dual-class structure: per the 20-F, "each holder of Class B Ordinary Shares is entitled to ten (10) votes per share" and CEO Chang holds 100% of Class B = 66.6% voting power. That control is now being weaponized via the take-private offer at $1.95 — a price below the prior trading range and at a discount to net cash. Filings flag that the dual-class structure may make Class A "ineligible for inclusion in any of these indices." Operating loss of $1.7M for FY25 reflects the company's reliance on financial income and a $2.0M goodwill impairment from January 2025's Wannaby acquisition. App-store dependency (Apple/Google) is explicit. Key Customer count just fell from 142 to 135 in a single quarter, hinting at B2B churn even as B2C subscriber count declined 4% sequentially.

The bull case: even excluding the Wannaby write-down, Perfect would have generated operating income; the underlying B2C subscription engine is growing, gross margin expanded to 80.5% in Q4 25, and the company expects 2026 revenue growth of ~10% (range plus/minus 2%). With $172M of liquid resources against a market cap that briefly dipped below cash, you are paying a negative price for a 14% revenue grower with a 77% gross margin business and recurring app/SaaS economics. The Chang/CyberLink take-private bid gives a hard floor; a competing or sweetened offer is plausible if minority holders push back, and the Special Committee process itself is a forcing function for a higher price.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Three filing-only items. (1) Related-party rents and the "Perfect CyberLink Education Foundation" tie the operating company tightly to CyberLink — an item that becomes very relevant in a take-private negotiation, since CyberLink is now both bidder and landlord. (2) Geographic disclosure: "revenue generated from the PRC accounted for less than 3% of our total revenue" — China exposure is small, removing one common emerging-tech overhang. (3) The Wannaby acquisition (January 2025, Lithuania-based) added headcount and R&D burn and was promptly written down; this is the kind of value-destructive M&A that makes a low-ball take-private easier to justify. Insider activity to watch: Chang, Wei-Hsin Tsen and Weichuan Liu collectively own minor Class A stakes — but Chang's full Class B block (16.79M shares = 16.5% economic, 66.6% voting) drives the deal math.

### CATALYSTS (next 6-12 months)
- Special Committee response to the March 18, 2026 Chang/CyberLink $1.95/share take-private proposal — a definitive agreement, rejection, or competing bid is the dominant catalyst.
- Q1 2026 earnings (~April 2026) testing whether the active-subscriber decline reverses after the price-increase initiative.
- Continued cash build vs. SBC dilution — the gap between cash flow ($13.3M) and reported net income ($4.6M) is largely SBC, so a tighter SBC budget would re-rate the multiple.
