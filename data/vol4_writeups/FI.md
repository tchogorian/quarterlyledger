### ANALYST SUMMARY
Fiserv is one of two global merchant-acquiring duopolists (with FIS) — operating Clover (SMB POS), Carat (enterprise digital commerce), the merchant acquiring book from the First Data acquisition, and a vast core-bank processing business serving thousands of community banks. Revenue base is ~$20B with mid-teens operating margins. In 2025 the stock collapsed 67% on a combination of: (1) Argentina exposure pressure, (2) Clover growth deceleration to mid-teens (vs market expecting 25%+), (3) competitive pressure from Stripe/Toast/Block in SMB POS, and (4) credibility loss after multiple guidance cuts. JANA targeted FI because the assets remain world-class — Clover alone is arguably worth $25-35B as a standalone — but management credibility and conglomerate discount have crushed the multiple to a level that prices in continued execution failure.

Bear case: Payments processing has structural revenue-per-transaction compression as competition intensifies — Stripe undercutting on enterprise, Toast winning restaurant SMB, Block winning micro-merchants. Clover's growth deceleration may not be cyclical — it may be share loss. Argentina/LatAm exposure remains a tail risk. Mike Lyons is a banker by background (ex-PNC President), not a payments operator — turnaround takes time and could disappoint. The "One Fiserv" platform consolidation is a multi-year IT project with high execution risk. Sub-$50 retest possible on next negative print.

Bull case: At ~12x EPS the stock prices in permanent multiple compression that doesn't match the asset quality. Lyons brings discipline, focus, and willingness to divest non-core (the Output Solutions printing business and parts of biller services are obvious carve-out candidates worth $3-5B). Capital return runway is enormous — $4B+ annual FCF, modest leverage, share count down 30% over 5 years. JANA's friendly posture is a tell: they don't make $150M bets on $30B caps without high conviction in the operator. If 2026 stabilizes and 2027 returns to algo (8-12% EPS growth), multiple re-rates to 15-17x = $80-100. Carat IPO/spin-off is the moonshot — could unlock $20B+ of latent value.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 67% drawdown in 2025 was preceded by a series of insider sales by prior CEO Frank Bisignano (now Trump administration SSA Commissioner) — the timing now looks especially well-marked. Mike Lyons' compensation package (disclosed in 2025 proxy supplement) has aggressive 3-year stock price targets that imply management believes $90+ is achievable. The "Output Solutions" business — printing and mailing financial statements for banks — is a stranded legacy asset clearly destined for divestiture; JANA almost certainly identified this in their work. The Clover business is reported as part of "Merchant Solutions" but standalone disclosure has improved over the past year — setting the stage for a possible spin or tracking-stock structure that the market hasn't priced in.
