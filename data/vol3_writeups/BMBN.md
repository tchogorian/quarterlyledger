### ANALYST SUMMARY
Benchmark Bankshares is the holding company for Benchmark Community Bank, a 1971-founded Virginia community bank headquartered in Kenbridge, Virginia, with approximately 18 branches across central Southside Virginia and northern North Carolina. Fiscal 2025 was a record year: net income of $19.8M ($4.46/share) versus $15.9M ($3.55) in 2024, a 25.2% increase driven by balance-sheet growth, NIM expansion, and an improved efficiency ratio. Total assets reached $1.4B (up 11.7%), loans held for investment $1.1B (up 13.5%), deposits $1.2B (up 11.2%), and shareholders' equity $126.9M (up 15.9%). The annualized dividend is $0.88/share for a yield of approximately 2.8%, with a payout ratio near 20.5%. Beta is approximately 0.17, reflecting the OTC, illiquid, deeply-held shareholder base typical of small-town Virginia community banks. P/E ~8.4x, P/B ~1.35x, ROE ~16%.

The bear case is liquidity and disclosure. BMBN deregistered with the SEC long ago — the latest EDGAR filings on file are from 2003-2004 — and now reports through state and OTC channels rather than full SEC 10-K cadence. That makes deep diligence harder for outside investors and creates a permanent valuation discount. Loan growth of 13.5% in a single year is fast for a community bank and, in a flat-to-down credit cycle, can mask coming asset-quality issues; with no detailed NPL/charge-off data in mainstream sources, you are buying on faith. Geographic concentration in rural southern Virginia exposes the bank to a narrow agricultural and small-business customer base. OTC float means a single seller can move the price 5-10% on any given day.

The bull case is exactly the boring, durable kind of community bank Munger would have admired: 50+ years in business, recognizable hometown brand, conservative balance sheet, growing book value, paying a meaningful dividend, and trading at modest multiples. ROE of 16% with a 20% payout ratio implies the retained earnings can compound book at 12-13% for years if credit holds. At 1.35x book, you're paying a slight premium but receiving genuine quality and underwriting discipline that has survived multiple cycles. The illiquidity discount is the price of admission, not a defect.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Because BMBN reports primarily through call reports and the OTC Markets disclosure channel rather than SEC 10-K, you should pull the FFIEC Call Report directly for granular detail: NIM, efficiency ratio, NPL ratio, allowance/loans, Tier 1 capital, CRE concentration, and uninsured-deposit ratio. Local press (Kenbridge Victoria Dispatch, Farmville Herald) has been the primary disclosure venue for headline earnings. Insider ownership is concentrated in long-tenured Virginia families; this is functionally a closely-held bank with public-market liquidity tacked on. Trading volume can be zero on many days. Tangible book per share is roughly $28-29 based on $126.9M equity / 4.4M shares, but verify against the Call Report.
