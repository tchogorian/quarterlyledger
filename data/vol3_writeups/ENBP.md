### ANALYST SUMMARY

ENB Financial Corp is the holding company for Ephrata National Bank, an OCC-chartered national bank founded April 11, 1881 and headquartered in Ephrata, Lancaster County, Pennsylvania. The bank serves the heart of Pennsylvania Dutch country with a customer base anchored by the Amish, Mennonite, and broader Plain communities — a rare deposit franchise built on multi-generational trust, low loan-to-deposit utilization, and exceptionally sticky non-interest-bearing demand accounts. As of December 31, 2025, total deposits stood at $1.873 billion, with non-interest-bearing demand at $649 million (~35% of deposits) — meaningfully better than most $2 billion peers. Book value supports ~5.69 million shares outstanding held by approximately 850 shareholders. Net interest margin expanded 32 basis points to 3.19% in 2025, and net income reached $21.6 million.

The bear case starts with the December 2024 acquisition of Cecil Bancorp (Maryland), which added $218.7 million in assets, $153.5 million in loans, and $187.4 million in deposits — meaningful integration risk for a bank with no recent M&A track record, and dilutive to the famously homogeneous Lancaster franchise. ENBP trades on the OTCQX (limited-volume), creating real liquidity friction for institutional buyers. The 2025 P/E of ~6.5 is partly distorted by a single-year boost from NIM expansion and a lower provision; normalized earnings sustain a higher multiple. Average non-interest-bearing deposit growth of just 3.6% suggests the deposit franchise is mature, not growing. Pennsylvania community banking faces headwinds from regional consolidation by Mid-Penn, Univest, and Penns Woods.

The bull case is one of the strongest deposit franchises in the small-bank universe trading at 0.87x book. Lancaster County's Amish/Mennonite community has the lowest loan demand and highest demand-deposit balances of any U.S. demographic — ENBP's 35% NIB ratio reflects this structural advantage. Net charge-offs were a trivial $92 thousand on a roughly $1+ billion loan book, indicating the credit culture is intact. With Cecil acquisition synergies still ahead and NIM having room to expand toward 3.4–3.5%, EPS could grow meaningfully into 2026. Dividends of $4.08 million on $21.6 million of net income (~19% payout) leave significant capital for buybacks under existing authorization. ENBP is the textbook below-book community bank with a real moat that screening data underweights.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The Lancaster County, PA customer base is genuinely differentiated. The Amish and Mennonite communities operate on cash-basis principles, hold large checking balances for farm and business operations, and transact through a small network of trusted local institutions — with Ephrata National being one of the dominant relationship banks for nearly 145 years. The bank also operates ENB Insurance, a full-service insurance agency offering commercial and personal lines, providing stable fee income. Average non-interest-bearing demand accounts grew $21.5 million in 2025, described as "the Corporation's cheapest source of funding for balance sheet growth." The Cecil acquisition extends the franchise into Maryland's Cecil County, geographically adjacent to Lancaster's southeast border.
