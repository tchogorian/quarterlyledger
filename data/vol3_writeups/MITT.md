# {NUMBER}. MITT — TPG Mortgage Investment Trust, Inc.
## DISTRESSED ARB | Score: 53/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY

MITT — formerly AG Mortgage Investment Trust, renamed TPG Mortgage Investment Trust effective December 26, 2025 — is an externally managed residential mortgage REIT focused primarily on residential whole loans, particularly Non-QM, Home Equity Loans, and Non-Agency RMBS collateralized by these loan types. The 10-K describes Arc Home, LLC, the company's residential mortgage loan originator in which MITT owns approximately 66%, as a strategic earnings driver. The manager is AG REIT Management, LLC, "a wholly-owned subsidiary of TPG (NASDAQ: TPG), a leading global alternative asset management firm." At December 31, 2025 reported book value was $10.48 per share; total dividends declared in 2025 were $0.85 (a 13.3% increase YoY), and MITT raised its Q1 2026 quarterly common dividend to $0.24 from $0.23. Screen prints P/B 0.73, P/E 8.7, MCap $248M.

The bear case is mortgage REIT 101 — and amplified by the externally-managed structure. Book value is "marked-to-market" and exquisitely sensitive to mortgage spreads, prepayment speeds, and the yield curve. The 10-K explicitly flags risks from "changes in interest rates and the fair value of our assets, including negative changes resulting in margin calls relating to the financing of our assets," changes in the yield curve, increased default/delinquency rates with decreased recovery rates, and the inherent risk that hedging "may or may not protect us from interest rate and credit risk volatility." The company also flags integration risk for the Western Asset Mortgage Capital Corporation ("WMC") acquisition. External management means a fee leakage to TPG that would otherwise accrue to common holders; preferred dividends ($19.2M in 2024) sit ahead of common.

The bull case is straightforward: a portfolio rotation story under TPG's well-capitalized parent. The new TPG branding (and Q4 2025 earnings call) emphasized capital rotation toward higher-yielding home equity, exercising call rights on legacy securitizations, and growing Arc Home's profitability. Trading at ~73% of book with an ~11-12% common dividend yield (recently raised), the discount overstates the credit risk on a portfolio that is overwhelmingly senior secured first-lien residential mortgages. Total return in 2025 exceeded 42% including dividends and price appreciation. If TPG can both narrow the book-discount and grow EPS via Arc Home and call-right rotation, the math compounds.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The 10-K reveals the under-the-hood Arc Home stake: an approximate 66% ownership in a residential originator that is a related-party entity, meaning a meaningful chunk of MITT's run-rate "earnings power" is contingent on origination volumes and gain-on-sale margins at a captive lender, not on mortgage REIT spread mechanics. This both adds optionality (a lender that produces MITT's own paper at scale) and adds a layer of opaque mark-to-market exposure: Arc Home values are subjective, can swing on origination conditions, and the 66% stake means consolidation/equity-method accounting noise. A non-trivial portion of the headline $0.85 in dividends is supported by Arc Home contribution; if origination volumes contract, the dividend math changes quickly.
