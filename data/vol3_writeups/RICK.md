# {NUMBER}. RICK — RCI Hospitality Holdings, Inc.
## CYCLICAL TROUGH | Score: 56/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY

RCI Hospitality is a Texas-domiciled holding company that operates two principal segments: Nightclubs (adult-entertainment clubs under brands such as Rick's Cabaret, Jaguars Club, Tootsie's Cabaret, XTC Cabaret, Club Onyx, and Hoops Cabaret) totaling 59 club locations, plus 11 Bombshells Restaurant & Bar units — a military-themed casual-dining concept. The screen flagged RICK at P/B 0.82, P/E 20.0 on a $215M market cap. The fiscal-2025 (year ended September 30, 2025) 10-K shows Nightclubs segment revenue down ~0.6% with income from operations up 20.1% (same-store sales -2.1%), while the Bombshells segment posted revenue down 29.2% but income from operations swinging to a $177K profit from a $10.8M loss prior year (same-store sales -13.6%). Total long-term debt is $214.6M. Management spent $11.9M on treasury-stock purchases and $2.5M on dividends in FY2025, paid down $20.5M of debt, and explicitly targets fiscal 2029 results of $400M revenue, $75M free cash flow, and 7.5M shares outstanding.

The bear case is governance turbulence on top of operational drag. The 10-K discloses that long-time CEO Eric Langan and CFO Bradley Chhay "subsequently stepped down from those positions in November 2025" amid a New York lawsuit naming both individuals and the company. Langan was the architect of the buyback machine and the operating philosophy; his departure is a material change that the headline screen does not capture. Bombshells has been a serial value-destroyer with cumulative impairments referenced in the 10-K (including $38.5M in 2024 across goodwill, SOB-license, property, ROU, and tradename impairments). Adult-entertainment regulation, local zoning fights, and labor-classification litigation (independent-contractor entertainer disputes) remain perpetual tail risks. The 86% drop in net income from 2022's record to 2025 indicates the business is well off peak. Continuing NASDAQ listing requires ongoing compliance — already flagged as a risk in the filing.

The bull case rests on RCI's structural cash-generation profile. Operating cash declined only 23% from the 2022 peak versus a 77% drop in net income — that gap is GAAP impairments rather than real economic harm. The company aggressively closed underperforming Bombshells, which improved the segment from a $10.8M operating loss to break-even. Buybacks of $11.9M plus debt paydown of $20.5M plus dividends totaled $34.8M of capital returned and de-levered in a single year — significant for a $215M cap company. Catalysts include continued Bombshells stabilization, opportunistic club M&A at 3-4x EBITDA (the Langan playbook), buyback acceleration if the multiple stays compressed, and a refreshed management team that may or may not maintain the same capital-allocation discipline. Insider ownership remains meaningful.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The most important missed item is the November 2025 management departure — Langan was the buyback machine, and a screen looking at trailing capital-return data is implicitly underwriting his continued tenure. A second item: the 10-K notes that certain commercial bank debts "are guaranteed by the Company's former CEO" — Langan's personal guarantee may need replacement now that he has stepped down, potentially triggering refinancing at higher rates or covenant changes. Third, the SOB (Sexually Oriented Business) license is a state and local asset that has historical impairment exposure but also represents real scarcity value; new licenses are nearly impossible to obtain in major markets, so existing operators have a regulatory moat. Fourth, the fiscal-2029 target of 7.5M shares outstanding (from current ~9M) implies a 17% share-count reduction over four years — credible only if cash generation holds and the new CEO maintains the buyback cadence. Texas-domicile means director and officer indemnification is broad.
