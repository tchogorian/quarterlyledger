# {NUMBER}. XYF — X Financial
## VALUE TRAP RISK | Score: 47/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
X Financial (Xiaoying) is a Shenzhen-based online consumer micro-lender that facilitates small-ticket unsecured loans (the "Xiaoying Card Loan" product) to underbanked Chinese borrowers via institutional funding partners. Loan facilitation amount grew from RMB73.7B in 2022 to RMB104.9B in 2024 to RMB130.6B in 2025. Disclosed FY2024 operating margin was 31.9%, net income margin 26.2%, ROE 24.1% — exceptional in absolute terms, which is why the screen flags a P/E of 0.9x and P/B of 0.16x. The company pays a dividend (a US$0.28/ADS semi-annual was declared with Q4 2025 results, payable May 2026) and has been buying back stock — share repurchase payable to a major shareholder ballooned in late 2024. On the surface this is the cheapest bank-like asset on a US exchange.

The bear case is rapidly worsening credit. Q4 2025 results showed delinquency rates 31–60 days past due rising to 2.90% from 1.17% a year earlier; 91–180 day delinquencies jumped to 6.31% from 2.48%. Net income dropped 85% YoY in Q4 to RMB57.2M as provisions absorbed the higher charge-offs. Total Q4 revenue fell 14% YoY and 25% sequentially. China's online consumer credit cycle has clearly turned, and XYF's borrower base — already at the lower-prime/subprime end — is exposed first. Regulatory overhang is also constant: PRC has been re-tightening online lending rules, capping rates, mandating institutional funding, and there is no shortage of headlines about defunct peer platforms. Valuation cheapness reflects regulatory tail risk and a credit-quality unknown — the standard "cheap because the earnings are about to disappear" trap.

The bull case rests on the management team continuing to actually return cash, plus the possibility that 2025 was the worst quarter of a normal cycle. The 20-F shows persistent ROE 18–24% over 2022–2024 even through tightening, the dividend is real, and management has incurred buybacks. If FY2026 delinquencies stabilize and the new conservative provisioning is sufficient, P/B 0.16x is absurd — even half of a normalized ROE on tangible book would imply multi-bagger upside. But this is the kind of name where being early is the same as being wrong, and you must underwrite the credit cycle, not the multiple.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 20-F discloses that "in 2016, 0.9% of our total loan facilitation amount were Xiaoying Card Loan, while in 2022, 2023 and 2024, such proportion was 99.8%, 100%, and 100%" — single-product concentration is total. The filing also flags institutional-funding dependence: virtually all loans are now off-balance-sheet, with funding partners taking the credit risk economically but XYF wearing reputational/regulatory risk of high delinquencies. The recent Q4 prints show provisions running ahead of delinquencies, which can be read two ways: prudent management or admitting that the back-book is impaired. Important: the company carries restricted cash of ~RMB749M (US$93M) as of FY2024 — that is risk-reserve money, not freely available for distribution.
