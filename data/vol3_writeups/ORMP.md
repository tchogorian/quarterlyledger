# {NUMBER}. ORMP — Oramed Pharmaceuticals Inc.
## CASH FORTRESS | Score: 52/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Oramed is a post-failure clinical-stage biotech now functioning largely as a holding/investment vehicle wrapped around the husk of an oral peptide platform. The company's lead asset, ORMD-0801 oral insulin, missed both co-primary endpoints in the Phase 3 ORA-D-013-1 trial in early 2023, vaporizing roughly two decades of program value. Since then, management has redeployed the residual cash hoard into structured credit (notably the Scilex Notes), an Israeli real estate JV, an investment in Lifeward (formerly ReWalk), and the carve-out of its POD oral-delivery platform into OraTech. Cash and equivalents stood at approximately $45.9 million at 12/31/2025, down from $54.4 million a year prior. The company has stated it intends to run a small 60-patient validation trial of the oral insulin formulation in 2H 2026.

The bear case is that ORMP looks cheap on screens only because the headline P/E reflects non-recurring gains and interest income on the cash pile, not durable operating earnings. The "ROE 37%" is a mirage. Operating activities used roughly $9.1 million of cash in 2025, and the company is now exposed to a third-party credit risk concentration in the Scilex Notes — Scilex itself disclosed substantial doubt about its ability to continue as a going concern, which would impair recoverability of the principal and severely dent book value. Capital allocation has also drifted from drug development into private real estate and equity stakes, which is a classic late-stage biotech "zombie" pattern. Management is also paying special dividends out of capital, accelerating the wind-down of the war chest.

The bull case is straightforward arithmetic: at roughly $50M market cap with ~$46M of cash plus carrying value of investments and the Scilex Notes, the operating biotech and the POD platform are essentially free. If the planned 60-patient bridging study in 2H 2026 reads out positively, oral insulin gets a second life with an institutional partner. The OraTech POD spin-out could surface hidden platform value if any oral-peptide partner (GLP-1 analogues being the obvious target) takes a license. Insider ownership remains elevated, the share count is small, and any positive catalyst into a $50M shell with $46M cash would re-rate sharply. Optionality, not earnings power, is the entire thesis.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The reported earnings are dominated by realized and unrealized investment gains plus interest on the cash and Scilex Notes — not drug royalties or licensing revenue. The 10-K explicitly flags that Scilex has disclosed substantial doubt about going concern, meaning a meaningful chunk of the "net cash" line item is actually senior secured paper of a distressed Nasdaq pharma. The OraTech share-purchase transaction shifts the POD oral peptide platform out of the parent, so even a successful 2H 2026 pilot would economically benefit OraTech, with Oramed retaining only contractual milestones. The Lifeward exit (medical robotics) and Israel real estate JV are unrelated diversifications that complicate the "biotech" label entirely.
