# {NUMBER}. ABEO — Abeona Therapeutics Inc.
## BIOTECH BINARY | Score: 58/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Abeona is a Cleveland-based gene-therapy company whose central asset is ZEVASKYN (prademagene zamikeracel, "pz-cel") — the FDA-approved autologous cell-based gene therapy for wounds in adult and pediatric patients with recessive dystrophic epidermolysis bullosa (RDEB). The 10-K describes ZEVASKYN as the only FDA-approved product to treat RDEB wounds with a single surgical application and notes Orphan Drug and Rare Pediatric Disease designation. As of March 2026 the company had activated four Qualified Treatment Centers (Lurie Children's Chicago first; UTMB Galveston was the fourth). The first commercial patient was treated in Q4 2025, a second has been treated in 2026, and "ZEVASKYN demand has more than doubled at QTCs, with approximately 30 eligible patients now identified." Product revenue for FY2025 was $2.4M; total revenue $5.8M ($3.4M license + $2.4M product). Critically, FY2025 net income was $71.2M — driven entirely by the gain on sale of the Priority Review Voucher received with approval; excluding the PRV gain the underlying loss was $81.2M.

The bear case is launch execution at small scale. RDEB is ultra-orphan: the U.S. addressable population is in the low thousands, ZEVASKYN requires biopsy collection, ex-vivo gene correction, then surgical re-application at a QTC — a complex logistics chain with a long manufacturing turnaround. With only four QTCs activated and 30 identified eligible patients, the ramp will be measured in patients per quarter, not per week. The cGMP Cleveland facility is a single point of failure for commercial supply. The accumulated deficit is $742.1M against a $191.4M cash + short-term investments + restricted cash base; management says cash is sufficient for "at least the next 12 months," meaning equity issuance becomes a question if launch revenue under-delivers. The 10-K also discloses Avenue Capital warrants exercisable at $6.07 — a small but real overhang.

The bull case: ZEVASKYN is a one-and-done curative-intent product with no FDA-approved competition for RDEB wound treatment. Payer coverage is already broad — United, Cigna, Aetna, Anthem, and most BCBS plans have published policies; CMS established J-code J3389 effective January 1, 2026. Ultra-orphan pricing for a gene therapy can plausibly be $2–3M per patient. If the company treats even 30–50 patients in FY2026, that is potentially $75–150M of net product revenue against a current ~$200M market cap. Plus there is a downstream pipeline: AAV204 capsid licensed to Beacon for ophthalmology, Ultragenyx and Taysha milestone/royalty options on legacy assets.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The PRV sale is the key one-time event the screen flags as a P/E of 5.4x — that is non-recurring. Strip the PRV gain and the company is a cash-burning launch-stage biotech, not a P/E-payable enterprise. The 10-K is also explicit on launch mechanics: "ZEVASKYN qualified treatment centers, a network of centers that are selected based on their expertise in cell and gene therapy and trained to administer ZEVASKYN" — meaning revenue is gated by the slower-of (a) QTC activations, (b) Cleveland manufacturing throughput, and (c) patient flow into specialty centers. The $191.4M cash base includes the PRV-sale proceeds; without that the runway would already be tighter. Royalty options on Ultragenyx and Taysha (legacy AAV programs) are not modeled by the sell side and could surprise.
