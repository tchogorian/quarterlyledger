### ANALYST SUMMARY

SR Bancorp is the Maryland-chartered holding company for Somerset Regal Bank, a 14-branch New Jersey community bank serving Essex, Hunterdon, Hudson, Middlesex, Morris, Somerset, Union and Livingston counties. The Bank was created via the September 19, 2023 mutual-to-stock conversion of Somerset Savings Bank and the simultaneous acquisition of Regal Bancorp. At June 30, 2025 the company reported total assets of $1.08 billion, deposits of $846.0 million, and total stockholders' equity of $193.8 million. The screen surfaces SRBK at P/B 0.71 and P/E 32.7 on a $139M market cap — classic post-conversion thrift pricing where the company sits on outsized capital but earns a depressed ROE while it deploys the IPO proceeds, integrates the merger, and amortizes the core deposit intangible from the Regal deal. Net income recovered to $3.7M ($0.44/share) for FY2025 versus a $10.9M loss in FY2024.

The bear case is straightforward: a sub-scale NJ community bank with too much capital, integrating a merger of equals while NIM gets squeezed by deposit competition. Management itself flags that with the additional capital raised, "we expect our return on equity to be low, which may reduce our stock price." Loan concentration in CRE and multi-family (typical NJ thrift mix) creates exposure if the local economy weakens — the 10-K notes a "prolonged or more severe downturn in the local economy could result in significant increases in non-performing loans." The IPO loaded the balance sheet with an expensive ESOP ($6.7M contra-equity) and a non-cash charitable foundation contribution. Allowance for credit losses sits at only $5.4M against ~$800M of loans — thin if CRE deteriorates. There is no organic growth catalyst beyond rate-cut driven NIM expansion.

The bull case rests on capital return mechanics. SRBK trades at ~71% of tangible book and management has been aggressive with buybacks: a first 950,793-share program completed in August, then a second 886,137-share authorization (~10% of outstanding) approved July 2025. A $0.05 quarterly dividend was initiated with the December 2025 record. Standard mutual-to-stock thrift playbook says: trade for ~3 years post-conversion, then become an acquisition target — second-step thrifts in NJ have routinely exited at 130-150% of book to a larger acquirer. With $193.8M of equity backing a $139M market cap and a clean credit book, downside is reasonably bounded. The merger gave the bank meaningful commercial lending expertise it previously lacked, expanding earnings potential as legacy lower-yielding investment securities reprice.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The reported $32.7 P/E dramatically understates run-rate earnings power because of the conversion's one-time drag. The 10-K notes the bank realized a "pre-tax realized loss of approximately $4 [million]" on the sale of lower-yielding investment securities to reposition the book, plus heavy core deposit intangible amortization from the Regal acquisition still flowing through expenses. The ESOP also creates an ongoing GAAP compensation expense as shares are released. CECL was only adopted on July 1, 2023 — meaning the FY2024/2025 reserve build absorbed the day-one accounting hit AND the Regal acquired-loan PCD reserve, both compressing reported earnings. Look at pre-provision pre-amortization earnings to see what the franchise can really earn once the conversion noise washes through.
