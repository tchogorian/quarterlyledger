# {NUMBER}. FNWD — Finward Bancorp

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Finward Bancorp is the Munster, Indiana-based holding company for Peoples Bank, an Indiana community bank serving Lake, Porter, and surrounding counties along with adjacent Cook County, Illinois. The Wealth Management Group oversaw $416.9 million in assets under management at year-end 2025, up $24.0 million year-over-year. Stockholders' equity totaled $174.7 million, or 8.6% of total assets, producing a book value per share of $40.37 against ~4.3 million shares outstanding. Net income for 2025 was $8.1 million ($1.88 diluted EPS) on average assets of roughly $2.05 billion. Net interest margin expanded meaningfully from 2.54% to 3.00%. ROA was a soft 0.39% and ROE just 5.10% — well below community-bank averages and the source of the persistent discount to book.

The bear case is that returns on capital are subscale. ROA below 0.4% and ROE near 5% mean the bank earns about its cost of equity in good quarters and below in bad ones — the textbook definition of a "destroying value" community bank. The 10-K discloses a memorandum of understanding requiring prior regulatory approval before paying cash dividends — a meaningful red flag on regulatory standing and a constraint on capital return. ACL to nonperforming loans of 156.84% (vs. 123.10% prior year) is fine but not pristine. Total deposits were essentially flat at $1.755 billion, suggesting a stagnant deposit franchise in a competitive Chicago-suburb market. The 18.4x P/E reflects depressed earnings, not a premium.

The bull case is straightforward NIM normalization plus regulatory cleanup. NIM jumped 46 basis points to 3.00% in a single year — if it can climb another 50–75 basis points toward peer averages, EPS roughly doubles purely on operating leverage. Wealth Management at $416.9 million AUM provides fee income and optionality. Net charge-offs collapsed to just $187 thousand in 2025 from $2.06 million in 2024 — a 91% improvement signaling that the legacy credit issues that landed FNWD in regulatory crosshairs are clearing. At 0.86x book, the market is pricing continued mediocrity; if the MOU is lifted and the dividend resumes meaningfully, a re-rate to 1.0–1.1x book delivers 15–30% upside on top of NIM-driven EPS growth. The 2014 buyback authorization (50,000 shares) was tiny but symbolic.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The single most important disclosure is the regulatory memorandum of understanding requiring prior approval to pay cash dividends — a holding-company-level constraint that signals supervisory concerns and sharply limits capital return optionality. The bank's geographic footprint includes Cook County, Illinois (Chicago suburbs), exposing it to higher-risk CRE than a pure-Indiana lender. The drop in net charge-offs from $2.06 million to $187 thousand and the $782 thousand provision (versus $199 thousand prior year) suggests management is rebuilding reserves while losses normalize — a classic pre-recovery setup, but only if the macro cooperates.
