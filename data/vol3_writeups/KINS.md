# {NUMBER}. KINS — Kingstone Companies, Inc.
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | Score: 71/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Kingstone is a small specialty insurance holding company whose operating subsidiary, Kingstone Insurance Company (KICO), writes personal-lines (predominantly homeowners) and commercial auto coverage primarily in New York, where it ranked the 12th largest writer of homeowners insurance in 2024. The defining catalyst is the Change in Market Dynamics: as Allstate, State Farm and other national carriers materially withdrew from the New York personal-lines market, Kingstone has been one of the primary admitted-market beneficiaries, picking up replacement policies at significantly improved pricing. The 2025 10-K reports net income of $40.8 million, compared with $18.4 million in 2024 — a $22.4 million improvement that reflects both rate adequacy and favorable market share dynamics. On April 14, 2025 KICO entered an agreement to offer replacement quotes to selected policyholders of a withdrawing competitor under a New York DFS-approved Withdrawal Plan.

The bear case is centered on tail risk and reserve adequacy. KICO's geographic concentration is acute: 98% of direct written premiums in 2025 came from New York policies (up from 96% in 2024), and Downstate New York exposes the book to coastal hurricane and noreaster losses that have historically blown up Kingstone's combined ratio. The reinsurance program (Swiss Re, Hanover Rück, Lancashire, SCOR, others) is high-quality but expensive, and pricing has hardened across all reinsurance towers in 2024-2025. Reserves on the legacy book of business — including the run-off of unprofitable policies that drove the 2022-2023 losses — could still develop adversely. Personal-lines insurance is also a regulated business; rate-increase approvals and regulatory scrutiny of the post-Withdrawal Plan growth could compress underwriting margins precisely when the reported numbers look best.

The bull case is a textbook insurance hard-market compounder. Combined ratio has been driven under 100, ROE is 43% on accounting numbers but trending toward a sustainable mid-teens to low-20s range as the new-business cohort earns out. The Change in Market Dynamics is a multi-year tailwind: dislocated competitors do not return overnight to a market they exited, and Kingstone retains the regulatory relationships, agent network, and underwriting infrastructure to absorb growth. The 12th-largest position in NY homeowners is meaningful share with room to expand. At 6.4x earnings and 2.18x book — high P/B for an insurer but justified by the ROE — and with policy-count growth running, this is the cleanest "right time, right place" thesis on the screen.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K specifically describes the Withdrawal Plan transaction with a competitor, which most equity screens will not capture as a discrete catalyst — this is regulator-blessed market-share transfer, not organic competition. The sale of the company's headquarters building to Ulster County (announced February 2025) generates one-time cash but also signals an asset-light operating model going forward. The reinsurance panel is concentrated among top-tier global reinsurers, which is positive for credit quality but means renewal pricing flows directly to underwriting margins. The Cosi Agency multi-state licensing creates an embedded option to extend the model beyond New York if and when the company chooses to redeploy capital geographically.
