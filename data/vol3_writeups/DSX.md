# {NUMBER}. DSX — Diana Shipping Inc.
## CYCLICAL TROUGH | Score: 49/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Diana Shipping is a Marshall Islands-domiciled, Greek-managed dry bulk owner that operates 36 vessels in service plus two Kamsarmax newbuilds for delivery in 2027 and 2028, with combined deadweight capacity around 4.1 million tons and a weighted-average fleet age of 12.3 years. The fleet spans Ultramax through Newcastlemax classes and is chartered out on staggered time charters to counterparties including Cargill, Stone Shipping, NYK and others. FY2025 time-charter revenues were $213.5M, down from $228.2M in 2024 and $262.1M in 2023 as average TCE slipped to $15,454/day. Net income was $17.8M and net income to common $12.1M after $5.8M of Series B preferred dividends. The company trades around 0.55x book on a $309M market cap with $502M of common equity, fitting the textbook below-book shipping profile.

The bear case is the dry bulk cycle. Substantially all revenue comes from one charter market and the 10-K candidly flags "cyclicality of the dry bulk shipping industry" and "volatility in charter hire rates" as the primary structural risk. Costs are a problem too — interest expense and finance costs were $43.0M in 2025 against $42.2M of operating income, meaning the equity earns very little once leverage is paid. Around 30% of operating expenses and half of G&A are denominated in Euros while revenue is in dollars, so a weaker dollar squeezes the cost base. The 8.875% Series B Cumulative Redeemable Perpetual Preferred sits ahead of common holders and will keep absorbing $5.8M annually regardless of common dividends, which were token at $0.04 per common share in 2025.

The bull case is a Capesize-led tightening into 2026–2027 paired with disciplined capital return. Recent disclosures show DSX has fixed a Capesize at $21,650/day for March 2026–April 2027 and locked in roughly 50% of 2026 revenue days at attractive rates, with implied $17,102/day on unfixed days using FFA references. The early-2025 $23M tender at $2.00/share retired 11.4M shares, the $100M repurchase authorization remains unused, and management put $0.04/share through the year while the Series B yields 8.875%. With NAV well above market, every up-cycle vessel sale (gain on sale of vessels was $3.7M in 2025, $5.8M in 2024) directly proves the discount.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Two structural items dilute the apparent earnings power. First, DSX is a Marshall Islands "C-corp" that pays out via 1099-DIV-equivalent foreign distributions through the Diana Wilhelmsen Management 50/50 JV with Wilhelmsen Ship Management — equity-method losses from JVs took $2.8M off net income in 2025 and the Bergen and other JV/newbuild capex commitments will keep pressuring cash conversion. Second, FY2025 net income was flattered by $14.7M of mark-to-market gains on equity securities (related to a position acquired during the year that drove a $118.2M working-capital increase from "investments in equity securities") — back that out and core operating earnings to common were thin. Investors taking the headline P/E of 22.7 should know it embeds the equity-securities gain.
