# {NUMBER}. FDBC — Fidelity D & D Bancorp, Inc.
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | Score: 72/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Fidelity D & D Bancorp is the Dunmore, Pennsylvania-based holding company for Fidelity Bank, a community bank operating roughly 21 full-service offices across Lackawanna, Luzerne, Lehigh, and Northampton Counties, plus a separately branded Fidelity Bank Wealth Management office in Schuylkill County. The franchise is unusually deposit-rich for its size: total assets reached $2.75 billion in 2025, with loans of $1.89 billion and deposits of $2.47 billion. The mix matters — a meaningful wealth management and brokerage business rides on top of the core banking franchise, plus a mortgage center, which give FDBC a more diversified non-interest income stream than a typical eastern PA community bank. Headline P/E of 9.1 and P/B of 1.06 understate the underlying quality: 2025 net income of $28.2 million and EPS of $4.86 was up 36% year-over-year, with non-performing assets at just 0.08% of total assets.

The bear case for FDBC is principally regional and rate-driven. Northeast Pennsylvania is a slow-growth demographic region; Scranton/Wilkes-Barre population trends are flat to declining, which caps long-term loan-growth potential without geographic expansion. Branch density at 21 offices for ~$2.75B in assets is on the higher side and represents real fixed cost. Net interest margin expansion that drove the 36% earnings jump in 2025 was partly a function of rate normalization — if the Fed cuts aggressively in 2026, NIM compression would reverse some of that benefit. CRE exposure across the loan book — especially small office, hotel, and multifamily in legacy industrial PA markets — is a credit risk to monitor through the next downturn. Wealth management AUM is a fee-income lever that depends on equity-market levels.

The bull case is that this is a high-quality franchise that has compounded book value steadily, with an extraordinary 0.08% NPA ratio that signals disciplined underwriting through multiple cycles. A double-digit ROE community bank with a well-developed wealth management business trading near tangible book is unusual; comparable franchises (Univest, ESSA, Penns Woods) typically trade 1.2–1.6x book. The 2025 result demonstrates operating leverage: revenue growth dropped substantially to the bottom line. Strategic optionality includes continued geographic expansion into Lehigh Valley (already underway), modest tuck-in M&A, or eventual sale to a larger PA/NJ bank acquirer at a meaningful premium to book. Dividend policy is stable, and the wealth platform provides earnings counter-balance to NII volatility.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The wealth management segment disclosure is critical — it is structurally higher-multiple than the spread bank and supports a sum-of-the-parts case for the stock. Read the loan-portfolio breakdown for CRE concentration ratios versus regulatory guidance (300% of capital threshold). The Schuylkill County wealth office expansion suggests intent to push the wealth franchise outside the home county, which is the highest-ROE growth lever available. Insider ownership and proxy voting concentration matter for any future M&A scenario — the recent DEF 14A discloses the relevant directors and pay structure. Also note: the bank has historically been conservative on capital return relative to peers, which is consistent with the long-term hold profile but caps near-term shareholder yield.
