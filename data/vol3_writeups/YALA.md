# {NUMBER}. YALA — Yalla Group Limited
## CASH FORTRESS | Score: 71/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Yalla Group operates the leading voice-chat and casual gaming platforms in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA) — flagship apps Yalla (a localized voice chat with the cultural "Majlis" room concept), Yalla Ludo (a Ludo and Domino casual gaming app with integrated voice), and adjacent titles. The numbers are striking: 2025 full-year revenue of approximately $342M, net income of about $148M (43.3% net margin), and Q4 average MAUs up 8.2% year over year to 44.8M. Cash and equivalents stood at approximately $754.6M at year-end 2025, dwarfing the roughly $250M market cap. The company executed about $56.6M of buybacks in 2025 and authorized a new $150M repurchase program in March 2026 over a 24-month window. P/E of 8.4x and P/B of 1.33x understate just how cheap this is on an enterprise basis: at MCap ~$250M minus ~$750M cash, the operating business is being priced at deeply negative EV — a textbook cash-fortress setup.

The bear case is governance and country risk. Yalla is a Cayman-domiciled ADR with a UAE-based operating footprint serving MENA users, and there is ongoing investor concern about the verifiability of paying-user metrics, the strength of revenue auditability, and the historical short-seller report (Swan Street Research, 2022) that questioned reported numbers — never fully resolved. Paying users actually declined to 10.4M in Q4 versus growing MAUs, hinting that monetization per user is sliding even as the top of the funnel grows. Cash is large but if held in jurisdictions with limited capital-controls flexibility, repatriation to ADR holders is uncertain. PCAOB audit access for MENA-focused issuers, while not a China-style binary, still presents friction.

The bull case is the cleanest in this batch: a profitable, growing, net-cash-rich consumer platform trading below the value of its cash. Even if you haircut cash 30% for repatriation/governance discount and assume the operating business is worth zero, you arrive at fair value above the current price. The $150M buyback at current prices could retire ~50% of the float over two years. Margins of 41% in Q4 imply genuine operating leverage. The MENA digital consumer market is growing, under-penetrated by Western-platform monetization, and Yalla has the localized product.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Read the 20-F section on cash held by jurisdiction — the historical disclosure shows large balances held at PRC-headquartered banks, partly in RMB; that is not the same as USD held in Dubai or Singapore. The Cayman holding/VIE structure here is for MENA-focused operations, not China-VIE risk in the traditional sense, but management roots and engineering centers have ties to mainland China that influence audit and capital-flow logistics. The Q4 paying-user decline (PUs down while MAUs up) is the single most important leading indicator — it suggests ARPU compression or churn at the top of the spending pyramid. Watch related-party transactions and the share-issuance pattern; a profitable platform with $750M cash that issues ADS to insiders is a red flag any quarter.
