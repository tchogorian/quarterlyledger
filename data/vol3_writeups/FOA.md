# {NUMBER}. FOA — Finance of America Companies Inc.
## DISTRESSED ARB | Score: 62/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Finance of America is the dominant U.S. reverse mortgage originator and servicer, with roughly 30% HECM (Home Equity Conversion Mortgage) issuance market share in 2025. The company emerged from a 2024 debt restructuring and is now profitable: 2025 net income of approximately $103M on revenue of about $497M, a sharp swing from prior-year losses. Adjusted net income of about $74M was up 429% year over year and adjusted EBITDA grew 138% to roughly $143M. The Retirement Solutions segment — proprietary and HECM reverse mortgages plus the emerging Home Equity Investment (HEI) product — is the entire forward thesis. Cash and equivalents grew to $90M from $47M in 2024. In late 2025 FOA announced an agreement to acquire PHH Mortgage's reverse mortgage servicing portfolio from Onity, and a $2.5B strategic partnership with Blue Owl that included a $50M equity investment. Blackstone's residual equity stake was repurchased in February 2026.

The bear case is that this is still a fair-value-accounted balance-sheet business whose reported earnings are extremely sensitive to interest-rate assumptions, prepayment speeds, and home-price appreciation embedded in the retained interests on securitized HECMs. A reverse mortgage originator carries massive gross balance-sheet exposure (HMBS-related obligations are tens of billions) against a tiny equity sliver — book value near $0.54x of equity reflects that the market does not fully trust the marks. The HEI product (shared appreciation in exchange for lump-sum cash) is unproven through a downturn; if U.S. home prices roll over, both retained HECM interests and HEI valuations re-mark down simultaneously. Consumer reverse-mortgage origination is regulator-sensitive and reputationally fragile.

The bull case is straightforward: post-restructuring, FOA is a now-profitable monopoly-like player in a structurally growing demographic market (aging boomers with home equity), and Blue Owl's $2.5B commitment gives it both capital and a credibility stamp. Trading near book at $188M market cap on $103M of GAAP earnings (P/E ~5.6x, P/B ~0.54), it is priced as if 2024's distress will recur. If reverse-mortgage volumes hold and HEI scales to meaningful size, the stock should clear book — implying ~85% upside before any earnings re-rating.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
GAAP earnings here are not cash. The retained beneficial interests in HMBS securitizations are Level-3 fair-value assets — the income statement gain on origination is partly an unrealized mark, and discount-rate or HPA-assumption changes can swing reported earnings sharply quarter-to-quarter. Read the 10-K's sensitivity tables on discount rates and prepayment assumptions. The Blue Owl partnership terms (warrants, preferred features, consent rights) materially affect common equity value and warrant close reading. The Blackstone equity buyback removed a structural overhang but used cash. Finally, HECM origination is dependent on FHA program continuity — any policy change to the HECM program (loan limits, mortgage insurance premiums, principal-limit factors) is a binary risk.
