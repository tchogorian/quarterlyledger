# {NUMBER}. UTGN — UTG, Inc.
## VALUE TRAP RISK | Score: 48/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY

UTG, Inc. is a holding company whose operating subsidiary, Universe Life Insurance Group (UG), administers a closed and run-off block of traditional life insurance policies headquartered in Stanford, Kentucky and Springfield, Illinois. Total assets were $491.4 million at year-end 2025, with shareholders' equity of $232.7 million producing book value near $73.85 per share against ~3.15 million shares outstanding. The franchise is unusual for a life insurer: rather than holding a typical fixed-income-heavy portfolio, UG concentrates its investment book in equity securities (62% of total investments) and limited liability companies that own real estate, mortgages, and oil & gas royalties. This means GAAP "earnings" depend heavily on realized investment gains, which collapsed from $63.3 million in 2024 to $23.4 million in 2025, dragging EPS from $15.80 to $5.42.

The bear case is structural: UTGN is effectively a private investment vehicle controlled by majority shareholder Jesse T. Correll and his First Southern entities, with related-party arrangements throughout the 10-K. The insurance liabilities are decaying — premiums and policy fees were just $7.3 million — so the equity is functionally a leveraged bet on a Correll-managed concentrated equity, real estate, and royalty portfolio. Public minorities have no real influence, governance is insider-friendly, and the auditor (Kerber, Eck & Braeckel) is a regional firm. Reported earnings are lumpy, hard to model, and largely a function of marks on illiquid LLC investments. The 2024 results were "(as restated)," indicating prior-period accounting issues. P/B ~0.84 looks cheap, but the discount to book has persisted for years.

The bull case rests on Correll's long-term track record allocating insurance float into hard assets and on the optionality of the oil & gas royalties (now 30% of investment real estate, up from 20%). If you believe in Correll the way Berkshire shareholders believe in Buffett, UTGN is a private compounder trading at 0.84x reported book, with thinly-traded shares, modest dividends, and a buyback program funded out of philanthropy-adjusted retained earnings. The company is overcapitalized, regulators leave it alone, and an eventual liquidation or going-private transaction at or near book would deliver a clean ~20% pop. Small float means even modest accumulation can move shares.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE

The 10-K explicitly defines a "related party" group around Correll and First Southern Funding/Bancorp (which itself owns 95% of First Southern National Bank). UTG owns common stock and other interests in these affiliates, and the LLCs that hold real estate are nested under UG to "provide additional protection to the policyholders." Park Avenue Life Insurance Company (PALIC) coinsures substantially all of UTG's pre-1996 paid-up life book. The investment strategy — Level 3 LLCs at $89.2 million fair value, equity securities at $265.7 million, oil & gas royalties — is essentially impossible for an outside analyst to mark. The 2024 figures are restated. This is a "trust the operator" stock, not a screenable bank.
