# {NUMBER}. SNFCA — Security National Financial Corporation
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | Score: 64/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Security National Financial is a Utah-based three-segment conglomerate: a life insurance and annuity carrier, a residential mortgage originator (SecurityNational Mortgage, 85 retail offices in 25 states), and an irreplaceable cemetery and mortuary footprint of 11 mortuaries plus 5 cemeteries in Utah, 1 cemetery in California, and 1 cemetery and 4 mortuaries in New Mexico. FY2025 consolidated revenues were $344.6M with net earnings of $32.2M. By segment: Life Insurance generated $29.4M of segment net earnings on $208.3M of revenue, the Cemetery/Mortuary segment generated $6.6M on $33.7M, and the Mortgage segment lost $3.9M on $110.4M of revenue. Total assets were $1.56B and segment assets show the life insurance business carries $1.41B. Trading at 0.58x book and 7.7x earnings on a $251M market cap, the screen identifies this as a profitable Berkshire-mini selling for a fraction of replacement.

The bear case begins with the mortgage segment, which lost money in 2025 and remains structurally exposed to the residential origination cycle, with cost-to-fund mortgage loans of $6.6M and commissions of $42.4M against thin secondary-market gains. The life insurance book is dominated by acquired blocks of business stretching back to acquisitions like Capital Investors Life (1994), Civil Service Employees Life (1999), Acadian Life (2002), and on through Kilpatrick Life and others — these are run-off books with concentrated state-regulator and reserve-adequacy risk. Insurance-segment investments include exposure to commercial mortgage portfolios across LTV and DSCR buckets, and the holding company carries cemetery perpetual care obligations that must be funded from restricted assets. The dual-class share structure (Class A and Class C) and stock-dividend-only payout policy mean common holders never receive cash.

The bull case is the cemetery moat plus optionality on the other two segments. Cemetery and mortuary revenue of $33.7M and segment earnings of $6.6M imply ~20% segment margin — nothing else in the portfolio comes close. Cemeteries are a finite-supply asset with multi-decade demand visibility and pre-need contract economics that compound. Strip out the mortgage drag and the steady-state earnings power of life plus cemetery is roughly $36M, or ~7x adjusted earnings. Capital allocation has been disciplined: management runs an annual stock dividend (5% scrip) rather than cash, builds book value, and the Bradley/insider ownership (Charles E. Bradley Jr. as CEO context applies to CPSS, but SNFCA insider ownership at the Quist/Pinegar level is similarly aligned). At 0.58x book the market is pricing the mortgage segment at zero or worse, with the cemetery moat thrown in for free.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The cemetery segment is the asymmetric kicker no screener captures. Inventory disclosure shows specific developed-versus-undeveloped acreage at named properties — Memorial Estates' Lakeview, Mountain View and Redwood cemeteries in the Salt Lake corridor, Holladay Memorial Park, Deseret Memorial's Lake Hills, plus the New Mexico locations — and pre-need cemetery and funeral receivables are funded into trust through the insurance investment portfolio. The "Cemetery Perpetual Care Obligation" is a regulated trust asset bucket that backs the perpetual maintenance of these grounds and is invested in Treasuries and state/political subdivision securities; this is real, segregated, hard-asset value that does not appear in any traditional valuation framework. Together these holdings represent decades of pre-sold revenue against a single-digit-percent-margin headline business — a free option the market gives away at this multiple.
