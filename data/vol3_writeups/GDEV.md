# {NUMBER}. GDEV — GDEV Inc.
## MELTING ICE CUBE | Score: 55/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
GDEV (formerly Nexters Global) is a Cyprus-domiciled mobile-game holding company whose primary asset is the long-running RPG franchise Hero Wars, plus the smaller title Island Hoppers. Hero Wars generated approximately 93% of 2025 revenue and has surpassed 185M lifetime installs, with the franchise itself having generated about $1.7B in cumulative revenue. The company emerged from a difficult corporate history: trading was halted by Nasdaq in February 2022 following Russia's invasion of Ukraine due to Nexters' Russian roots, and the company subsequently relocated critical personnel from Russia, Ukraine, and Belarus to Cyprus, Kazakhstan, and Armenia, divesting its Russian subsidiaries to local management. The 2023 reorganization placed GDEV as the publicly traded holding company. Reported metrics today: P/E ~4.2x, EV/EBITDA ~2.3x, with net cash representing roughly 39% of market cap — multiples that scream "the market expects this to die."

The bear case is unambiguous: bookings fell 4% in 2024 and another 13% in 2025; Hero Wars Monthly Paying Users declined 8% in 2024 and 18% in 2025; Q4 2025 revenue of about $90M was down 8% year over year. This is a single-product mobile game in late franchise life, where the user-acquisition economics across the mobile gaming sector have deteriorated significantly post-IDFA, and the marginal Hero Wars cohort produces less LTV than the cost of acquiring it. The 4.2x P/E reflects analysts pricing terminal-value decline. Russian-origin reputation creates a permanent partner, payments, and platform-relationship overhang — Apple, Google, ad networks, and institutional investors all impose friction. Regulatory and sanctions risk is not zero even after the relocation; KYC on remaining cash flows, wallet structures, and historical IP transfers will continue to be scrutinized.

The bull case requires only that the decline rate slow. Net cash of ~39% of market cap means the operating business trades at roughly 2.5x EBITDA. If management runs Hero Wars for cash, returns capital aggressively, and avoids burning balance-sheet on doomed new launches, even a 10-15% annual revenue decline produces an IRR above 20%. A small $30-50M buyback at current levels could retire 15-25% of float. Optionality on a new hit, however small the probability, is essentially free at this multiple.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
Read the 20-F's risk-factor section on sanctions, the divestiture mechanics of the Russian subsidiaries, and the corporate-residency status of senior management — these are the actual binary risks, not the operating P&L. Cash held by jurisdiction matters: balances in Cyprus, Armenia, or Kazakhstan banks have very different liquidity-to-shareholders profiles than USD held in tier-1 Western banks. Watch UA spend trends quarter to quarter — if management is still leaning on user acquisition to maintain DAU, the cash will be consumed faster than headline FCF suggests. Player retention curves for Hero Wars cohorts (1-day, 7-day, 30-day) by year are the truest measure of franchise health and are buried in management commentary, not screens.
