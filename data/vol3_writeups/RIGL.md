# {NUMBER}. RIGL — Rigel Pharmaceuticals, Inc.
## BIOTECH BINARY | Score: 67/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Rigel is one of the very few sub-$1B commercial-stage biotechs that is actually profitable. The 2025 10-K shows total revenues of $294.3 million (up from $179.3 million in 2024), driven by $232.0 million in net product sales and $62.0 million in collaboration revenue. The portfolio is built around three commercialized assets in hematology/oncology: TAVALISSE (fostamatinib, oral SYK inhibitor for chronic ITP), REZLIDHIA (olutasidenib, mIDH1 R/R AML), and GAVRETO (pralsetinib, RET-fusion NSCLC and thyroid cancers — in-licensed from Blueprint in Feb 2024). 2025 product mix: TAVALISSE $158.8M (+51.6% YoY), REZLIDHIA $31.0M (+34.8% YoY), and the rest from GAVRETO and partner royalties (Kissei in Japan, Grifols in EU). Cash + ST investments were $155 million at year-end 2025.

The bear case for RIGL is execution risk on three small drugs against a backdrop of intensifying ITP and AML competition. TAVALISSE is now seven years post-launch and faces longer-term competition from newer ITP agents; REZLIDHIA is a niche second-line AML drug for an mIDH1-mutated subset that is being squeezed by Servier's Tibsovo and other emerging agents. GAVRETO acquired from Roche/Blueprint comes with execution and supply-chain risk. McKesson alone was 42% of revenue in 2025, an uncomfortable specialty-pharma concentration. Management explicitly warns recent operating income may not be sustainable. Pipeline depth is thin; without a fresh in-licensing deal, the long-term growth trajectory flattens, and the biotech-binary nature returns.

The bull case is that Rigel is doing what the entire sector wishes it could: generating profitable cash flow from a small commercial book while running a nearly debt-free balance sheet. The 2025 P/E around 1.6x reflects skepticism that earnings persist, but TAVALISSE 51% growth and REZLIDHIA 35% growth tell a different story — these drugs are still ramping. Rigel has demonstrated it can integrate in-licensed assets (GAVRETO from Blueprint, REZLIDHIA from Forma) at attractive economics. Total assets of $513.6 million versus $164.0 million a year earlier reflect the addition of long-term investments. As a small profitable hematology platform with three commercial drugs and partner royalty streams, Rigel is a logical M&A target for a mid-cap specialty pharma — the optionality value alone may underwrite the current multiple.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The "P/E 1.6" is misleading because 2025 net income includes large one-time items from collaboration revenue and milestone recognition; the underlying recurring product-driven earnings are lower. Forma deal economics: Rigel pays tiered royalties on REZLIDHIA net sales from low-teens to mid-thirties percentages — meaning incremental margin compresses as REZLIDHIA scales. The Kissei (Japan, fostamatinib) and Grifols (EU) royalty streams are small but high-margin. Customer concentration is severe: McKesson at 42% of total revenue. Cash $40.6M but short-term investments $114.4M = ~$155M liquidity, which is not the "fortress" some screens might imply. Pipeline thin — most R&D spend is on lifecycle extension rather than new molecules.
