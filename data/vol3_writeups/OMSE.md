# {NUMBER}. OMSE — OMS Energy Technologies Inc.
## CYCLICAL TROUGH | Score: 60/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
OMS Energy Technologies is a Singapore-based oilfield equipment manufacturer providing surface wellheads, Christmas trees, and specialty connectors and pipes (SC tubular solutions) primarily to upstream operators in the Middle East (Saudi Arabia is the dominant geography) and the Asia-Pacific region. The 20-F discloses revenue of $97.5M for fiscal year ended March 31, 2023 (Predecessor) rising to $203 million in the most recent full successor year ended March 31, 2025, with gross profit reaching the high-$50M range — gross margin in the high-20s to low-30s. The Saudi Arabian Aramco IKTVA (In-Kingdom Total Value Add) framework — which incentivizes domestic-content procurement — is a structural tailwind for OMSE's KSA-incorporated subsidiary, which is positioned as a qualified local supplier for surface production equipment.

The bear case is that OMSE is structurally a small player in an oligopolistic global wellhead market dominated by Schlumberger (Cameron), Baker Hughes, Halliburton, and NOV. Customer concentration is explicitly flagged as a material risk: a small number of customers — likely Aramco-affiliated and other NOC-linked operators — drive a disproportionate share of revenue, and the loss of any one creates a step-function in the income statement. Backlog is described in the filing as subject to "unexpected adjustments and cancellations." Fixed-price contracts expose the company to commodity input cost (steel, alloy) inflation. As a recent IPO (the Predecessor/Successor accounting reflects a 2023 reorganization), OMSE has limited public-market track record, making historical multiples unreliable. Geopolitical risk (KSA, broader Middle East) and oil-price cyclicality remain ever-present.

The bull case is straightforward cyclical leverage. OMSE benefits from sustained high upstream capex in KSA driven by Aramco's commitment to maintain production capacity and Jafurah unconventional gas development; IKTVA provides preferential local-content access; and the Asian platform diversifies the demand base. At 6.5x earnings, 1.09x book, and net cash equal to 63% of market cap with ROE of 21%, the equity has minimal balance-sheet risk. If oil prices and rig counts hold, backlog converts cleanly to revenue and gross margin holds in the high-20s, this is a 1.0-1.5x book story trading at trough cyclical sentiment. The Singapore listing/US ADR structure also makes it an underfollowed name with potential rerating if institutional coverage builds.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The Predecessor/Successor accounting from the June 2023 reorganization (Sumitomo Corporation involvement) means GAAP-reported historical comparisons are not apples-to-apples — investors keying off LTM multiples need to reconstruct underlying revenue and margin trajectory. The IKTVA scoring under the Aramco framework is a meaningful competitive differentiator that screens cannot capture; loss of qualified-local-supplier status would be devastating. The 20-F flags multiple geographic-concentration and customer-concentration risks but doesn't disclose top-customer percentages in line; investors should assume Aramco and a small number of NOCs collectively drive 40%+ of revenue. The wellhead and Christmas tree product line is where OMSE earns its highest gross margin; SC tubular solutions is more competitive.
