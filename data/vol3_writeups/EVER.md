# {NUMBER}. EVER — EverQuote, Inc.
## HIDDEN COMPOUNDER | Score: 72/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
EverQuote is the leading online marketplace connecting auto-insurance shoppers with carriers and agents, monetizing per-referral via a real-time auction model where carriers bid for high-intent consumer leads. The 2025 10-K shows the recovery from the 2022-23 carrier-pullback shock is now fully in motion: revenue of $692.5 million for 2025, up 38.5% from $500.2 million in 2024, with the automotive vertical accounting for $183.7 million of the $192.3 million revenue increase as carriers reopened marketing budgets. Net income flipped to $99.3 million in 2025 versus $32.2 million in 2024 and a $51.3 million net loss in 2023. Adjusted EBITDA of $94.6 million ($58.2M in 2024, $0.5M in 2023) confirms the operating leverage. The board authorized a share repurchase program in July 2025; cash position is healthy with no long-term debt of consequence.

The bear case is two-pronged. First, customer concentration is severe and worsening: the largest customer was 38% of total revenue in 2025 and the second-largest 11%, meaning a single carrier withdrawal could erase ~40% of the top line — exactly what happened in 2022 when Allstate, Progressive, and others abruptly cut acquisition spend during the auto-insurance underwriting crisis. EverQuote exited its health-insurance vertical in 2023, so the business is now functionally a single-vertical (auto P&C) lead-gen platform with limited diversification beyond home/renters as a small adjacency. Second, this is fundamentally a cyclical business tied to the auto-insurance underwriting cycle: when combined ratios spike, carriers slash acquisition spending and EverQuote's revenue craters with little of its own causation. Today's 38% growth is the upcycle; the next downturn looks much like 2022-23.

The bull case is that 2025 demonstrates the EverQuote business model still works structurally. The auto-insurance industry is shifting acquisition dollars from offline (TV, direct-mail) to online lead-gen at a secular rate, and EverQuote is positioned as the largest pure-play marketplace. Operating leverage is dramatic — 2025 adjusted EBITDA margin of 13.7% versus 11.6% in 2024 versus 0.1% in 2023 — and there's room to expand further as variable marketing dollars scale faster than fixed costs. The Class B controlling stake held by Link Ventures (controlled-company status) provides governance stability. With $326.9M total assets, no material debt, and a fresh repurchase authorization, capital allocation now turns from survival mode to shareholder return. P/E 6.1 and EV/EBITDA 5.8x bake in another carrier pullback that may not arrive for years.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The "VMD/VMM" non-GAAP metric (variable marketing dollars and margin) is the single most important operating KPI — it isolates revenue net of paid-media costs and tells you the unit economics of each acquired consumer. The 2023 carrier-pullback was driven by industry-wide auto loss ratios crossing 100%, not by EverQuote's competitive position; carriers are now writing profitably again and reopening online channels. The S-3 shelf registration permits up to $150 million of further capital-markets issuance, which could overhang the equity but also gives flexibility. Health-insurance vertical exit in 2023 was painful but de-risked the business from CMS ACA marketplace volatility. The dual-class structure means Link Ventures retains voting control even with low economic ownership — relevant if/when activists eye the cash on the balance sheet.
