# {NUMBER}. BDTX — Black Diamond Therapeutics, Inc.
## BIOTECH BINARY | Score: 56/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
Black Diamond is a precision oncology platform whose entire enterprise value now sits on one molecule: silevertinib (formerly BDTX-1535), a fourth-generation oral mutant-EGFR inhibitor being developed in two distinct lung-cancer settings — non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) and EGFR-altered glioblastoma (GBM). In March 2025 the company struck a partnership with Servier (a private French oncology company) that brought capital and validation onto the platform. The 10-K reports the company had cash, cash equivalents and investments of $128 million at year-end 2025, against an accumulated deficit of $464 million and a 2025 net loss of $69.7 million, with cash provided by operating activities of $29.6 million for the year (driven by the Servier upfront, not by product sales). The reported "net income" line is partnership-driven and does not represent recurring earnings power.

The bear case is that single-asset, single-target oncology biotechs trade at razor-thin margins of safety. The reported P/E of 7.3 is meaningless — it reflects one-time deal income. EGFR exon-20 and atypical-EGFR NSCLC is a competitive arena (Takeda's Exkivity was withdrawn; Johnson & Johnson's Rybrevant + Lazcluze and Dizal's sunvozertinib all compete). GBM enrollment is notoriously slow and expensive, with high failure rates. Cash runway depends on burn pace post-Servier; with operating losses of $69.7M and roughly $128M of cash, a non-deal-driven runway is approximately 18-22 months absent further partnerships or financings. A negative Phase 2 readout, partner walkaway, or competitive data readout from an established EGFR program could compress the equity by 50-70% within a single trading session.

The bull case rests on silevertinib's differentiated profile: fourth-generation EGFR inhibitors target both classical EGFR mutations and the family of "atypical" mutations that escape osimertinib, plus brain penetrance which matters for both NSCLC CNS metastases and primary GBM. If the GBM Phase 2 produces a clinically meaningful response rate in newly-diagnosed EGFR-altered glioblastoma — a setting with no approved targeted therapy and median survival under 18 months — the molecule could earn breakthrough designation and a partner-funded pivotal program. The Servier deal already derisks development costs, and Black Diamond retains meaningful economics. Trading at 1.49x book with $128M of cash, the binary skews positive on a successful readout.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 10-K's revenue and net-income lines are dominated by Servier collaboration recognition (upfront and milestones), not recurring product cash flow — investors who anchor on the screen-reported P/E of 7.3 are mis-pricing the asset. The accumulated deficit of $464 million highlights the cash-incineration history typical of platform biotechs. Both pivotal indications (NSCLC and GBM) are running concurrently, doubling enrollment burden and execution risk. Patient-enrollment difficulty in EGFR-altered GBM is explicitly flagged as a forward-looking risk in the filing. The shelf registration and at-the-market equity program are live, meaning dilution at any positive datapoint is the base case for funding pivotal work.
