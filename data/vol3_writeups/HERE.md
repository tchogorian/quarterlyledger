# {NUMBER}. HERE — Here Group Limited (formerly QuantaSing Group)
## CASH FORTRESS | Score: 51/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
HERE is the rebranded shell of QuantaSing Group, a Chinese online adult-learning company that has explicitly pivoted out of education and into pop toys. The 20-F is candid: "QuantaSing Group Limited is a pop toy company," operating the WAKUKU and other IP under the "HERE奇梦岛" brand following the March 2025 acquisition of Shenzhen Letsvan (Yiqi Culture). The company terminated its VIE contractual arrangements during 2025 and exited individual online learning. Ticker changed from QSG to HERE on Nasdaq in November 2025. FY2025 (June year-end) total revenue was RMB2,725.6 million (~US$380.5M), with FY2026 pop-toy revenue guided to RMB750–800M. The screen flag — net cash near US$700M against a sub-US$200M market cap — reflects accumulated education-era profits plus IPO proceeds, with FY2025 net income of RMB356.6M and a US$0.067/share special dividend already paid.

The bear case: the entire investment case is a category pivot from regulated ed-tech (with collapsing economics) into trend-driven plush collectibles (WAKUKU vs. Pop Mart's Labubu). Trend toy IP is fashion — high gross margins one cycle, unsold inventory the next. The reported cash is consolidated, but a substantial chunk sits inside the now-unwound VIE structure and at mainland subsidiaries; offshore distribution requires PRC SAFE approvals. The Letsvan deal added goodwill and minority interests; the legacy education business is being deconsolidated, so reported FY2025 numbers are not "normalized." HFCAA inspection risk remains. And consumer-facing pop toys give zero recurring revenue base — every quarter is an IP-by-IP execution test.

The bull case: the company has already returned cash (the FY2025 cash flow statement shows "cash paid for repurchase of the ADSs of RMB25.8 million... and cash paid for a special dividends of RMB78.1 million"). FY2026 pop-toy revenue guidance of RMB750–800M (vs. the consolidated 2025 base) implies the new business alone could approximate the market cap if margins normalize toward Pop Mart's. WAKUKU has reportedly crossed 1 billion organic digital impressions. With ~US$700M of cash and a clean exit from regulated learning, management has optionality — buyback, dividend, or M&A — that the share price doesn't credit.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The 20-F notes the company "began to operate in 2025 along with the acquisition of equity interests in Shenzhen Letsvan" and that prior-period statements are "not directly comparable with or indicative of the performance of our current business." Translation: every historical line — revenue mix, gross margin, ROE, even the "ed-tech VIE" risk factor — is being rewritten. The 20-F also discloses that net cash used in financing for FY2025 was RMB105.6 million driven by ADS repurchases and the special cash dividend funded "by surplus cash and to be paid" — meaning offshore cash is reachable, the company has used it, and the precedent matters more than future capital allocation promises. The pop-toy P&L is also new, so revenue concentration in WAKUKU and 14 other IPs is a hidden Herfindahl risk.
