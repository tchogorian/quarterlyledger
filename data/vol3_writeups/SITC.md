# {NUMBER}. SITC — SITE Centers Corp.
## DISTRESSED ARB | Score: 58/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY
SITE Centers is the residual real estate vehicle left over after the October 1, 2024 spin-off of Curbline Properties (CURB), which received the company's 79 convenience retail properties — the highest-quality, lowest-cap-rate slice of the portfolio. What remains at SITC is a thin and shrinking collection of larger open-air shopping centers, predominantly community/power-center format, in active disposition mode. The 10-K describes the entity as essentially executing a wholly-owned wind-down: management is "currently in various stages of marketing several wholly-owned assets," and the company expressly references the "anticipated winding up of its operations." Aggregate occupancy was 85.9% on a pro rata basis at 12/31/2025, down from 90.6% the year prior — a meaningful step-down that reflects both portfolio mix and tenant attrition.

The bear case is that the 1.6x P/E and 0.85x P/B are accounting artifacts, not fundamental value signals. Earnings have been inflated by gains on disposition; book value will continue to be eroded by any disposition prices below carrying value, plus continued G&A drag during the wind-down. The remaining centers are the harder-to-sell, lower-quality power-center boxes that institutional buyers are walking away from. The Mortgage Facility imposes ongoing financing friction, and the company has paid out substantial special dividends ($227.7 million increase in dividends in 2025), shrinking the equity base further. There is real risk that the residual NAV falls short of the current market quote once dispositions clear at realized prices, particularly if cap rates expand for off-mall power centers.

The bull case is a classic liquidation arbitrage: the market is mis-pricing a vehicle whose stated business is to monetize its remaining assets and return capital. If the disposition program clears at or near carrying value, the stub equity should distribute meaningfully more in special dividends than the current market cap implies. The 10-K confirms management is actively marketing assets and the Mortgage Facility provided liquidity to retire senior unsecured notes, simplifying the capital structure. Class A Preferred shares were redeemed for $175.0 million in 2024, which removes a senior claim. For a patient distressed-arb investor with a 12-24 month horizon willing to underwrite real-estate broker quotes, this is exactly the type of vehicle that screens hate but settles cleanly.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
The post-spin SITC is structurally not a going-concern REIT — the 10-K openly references the "anticipated winding up of its operations," which most data providers do not flag. The remaining unencumbered asset pool is shrinking (the Mortgage Facility encumbers many of the better assets), so timing of dispositions matters more than reported FFO. The Shared Services Agreement with Curbline means a meaningful portion of the cost base is contractually tied to a separate company's operating tempo. Combined GLA, occupancy step-down to 85.9%, and ABR of $22.61/sf are below peer open-air averages, confirming the lower-quality residual. Dividend cadence going forward should be lumpy special distributions, not a steady REIT yield.
