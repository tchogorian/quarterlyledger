# {NUMBER}. SEVN — Seven Hills Realty Trust
## BELOW-BOOK QUALITY | Score: 53/100

### ANALYST SUMMARY

Seven Hills Realty Trust is an externally managed commercial-mortgage REIT whose entire portfolio is 24 floating-rate first-mortgage loans on transitional middle-market CRE properties, with aggregate loan commitments of $724.5M, a weighted-average all-in yield of 7.92% and weighted-average maximum maturity of 2.6 years. As of December 31, 2025 total assets were $820.9M, loans held for investment net of $8.8M CECL reserve were $676.9M, secured financing facilities totaled $487.7M and shareholders' equity was $328.7M against 22.6M shares outstanding (book value ~$14.55). FY2025 net income was $15.4M ($1.00 per share) on $26.9M of net interest income from loans plus $2.5M of REO income. The REIT distributed $1.26 per share in 2025 and recently set the Q1 2026 dividend at $0.28 ($1.12 annualized), a 22% step down following the December 2025 rights offering.

The bear case is in the watch list. Of 24 loans, four (17% of portfolio amortized cost, $115.5M) are risk-rated 4 — meaning higher than expected risk of loss — and three of those four are Office collateral in Dallas, Plano and Carlsbad with 72%–78% LTVs and 2026 maturities. Office is also the second-largest property type at 19% of the book. The CRE bridge lending book is by definition a "transitional CRE" book, which the 10-K defines as properties undergoing redevelopment that may or may not stabilize on schedule. Fee leakage to RMR/Tremont (base management $4.4M plus incentive $0.6M plus reimbursed shared services $2.0M = $7.0M, or ~24% of total revenue) is a permanent drag relative to internally managed peers.

The bull case is the recapitalized balance sheet at a discount to book. The fully-backstopped December 2025 rights offering raised $61.4M in net proceeds at $8.65, lifted share count from 14.9M to 22.6M, and equipped Tremont with capacity to deploy more than $200M of new loans into a market where competitors are wounded. Cash on hand jumped from $70.8M to $123.5M. Adam Portnoy/ABP Trust and Tremont participated in the offering, signaling sponsor alignment. With shares trading around $8.40 versus book of $14.55, you pay 0.58x book for a portfolio yielding nearly 8% with $36.9M of unfunded commitments to redeploy as rates and credit spreads grind.

### WHAT A SCREEN CAN'T SEE
SEVN is small and concentrated to a degree the screen does not display: 24 loans means a single risk-4 office defaulting at, say, 25% loss-given-default would consume roughly two years of net income at a stroke. The Dallas Office loan ($46.8M / $44.2M funded, 72% LTV, August 2026 maturity) alone is 6.5% of the portfolio. The CECL reserve at $8.8M (1.28% of loans) is thinner than peers carrying meaningful office exposure. The external manager structure also has a real-money cost: the November 2025 RMR Group transaction shows the REIT acquired loans from an affiliated transaction, demonstrating the related-party flow of paper between SEVN and the manager. That dependency has historically helped origination volume but is a governance item the screen would never flag.
