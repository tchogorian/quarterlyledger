Methodology

How the Register is built.

A ten-thousand-filing funnel, a ten-point screen, a four-tier classification, and a hand-written tear sheet for every name that survives.

The universe

Each quarter we pull the full XBRL filing set from EDGAR plus structured data for foreign issuers traded in the U.S. market. The April 2026 starting universe was 10,391 issuers. We exclude blank-check SPACs pre-merger, pink-sheet shells without meaningful filings, and ETFs/passive vehicles.

The screen

Every issuer is scored against ten filing-level criteria drawn from the Munger and Buffett frameworks. The score runs 0–100; passing is a composite judgment, not a line.

  1. Return on invested capital — stability across a full cycle, not one good year.
  2. Free cash flow conversion — reported FCF vs. reported net income, adjusted for stock-based comp.
  3. Balance sheet — net debt / EBITDA, interest coverage, and maturity schedule.
  4. Gross margin durability — five-year trend, not one quarter.
  5. Unit economics — per-customer, per-store, or per-well depending on industry.
  6. Insider alignment — ownership, option grants, and recent transaction records.
  7. Capital allocation — buybacks vs. issuance, dividends vs. reinvestment, history of accretive M&A.
  8. Competitive position — market share trend, pricing power, switching costs as evidenced in filings.
  9. Accounting quality — auditor comments, restatements, SBC as percent of revenue.
  10. Price vs. intrinsic range — current EV vs. a conservative DCF band using filing-level data.

The tiers

Tier I

Institutional Orphans

Zero or near-zero sell-side coverage. Float too small or too insider-controlled for most funds. Priced where no one is looking. The strongest members of this tier pass the full ten-point screen and trade at multiples that make no sense if the filings are taken seriously.

Tier II

Special Situations

Forced selling, tender offers, spin-offs, activist arbitrage, capital-structure dislocations. These names have a time horizon attached — a clock on the process, a catalyst on the calendar. The tier-II register tracks the progress of each situation between issues.

Tier III

Quality at Panic Prices

Filing-verified franchises — durable ROIC, strong balance sheet, predictable cash generation — trading at historically low multiples. These are not cheap; they are cheap for what they are. The register tracks each against its own ten-year multiple band.

Tier IV

Quantitative Survivors

Names that pass the full ten-point screen without an obvious qualitative story. No activist, no forced seller, no panic — just financials that hold up under every cut. These tend to be the quietest entries in the register and often move tiers as qualitative work catches up.

What the tear sheets contain

Every tier and extended-register name gets a full tear sheet: masthead, eyebrow, classification; a metric rail (market cap, revenue, net income, free cash flow, leverage, yield); a chart frame (price history, OHLC, loaded from our quarterly price snapshot); analyst summary; thesis; catalysts; risks; and what a screen can't see — filing-level detail that isn't encoded in any database.

Revisions

Between issues, material events — earnings surprises large enough to change tier, activist disclosures, closed tenders, restatements — are logged as revision notes on the affected tear sheet. The full re-ranking happens each quarter.

What we do not do

No price targets. No twelve-month return forecasts. No quarterly earnings predictions. No trading signals. No paid promotion. No sponsored content. The Register is independent and unfunded by issuer relationships.
